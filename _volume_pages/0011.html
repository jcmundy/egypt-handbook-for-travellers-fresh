{% raw %}PBEFACE.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Ever since the attention of the civilized world was re¬{% endraw %}
{% raw %}directed to Egypt at the beginning of the 19th century, the{% endraw %}
{% raw %}scientific investigation of its innumerable monuments has{% endraw %}
{% raw %}pointed with ever-growing certainty to the valley of the Nile{% endraw %}
{% raw %}as the cradle of history and of human culture. At the same{% endraw %}
{% raw %}time Egypt, like other Eastern countries, possesses high nat¬{% endraw %}
{% raw %}ural attractions, in the peculiar charms of its Oriental cli¬{% endraw %}
{% raw %}mate, the singularly clear atmosphere, the wonderful colour¬{% endraw %}
{% raw %}ing and effects of light and shade, the exuberant fertility{% endraw %}
{% raw %}of the cultivated districts contrasted with the solemn, awe-{% endraw %}
{% raw %}inspiring desert, and the manners, customs, and appearance{% endraw %}
{% raw %}of a most interesting and most diversified population.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}The present fifth edition of the Handbook to Egypt{% endraw %}
{% raw %}comprises in its scope the regions previously treated of in{% endraw %}
{% raw %}separate volumes devoted to Lower Egypt and Upper Egypt{% endraw %}
{% raw %}respectively. Though the text has thus been curtailed by at{% endraw %}
{% raw %}least one-third, the Editor believes, that by confining himself{% endraw %}
{% raw %}to essential points and by carefully arranging his material,{% endraw %}
{% raw %}he has succeeded in rendering the present edition no less{% endraw %}
{% raw %}useful than the previous ones in supplying the traveller with{% endraw %}
{% raw %}the necessary information regarding the country and the{% endraw %}
{% raw %}people he is about to visit, in protecting him against extor¬{% endraw %}
{% raw %}tion and in rendering him, as far as the nature of the case{% endraw %}
{% raw %}permits, independent of outside assistance. An attempt has{% endraw %}
{% raw %}been made to indicate clearly the most important among the{% endraw %}
{% raw %}bewildering multiplicity of the monuments of antiquity; and{% endraw %}
{% raw %}the descriptions of these have been so arranged that, assum¬{% endraw %}
{% raw %}ing the traveller to have previously read at his leisure our{% endraw %}
{% raw %}account of the origin, history, and significance of a partic¬{% endraw %}
{% raw %}ular temple, or tomb, etc., he will find adequate guidance{% endraw %}
{% raw %}on the spot in that portion of our description that is printed{% endraw %}
{% raw %}in larger type, while those who have time and inclination for{% endraw %}
{% raw %}a more thorough examination, will find additional particulars{% endraw %}
{% raw %}in small type. A first visit to the Temple of Dendera, for{% endraw %}
{% raw %}example, may in this manner be accomplished in about an{% endraw %}
{% raw %}hour, {% endraw %}{% raw %}which is approximately the time allowed to passengers{% endraw %}
{% raw %}by mail-steamer.{% endraw %}
