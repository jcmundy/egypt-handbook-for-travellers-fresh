{% raw %}PREFACE.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}The materials for the first edition of the volume on Lower{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Egypt were mainly furnished in 1877 by Professor G. Ebers{% endraw %}
{% raw %}of Leipsic. Among the contributions prepared for the{% endraw %}
{% raw %}English version were those of the distinguished Egyptolo¬{% endraw %}
{% raw %}gist, Dr. Samuel Birch. The volume on Upper Egypt, orig¬{% endraw %}
{% raw %}inally published in 1891, was founded on material contributed{% endraw %}
{% raw %}by Professor Ebers and Professor J. Diimichen of Strassburg.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}The present combined edition, like the preceding, has been{% endraw %}
{% raw %}completely revised by Professor Georg Steindorff of Leipsic,{% endraw %}
{% raw %}who devoted a journey to Egypt in 1900 mainly to this object.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}The introductory articles bearing their names have also been{% endraw %}
{% raw %}specially revised by Franz-Pasha and Dr. Georg Schweinfurth{% endraw %}
{% raw %}of Cairo. For other important contributions the Editor is{% endraw %}
{% raw %}obliged to Dr. Borchardt of Cairo, to Dr. Leigh Canney of{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Assuan, to Mr. Percy E. Newberry of London, to Dr. H. Schafer{% endraw %}
{% raw %}of Berlin, to Dr. H. Thiersch of Munich, to Prof. Moritz of{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Cairo, and to Dr. J. David of Khartum.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}The Maps and Plans have been the object of the Editor's{% endraw %}
{% raw %}special care, and all have been carefully revised by Prof.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Steindorff, with the aid of the most recent publications and{% endraw %}
{% raw %}of much valuable unpublished material, kindly placed at his{% endraw %}
{% raw %}disposal by Prof. Loret of Lyons, M. G. Legrain of Cairo,{% endraw %}
{% raw %}and others. To the present edition have been added entire¬{% endraw %}
{% raw %}ly new maps of the Fayum, of the Nile from Cairo to Assuan{% endraw %}
{% raw %}(3 sheets; 1:500,000), and of the environs of Assdan; new{% endraw %}
{% raw %}plans of Port Sa'id, Luxor, etc.; and many new ground-plans.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}The spelling of the names on the new maps follows the offi¬{% endraw %}
{% raw %}cial French system of transliteration adopted in the 'Recense-{% endraw %}
{% raw %}ment general de l'Egypte du 1 juin 1897', published in 1898.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}In all the maps and plans the North is at the top of the page,{% endraw %}
{% raw %}except where there is an express indication to the contrary.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Ancient Egyptian names are transliterated on the system{% endraw %}
{% raw %}indicated at p. cvi. Arabic names have been specially revised{% endraw %}
{% raw %}by Professor Socin of Leipsic.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Hotels, etc., see p. xviii. Hotels which cannot be ac¬{% endraw %}
{% raw %}curately characterized without exposing the Editor to the risk{% endraw %}
{% raw %}of legal proceedings are left unmentioned.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}To hotel - proprietors, tradesmen, and others the Editor{% endraw %}
{% raw %}begs to intimate that a character for fair dealing and cour¬{% endraw %}
{% raw %}tesy towards travellers forms the sole passport to his com¬{% endraw %}
{% raw %}mendation, and that advertisements of every kind are strictly{% endraw %}
{% raw %}excluded from his Handbooks. Hotel-keepers are also warned{% endraw %}
{% raw %}against persons representing themselves as agents for Bae¬{% endraw %}
{% raw %}deker's Handbooks.{% endraw %}
