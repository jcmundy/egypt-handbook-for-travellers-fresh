{% raw %}I. Preliminary Information.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}(1). Flan of Tour. Season. Expenses. Money. Equipment.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Travelling Companions.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Plan. The facilities for travel in Egypt are now such that the{% endraw %}
{% raw %}intending visitor may make an outline of his tour at home with{% endraw %}
{% raw %}almost as great ease as for most of the countries of Europe. During{% endraw %}
{% raw %}the travelling season, moreover, the weather is always fine (comp.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}below), and never causes disappointment and derangement of plans{% endraw %}
{% raw %}as in most other countries. A glimpse of the country may be ob¬{% endraw %}
{% raw %}tained in four or five weeks (exclusive of the journey out) as fol¬{% endraw %}
{% raw %}lows: 2-3 days may be devoted to Alexandria and the journey{% endraw %}
{% raw %}thence to Cairo, 10-12 days may be spent in Cairo and its neigh¬{% endraw %}
{% raw %}bourhood in the manner suggested at p. 30, 3 days may be given to{% endraw %}
{% raw %}the Fayum, 3-4 days may be occupied by the Suez Canal and ex¬{% endraw %}
{% raw %}cursions from it, and 14 days may be devoted to Upper Egypt (rail¬{% endraw %}
{% raw %}way to Assuan), while a few days must be set aside for resting.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}The whole time, however, might very pleasantly be spent at Cairo{% endraw %}
{% raw %}alone, the most interesting point in the tour.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Season. The best time for a tour in Egypt is between Nov. 1st{% endraw %}
{% raw %}and May 1st. In Alexandria stormy and rainy weather prevails from{% endraw %}
{% raw %}December to March, but in the interior of Egypt, to the S. of a{% endraw %}
{% raw %}line joining Damanhur, Tanta, and Mansura, the case is completely{% endraw %}
{% raw %}altered. Even in the Delta, however, marked falls in temperature{% endraw %}
{% raw %}(sometimes to 43° Fahr ) occur between the end of November and{% endraw %}
{% raw %}the end of March, and rain-storms, rendering the roads almost im¬{% endraw %}
{% raw %}passable, are not infrequent. December and January are sometimes{% endraw %}
{% raw %}very cold in Cairo, which is the more inconvenient as there are no{% endraw %}
{% raw %}adequate heating-arrangements in the houses; but Nov., Feb., and{% endraw %}
{% raw %}March are very fine, as also usually are Oct., April, and May,{% endraw %}
{% raw %}especially for travellers who do not object to a little heat. In Upper{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Egypt, from the beginning of November till the middle or end of{% endraw %}
{% raw %}April, there are but few days of bad weather (comp. p. xxvi); the{% endraw %}
{% raw %}prevalent temperature is that of a delicious spring or moderate{% endraw %}
{% raw %}summer. The fertilising inundation of the Nile (p. 1) has by this{% endraw %}
{% raw %}time subsided, and the whole face of the country smiles with fresh{% endraw %}
{% raw %}verdure. Those who intend to winter in Egypt should spend Nov.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}in Cairo, move on thence in Dec, on the approach of cold weather,{% endraw %}
{% raw %}to Upper Egypt (Luxor, Assuan), and return to Cairo in February.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}— In summer prices are naturally much lower.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Expenses. The cost of a tour in Egypt, and in Oriental coun¬{% endraw %}
{% raw %}tries generally, is greater than that of a visit to most parts of Europe,{% endraw %}
{% raw %}and the traveller should estimate his average daily expenditure at{% endraw %}
{% raw %}not less than 25-30s. (Steamboat-fares are of course extra; pp. 1-4.){% endraw %}
