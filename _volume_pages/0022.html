{% raw %}STEAMERS.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}(3). Conveyances.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Steamers. Egypt may be reached from England cither 'by{% endraw %}
{% raw %}steamer direct or by overland route to one of the principal Mediter¬{% endraw %}
{% raw %}ranean ports and thence by steamer. Particulars ofthe various routes{% endraw %}
{% raw %}are given in R. 1. Whether the traveller returns westwards on leav¬{% endraw %}
{% raw %}ing Egypt, or intends to proceed to Syria or elsewhere, it is import¬{% endraw %}
{% raw %}ant that he should be familiar with the principal steamboat services.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}The vessels of the principal lines are nearly on a par with regard to{% endraw %}
{% raw %}comfort and speed, the British and German steamers being perhaps{% endraw %}
{% raw %}slightly superior, and the Italian steamers slightly inferior to the{% endraw %}
{% raw %}others. In autumn and winter vessels bound for Egypt, and in spring{% endraw %}
{% raw %}those returning westwards are apt to be crowded.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}The time-tables of the Peninsular & Oriental Steam Navigation Co. may{% endraw %}
{% raw %}be obtained in London at 122 Leadenhall St., E.C., or at Northumberland{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Avenue, S.W.; and those of the Orient & Pacific Co. (' Orient-Pacific Line'1){% endraw %}
{% raw %}at 0 Fenchurch Avenue, E.C., or at 16 Cockspur St., S.W. The North{% endraw %}
{% raw %}German Lloyd Co. has agencies at 2 King William St., E.C., and 32 Cock-{% endraw %}
{% raw %}spur St., S.W., and the Navigazione Generate Italiana at 8 Leadenhall St.,{% endraw %}
{% raw %}E.C. Those who purpose including Syria, Greece, and Constantinople in{% endraw %}
{% raw %}their Oriental tour should also, before leaving home, write to the lAd¬{% endraw %}
{% raw %}ministration des Services des Messageries Maritimes, 16 Rue Cannebiire,{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Marseilles'' for a '■Livret des Lignes de la Miditerranie et de la Mer Noire'',{% endraw %}
{% raw %}and to the 'Oesterreichische Lloyd, Trieste' for 'Information for Passengers{% endraw %}
{% raw %}by the Austrian Lloyd's Steam Navigation Company' (published in Englishl.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}With the aid of these time-tables, the traveller will have little difficulty{% endraw %}
{% raw %}in making out his programme. See also 'Baedeker's Palestine and Syria'{% endraw %}
{% raw %}(sold at the bookshops of Alexandria and Cairo).{% endraw %}
{% raw %}The Food , which is included in the first-class fare and usually in{% endraw %}
{% raw %}the second also, is always abundant and of good quality. Wine is not{% endraw %}
{% raw %}included in the fare except on board the French, Austrian, and Italian{% endraw %}
{% raw %}steamers. Many travellers prefer the cookery on board the French and{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Austrian steamers as being lighter and better suited to the climate than{% endraw %}
{% raw %}that of the British vessels. Passengers who are prevented by sickness{% endraw %}
{% raw %}from partaking of the regular repasts are supplied with lemonade and{% endraw %}
{% raw %}other refreshments gratis.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}The Steward's Fee, which the passenger pays at the end ofthe{% endraw %}
{% raw %}voyage, is generally from 1/2 fr. to 1 fr. per day; but more is expected{% endraw %}
{% raw %}if unusual trouble has been given.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}The Baths provided for the use of passengers in the British and{% endraw %}
{% raw %}some of the other vessels may be used without extra charge, but the{% endraw %}
{% raw %}attendant expects a fee at the end of the voyage.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Tickets should never be taken at foreign ports through the medium{% endraw %}
{% raw %}of commissionnaires or other persons who offer their services, but the{% endraw %}
{% raw %}traveller should, if possible, purchase them at the office in person. The{% endraw %}
{% raw %}tickets bear the name of the passenger and the name and hour of depar¬{% endraw %}
{% raw %}ture of the vessel. Return or circular tickets (to Syria and Constantinople){% endraw %}
{% raw %}and family tickets for three or more persons are generally issued at a{% endraw %}
{% raw %}reduced rate, hut no reduction is made on the charge for food. A child{% endraw %}
{% raw %}of 2-10 years pays half-fare, but must share the berth of its attendant;{% endraw %}
{% raw %}for two children a whole berth is allowed.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Luggage of 150-220 lbs. is allowed to first-class, and of 85-135 lbs. to{% endraw %}
{% raw %}second-class passengers.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Embarkation. Passengers should be on board an hour before the{% endraw %}
{% raw %}advertised time of starting. At Marseilles, Trieste, and Brindisi the{% endraw %}
{% raw %}vessels start from the quays, so that passengers can walk on board; but{% endraw %}
{% raw %}at Venice and Naples passengers are conveyed to the steamers in small{% endraw %}
{% raw %}boats, for which the charge at all the Italian ports is 1 franc or lira for{% endraw %}
