{% raw %}POST AND TELEGRAPH OFFICES. xix{% endraw %}
{% raw %}In other towns the hotels are much inferior. The more remote{% endraw %}
{% raw %}a place is from the ordinary track of European travellers, the poorer{% endraw %}
{% raw %}the inns are according to European ideas; and houses hearing most{% endraw %}
{% raw %}pretentious names are often nothing more than miserable inns.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}(5). Fost and Telegraph. Offices.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}The Egyptian Postal System (pp. 5, 26) is admirably organised,{% endraw %}
{% raw %}not only in all the principal towns but also in the smaller towns of{% endraw %}
{% raw %}the Delta and Upper Egypt. The officials are civil and attentive.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}The addresses of letters destined for Egypt should always he written{% endraw %}
{% raw %}very distinctly (particularly the initial letters). They had better be{% endraw %}
{% raw %}directed to the hotel at which the traveller intends to stay, or the{% endraw %}
{% raw %}traveller may leave his local address at the Cairo Post Office and{% endraw %}
{% raw %}have his letters forwarded thence. On leaving for Upper Egypt{% endraw %}
{% raw %}travellers should notify the postal authorities at Cairo, so that let¬{% endraw %}
{% raw %}ters may be punctually forwarded; passengers by the Nile steamers{% endraw %}
{% raw %}may have their correspondence looked after by the steamboat com¬{% endraw %}
{% raw %}pany. — Registered Letters not addressed to a hotel are not delivered{% endraw %}
{% raw %}to the addressee unless he gets a resident or the consular kavass{% endraw %}
{% raw %}(p. xx) to testify to his identity; those addressed to a hotel are{% endraw %}
{% raw %}delivered on presentation of the official notification of their arrival,{% endraw %}
{% raw %}bearing the stamp of the hotel. — The Postage for letters within{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Egypt is 5 milliemes; to other countries in the Postal Union 10 mil¬{% endraw %}
{% raw %}liemes; foreign Post Cards, 4 milliemes. — Parcels not exceeding{% endraw %}
{% raw %}11 lbs. in weight may be sent to the countries of the Union for{% endraw %}
{% raw %}9 piastres, and must be accompanied by two declarations (one in{% endraw %}
{% raw %}French, one in the language of the country of destination). Parcels{% endraw %}
{% raw %}not exceeding 3 lbs. may be sent from England via P. & 0. steamer{% endraw %}
{% raw %}for Is., from 3 lbs. to 7lbs. 2s., from 7 lbs. to 1 libs.3s.; via France{% endraw %}
{% raw %}and Italy the rates are 2s., 3s., 4s. — Post Office Orders are issued{% endraw %}
{% raw %}in Great Britain for payment in Egypt at the following rates of com¬{% endraw %}
{% raw %}mission: for sums not exceeding 1l., 6d.; Ql., Is.; 10l., Is. 6d.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Telegraphs. There are two telegraph - systems in Egypt, the{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Egyptian and the English. Messages within Egypt may be sent only{% endraw %}
{% raw %}by the former, which has over 200 stations, of which about 30 are{% endraw %}
{% raw %}open day and night. The tariff is 2 pias. for 8 words or less, and{% endraw %}
{% raw %}V2Pias. for each additional word. Telegrams may be sent in any{% endraw %}
{% raw %}European language, except from the smaller stations, where Arabic{% endraw %}
{% raw %}messages only are accepted. — Telegrams to Europe should he sent{% endraw %}
{% raw %}by the English Eastern Co., via, Malta. The following is the tariff{% endraw %}
{% raw %}of the English telegraph: each word (not exceeding ten letters; if{% endraw %}
{% raw %}longer, it is reckoned as two words) to Great Britain 83 mill.; to{% endraw %}
{% raw %}North America 120-200 mill.; to Austria 73 mill.; to France 74 mill.;{% endraw %}
{% raw %}to Italy 65 mill.; to Switzerland 69 mill.; to Germany 76mill.; to{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Russia 88 mill. — A telegram from Great Britain to Alexandria costs{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Is. Id. per word; to other parts of Egypt Is. 10d., 2s., 2s. 3d., or{% endraw %}
{% raw %}2s. Gd.{% endraw %}
