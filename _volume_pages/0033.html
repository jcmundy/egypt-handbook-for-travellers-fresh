{% raw %}Mena House Hotel (p. 25), 8 M. to the W. of Cairo, stands near{% endraw %}
{% raw %}the N. side of the Great Pyramid of Gizeh, on the verge of the{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Libyan Desert. The mean maximum temperature is 69° in Dec,{% endraw %}
{% raw %}66° in Jan., 72° in Feb., 74° in March, and 80° in April. The{% endraw %}
{% raw %}mean minimum for the four months Dec. to April is 50°. The{% endraw %}
{% raw %}relative humidity (i.e. the amount of moisture, in relation to the{% endraw %}
{% raw %}temperature at the time, that the air holds out of a possible 100 per{% endraw %}
{% raw %}cent) from Dec. to March is 58 per cent by day (8 a. m. to 6 p. in.){% endraw %}
{% raw %}and 80 per cent at night (8 p. m. to 6 a. m.). Dew falls in winter{% endraw %}
{% raw %}on about two nights out of three. At both Mena House and Helwan{% endraw %}
{% raw %}the prevailing winds and the amount of riin are probably much the{% endraw %}
{% raw %}same as in Cairo. The purity of the air at both places is marked. —{% endraw %}
{% raw %}The medical and sanitary arrangements are excellent; and there is a{% endraw %}
{% raw %}resident nurse under the orders of the physician who resides there.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Helwan (p. 154), 15 M. to the S. of Cairo and ll/2 M. from the{% endraw %}
{% raw %}cultivated land, is 115 ft. above the river. The mean maximum{% endraw %}
{% raw %}temperature is 70° in Dec, 67° in Jan., 73° in Feb., and 76° in{% endraw %}
{% raw %}March. The mean minimum for these four months is 50°. The{% endraw %}
{% raw %}daily range of temperature is 21°. Dew rarely falls. The relative{% endraw %}
{% raw %}humidity from Dec. to March is 47 per cent by day, 66 per cent at{% endraw %}
{% raw %}night. — Helwan has the advantage of being in the desert in a pure{% endraw %}
{% raw %}atmosphere. It also has warm natural springs of three kinds: viz.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}sulphurated, carbonated iron, and saline water, each of which is{% endraw %}
{% raw %}richer in natural constituents than the corresponding springs at Aix-{% endraw %}
{% raw %}les-Bains, Harrogate, Buxton, etc The cases suitable for the baths{% endraw %}
{% raw %}here are such as would derive benefit from hydrotherapeutic treat¬{% endraw %}
{% raw %}ment as carried on at Harrogate, Bath, Aix, etc. A large new Bath{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Establishment has been erected. Two English physicians and a{% endraw %}
{% raw %}trained English nurse are resident at Helwan.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Luxor (p. 233) is situated about 450 M. to the S. of Cairo, in{% endraw %}
{% raw %}the Theban plain and on the right bank of the river. The prevailing{% endraw %}
{% raw %}winds are N.W. and N., but the force of the wind is probably less{% endraw %}
{% raw %}than at any of the other stations in Egypt. The mean maximum{% endraw %}
{% raw %}temperature is 76° in Dec, 74° in Jan., 78° in Feb., and 85° in{% endraw %}
{% raw %}March. The mean minimum for these four months is 50°. The{% endraw %}
{% raw %}relative humidity is 41 per cent by day, 64 per cent at night. Dew{% endraw %}
{% raw %}falls about every third night in Jan.; it rarely falls in other months.{% endraw %}
