{% raw %}HEALTH RESORTS.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}It has three or four hotels, two European physicians, and a resident{% endraw %}
{% raw %}nurse. — The cooking and invalid cooking at the Luxor Hotel are{% endraw %}
{% raw %}very good. The Grand Hotel is pleasantly situated, its rooms are{% endraw %}
{% raw %}spacious, and its sanitary arrangements good, while the Karnak Hotel{% endraw %}
{% raw %}is also well adapted for invalids. — The temperature is 6-8° warmer{% endraw %}
{% raw %}than at Mena House and Helwan.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Assuan (p. 327) is situated at the First Cataract, also on the{% endraw %}
{% raw %}right bank of the river. The prevailing winds are, as at Luxor,{% endraw %}
{% raw %}N.W. and N. in winter. The mean maximum temperature is 78° in{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Dec. and Jan., 82° in Feb., and 91° in March. The mean minimum{% endraw %}
{% raw %}for these four months is 55°; and the relative humidity is 35 per{% endraw %}
{% raw %}cent by day, 49 per cent at night. Dew does not fall at Assuan.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}— Assuan is more under the immediate influence of the desert,{% endraw %}
{% raw %}but it is exposed to a rather stronger wind than Luxor. The air is{% endraw %}
{% raw %}bracing, although 4° warnjer than at Luxor, and it is purer than the{% endraw %}
{% raw %}air of any other Egyptian resort. The importance of the extra{% endraw %}
{% raw %}warmth of Upper Egypt must not be lost sight of, in cases where it{% endraw %}
{% raw %}is imperative that the action of the skin should be at its highest{% endraw %}
{% raw %}level — especially as with this warmth a bracing effect is obtained{% endraw %}
{% raw %}from the dryness of the air. The relative humidity is low, partly{% endraw %}
{% raw %}on account of the higher temperature and partly because the air is{% endraw %}
{% raw %}absolutely drier than that of other resorts. The beauty of the sur¬{% endraw %}
{% raw %}roundings and the interest of the Cataract lend a peculiar charm to{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Assuan. — The accommodation for invalids is very good. Invalid{% endraw %}
{% raw %}cooking is specially attended to and the sanitary arrangements are{% endraw %}
{% raw %}good. An English physician is in residence at Assuan in winter.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Invalids who have decided to visit Egypt should remember that{% endraw %}
{% raw %}a stay of six weeks, even in Upper Egypt, can hardly be expected{% endraw %}
{% raw %}to make a permanent improvement in their health; they should{% endraw %}
{% raw %}resolve to spend the whole winter there. The best time to arrive in{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Egypt is the beginning of November, for those bound for Helwan or{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Mena House, and about Nov. 10th for those going straight to Luxor{% endraw %}
{% raw %}or Assuan. The descent of the Nile before the middle of March is{% endraw %}
{% raw %}not recommended, for the N. wind is very cold and in some cases{% endraw %}
{% raw %}the patient must remain in the cabin or go down by train. An{% endraw %}
{% raw %}English physician can be consulted on the larger steamers.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Patients should not leave Egypt until the third week in April{% endraw %}
{% raw %}at the earliest. They will find at Beyrout, Athens, Corfu, Sicily,{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Capri and other points near Naples admirable transition-stations,{% endraw %}
{% raw %}with beautiful weather in spring.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Medical Hints. As regards clothing, invalids must remember that{% endraw %}
{% raw %}flannel or woollen materials are desirable, as it is often very cold in{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Egypt. A fur coat or similar garment is of use. The morning is colder{% endraw %}
{% raw %}than the evening, and it is easier to get a chill in the morning than{% endraw %}
