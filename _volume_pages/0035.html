{% raw %}still later, 6 p. m. (except in Jan.), and 8 p. m. in March; and{% endraw %}
{% raw %}latest of all at Assuan, — it being always understood that pre¬{% endraw %}
{% raw %}cautions as to extra clothing have been taken.— Those who are not{% endraw %}
{% raw %}invalids, and even in some cases invalids also, may sleep with the{% endraw %}
{% raw %}windows open with safety.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}If a cold is caught it is apt to result in fever or in diarrhoea,{% endraw %}
{% raw %}which may turn to dysentery. There are good chemists at Alex¬{% endraw %}
{% raw %}andria, Cairo, Luxor, and Assuan, from whom small medicine-{% endraw %}
{% raw %}chests adapted for the climate may he purchased. The advice of{% endraw %}
{% raw %}the traveller's physician at home will he useful in stocking such a{% endraw %}
{% raw %}medicine-chest. In serious cases of illness a European doctor, when{% endraw %}
{% raw %}procurable, should always be consulted, as the traveller's own ex¬{% endraw %}
{% raw %}perience acquired at home is of little avail in the climate of Egypt.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Fits of shivering are the usual prelude to an attack of fever. Qui¬{% endraw %}
{% raw %}nine is the best remedy, of which 1-3 doses should be taken on the days{% endraw %}
{% raw %}when the patient is free from fever. Rest and copious perspiration will{% endraw %}
{% raw %}also afford relief.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Diarrhoea, which is apt to turn to dysentery, is a very common com¬{% endraw %}
{% raw %}plaint in this climate, and is generally the result of eating unripe fruit{% endraw %}
{% raw %}or of catching cold. The patient should first take a slight aperient, and{% endraw %}
{% raw %}afterwards tincture of opium or concentrated tincture of camphor. A{% endraw %}
{% raw %}simple farinaceous diet (such as well-boiled rice), with tea or well{% endraw %}
{% raw %}matured, unfortified, and unsweetened red wine, will be beneficial, while{% endraw %}
{% raw %}fruit, meat, and fatty substances should be avoided. In cases both of{% endraw %}
{% raw %}diarrhoea and fever all remedies are sometimes unavailing except change{% endraw %}
{% raw %}of climate, especially if the patient is in a marshy or unhealthy locality.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Sprains, which often result from exploring ruins and caverns, are{% endraw %}
{% raw %}most effectually treated with cold compresses, while the injured limb{% endraw %}
{% raw %}should he tightly bandaged and allowed perfect rest.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}The sting of a scorpion (seldom dangerous) or bite of a snake is{% endraw %}
{% raw %}usually treated with ammonia.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Sunstroke is very common in Egypt, even in spring when the air is{% endraw %}
{% raw %}still cool. The head and neck should therefore always be carefully{% endraw %}
{% raw %}shielded in one of the ways indicated at p. xiv. The usual remedies are{% endraw %}
{% raw %}rest and shade, cold compresses, and warm baths with cold douches{% endraw %}
{% raw %}applied to the head and neck. Syringing the ears with cold salt- and-{% endraw %}
{% raw %}water is one of the best remedies.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Grey (better than blue) spectacles or veils may be used with advan¬{% endraw %}
{% raw %}tage when the eyes suffer from the glare of bright weather. Zinc eye¬{% endraw %}
{% raw %}wash, or some other innocuous lotion, should he used in such cases.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Sticking-plaster, lint, as well as all effervescing powders, and other{% endraw %}
{% raw %}medicines should he carefully kept from exposure to moisture.{% endraw %}
