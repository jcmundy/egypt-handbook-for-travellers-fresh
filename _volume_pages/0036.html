{% raw %}(11). Tobacco.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Cigar-smokers will find it very difficult to become accustomed{% endraw %}
{% raw %}to the Oriental tobacco, but they- will find tolerable cigar-shops at{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Alexandria and Cairo, most of which have been established quite{% endraw %}
{% raw %}recently. As a general rule smokers are recommended to carry with{% endraw %}
{% raw %}them, both in going to and returning from Egypt, as little tobacco{% endraw %}
{% raw %}as possible, especially if they travel by the overland route, as a{% endraw %}
{% raw %}rigorous search is often made and a heavy duty exacted, both at{% endraw %}
{% raw %}the Egyptian, and at the French, Austrian, and Italian frontiers.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Travellers returning to England direct, with their luggage booked{% endraw %}
{% raw %}through, are allowed half-a-pound of tobacco or cigars free of Eng¬{% endraw %}
{% raw %}lish duty, or they may bring three pounds on payment of the duty{% endraw %}
{% raw %}(5s. per lb.) and a small fine.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Tobacco (dukhkhdn) is kept in good condition by covering it with{% endraw %}
{% raw %}a moist cloth, with which, however, it must not come in contact.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Strong (hdmi) or mild (bdrid) may he asked for according to taste.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Stambuli is a long and fine cut tobacco, the best qualities of which{% endraw %}
{% raw %}(40-60 fr. per okka = 2 lbs. HV2 oz.) come from Roumelia and Ana¬{% endraw %}
{% raw %}tolia, and the inferior from the Greek islands. The Syrian tobacco{% endraw %}
{% raw %}(15-20 fr. per okka), which is cut less regularly, and contains parts{% endraw %}
{% raw %}of the stalk, is considered less drying to the palate than the Turkish.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}It is of two kinds, the kurdni, or light-brown, and the gebeli,{% endraw %}
{% raw %}or dark-brown, a mixture of which may be used. The latter, which{% endraw %}
{% raw %}derives its colour from being dried in the smoke of resinous woods,{% endraw %}
{% raw %}is known in Europe as 'Latakia', from the region of N. Syria where{% endraw %}
{% raw %}it is chiefly grown (Ladikiyeh), but that name is not applied to it{% endraw %}
{% raw %}in the East. The native Egyptian tobacco (dukhkhdn beledi, or{% endraw %}
{% raw %}akhdar, green tobacco), now no longer grown, was of very inferior{% endraw %}
{% raw %}quality. The natives used often to gather the leaves from the plant,{% endraw %}
{% raw %}dry them in the sun, rub them to pieces, and smoke them quite fresh.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Tumbdk, or Persian tobacco, is used in a moistened condition in{% endraw %}
{% raw %}the long nargllehs or water-pipes only, and is lighted with a parti¬{% endraw %}
{% raw %}cular kind of charcoal. The smoke of these pipes is drawn into{% endraw %}
{% raw %}the lungs.{% endraw %}
