{% raw %}The chief official in every province is the Mudlr. Each mudir{% endraw %}
{% raw %}and mohafez is assisted by a council, or 'diwan', of other officers.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}This council in the provinces consists of a Wekll, or vice-governor;{% endraw %}
{% raw %}a chief clerk, tax-gatherer, and accountant, who is always a Copt;{% endraw %}
{% raw %}a Kadi, or supreme judge, and the chief authority in spiritual mat¬{% endraw %}
{% raw %}ters; sometimes the president of a chamber of commerce and chief{% endraw %}
{% raw %}authority in civil affairs; a superintendent of police ; an architect{% endraw %}
{% raw %}for the supervision of canals and other public works; and lastly the{% endraw %}
{% raw %}chief physician of the province. The provinces are subdivided into{% endraw %}
{% raw %}districts, called Markaz, the chief officials of which (Ma'mur) are{% endraw %}
{% raw %}directly subordinate to the mudir and have their official residence{% endraw %}
{% raw %}in the more important towns. Subordinate to the ma'mur again is{% endraw %}
{% raw %}the 'Omdeh, or chief magistrate of the Nahlyeh, or commune, which{% endraw %}
{% raw %}may be either rural or urban. In the larger communes the 'omdeh{% endraw %}
{% raw %}is assisted by the Shekh el-beled, or mayor. The larger towns are{% endraw %}
{% raw %}divided into quarters each of which has its magistrate; several such{% endraw %}
{% raw %}quarters form an urban district under the jurisdiction of a Shekh et-{% endraw %}
{% raw %}tumn, or prefect, corresponding in rank to the governor of a provincial{% endraw %}
{% raw %}district.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}According to the census of 1897 the Population of Egypt proper{% endraw %}
{% raw %}was 9,734,405, of whom 9,020,404 were settled (as compared with{% endraw %}
{% raw %}6,533,261 in 1882), 601,427 were Beduins. and 112,574 were{% endraw %}
{% raw %}foreigners. The numbers of males and females were approximately{% endraw %}
{% raw %}equal. The settled population was distributed in 3692 towns and{% endraw %}
{% raw %}villages and 14,449 hamlets, farms, etc. Taking the cultivable area{% endraw %}
{% raw %}of the country into account (see p. xxxi), the above figures show a{% endraw %}
{% raw %}population of 750 per square mile, a density unequalled by any{% endraw %}
{% raw %}country in Europe (Great Britain 330 per sq. M. ; Belgium 520{% endraw %}
{% raw %}per sq. M.).{% endraw %}
