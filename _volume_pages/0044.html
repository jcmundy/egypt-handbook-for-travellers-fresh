{% raw %}There have been many hypotheses as to the origin of the Egyp¬{% endraw %}
{% raw %}tians. In all probability the rise and development of that people{% endraw %}
{% raw %}followed essentially the same course as those of other great races,{% endraw %}
{% raw %}whose geographical positions exposed them to a similar variety of{% endraw %}
{% raw %}external influences. In the course of its history this people attained{% endraw %}
{% raw %}a characteristic development of its own; but we have to enquire as{% endraw %}
{% raw %}to the nature of the original prehistoric stock. In classical antiquity{% endraw %}
{% raw %}the Egyptians were considered to he of African origin, and Diodorus{% endraw %}
{% raw %}has given expression to this view by quoting a tradition of the{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Ethiopians, according to which the Egyptians were originally an{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Ethiopian colony, just as their country itself is a product of the{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Nile. But the Greeks and Romans knew little of Central Africa,{% endraw %}
{% raw %}and, more especially, they were acquainted with none ofthe peoples{% endraw %}
{% raw %}of the Nile district except those whom we now distinguish as Hami-{% endraw %}
{% raw %}tic. When these peoples began to find their way from Asia across{% endraw %}
{% raw %}the Red Sea into Africa, they no doubt pushed down the Nile, after{% endraw %}
{% raw %}subduing the primaeval inhabitants of the river-valley. The Ethio¬{% endraw %}
{% raw %}pian tradition thus agrees with the Biblical, which describes Ham{% endraw %}
{% raw %}as the father of Mizraim and Cush — names under which the ancient{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Hebrews used to personify Egypt and Ethiopia. But since the days{% endraw %}
{% raw %}of Lepsius (1810-84) the term Hamites or Hamitic races has been{% endraw %}
{% raw %}used to distinguish that great ethnographic group of peoples which{% endraw %}
{% raw %}has, in the course of ages, altered the population of half Africa,{% endraw %}
{% raw %}ever pressing from E. to W., in a course as determined as that of{% endraw %}
{% raw %}the heavenly bodies, and driving out the primaeval population before{% endraw %}
{% raw %}them. The final stages of this migration, which, like those of the{% endraw %}
{% raw %}horse and camel, falls partly within the historic period, were reached{% endraw %}
{% raw %}when the Hamites came in contact with the Semitic races.{% endraw %}
