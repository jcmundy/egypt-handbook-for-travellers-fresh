{% raw %}The Modern Egyptians. The population of Egypt is composed{% endraw %}
{% raw %}of the following ten different elements.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}(1). The Fellahin (sing, fellah), the 'tillers' or 'peasants', with{% endraw %}
{% raw %}whom must be reckoned the Coptic peasants of Upper Egypt, form{% endraw %}
{% raw %}the bulk of the population and may be regarded as the sinews of the{% endraw %}
{% raw %}national strength. They are generally slightly above the middle{% endraw %}
{% raw %}height; their bones, and particularly their skulls, are strong and{% endraw %}
{% raw %}massive; and their wrists and ankles are powerful and somewhat{% endraw %}
{% raw %}clumsy. In all these respects the fellahin, as well as their domestic{% endraw %}
{% raw %}animals, contrast strongly with the inhabitants of the desert, the{% endraw %}
{% raw %}fellah and the Beduin differing from each other precisely in the{% endraw %}
{% raw %}same points as their respective camels. Notwithstanding this large¬{% endraw %}
{% raw %}ness of frame, however, the fellah never grows fat. The woman and{% endraw %}
{% raw %}girls are particularly remarkable for their slender build. The men{% endraw %}
{% raw %}generally keep their heads shaved, but the hair of the soldiers and{% endraw %}
{% raw %}the long tresses of the girls, though always black and thick, is smooth{% endraw %}
{% raw %}and wavy, seldom curly. The hair on the faces of the men is scantier{% endraw %}
{% raw %}and more curly.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}The chief peculiarity of the Egyptians is the remarkable close¬{% endraw %}
{% raw %}ness of their eyelashes on both lids, forming a dense, double, black{% endraw %}
{% raw %}fringe, which gives so animated an expression to their almond-{% endraw %}
{% raw %}shaped eyes. The very ancient and still existing custom of blacken¬{% endraw %}
{% raw %}ing the edges of the eyelids with antimony ('kohl'), which is said{% endraw %}
{% raw %}to serve a sanitary purpose, contributes to enhance this natural{% endraw %}
{% raw %}expression. The eyebrows are always straight and smooth, never{% endraw %}
{% raw %}bushy. The mouth is wide and thick-lipped, and very different{% endraw %}
{% raw %}from that of the Beduin or inhabitant ofthe oases. The high cheek¬{% endraw %}
{% raw %}bones, the receding forehead, the lowness of the bridge ofthe nose,{% endraw %}
{% raw %}which is always distinctly separated from the forehead, and the{% endraw %}
{% raw %}flatness of the nose itself, are the chief characteristics of the{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Egyptian skull; but, as the jaws project less than those of most{% endraw %}
{% raw %}ofthe other African coloured races, it has been assumed that the{% endraw %}
{% raw %}skull is Asiatic, and not African in shape. The Egyptian peasantry{% endraw %}
{% raw %}have a much darker complexion than their compatriots in the towns,{% endraw %}
{% raw %}and their colour deepens as we proceed southwards, from the pale{% endraw %}
{% raw %}brown of the inhabitant of the Delta to the dark bronze hue of the{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Upper Egyptians. There is, however, a difference between the tint{% endraw %}
{% raw %}of the Nubians and that of the Upper Egyptians, even where they{% endraw %}
{% raw %}live in close contiguity, the former being more of a reddish-brown.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}In the ancient representations women are painted yellow and men{% endraw %}
{% raw %}red, merely because the former were paler owing to their indoor life{% endraw %}
{% raw %}while the men were browned by labouring in the open air (Virchow).{% endraw %}
