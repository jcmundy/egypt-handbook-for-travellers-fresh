{% raw %}the Delta for the first time, must not suppose that the miserable,{% endraw %}
{% raw %}ruinous huts that meet his eye are typical of all peasants' dwellings{% endraw %}
{% raw %}in Egypt. In Central and Upper Egypt he will obtain a much more{% endraw %}
{% raw %}favourable impression. The fact is, that beneath an Egyptian sky,{% endraw %}
{% raw %}houses are not of the same paramount importance as in more northern{% endraw %}
{% raw %}regions, all that is wanted being shelter for the night. The day is{% endraw %}
{% raw %}spent in the open air, on the court in front of the hut, shaded by{% endraw %}
{% raw %}acacia trees, among whose branches the pigeons coo. Here the fellah{% endraw %}
{% raw %}spends his leisure, chatting with his neighbours and spinning wool{% endraw %}
{% raw %}from a spindle that he turns in his hand.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}The poorer peasant's mode of life is frugal in the extreme.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}The staple of his food consists of a peculiar kind of bread made of{% endraw %}
{% raw %}sorghum flour in Upper Egypt, or of maize in the Delta, wheaten{% endraw %}
{% raw %}bread being eaten by the wealthier only. This poor kind of bread{% endraw %}
{% raw %}often has a greenish colour, owing to an admixture of flour made from{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Foenum Graecum (see below). Next in importance nrthe bill of fare{% endraw %}
{% raw %}are broad beans (fill). For supper, however, even the poorest cause a{% endraw %}
{% raw %}hot repast to be prepared. This usually consists of a highly salted{% endraw %}
{% raw %}sauce made of onions and butter, or in the poorer houses of onions{% endraw %}
{% raw %}and linseed or sesame oil. Into this sauce, which in summer{% endraw %}
{% raw %}acquires a gelatinous consistency by the addition of the universal{% endraw %}
{% raw %}bamia (the capsular fruit of the Hibiscus) and various herbs, each{% endraw %}
{% raw %}member of the family dips pieces of bread held in the fingers.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Both in town and country, goats', sheep's, or buffaloes' milk also{% endraw %}
{% raw %}forms a daily article of food, but always in a sour condition or half{% endraw %}
{% raw %}converted into cheese, and in very moderate quantities only. In{% endraw %}
{% raw %}the height of summer the consumption of fruit of the cucumber{% endraw %}
{% raw %}and pumpkin species, which the land yields in abundance, is{% endraw %}
{% raw %}enormous. In spring large quantities of lettuce, radish-leaves, and{% endraw %}
{% raw %}similar green vegetables are eaten; and the lower classes consume,{% endraw %}
{% raw %}for medical purposes during the same season, considerable amounts{% endraw %}
{% raw %}of Fcenum Graecum, a clover-like plant with a somewhat disagreeable{% endraw %}
{% raw %}odour (p. lvii). In the month of Ramadan alone (p. lxxvi), when a{% endraw %}
{% raw %}rigorous fast is observed during the day, and on the three days of{% endraw %}
{% raw %}the great Beiram festival (Korhan Beiram), even the poorest members{% endraw %}
{% raw %}ofthe community indulge in meat, and it is customary to distribute{% endraw %}
{% raw %}that rare luxury to beggars at these seasons.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}The dress of the Egyptian peasant calls for little remark, espe¬{% endraw %}
{% raw %}cially as he usually works in the fields divested of everything. The{% endraw %}
{% raw %}chief articles of his wardrobe at other times are an indigo-dyed cot¬{% endraw %}
{% raw %}ton shirt (kamls), a pair of short and wide cotton breeches, a kind{% endraw %}
{% raw %}of cloak of brown, home-spun goats' wool (za'btit, 'abdyeh, or 'aba).{% endraw %}
