{% raw %}The sole wealth of Egypt is derived from its agriculture, and to{% endraw %}
{% raw %}the fellahin alone is committed the important task of tilling the soil.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}They are, indeed, neither fitted nor inclined for other work, a circum¬{% endraw %}
{% raw %}stance which proves how completely the stationary character of the{% endraw %}
{% raw %}ancient Egyptians has predominated over the restless Arabian blood,{% endraw %}
{% raw %}which has been largely infused into the native population ever since{% endraw %}
{% raw %}the valley ofthe Nile was conquered by the armies of El-Islam. The{% endraw %}
{% raw %}ancient Egyptian racial type has been preserved in extraordinary{% endraw %}
{% raw %}purity in many fellah families, especially in Upper Egypt. This is{% endraw %}
{% raw %}particularly evident in the case of the children and women, whose{% endraw %}
{% raw %}features are not concealed and distorted by veils (which the ancient{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Egyptians despised). Even among the Berbers (p. xlv), between{% endraw %}
{% raw %}the first and second cataracts, faces occur that might almost lead us{% endraw %}
{% raw %}to think that some of the pictures of the period of the old Pharaohs{% endraw %}
{% raw %}had come to life, and stood before us in flesh and blood. The modern{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Egyptians, moreover, resemble the ancient in the lot to which they{% endraw %}
{% raw %}are condemned. In ancient times the fellah, pressed into the service{% endraw %}
{% raw %}of the priests and the princes, was compelled to yield up to them{% endraw %}
{% raw %}the fruits of his toil, and his position is nearly the same at the present{% endraw %}
{% raw %}day, save that the names of his masters are changed, and he has{% endraw %}
{% raw %}obtained some relief owing to the almost entire abolition of com¬{% endraw %}
{% raw %}pulsory work.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}In early life the Egyptian peasant is remarkably docile, active,{% endraw %}
{% raw %}and intelligent, but at a later period this freshness and buoyancy are{% endraw %}
{% raw %}crushed out of him by care and poverty and his never-ceasing task{% endraw %}
{% raw %}of filling the pitcher of the Dana'ides. He ploughs and Teaps, toils{% endraw %}
{% raw %}and amasses, but he cannot with certainty regard his crops as his{% endraw %}
{% raw %}own, and the hardly earned piastre is too frequently wrested from{% endraw %}
{% raw %}him. His character, therefore, becomes like that of a gifted child,{% endraw %}
{% raw %}who has been harshly used and brought up to domestic slavery, but{% endraw %}
{% raw %}at length perceives that he has been treated with injustice, and{% endraw %}
{% raw %}whose amiability and intelligence are then superseded by sullenness{% endraw %}
{% raw %}and obstinacy. Thus down to a few years ago, as in the time of{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Ammianus Marcellinus, the fellah would often suffer the most cruel{% endraw %}
{% raw %}blows in dogged silence rather than pay the taxes demanded of him.{% endraw %}
