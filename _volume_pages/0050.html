{% raw %}Few nations in the East embraced the Gospel more zealously{% endraw %}
{% raw %}than the dwellers on the Nile. Accustomed as they had long been{% endraw %}
{% raw %}to regard life as a pilgrimage to death, as a school of preparation for{% endraw %}
{% raw %}another world, and weary of their motley and confused Pantheon{% endraw %}
{% raw %}of divinities, whose self-seeking priesthood designedly disguised{% endraw %}
{% raw %}the truth, they eagerly welcomed the simple doctrines of Christianity,{% endraw %}
{% raw %}which appeared so well adapted to their condition and promised{% endraw %}
{% raw %}them succour and redemption. Like Eutyches, they revered the{% endraw %}
{% raw %}divine nature of the Saviour only, in which they held that every{% endraw %}
{% raw %}human element was absorbed; and when the Council of Chalcedon{% endraw %}
{% raw %}in 451 sanctioned the doctrine that Christ combined a human with{% endraw %}
{% raw %}a divine nature, the Egyptians, with their characteristic tenacity{% endraw %}
{% raw %}adhered to their old views, and formed a sect termed Eutychians, or{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Monophysites , to which the Copts of the present day still belong.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}The name ofthe Copts is an ethnical one, being simply an Arabic cor¬{% endraw %}
{% raw %}ruption of the Greek name of Egyptians. The theory is now exploded that{% endraw %}
{% raw %}they derive their name from a certain itinerant preacher named Jacobus,{% endraw %}
{% raw %}who according to Makrizi was termed El-Beradi'i, or 'blanket-bearer', from{% endraw %}
{% raw %}the old horse-cloth worn by him when he went about preaching. This{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Jacobus promulgated the monophysite doctrine of Eutyches, which had{% endraw %}
{% raw %}found its most zealous supporter in Dioscurus, a bishop of Alexandria,{% endraw %}
{% raw %}who was declared a heretic and banished after the Council of Chalcedon ;{% endraw %}
{% raw %}and his disciples were sometimes called Jacobites. If this name had ever{% endraw %}
{% raw %}been abbreviated to Cobit or Cobt, it would probably have occurred{% endraw %}
{% raw %}frequently in the writings of Monophysites ; but there we find no trace{% endraw %}
{% raw %}of it. It is, on the other hand, quite intelligible that the word Copt,{% endraw %}
{% raw %}though originally synonymous with Egyptian, should gradually have come{% endraw %}
{% raw %}to denote a particular religious sect; for, at the period when the valley{% endraw %}
{% raw %}of the Nile was conquered by 'Amr, the native Egyptians, who almost{% endraw %}
{% raw %}exclusively held the monophysite creed, were chiefly distinguished by{% endraw %}
{% raw %}their religion from their invaders, who brought a new religious system{% endraw %}
{% raw %}from the East.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}These Egyptian Christians strenuously opposed the resolutions of{% endraw %}
{% raw %}the Council of Chalcedon, and thousands of them sacrificed their{% endraw %}
{% raw %}lives or their welfare in the fierce and sanguinary conflicts of the{% endraw %}
{% raw %}6th century, the causes of which were imperfectly understood by{% endraw %}
{% raw %}the great majority of the belligerents. The subtle dogmatic dif¬{% endraw %}
{% raw %}ferences which gave rise to these wars aroused such hatred among{% endraw %}
{% raw %}these professors of the religion of love, that the defeated Monophys¬{% endraw %}
{% raw %}ites readily welcomed the invading armies of El-Islam, or perhaps{% endraw %}
{% raw %}even invited them to their country.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}After the conquest of Egypt by the Arabs (p. 32) the Copts were{% endraw %}
{% raw %}at first treated with lenity, and were even appointed to the highest{% endraw %}
{% raw %}government offices; but they were soon doomed to suffer persecutions{% endraw %}
{% raw %}and privations of every description. These persecutions were mainly{% endraw %}
