{% raw %}In spite, however, of all these disasters, a numerous community{% endraw %}

{% raw %}accounted for by the remarkable tenacity and constancy of the{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Egyptian character. Owing, however, to the continual oppres¬{% endraw %}
{% raw %}sion and contempt to which they have been subjected, the grave{% endraw %}
{% raw %}disposition of the subjects of the Pharaohs has degenerated into{% endraw %}
{% raw %}sullen gloom, and their industry into cupidity. The rancour which{% endraw %}
{% raw %}they have so long cherished has embittered their character, while{% endraw %}
{% raw %}the persecutions they have suffered have taught them to be at one{% endraw %}
{% raw %}time cringing, and at another arrogant and overbearing. They are{% endraw %}
{% raw %}in very few respects superior to their Mohammedan countrymen.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}They generally possess a hereditary aptitude for mathematical{% endraw %}
{% raw %}science, and are therefore in great request as hook-keepers and{% endraw %}
{% raw %}accountants, but on the other hand they are entirely destitute of the{% endraw %}
{% raw %}generous and dignified disposition of the Arabs. They obey their{% endraw %}
{% raw %}law which forbids polygamy, but constantly abuse that which per¬{% endraw %}
{% raw %}mits them to indulge in spirituous liquors, drunkards being fre¬{% endraw %}
{% raw %}quently met with, even among their priests. Their divine worship{% endraw %}
{% raw %}will strike the traveller as strange, and anything but edifying or{% endraw %}
{% raw %}elevating (comp. p. 71).{% endraw %}
{% raw %}The traveller may distinguish the Copts from the Arabs by their{% endraw %}
{% raw %}dark turbans, which are generally blue or black, and their dark-{% endraw %}
{% raw %}coloured clothes. This costume was originally prescribed by their{% endraw %}
{% raw %}oppressors, and they still take a pride in it as a mark of their origin,{% endraw %}
{% raw %}though now permitted to dress as they please. A practised eye will{% endraw %}
{% raw %}also frequently detect among them the ancient Egyptian cast of{% endraw %}
{% raw %}features. Towards strangers the Copt is externally obliging, and{% endraw %}
{% raw %}when anxious to secure their favour he not unfrequently appeals to{% endraw %}
{% raw %}his Christian creed as a bond of union. Many Copts have recently{% endraw %}
{% raw %}been converted to Protestantism by American missionaries, partic¬{% endraw %}
{% raw %}ularly in Upper Egypt, chiefly through the foundation of good{% endraw %}
{% raw %}schools and the distribution of cheap Arabic Bibles. Even the{% endraw %}
{% raw %}orthodox Copts have a great reverence for the sacred volume, and it{% endraw %}
{% raw %}is not uncommon to meet with members of their sect who know the{% endraw %}
{% raw %}whole of the Gospels by heart. The Roman propaganda, which was{% endraw %}
