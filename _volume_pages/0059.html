{% raw %}c. The Nile.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}The Nile ranks with the Amazon and the Congo as one of the{% endraw %}
{% raw %}three longest rivers in the world (about 4000 miles), since its head-{% endraw %}
{% raw %}stream was proved by Dr. 0. Baumann in 1892 to be the Kagera,{% endraw %}
{% raw %}.which rises three degrees to the S. of the Equator. Throughout{% endraw %}
{% raw %}nearly the whole of its course the river is navigable, with two great{% endraw %}
{% raw %}interruptions only (between Abu Hammed and Barkal and between{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Dongola and Wadi Haifa).{% endraw %}
{% raw %}The Nile is formed by the confluence of the White and the Blue{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Nile at the town of Khartum, from which point to its principal{% endraw %}
{% raw %}mouths at Damietta and Rosetta, a distance of 1350 miles, it{% endraw %}
{% raw %}traverses an absolutely barren country, and receives one tributary{% endraw %}
{% raw %}only, the Atbara, on the east side, about 140 miles below Khar¬{% endraw %}
{% raw %}tum. Notwithstanding the immense length of the river, it very rarely{% endraw %}
{% raw %}presents the picturesque appearance of some of the great European{% endraw %}
{% raw %}and other rivers, as its banks are generally flat and monotonous,{% endraw %}
{% raw %}and it contains hardly a single island worthy of mention. The broad¬{% endraw %}
{% raw %}est parts of this portion of the Nile are a little below Khartum, a{% endraw %}
{% raw %}little above its bifurcation near Cairo, and also near Minyeh, at each{% endraw %}
{% raw %}of which places it attains a width of about 1100 yds., while the{% endraw %}
{% raw %}White Nile is of greater breadth throughout a long part of its lower{% endraw %}
{% raw %}course. As the river pursues its tortuous course through thirsty{% endraw %}
{% raw %}land, for a distance of 15 degrees of latitude, much of its water is{% endraw %}
{% raw %}consumed by evaporation and infiltration (a process by which it is{% endraw %}
{% raw %}probable that the Libyan oases are supplied with water from the{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Nubian Nile), and still more by the extensive system of artificial{% endraw %}
{% raw %}canals requisite for the irrigation of a whole kingdom. At the con¬{% endraw %}
{% raw %}fluence of the White and Blue Nile their average volumes are in the{% endraw %}
{% raw %}proportion of three to one, but the latter assumes far greater im¬{% endraw %}
{% raw %}portance when swollen by the Abyssinian rains. The Blue Nile is{% endraw %}
