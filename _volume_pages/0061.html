{% raw %}Egypt is now no longer a vast lake during the inundation as{% endraw %}
{% raw %}it formerly was, nor does the overflow of the fields take place in a{% endraw %}
{% raw %}direct manner as is commonly supposed. In Upper Egypt the water{% endraw %}
{% raw %}is conducted into a vast network of reservoirs and canals, and dis¬{% endraw %}
{% raw %}tributed as required, and special engineers are appointed for their{% endraw %}
{% raw %}supervision. The whole of the cultivable land is divided into huge{% endraw %}
{% raw %}basins (hod), in which the water introduced by the canals is main¬{% endraw %}
{% raw %}tained at a certain height until it has sufficiently saturated the soil{% endraw %}
{% raw %}and deposited the requisite quantity of mud. After the water in the{% endraw %}
{% raw %}river has subsided, that in the basins may either be discharged into{% endraw %}
{% raw %}the river or into the canals, or it may be used for filling other ba¬{% endraw %}
{% raw %}sins lying at a lower level. During these operations many of the{% endraw %}
{% raw %}villages are connected by means of embankments only, while others{% endraw %}
{% raw %}can only he reached by boat, and the whole country presents a{% endraw %}
{% raw %}very peculiar and picturesque appearance. With the help of the{% endraw %}
{% raw %}great Barrage (p. Ill), the Delta is irrigated all the year round by{% endraw %}
{% raw %}means of a network of canals. The principal Canals in Upper Egypt{% endraw %}
{% raw %}are the Sohdglyeh (Souhagieh), which quits the river at Sohag (p. 191);{% endraw %}
{% raw %}the Ibrahimlyeh, which begins below Assiut and ends to the N. of{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Benisuef; and the Bahr Yusuf, which serves the Fayum. In Lower{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Egypt the chief canals are the Mdhmudiyeh and Khatatbeh, in the{% endraw %}
{% raw %}W.Delta; the Bahr Tira, the Bahr Shibln, and En-Sdhtl, in the{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Central Delta; the Tewflklyeh, with its continuations the Man-{% endraw %}
{% raw %}surtyeh and Bahr es-Sughair, the Bahr Buhlyth, the Bahr Mu'izz,{% endraw %}
{% raw %}and the Isma'lliyeh Canal, in the E. Delta.{% endraw %}
