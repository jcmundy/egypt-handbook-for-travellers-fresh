{% raw %}This remarkable river has exercised a unique influence on the{% endraw %}
{% raw %}history of civilisation. The necessity of controlling its course and{% endraw %}
{% raw %}utilising its water taught the ancient Egyptians the art of river-{% endraw %}
{% raw %}engineering and the kindred science of land-surveying, while in the{% endraw %}
{% raw %}starry heavens they beheld the eternal calendar which regulated the{% endraw %}
{% raw %}approach and the departure of the inundation, so that the river may{% endraw %}
{% raw %}perhaps have given the first impulse to the study of astronomy. As{% endraw %}
{% raw %}the annual overflow of the water obliterated all landmarks, it was{% endraw %}
{% raw %}necessary annually to measure the land anew, and to keep a register{% endraw %}
{% raw %}of the area belonging to each proprietor ; and above all it became{% endraw %}
{% raw %}an important duty of the rulers of the people to impress them with{% endraw %}
{% raw %}a strong sense of the sacredness of property. Every succeeding year,{% endraw %}
{% raw %}however, there arose new disputes, and these showed the necessity{% endraw %}
{% raw %}of establishing settled laws and enforcing judicial decisions. The{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Nile thus led to the foundation of social, legal, and political order.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Subsequently, when the engineers and architects, in the service{% endraw %}
{% raw %}of the state or in the cause of religion, erected those colossal struc¬{% endraw %}
{% raw %}tures with which we are about to become acquainted, it was the{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Nile which materially facilitated the transport of their materials,{% endraw %}
{% raw %}and enabled the builders of the pyramids and the other ancient{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Egyptians to employ the granite of Assuan for the structures of{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Memphis, and even for those of Tanis, on the coast of the Medi¬{% endraw %}
{% raw %}terranean. As the river, moreover, not only afforded a convenient{% endraw %}
{% raw %}route for the transport of these building-materials, but also an ad¬{% endraw %}
{% raw %}mirable commercial highway, we find that the Egyptians had acquired{% endraw %}
{% raw %}considerable skill at a very early period in constructing vessels{% endraw %}
{% raw %}with oars, masts, sails, and even cabins and other appliances.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}From the earliest historical period down to the present time the{% endraw %}
{% raw %}course of the Nile, from the cataracts down to its bifurcation to the north{% endraw %}
{% raw %}of Cairo, has undergone very little change. This, however, is not the{% endraw %}
{% raw %}case with its Embouchures; for, while ancient writers mention seven{% endraw %}
{% raw %}(the Pelusiac, the Tanitie, the Mendesian, the Bucolic or Phatnitic, the{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Sebennytic, the Bolbitinie, and the C'anopic), there are now practically{% endraw %}
{% raw %}two channels only through which the river is discharged into the sea.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}These are the mouths at Rosetta (Rashidl and Damietta (Dumyat), situ¬{% endraw %}
{% raw %}ated near the middle of the Delta, while the Pelusiac and Canopic mouths,{% endraw %}
{% raw %}the most important in ancient times, lay at the extreme east, and west{% endraw %}
{% raw %}ends of the coast respectively.{% endraw %}
