{% raw %}III. Agricultural Seasons. (1) The Winter Crop, or 'Esh-{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Shitdwi\ grown exclusively on the 'Rai' land (p. lv), is sown im¬{% endraw %}
{% raw %}mediately after the subsidence of the inundation. In Upper Egypt{% endraw %}
{% raw %}seed-time accordingly begins as early as the middle of October, in{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Central Egypt (from Assiut to Cairo) at the beginning of November,{% endraw %}
{% raw %}and in the Delta about the end of December. The ground is seldom{% endraw %}
{% raw %}prepared for sowing by the use of the plough. The seed is scattered{% endraw %}
{% raw %}over the still soft and moist soil, and is then either pressed into it{% endraw %}
{% raw %}by means of a wooden roller, beaten into it with pieces of wood, or{% endraw %}
{% raw %}trodden in by oxen t. Throughout the whole country a period of four{% endraw %}
{% raw %}months elapses between seed-time and the completion ofthe har¬{% endraw %}
{% raw %}vest. The winter-harvest is, therefore, over in Upper Egypt about the{% endraw %}
{% raw %}middle of February, in Central Egypt about the middle of March,{% endraw %}
{% raw %}and in the Delta towards the end of April. The principal crop{% endraw %}
{% raw %}everywhere is wheat, next to which are barley, clover, and broad{% endraw %}
{% raw %}beans (p. lvii).{% endraw %}
{% raw %}(2) The Summer Crops ('Es-SefV or 'El-Kedt) occupy the period{% endraw %}
{% raw %}from April to August; but many of the plants grown at this season{% endraw %}
{% raw %}require a longer period of development, extending throughout the{% endraw %}
{% raw %}whole of the autumn and even part of the winter. This is particul¬{% endraw %}
{% raw %}arly the case with the rice-crop, which is sown in May, but does{% endraw %}
{% raw %}not attain maturity till the middle of November, and with the cot¬{% endraw %}
{% raw %}ton-plant, sown in April, and harvested in November or December.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}A large quantity of cotton is also yielded by a second harvest from{% endraw %}
{% raw %}the pruned plant in the month of August, in the second year of its{% endraw %}
{% raw %}growth. The cultivation of tobacco has recently been forbidden in{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Egypt, in the interest of the customs duties.{% endraw %}

{% raw %}form of which is precisely the same as it was 5000 years ago; and the{% endraw %}
{% raw %}traveller will recognise it on many of the monuments and in the system{% endraw %}

{% raw %}fud, literally 'hedgehog'). The only tool used by the natives on their{% endraw %}
{% raw %}fields, or in making embankments of earth, is a kind of hoe or shovel{% endraw %}
{% raw %}(migrafeh). The process of reaping consists of cutting the grain with a{% endraw %}
{% raw %}sickle (rningal), or simply uprooting it by hand. The ndrag, or 'threshing-{% endraw %}
{% raw %}sledge' , consists of a kind of sledge resting on a roller provided with{% endraw %}
{% raw %}sharp semi-circular pieces of iron, and drawn by oxen or buffaloes. This{% endraw %}
{% raw %}primitive machine, being driven over the wheat, peas, or lentils to be{% endraw %}
{% raw %}threshed, crushes the stalks and ears and sets free the grain or seeds.{% endraw %}
