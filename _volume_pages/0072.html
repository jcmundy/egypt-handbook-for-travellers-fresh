{% raw %}III. Doctrines of El-Islam.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Manners and Customs and Religious and Popular Festivals{% endraw %}
{% raw %}of the Mohammedans.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}By Prof. Socin.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}El-Islam is the most widely spread religion in the world, and{% endraw %}
{% raw %}has not yet ceased to spread.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Mohammed t, as a religious teacher, took up a position hostile{% endraw %}
{% raw %}to the 'age of ignorance and folly', as he called heathenism. The{% endraw %}
{% raw %}revelation which he believed it was his mission to impart was,{% endraw %}
{% raw %}t Mohammed ('the praised', or 'to be praised') was a scion on the{% endraw %}
{% raw %}paternal side of the family of Hashim, a less important branch of the{% endraw %}
{% raw %}noble family of Kureish, who were settled at Mecca, and were custodians{% endraw %}
{% raw %}of the Ka'ba. His father 'Abdallah died shortly before his birth (about{% endraw %}
{% raw %}570). In his sixth year his mother Amina took him on a journey to{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Medina, but died on her way home. The boy was then educated by his{% endraw %}
{% raw %}grandfather 'Abd el-Muttalib, and, after the death of the latter two years{% endraw %}
{% raw %}later, by his uncle Abu Talib. For several years Mohammed tended{% endraw %}
{% raw %}sheep. He afterwards undertook commercial journeys, at first in company{% endraw %}
{% raw %}with his uncle, and then, when about twenty-five years of age, in the{% endraw %}
{% raw %}service of the widow Khadija, who became his first wife. On one of these{% endraw %}
{% raw %}journeys he is said to have become acquainted with the Christian monk{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Bahira at Bosra.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}About that period a reaction in the religious life of the Arabs had{% endraw %}
{% raw %}set in, and when Mohammed was about forty years of age he too was{% endraw %}
{% raw %}struck with the vanity of idolatry. He suffered from epilepsy, and during{% endraw %}
{% raw %}his attacks imagined he received revelations from heaven. He can scarcely,{% endraw %}
{% raw %}therefore, he called an impostor in the ordinary sense. A dream which{% endraw %}
{% raw %}he had on Mt. Hira, near Mecca, gave him the first impulse, and he soon{% endraw %}
{% raw %}began with ardent enthusiasm to promulgate monotheism, and to warn{% endraw %}
{% raw %}his hearers against incurring the pains of hell. It is uncertain whether{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Mohammed himself could read and write. His new doctrine was called{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Islam, or subjection to God. At first he made converts in his own family{% endraw %}
{% raw %}only, and the 'Muslims' were persecuted by the Meecans. Many of them,{% endraw %}
{% raw %}and at length Mohammed himself (622), accordingly emigrated to Medina,{% endraw %}
{% raw %}where the new religion made great progress. Alter the death of Khadija,{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Mohammed took several other wives, partly from political motives.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}He now endeavoured to stir up the Meecans, and war broke out in{% endraw %}
{% raw %}consequence. He was victorious at Bedr, but lost the battle of the Uhud.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}His military campaigns were thenceforth incessant. He obtained great{% endraw %}
{% raw %}influence over the Beduins, and succeeded in uniting them politically.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}In 630 the Muslims at length captured the town of Mecca, and the idols{% endraw %}
{% raw %}in it were destroyed. Mohammed's health, however, had been completely{% endraw %}
{% raw %}undermined by his unremitting exertions for about twenty-four years;{% endraw %}
{% raw %}he died on 8th June, 632, at Medina, and was interred there.{% endraw %}
