{% raw %}The Koran itself was early believed to be of entirely super¬{% endraw %}
{% raw %}natural origin. The name signifies 'rehearsal', or 'reading', and{% endraw %}
{% raw %}the book is divided into chapters called Surehs. The first revelation{% endraw %}
{% raw %}vouchsafed to the Prophet took place in the 'blessed night' in the{% endraw %}
{% raw %}year 609. With many interruptions, the 'sending down' of the{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Koran extended over twenty-three years, until the whole book,{% endraw %}
{% raw %}which had already existed on 'well-preserved tables' in heaven,{% endraw %}
{% raw %}was in the prophet's possession. During the time of the 'Ahbaside{% endraw %}
{% raw %}khalifs it was a matter ofthe keenest controversy whether the Koran{% endraw %}
{% raw %}was created or uncreated. (The Oriental Christians have likewise{% endraw %}
{% raw %}always manifested a great taste for subtle dogmatic questions, such{% endraw %}
{% raw %}as the Procession of the Holy Ghost.) The earlier, or Meccan Surehs,{% endraw %}
{% raw %}which on account of their brevity are placed at the end ofthe book,{% endraw %}
{% raw %}are characterised by great freshness and vigour of style. They are in{% endraw %}
{% raw %}rhyme, but only partially poetic in form. In the longer Surehs of a{% endraw %}
{% raw %}later period the style is more studied and the narrative often{% endraw %}
{% raw %}tedious. The Koran is nevertheless regarded as the masterpiece of{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Arabic literature. The prayers of the Muslims consist almost exclus¬{% endraw %}
{% raw %}ively of passages from it, although they are ignorant of its real{% endraw %}
{% raw %}meaning. Even by the early commentators much of the Koran was{% endraw %}
{% raw %}imperfectly understood, for Mohammed, although extremely proud{% endraw %}
{% raw %}of his 'Arabic Book', was very partial to the use of all kinds of{% endraw %}
{% raw %}foreign words. The translation ofthe Koran being prohibited, Pers¬{% endraw %}
{% raw %}ian, Turkish, and Indian children learn it entirely by rote.{% endraw %}
