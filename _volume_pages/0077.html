{% raw %}DOCTRINES OF EL-ISLAM. Ixvii{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Although Polygamy is sanctioned, every Muslim being permit¬{% endraw %}
{% raw %}ted to have four wives at a time, yet among the bulk of the popu¬{% endraw %}
{% raw %}lation monogamy is far more frequent, owing to the difficulty of{% endraw %}
{% raw %}providing for several wives and families at once. The wives, more¬{% endraw %}
{% raw %}over, are very apt to quarrel, to the utter destruction of domestic{% endraw %}
{% raw %}peace, unless the husband can afford to assign them separate houses.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Few men remain unmarried. The treatment of women as mere chat¬{% endraw %}
{% raw %}tels, which is of very remote Oriental origin, constitutes the greatest{% endraw %}
{% raw %}defect of the system of El-Islam, although the position of the female{% endraw %}
{% raw %}sex among the Oriental Christians and Jews is little better than{% endraw %}
{% raw %}among the Muslims. It is probably owing to this low estimate of{% endraw %}
{% raw %}women that the Muslims generally dislike to see them praying or{% endraw %}
{% raw %}occupying themselves with religion. The practice of wearing veils{% endraw %}
{% raw %}dates from very remote times (Genesis xxiv, 65; Isaiah iii, 23), though{% endraw %}
{% raw %}it is doubtful whether it was customary among the ancient Egyptians,{% endraw %}
{% raw %}as veiled women never appear upon the monuments. A Muslim is{% endraw %}
{% raw %}not permitted to see any women unveiled except his wife and slaves{% endraw %}
{% raw %}and his blood-relations; when there are lady visitors in the harem,{% endraw %}
{% raw %}he must announce his approach before entering, so as to afford them{% endraw %}
{% raw %}an opportunity of retiring. An Oriental lady would, indeed, regard{% endraw %}
{% raw %}it as an affront to be permitted to mingle in society with the same{% endraw %}
{% raw %}freedom as European ladies. Even in the Christian churches (ex¬{% endraw %}
{% raw %}cept the Protestant) the place for women is often separated from{% endraw %}
{% raw %}the men's seats by a railing. The peasant and Beduin women, on{% endraw %}
{% raw %}the other hand, are often seen unveiled. The ease with which El-{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Islam permits divorce is due to Mohammed's personal proclivities.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}A single word from the husband suffices to banish the wife from his{% endraw %}
{% raw %}house, but she retains the marriage-portion which she has received{% endraw %}
{% raw %}from her husband. The children are brought up in great subjection{% endraw %}
{% raw %}to their parents, often showing more fear than love for them.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}The repetition of Prayers (sola) five times daily is one of{% endraw %}
{% raw %}the chief duties of faithful Muslims. The hours of prayer (addn){% endraw %}
{% raw %}are proclaimed by the mueddins (or muezzins) from the minarets of{% endraw %}
{% raw %}the mosques: (1) Maghrib, a little after sunset; (1)'Isheh, night¬{% endraw %}
{% raw %}fall, about l1/? hour after sunset; (3) Subh, daybreak; (4) Duhr,{% endraw %}
{% raw %}midday; (5) Asr, afternoon, about 3 hours after midday. These{% endraw %}
{% raw %}periods of prayer also serve to mark the divisions of the day. The{% endraw %}
{% raw %}day is also divided into two periods of 12 hours each, beginning{% endraw %}
{% raw %}from sunset, so that where clocks and watches are used they require{% endraw %}
{% raw %}to be set daily. Most people however content themselves with the{% endraw %}
{% raw %}sonorous call of themueddin: Alldhu akbar(three times); ashhadu{% endraw %}
