{% raw %}The person praying must remove his shoes or sandals and turn{% endraw %}

{% raw %}by putting his hands to the lobes of his ears, and then holds them{% endraw %}
{% raw %}a little below his girdle; and he intersperses his recitations from{% endraw %}
{% raw %}the Koran with certain prostrations performed in a given order. On{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Fridays the midday recital of prayer takes place three quarters of an{% endraw %}
{% raw %}hour earlier than usual, and is followed by a sermon. Friday is not,{% endraw %}
{% raw %}however, regarded as a day of rest, business being transacted. It has,{% endraw %}
{% raw %}however, of late become customary to close the courts of justice in{% endraw %}
{% raw %}imitation of the Christian practice of keeping Sunday. — The Mus¬{% endraw %}
{% raw %}lims frequently recite as a prayer the first Stireh of the Koran, one{% endraw %}
{% raw %}ofthe shortest, which is used as we employ the Lord's prayer. It is{% endraw %}
{% raw %}called el-fatha ('the commencing'), and is to the following effect: —{% endraw %}
{% raw %}'In the name of God, the merciful and gracious. Praise be to God,{% endraw %}
{% raw %}the Lord of creatures, the merciful and gracious, the Prince of the{% endraw %}
{% raw %}day of judgment; we serve Thee, and we pray to Thee for help ;{% endraw %}
{% raw %}lead us in the right way of those to whom thou hast shown mercy,{% endraw %}
{% raw %}upon whom no wrath resteth, and who go not astray. Amen'.{% endraw %}
