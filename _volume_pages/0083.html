{% raw %}Remarks on Mohammedan Customs.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}The birth of a child is celebrated on the seventh day of its life{% endraw %}
{% raw %}by a domestic festival, attended by the kadi or some learned theo¬{% endraw %}
{% raw %}logian, who dissolves in his mouth a piece of sugar-candy presented{% endraw %}
{% raw %}to him by the host and drops a little of his sweetened saliva into{% endraw %}
{% raw %}the infant's mouth, as if to give it a sweet foretaste of the world's{% endraw %}
{% raw %}gifts, and also for the purpose of 'giving it a name out of his mouth'.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Muslims, it is well known, are usually named by their praenomens{% endraw %}
{% raw %}only. If a more precise designation is desired, the name of the{% endraw %}
{% raw %}father is placed after the praenoinen, with or without the word ibn{% endraw %}
{% raw %}('son of) placed between the names. Nicknames, such as 'the one-{% endraw %}
{% raw %}eyed', etc., are also not uncommon.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}When the child is forty days old the mother takes it to the bath,{% endraw %}
{% raw %}and causes forty bowls of water (thirty-nine if a girl) to be poured{% endraw %}
{% raw %}over it. This bath forms the purification of both mother and child.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}The rite of circumcision is performed on boys up to the age of{% endraw %}
{% raw %}six or seven, or even later, the ceremony being attended with great{% endraw %}
{% raw %}pomp. The child is previously conducted'through the streets in holiday{% endraw %}
{% raw %}attire, the procession being frequently united with some bridal party,{% endraw %}
{% raw %}in order to diminish the expense of the proceedings. The boy gener¬{% endraw %}
{% raw %}ally wears a turban of red cashmere, girls' clothes of the richest{% endraw %}
{% raw %}possible description, and conspicuous female ornaments, which are{% endraw %}
{% raw %}designed to attract attention, and thus avert the evil eye from his{% endraw %}
{% raw %}person. He half covers his face with an embroidered handkerchief;{% endraw %}
{% raw %}and the barber who performs the operation and a noisy troop of{% endraw %}
{% raw %}musicians head the procession. The first personage in the procession{% endraw %}
{% raw %}is usually the barber's boy, carrying the 'hemV, or barber's sign, a{% endraw %}
{% raw %}kind of cupboard made of wood, in the form of a half-cylinder, with{% endraw %}
{% raw %}four short legs. The flat front of the heml is adorned with pieces{% endraw %}
{% raw %}of looking-glass and embossed brass, while the back is covered with{% endraw %}
{% raw %}a curtain. Two or more boys are often thus paraded together, being{% endraw %}
{% raw %}usually driven in a carriage and attended by music.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Girls are generally married in their 12th or 13th, and some¬{% endraw %}
{% raw %}times as early as their 10th year. A man in search of a bride{% endraw %}
{% raw %}employs the services of a relative, or of a professional female{% endraw %}
{% raw %}match-maker, and he never has an opportunity of seeing his bride{% endraw %}
{% raw %}until the wedding-day, except when the parties belong to the{% endraw %}
{% raw %}lowest classes. When everything is arranged, the affianced bride¬{% endraw %}
{% raw %}groom has to pay a bridal-portion (mahr) amounting to about 25?.,{% endraw %}
{% raw %}more being paid when the bride is a spinster than if she is a widow.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Generally speaking, about two-thirds of the sum, the amount{% endraw %}
{% raw %}of which always forms a subject of lively discussion, is paid{% endraw %}
{% raw %}down, while one-third is settled upon the wife, being payable on{% endraw %}
{% raw %}the death of the husband, or on his divorcing her against her will.{% endraw %}

{% raw %}bride is conducted in gala attire and with great ceremony to the{% endraw %}
{% raw %}bath. This procession is called 'Zeffet et Hammdm'. It is headed{% endraw %}
