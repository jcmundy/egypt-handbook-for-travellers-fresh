{% raw %}The ceremonies observed at funerals are not less remarkable{% endraw %}
{% raw %}than those which attend weddings If the death occurs in the morn¬{% endraw %}
{% raw %}ing, the funeral takes place the same day; but if in the evening,{% endraw %}
{% raw %}it is postponed till next day. The body is washed and mourned{% endraw %}
{% raw %}over by the family and the professional mourning women (nedda-{% endraw %}
{% raw %}behs); the fiklh, or schoolmaster, reads several Surehs of the Koran{% endraw %}
{% raw %}by its side; after this, it is wrapped in its winding sheet, placed{% endraw %}
{% raw %}on the bier, covered with a red or green cloth, and then carried{% endraw %}
{% raw %}forth in solemn procession. The foremost persons in the cortege are{% endraw %}
{% raw %}usually six or more poor, and generally blind, men, who walk in{% endraw %}
{% raw %}twos or threes at a slow pace, chanting the creed—'There is no{% endraw %}
{% raw %}God hut God; Mohammed is the ambassador of God; God he gra¬{% endraw %}
{% raw %}cious to him and preserve him!' These are followed by several{% endraw %}
{% raw %}male relatives of the deceased, and sometimes by a number of{% endraw %}
{% raw %}dervishes with the flags of their order, and then by three or more{% endraw %}
{% raw %}schoolboys, one of whom carries a copy of the Koran, or of parts of{% endraw %}
{% raw %}it, on a stand made of palm-branches, covered with a cloth. The{% endraw %}
{% raw %}boys usually chant in a loud and shrill voice several passages from{% endraw %}
{% raw %}the 'Hashrlyeti, a poem describing the last judgment. The bier,{% endraw %}
{% raw %}with the head of the deceased foremost, comes next, being borne{% endraw %}
{% raw %}by three or four of his friends, who are relieved from time to time{% endraw %}
{% raw %}by others. After the bier come the female relatives, with dishevel¬{% endraw %}
{% raw %}led hair, sobbing aloud, and frequently accompanied by professional{% endraw %}
{% raw %}mourning women, whose business it is to extol the merits of the{% endraw %}
{% raw %}deceased. If the deceased was the husband or father of the family,{% endraw %}
{% raw %}one of the cries is : '0 thou camel of my house', the camel being{% endraw %}
{% raw %}the emblem of the bread-winner of the household.{% endraw %}

{% raw %}saints the relatives entertain the greatest veneration, and prayers{% endraw %}

{% raw %}front of the tomb of the saint, and prayers and chants have again{% endraw %}
{% raw %}been recited, the procession is formed anew and moves towards the{% endraw %}
