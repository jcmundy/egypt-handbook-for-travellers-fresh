{% raw %}IV. Outline of the History of Egypt.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}I. Ancient History.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}By Prof. G. Steindorff.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}a. From the Earliest Times to the Macedonian Conquest in 332 B.C.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Exact systems of chronology were as little known to the ancient{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Egyptians as to the other peoples of antiquity. The events they{% endraw %}
{% raw %}desired to record were dated according to the years of the king{% endraw %}
{% raw %}reigning at the time. In order to determine at what period a partic¬{% endraw %}
{% raw %}ular king had reigned, the priests drew up long lists of monarchs,{% endraw %}
{% raw %}fragments of which have survived to the present day (p. 222). The{% endraw %}
{% raw %}chronological epitomes, moreover, which are all that has been trans¬{% endraw %}
{% raw %}mitted to us of the 'Egyptian History', written in Greek by the{% endraw %}
{% raw %}priest Manethot, were founded on these native registers. Manetho{% endraw %}
{% raw %}arranged all the rulers of Egypt, from Menes, the first king, to{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Alexander the Great, in 31 Dynasties, which correspond, generally{% endraw %}
{% raw %}speaking, to the various royal houses that held sway in Egypt suc¬{% endraw %}
{% raw %}cessively or (at certain periods) contemporaneously. This arrange¬{% endraw %}
{% raw %}ment has been generally adopted by writers on the subject; but at{% endraw %}
{% raw %}the same time, for the sake of convenience, several dynasties are{% endraw %}
{% raw %}frequently grouped together under the name of a 'period', 'empire', or{% endraw %}
{% raw %}'monarchy'. The lack of any settled chronology renders it, of course,{% endraw %}
{% raw %}impossible to assign anything like exact dates for the kings before{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Psammetikh I. The dates, therefore, in the following outline are{% endraw %}
{% raw %}given as approximate merely, and in the earliest period may some¬{% endraw %}
{% raw %}times be even centuries out.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}1. The Prehistoric Period.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}The dark prehistoric period, which later traditions fill up with{% endraw %}
{% raw %}dynasties of gods and demigods, is illumined by a few scattered{% endraw %}
{% raw %}rays of light only. It may be taken as certain that the country did{% endraw %}
{% raw %}not originally form one single kingdom, but was divided into two{% endraw %}
{% raw %}states — the 'North Land', corresponding to the Delta, and the{% endraw %}
{% raw %}'South', stretching from the neighbourhood of Memphis (Cairo) to{% endraw %}
{% raw %}t Manetho of Sebennytos flourished in the reigns of Ptolemy I. and{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Ptolemy II. He was probably a priest at Heliopolis and wrote his three{% endraw %}
{% raw %}hooks of AiyurtTioxa Tnou,vrVaTa in the reign of Philadelphus.{% endraw %}
