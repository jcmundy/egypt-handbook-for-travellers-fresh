{% raw %}Amenophis IV. (1392-1374 B.C.) endeavoured to replace the old{% endraw %}
{% raw %}religion by the worship of a single deity, vis. the sun, an{% endraw %}

{% raw %}during the New Empire, had thrown all others into the shade.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Many of the ancient deities, especially those of Thebes, were{% endraw %}
{% raw %}fanatically 'persecuted', their images and names being removed{% endraw %}
{% raw %}from all monuments. For his own original name, in which the{% endraw %}
{% raw %}name of Ammon occurs, the king substituted that of Ekh-en-aton{% endraw %}
{% raw %}or Spirit of the Sun. Tell el-'Amarna (p. 203), near which is the{% endraw %}
{% raw %}king's tomb (p. 209), was made the capital instead of Thebes.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}After the death of Amenophis internal commotions broke out{% endraw %}
{% raw %}and the recently introduced'religion was again abolished.{% endraw %}
