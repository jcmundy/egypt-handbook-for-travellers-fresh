{% raw %}*XXVI. DYNASTY (663-525 B.C.).{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Egypt now enjoyed another period of prosperity. Trade began to{% endraw %}
{% raw %}flourish owing to the new relations with Greece. Art also received{% endraw %}
{% raw %}a fresh impetus; even under the Ethiopian kings artists had begun{% endraw %}
{% raw %}to imitate the models ofthe classic period of Egyptian art under{% endraw %}
{% raw %}the Early Empire and to revive the ancient forms. This reversion{% endraw %}
{% raw %}to the ancient times appeared also in other departments, such as{% endraw %}
{% raw %}literature, the spelling of inscriptions, and even the titles of offi¬{% endraw %}
{% raw %}cials, so that the period of the 26th Dyn. may be styled the Egypt¬{% endraw %}
{% raw %}ian Renaissance.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}663-610. Psammetikh I. (Psamtik) strengthened his position in Upper{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Egypt by marrying Shep-en-wepet, niece of Shabako, and{% endraw %}
{% raw %}daughter of Princess Amenertdis.{% endraw %}

{% raw %}deadly contest with the Babylonians and Medes, Nekho{% endraw %}
{% raw %}invaded Syria, defeating and slaying Josiah, King of{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Judah, at the battle of Megiddo. The Egyptians were,{% endraw %}
{% raw %}however, defeated at Karkemish by Nebuchadnezzar, King{% endraw %}

{% raw %}Palestine. — Nekho began to construct a canal from the{% endraw %}
