{% raw %}HISTORY.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Amasis (Ahmose) secured his supremacy by marriage with{% endraw %}
{% raw %}a daughter of Psammetikh II. A campaign undertaken{% endraw %}
{% raw %}by Nebuchadnezzar against Egypt led to the final aban¬{% endraw %}
{% raw %}donment of the Egyptian claims upon Syria. Amasis as¬{% endraw %}
{% raw %}signed the city of Naucratis (p. 21) to Greek colonists,{% endraw %}
{% raw %}who speedily made it the most important commercial town{% endraw %}
{% raw %}in the empire. A friendly alliance was made with Poly-{% endraw %}
{% raw %}krates, tyrant of Samos. During this reign Cyrus founded{% endraw %}
{% raw %}his great Persian empire (550), including the whole of{% endraw %}
{% raw %}W. Asia.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Psammetikh III. was defeated at Pelusium by the Persian{% endraw %}
{% raw %}king Cambyses, and Egypt became a Persian province.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}XXVII. DYNASTY. PERSIAN DOMINATION.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}The Persian monarchs appeared as successors to the native{% endraw %}
{% raw %}rulers and by their moderation found favour with the greater{% endraw %}
{% raw %}part of the population. The old religion was unmolested.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Cambyses led an unsuccessful expedition against the oasis{% endraw %}
{% raw %}of Ammon (Siwa) and a campaign against Ethiopia.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Darius I. endeavoured to promote the prosperity of Egypt{% endraw %}
{% raw %}in every possible way. The canal from the Nile to the{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Red Sea was completed (p. lxxxv). A strong garrison was{% endraw %}
{% raw %}sent to the oasis of Khargeh and a temple was built there{% endraw %}
{% raw %}to Ammon. After the battle of Marathon the Egyptians,{% endraw %}
{% raw %}headed by Khabash, revolted and expelled the Persians.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}The insurrection, however, was quelled by —{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Xerxes I., who appointed his brother Achaemenes satrap.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Artaxerxes I. During this reign the Egyptians again revolt¬{% endraw %}
{% raw %}ed. Inaros, prince of Marea, aided by the Athenians, de¬{% endraw %}
{% raw %}feated Achaemenes, the Persian satrap, but the allied{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Egyptians and Greeks were in turn defeated by the Per¬{% endraw %}
{% raw %}sian general Megabyzos near Propositis, an island in the{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Nile, and Inaros was crucified. Amyrtaeos, a partizan of{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Inaros, then sought an asylum in the marshy district of{% endraw %}
{% raw %}the Delta, where he succeeded in maintaining his inde¬{% endraw %}
{% raw %}pendence.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Herodotus visited Egypt.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Darius II. The Persian power gradually declined. Under —{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Artaxerxes II. and his successor —{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Artaxerxes III. the Egyptians once more revolted and suc¬{% endraw %}
{% raw %}ceeded in regaining their independence for a brief period{% endraw %}
{% raw %}under native rulers, whom Manetho assigns to the 28th-{% endraw %}
{% raw %}30th Dynasties.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}XXVIII. DYNASTY.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Amyrtseos of Sa'is maintained his authority for a short time{% endraw %}
{% raw %}only. In Lower Egypt several dynasties contended for{% endraw %}
{% raw %}sovereignty.{% endraw %}
