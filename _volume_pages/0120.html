{% raw %}It may here be remarked that the Egyptian names occurring in{% endraw %}
{% raw %}the Handbook are, wherever practicable, written in the traditional{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Greek form and not in the native Egyptian; e.g. Sethos instead of{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Sti, Kheops (Cheops) instead of Khwfw. For names of which there{% endraw %}
{% raw %}are no known Greek transliterations the Egyptian forms are given,{% endraw %}
{% raw %}with vowels inserted on the principles explained above. In these{% endraw %}
{% raw %}cases, however, the dots under the letters are omitted, so that no dif¬{% endraw %}
{% raw %}ference is made between t and t, z and z, k and k, or h and h; w is{% endraw %}
{% raw %}sometimes represented by u; y by i; h and h by kh; s by s; and in{% endraw %}
{% raw %}certain cases y is altogether omitted. The apostrophes ' and ' are{% endraw %}
{% raw %}uniformly omitted. In short, the general rules adopted by the{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Greeks for the transliteration of Egyptian words are followed. —{% endraw %}
{% raw %}The final e in Egyptian names does not mean that the vowel is long,{% endraw %}
{% raw %}but merely that it is to be pronounced as a separate syllable.{% endraw %}
