{% raw %}The origin of the world, the movements of the heavenly bodies,{% endraw %}
{% raw %}and the alternation of day and night awoke speculation in Egypt at{% endraw %}
{% raw %}a very early date, the result of which was a naive belief that the{% endraw %}
{% raw %}world was created by supernatural beings, who revealed themselves{% endraw %}
{% raw %}in the heavenly bodies and controlled the processes of nature.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}According to a wide-spread belief the earth was a god named Keb,{% endraw %}
{% raw %}the sky a goddess named Newt. Originally these were united, but{% endraw %}
{% raw %}they were afterwards separated by the god Show, who raised the{% endraw %}
{% raw %}sky-goddess aloft in his arms. Another and more materialistic view{% endraw %}
{% raw %}regarded the earth as a huge oval plain, floating upon the ocean,{% endraw %}
{% raw %}and the sky as a flat slab resting upon the mountains at the extrem{% endraw %}
{% raw %}ities of the earth, with the stars hanging from it like lamps. The{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Sun, the principal heavenly body, was in particular the subject of{% endraw %}
{% raw %}many theories, probably representing the teachings of the different{% endraw %}
{% raw %}colleges of priests throughout the country. In one place the sun{% endraw %}
{% raw %}god Re was conceived of as sailing across the ocean of heaven in a{% endraw %}
{% raw %}boat; in another the sun was regarded as a brilliantly plumaged{% endraw %}
{% raw %}hawk flying across the firmament and driving away the hostile{% endraw %}
{% raw %}clouds; and in a third the sun was a powerful young hero, new¬{% endraw %}
{% raw %}born every morning from the goddess of the sky, and waging a{% endraw %}
{% raw %}ceaseless combat with the powers of darkness. It was also con¬{% endraw %}
{% raw %}ceived of under the form of a scarabaeus or beetle. The myth of Re{% endraw %}
{% raw %}seems to have been chiefly developed in Heliopolis, where the sun{% endraw %}
{% raw %}god Re - Harmachis was worshipped along with the local deity{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Atum. Orion and Sothis played the leading roles among the stars ;{% endraw %}
{% raw %}and among the other powers of nature a prominent place was filled{% endraw %}
{% raw %}by the Nile God, to whom indeed the country owed its prosperity.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}All these deities received general worship, though none of them{% endraw %}
{% raw %}had particular temples of their own.{% endraw %}
