{% raw %}RELIGION.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}a picture of the life of the ancient Egyptians. Under the ancient{% endraw %}
{% raw %}empire the grandees alone were allowed to build themselves tombs,{% endraw %}
{% raw %}and that probably only by favour of the king. The ordinary citizens{% endraw %}
{% raw %}had to content themselves with simple graves in which the ne¬{% endraw %}
{% raw %}cessaries for the future life were buried with the bodies. But at a{% endraw %}
{% raw %}later period even the lower ranks of society built 'everlasting houses'{% endraw %}
{% raw %}for themselves, at least so far as they possessed the means to do so.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}The dead were under the protection of the local deities, and in some{% endraw %}
{% raw %}instances also under special gods of the dead. Thus at Memphis{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Sokaris was the god of the dead and provided for their support, while{% endraw %}
{% raw %}these functions were discharged at Assiut by Wep-wat and at Abydos{% endraw %}
{% raw %}by the 'Lord of the West'. But even at an early date these local gods{% endraw %}
{% raw %}retired in favour of Osiris, who was originally the local deity of{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Busiris in the Delta, where he ruled the dead in the fruitful fields.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}The death which Osiris suffered according to the legend (p. cxx){% endraw %}
{% raw %}was the common lot of mortals; but just as Osiris rose again, so a{% endraw %}
{% raw %}man also could begin a new life, provided that the same formulae{% endraw %}
{% raw %}were pronounced for him by some faithful son; he went to Osiris,{% endraw %}
{% raw %}became united with the slain god, in fact was himself Osiris. Ad¬{% endraw %}
{% raw %}mission to the realm of Osiris depended upon the recitation of{% endraw %}
{% raw %}magical formulae and incantations, a knowledge of which must be{% endraw %}
{% raw %}communicated to the deceased. At the same time a virtuous life{% endraw %}
{% raw %}upon earth was required to assure the deceased eternal happiness,{% endraw %}
{% raw %}and he had therefore to undergo a trial before Osiris and to prove{% endraw %}
{% raw %}before forty-two judges that he was free from mortal sin. Before{% endraw %}
{% raw %}this took place, and before his heart had been weighed by Thout{% endraw %}
{% raw %}in the scales of righteousness and found perfect, he might not enter{% endraw %}
{% raw %}the future land. Opinions differed as to the place of abode of the{% endraw %}
{% raw %}blessed dead. Their dwelling was usually located in the West, among{% endraw %}
{% raw %}the mountains, and in the desert where the sun set. Some helieved{% endraw %}
{% raw %}that they inhabited the fertile fields of Earn, a fruitful country{% endraw %}
{% raw %}where ploughing and reaping were carried on as upon earth, and{% endraw %}
{% raw %}where the corn grew to the height of seven ells, forming a veritable{% endraw %}
{% raw %}paradise for the Egyptian peasant. As the labour in this future land{% endraw %}
{% raw %}might often be too great for the strength of the deceased, it became{% endraw %}
{% raw %}the custom at the period of the Middle Empire to place Ushabtis in{% endraw %}
{% raw %}the tomb along with him. These were little figures of men, which{% endraw %}
{% raw %}were imbued with life by a magic spell written upon them, and{% endraw %}
{% raw %}assisted the deceased in his labours.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Another doctrine sought to unite the different conceptions ofthe{% endraw %}
{% raw %}future life and placed the abodes ofthe blessed in Twat, the under¬{% endraw %}
{% raw %}world. This was the country through which the sun passed at night. It{% endraw %}
{% raw %}was believed to lie under the earth, to be roofed like the earth by a{% endraw %}
{% raw %}sky, and to be traversed by a river. It was divided into twelve parts,{% endraw %}
{% raw %}corresponding to the twelve hours of night, and, according to a{% endraw %}
{% raw %}certain view, separated from each other by massive doors (comp.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}P. 264).{% endraw %}
