{% raw %}Houses, which in many respects were identical with the Arab{% endraw %}

{% raw %}farmer or artizan was as simple then as it is to-day. An open court,{% endraw %}
{% raw %}in which the family spent the day, was adjoined by a few dark{% endraw %}
{% raw %}sleeping-rooms and stables for the cattle, while a staircase led from{% endraw %}
{% raw %}the court to the flat roof, upon which a few smaller apartments{% endraw %}
{% raw %}were often found. The houses of the more prosperous Egyptians{% endraw %}
{% raw %}also had a court as their central point, at the back of which was a{% endraw %}
{% raw %}colonnade or vestibule of light columns, open in front, affording{% endraw %}
