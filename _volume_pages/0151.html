{% raw %}In many temples the colonnaded hall is further separated from{% endraw %}
{% raw %}the sanctuary by one or more Smaller Halls (with or without col¬{% endraw %}
{% raw %}umns) of narrower proportions and diminishing in height. Fre¬{% endraw %}
{% raw %}quently also the sanctuary is followed by several other halls and{% endraw %}
{% raw %}chambers; and not unfrequently the great hall is preceded by two{% endraw %}
{% raw %}colonnaded courts instead of by one, while in these the colonnade{% endraw %}
{% raw %}on the rear-side is placed on a terrace-like structure above the level{% endraw %}
{% raw %}of the pavement. The particular purposes of all these various rooms{% endraw %}
{% raw %}are hard to determine; with the exception ofthe open court they{% endraw %}
{% raw %}were probably all closed to the general public and accessible to the{% endraw %}
{% raw %}priests alone. Only the king or his representative, the high-priest,{% endraw %}
{% raw %}might enter the inner sanctuary and there 'gaze upon the god'.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Though many temples, such as the temple at Luxor and the{% endraw %}
{% raw %}great temple of Ammon at Karnak, exhibit a much more com¬{% endraw %}
{% raw %}plicated form than that just described, the explanation is that they{% endraw %}
{% raw %}were not built on one uniform plan but owe their construction to{% endraw %}
{% raw %}various builders. In the descriptions of the particular temples con¬{% endraw %}
{% raw %}cerned this matter is treated with due attention to detail.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Occasionally the nature of the site compelled farther deviations{% endraw %}
{% raw %}from the above-described form. In Lower Nubia the sandstone{% endraw %}
{% raw %}rocks approach so close to the bank of the Nile that the temple{% endraw %}
{% raw %}must be partly or wholly constructed in the rock, the necessary{% endraw %}
{% raw %}rooms being hewn out. At Gerf-Husen (p. 360) the court is built{% endraw %}
{% raw %}as usual, while the colonnaded hall and the sanctuary are hewn out{% endraw %}
{% raw %}of the rock. The larger temple of Abu Simbel is entirely a rock-{% endraw %}
{% raw %}building, the pylon and the colossi included. At Abydos the difficulty{% endraw %}
{% raw %}of excavating the rock was avoided by placing the part of the temple{% endraw %}
