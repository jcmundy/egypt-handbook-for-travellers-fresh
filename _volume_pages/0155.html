{% raw %}The custom of placing their tombs beside that of the monarch{% endraw %}
{% raw %}was gradually abandoned by the nobles at the close of the Ancient{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Empire; they preferred to be buried near their own homes. Like{% endraw %}
{% raw %}the Pharaohs they built for themselves small brick-pyramids upon{% endraw %}
{% raw %}square or rectangular bases. The.tomb-chamber was formed in the{% endraw %}
{% raw %}thickness of the wall and a tombstone was placed on the outside,{% endraw %}
{% raw %}before which the survivors recited their prayers or presented their{% endraw %}
{% raw %}offerings. But the high and steep declivity of the desert-plateau{% endraw %}
{% raw %}did not always offer space enough for such free-standing tombs; and{% endraw %}
{% raw %}at various points (e.g. Benihasan, Assiut, Assuan, etc.) graves were{% endraw %}
{% raw %}hewn in the rock, a practice of which there were isolated examples{% endraw %}
{% raw %}even under the Ancient Empire (p. 128). In accordance with the{% endraw %}
{% raw %}fundamental conception of the tomb as the House of the Dead, each{% endraw %}
{% raw %}grave must contain the three principal divisions of the ancient Egypt-r{% endraw %}
{% raw %}ian dwelling-house — the open court, the broad hall, and the deep{% endraw %}
{% raw %}and narrow eating-room. Thus a Fore Court, surrounded with a{% endraw %}
{% raw %}brick-wall, was provided in the open air in front of the tomb,{% endraw %}
{% raw %}generally ending in a small colonnade with two pillars or columns{% endraw %}
{% raw %}hewn in the solid rock. Beyond this was a large Chamber with{% endraw %}
{% raw %}columns, followed by a small Chamber or Recess, which contained{% endraw %}
{% raw %}the statue of the deceased, frequently accompanied by that of his{% endraw %}
{% raw %}wife, hewn out of the rock, and thus corresponded to the serdab of{% endraw %}
{% raw %}the old mastabas.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}This dwelling-house arrangement is most distinctly seen in the{% endraw %}
{% raw %}rock-tombs of Benihasan and Assuan (comp. pp. 197, 332). The{% endraw %}
{% raw %}inner walls are covered with inscriptions and representations, which,{% endraw %}
{% raw %}though more varied in subject than those of the earlier tombs, agree{% endraw %}
{% raw %}with them in being intended to provide for the enjoyment of the{% endraw %}
{% raw %}deceased. The unembellished sarcophagus-chamber was reached{% endraw %}
{% raw %}by a perpendicular shaft hewn in the rock from the first hall.{% endraw %}
