{% raw %}HISTORY OP ART.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Egyptian reliefs are either Bas-Reliefs, the earliest and at all{% endraw %}
{% raw %}periods the commonest form, or Incised Reliefs ('reliefs en creux'),{% endraw %}
{% raw %}to which the design is sunk below the surface. Painting occurs in{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Egypt only as an accompaniment of or as a substitute for sculpture.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Statues and reliefs were almost invariably painted, and even stones{% endraw %}
{% raw %}that are naturally coloured, such as granite, basalt, etc., appear to{% endraw %}
{% raw %}have been subjected to this general rule of polychromatic embel¬{% endraw %}
{% raw %}lishment. When painting was used instead of sculpture (as, e.g., in{% endraw %}
{% raw %}the tombs of the 18th Dyn.), it was so either in order to save ex¬{% endraw %}
{% raw %}pense or because the available stone was not suitable for carvings.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}That the same rules of drawing applied to paintings as applied to{% endraw %}
{% raw %}reliefs needs scarcely be stated.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Egyptian relief, like Egyptian statuary, attained its highest{% endraw %}
{% raw %}point under the 5th Dyn. (p. lxxx). The high level of technical and{% endraw %}
{% raw %}artistic skill attained at that period is best illustrated in the Mastabas{% endraw %}
{% raw %}of Ti and Ptahbotep at Sakkara (pp. 137,139). Under the 6th Dyn. and{% endraw %}
{% raw %}during the Middle Empire both carving and composition had begun{% endraw %}
{% raw %}to decline, though it must be admitted that the artists of Benihasan{% endraw %}
{% raw %}(p. 197) added a valuable series of new motives (military scenes,{% endraw %}
{% raw %}provincial life, funeral scenes) to the previous list of subjects. The{% endraw %}
{% raw %}free and naturalistic tendency received a fresh impetus in the middle{% endraw %}
{% raw %}ofthe 18th Dyn., when the new political relations of Egypt with{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Asia Minor opened up a new horizon to the artist, and the strange{% endraw %}
{% raw %}importations from foreign countries supplied him with novel sugges¬{% endraw %}
{% raw %}tions. In particular, the wonderfully faithful representations of{% endraw %}
{% raw %}animal-life during this period may challenge comparison with the{% endraw %}
{% raw %}best achievements of other nations in this direction. The traveller{% endraw %}
{% raw %}will find the finest specimens of these works in the tombs of Shekh{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Abd el-Kurna (p. 288).{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Unfortunately, however, this naturalistic tendency soon ran into{% endraw %}
{% raw %}exaggeration. In the reign of Amenophis IV., doubtless in connec¬{% endraw %}
{% raw %}tion with the religious reformation of that time (p. lxxxii), it found{% endraw %}
{% raw %}its way into the official art of the court and influenced the representa¬{% endraw %}
{% raw %}tions of the king and the court, but it there quickly degenerated{% endraw %}
{% raw %}into mannerism. Reaction was the natural consequence, and a{% endraw %}
{% raw %}return was soon made to the ancient style, hampered as it was with{% endraw %}
{% raw %}its strict rules of composition. But in spite of all traditional stiffness{% endraw %}
{% raw %}of composition and grouping, this reactionary art attained a high{% endraw %}
{% raw %}level once more under Sethos I., from whose reign date the delicate{% endraw %}
{% raw %}and graceful reliefs in the temple at Abydos. But a rapid decline{% endraw %}
{% raw %}set in under Ramses II., probably in consequence of the too lavish{% endraw %}
{% raw %}demands made upon artistic resources for the decoration of the nu¬{% endraw %}
{% raw %}merous new temples. — Although the reaction against the art of{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Amenophis IV. expunged a number of subjects from the list of those{% endraw %}
{% raw %}available in official representations, the warlike exploits of the{% endraw %}
{% raw %}monarchs of the 19th and 20th Dyn. provided new material in{% endraw %}
{% raw %}compensation. No advance towards lucidity of grouping is shown{% endraw %}
