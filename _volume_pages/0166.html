{% raw %}The sculptors of Smalleb Wobks of Abt also were at first{% endraw %}
{% raw %}entirely dependent upon the Greek motherland. Thus for terracottas{% endraw %}
{% raw %}they borrowed models from Tanagra, so that numerous Egyptian{% endraw %}
{% raw %}terracottas have been found absolutely identical with the well-known{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Tanagra figures. The best specimens from the necropoles of Alex¬{% endraw %}
{% raw %}andria are to be seen in the museum of that city (Room I, Case BB,{% endraw %}
{% raw %}lower shelf). But it was just in the studios of these sculptors that{% endraw %}
{% raw %}an independent spirit manifested itself at a very early date. Their{% endraw %}
{% raw %}art emancipated itself from the imported forms and motives, and{% endraw %}
{% raw %}found a new method of expression for fresh materials supplied{% endraw %}
{% raw %}directly from the experiences and environment of the artists. This{% endraw %}
{% raw %}peculiarly Alexandrian art shows a character in complete contrast{% endraw %}
{% raw %}with the above-mentioned ideal school; its basis is the most uncom¬{% endraw %}
{% raw %}promising Realism. Its favourite subjects, frequently treated in a{% endraw %}
{% raw %}humorous or satirical vein, were common scenes from the street{% endraw %}
{% raw %}life of the metropolis, which seems in many points to have been{% endraw %}
{% raw %}similar to that of the modern Arab quarters of Cairo and Alexandria.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Thus, for example, in a bronze group from the Delta (now in the{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Polytechnic at Athens) we see a Nubian fruit-seller crouching on the{% endraw %}
{% raw %}ground and snatching a siesta behind his tastefully arranged wares,{% endraw %}
{% raw %}while an ape, seated on his shoulder, carries on investigations in his{% endraw %}
{% raw %}master's hair. Another bronze, of similar origin and in the same{% endraw %}
{% raw %}collection, represents a slave, with the typical head of the Akka{% endraw %}
{% raw %}negroes somewhat caricatured, hastily devouring a stolen piece of{% endraw %}
{% raw %}meat. From the same place also comes the basalt figure (now at{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Athens) of a Nubian boy carrying a burden on his raised left hand,{% endraw %}
{% raw %}while the elbow is supported on the hip, in a manner customary to{% endraw %}
{% raw %}this day. These three statuettes are masterpieces, not only in tech¬{% endraw %}
{% raw %}nique but even more in their keen conception and reproduction of{% endraw %}
{% raw %}racial characteristics. Numerous specimens of similar street-types{% endraw %}
{% raw %}will be found among the Greek terracottas in the museums at Cairo{% endraw %}
{% raw %}and Alexandria. We can best see what were the favourite subjects{% endraw %}
{% raw %}of the Alexandrian sculptors, and how they were treated, from those{% endraw %}
{% raw %}familiar works of humbler art; from the designs on terracotta lamps;{% endraw %}
{% raw %}and from the small bronze figures which are found in great numbers{% endraw %}
{% raw %}in the Nile Delta. These small works had, of course, their models{% endraw %}
{% raw %}in the higher branches; comp. the realistic figure of a woman in the{% endraw %}
