{% raw %}X. Buildings of the Mohammedans.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}By Franz Pasha.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}The Mohammedan style of architecture in the valley ofthe Nile{% endraw %}
{% raw %}was founded upon the forms of art which the victorious Arabs{% endraw %}
{% raw %}found in vogue among the Byzantines and the Copts, and upon those{% endraw %}
{% raw %}of Persian art of the era of the Sassanides. The buildings in Egypt{% endraw %}
{% raw %}exhibit a considerable variety coupled with 'a certain finish of style,{% endraw %}
{% raw %}but none of them dates back to the first period of Islam dominion;{% endraw %}
{% raw %}for the professors of the new religion were for centuries content{% endraw %}
{% raw %}merely to adapt the religious edifices of the conquered countries as{% endraw %}
{% raw %}mosques. This was a process of little difficulty, for the ceremonial{% endraw %}
{% raw %}requirements of the new religion were comparatively simple, and{% endraw %}
{% raw %}it took place in all parts of the great empire of the Khalifs. From{% endraw %}
{% raw %}casual references by the Arabian chroniclers we learn that the{% endraw %}
{% raw %}earliest prayer-houses built by the Arabs were merely enclosed courts,{% endraw %}
{% raw %}along the walls of which ran covered passages, supported by palm-{% endraw %}
{% raw %}trunks, in order to shelter the worshippers from sun and rain.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Costly mosques, with marble arcades, began to appear very gradually,{% endraw %}
{% raw %}under the influence of the ancient edifices and of the increasing{% endraw %}
{% raw %}wealth flowing from the military successes of the Mohammedans.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Columns from Greek and Roman temples were freely employed in{% endraw %}
{% raw %}these later mosques, but the early - Egyptian columns were too{% endraw %}
{% raw %}colossal to he used except occasionally as supports for domes or{% endraw %}
{% raw %}mausolea or for the kiblas. This employment of ancient columns{% endraw %}
{% raw %}in the mosques, frequently without any regard to harmony of style{% endraw %}
{% raw %}or size, brings it about that uniformity in the architecture of the{% endraw %}
{% raw %}arcades is observed only when the abacus is reached. No distinct{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Arabian order of columns was thus ever developed in Egypt. Only{% endraw %}
