{% raw %}ARABIC LANGUAGE.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}clxix{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Nile differs considerably in the pronunciation of the consonants,{% endraw %}
{% raw %}vocalisation, and accent from the ordinary Arabic dialects of Syria{% endraw %}
{% raw %}and elsewhere. Thus the letter -r is pronounced hard in Egypt and{% endraw %}
{% raw %}soft in Syria (see p. clxx). The variations, however, are not{% endraw %}
{% raw %}so great as to prevent the Syrians and Egyptians being mutually{% endraw %}
{% raw %}intelligible. There are, moreover, variations in the dialects spoken{% endraw %}
{% raw %}in Egypt; the Arabic of the Beduins is different from that spoken in{% endraw %}
{% raw %}the towns. The following remarks apply especially to the language{% endraw %}
{% raw %}as spoken in Cairo.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}The pronunciation of the vowels is apparently liable to variation:{% endraw %}
{% raw %}thus besides the more correct Mimbar the form Mamlar is also used;{% endraw %}
{% raw %}besides Maiddn, both Mtddn and Mlddn are heard. A sharply defined{% endraw %}
{% raw %}and exact pronunciation of the consonants is characteristic of Arabic{% endraw %}
{% raw %}and is absolutely essential to any satisfactory use of the language.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}The learner should endeavour at once to master the pronunciation{% endraw %}
{% raw %}of the more difficult Arabic consonants, such asr, p", Jo, 9,{% endraw %}
{% raw %}and LP, so as, for example, to be able to make a distinct difference{% endraw %}
{% raw %}between bet (house) and bed (eggs). Many of the sounds have no{% endraw %}
{% raw %}representatives in English.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}The Arabic alphabet was developed from that of the Nabataeans,{% endraw %}
{% raw %}who in turn adopted their written characters from the Palmyrenes.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}In spite of its external attractions, it is one of the most imperfect{% endraw %}
{% raw %}in existence. In written or printed Arabic the short vowels are{% endraw %}
{% raw %}usually omitted and have to be supplied by the reader, a feat which{% endraw %}
{% raw %}demands considerable skill and experience. In the Koran, however,{% endraw %}
{% raw %}the vowels are all indicated by appropriate signs.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Owing to the increasing intercourse between the native Egyptians{% endraw %}
{% raw %}andEurope, the former have of late adopted many words from other{% endraw %}
{% raw %}languages, chiefly from Italian, French, and English. Many Arabic{% endraw %}
{% raw %}words have, moreover, long since been replaced by Turkish equiÂ¬{% endraw %}
{% raw %}valents. The Egyptian dialect also contains some Coptic or ancient{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Egyptian words. Very few Europeans learn to pronounce Arabic{% endraw %}
{% raw %}accurately, even after a residence of many years in the country.{% endraw %}
