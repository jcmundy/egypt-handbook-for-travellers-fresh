{% raw %}Quantity and Accentuation of Vowels. Vowels with a circumflex{% endraw %}
{% raw %}accent (*) are long; other vowels are short. The accent falls on the last{% endraw %}
{% raw %}syllable when that is long (indicated by *). It falls on the third syllable{% endraw %}
{% raw %}from the end when that and the penultimate are short and neither vowel{% endraw %}
{% raw %}is followed by two consonants. It falls on the penultimate (1) when that{% endraw %}
{% raw %}is long or ends in two consonants and (2) when it is fhort and does not{% endraw %}
{% raw %}end in two consonants, but when the preceding syllable ends in two con¬{% endraw %}
{% raw %}sonants. Diphthongs (ai, ei, au) must be reckoned as equivalent to long{% endraw %}
{% raw %}vowels. There are exceptions to these rules.{% endraw %}
