{% raw %}1. Approaches to Egypt.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}The time-tables and handbooks of the various steamship companies{% endraw %}
{% raw %}(see below) give full information both as to the direct sea-routes from{% endraw %}
{% raw %}England and as to the steamers from Mediterranean ports. Particulars{% endraw %}
{% raw %}of the overland routes (see p. 2) from England to the Mediterranean{% endraw %}
{% raw %}will be found in Bradshaw^s Continental Railway Guide (2s.), the advertise¬{% endraw %}
{% raw %}ment pages of which also contain some shipping information. The 'P. & 0.'{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Co. issues tickets for the sea-route out and the overland route home, or{% endraw %}
{% raw %}vice versd. Heavy baggage should in all cases, if possible, be sent round{% endraw %}
{% raw %}by steamer. — Travellers from the United States may sail direct from{% endraw %}
{% raw %}New York to Naples or Genoa by German steamer (first-class only) and{% endraw %}
{% raw %}proceed thence by one of the steamers mentioned at pp. 2-4.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Travellers who desire to return from Egypt by one of the larger mail{% endraw %}
{% raw %}lines should secure a berth as soon as possible by applying to the ship¬{% endraw %}
{% raw %}ping offices in Cairo (p. 26), as these steamers are apt to be crowded{% endraw %}
{% raw %}from February to April inclusive. Information as to available accom¬{% endraw %}
{% raw %}modation is telegraphed from Aden to Cairo. The days and hours given{% endraw %}
{% raw %}below for the arrival and sailing of the steamers are approximate only,{% endraw %}
{% raw %}except in the case of the terminal ports. At intermediate ports the steamers{% endraw %}
{% raw %}are sometimes behind itinerary time, and not unfrequently a day or two{% endraw %}
{% raw %}in advance. In either case they proceed at once on their voyage.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Alexandria, the chief seaport of Egypt, is regularly visited by British,{% endraw %}
{% raw %}French, Austrian, Italian, Russian, Greek, and Egyptian steamers. Port{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Sa'id, on the Suez Canal, is touched at by the vessels of the great{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Australian, Asiatic, and East African lines. — Cairo may be reached by{% endraw %}
{% raw %}rail in 3V2 hrs. from Alexandria, and in 7 hrs. from Port Sa'id. The rail¬{% endraw %}
{% raw %}way from Port Sa'id is a narrow-gauge line as far as Isma'iliya, where{% endraw %}
{% raw %}carriages must be changed (p. 164; 1 hour's halt).{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Alexandria is almost 30° E. of Greenwich, and its time is 1 hr. 59 min.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}in advance of Greenwich time; that of Cairo is 2 hrs. 5 min. and that{% endraw %}
{% raw %}of Port Sa'id 2 hrs. 10 min. in advance of Greenwich. 'Central Europe'{% endraw %}
{% raw %}time (observed on German and Italian railways, etc.) is 1 hr. in advance{% endraw %}
{% raw %}of Greenwich.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Owing to the advance in the price of coal, a surtax of 10 per cent is{% endraw %}
{% raw %}at present charged on sea-passage fares to Egypt.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}a. Steamers from England direct.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}1. Steamers of the Peninsular and Oriental Steam Navi¬{% endraw %}
{% raw %}gation Co. ('P. & 0.'), leaving London (Royal Albert Docks) every{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Thurs., Frid., or Sat., sail via, Gibraltar and Marseilles, or via{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Gibraltar and Malta (see p. 2), in 12 days to Port Sa'id (fares 1st. cl.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}20/., 2nd cl. 12/.). Steamers ofthe same company occasionally call{% endraw %}
{% raw %}at Alexandria during the Egyptian season (same fares as to Port{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Sa'id). Return-tickets to Egypt are not issued by this line, but a{% endraw %}
{% raw %}reduction of one-third is allowed for the return-voyage if made{% endraw %}
{% raw %}within four months (20 per cent if made within six months).{% endraw %}
{% raw %}2. Steamers of the Orient-Pacific Line, leaving London (Til¬{% endraw %}
{% raw %}bury Docks) every alternate Frid., sail via Plymouth, Gibraltar,{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Marseilles, and (9 days) Naples to Port Sa'id and Isma'iliya (fares{% endraw %}
{% raw %}and return-voyage allowances as above).{% endraw %}
{% raw %}3. Steamers of the North German Lloyd ('Norddeutscher{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Lloyd'), for Australia or China, leaving Southampton fortnightly,{% endraw %}
{% raw %}sail via Genoa and Naples to Port Sa'id and Isma'iliya (fares from{% endraw %}
{% raw %}London, 20/. 6s., 12/. 6s.; return-tickets 32/. lls>, 19/. 10s.). Thence{% endraw %}
{% raw %}to Cairo by rail (fares from London, 21/., 12/. 13s. 6c/.; return{% endraw %}
{% raw %}tickets 34/., 20/. 5s.). Return-tickets are available for six months.{% endraw %}
