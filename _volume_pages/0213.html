{% raw %}Alexandria, called Iskanderieh by the Arabs and Turks, the sec¬{% endraw %}
{% raw %}ond town of Egypt, and one of the most important commercial cities{% endraw %}
{% raw %}on the Mediterranean, is situated at the W. extremity of the Nile-{% endraw %}
{% raw %}delta, on the narrow sandy strip separating Lake Mareotis from the{% endraw %}
{% raw %}sea, inE. long. 29°58'30", and N. lat. 31°13'5". It contains{% endraw %}
{% raw %}(1897) a population of 320,000, of whom about 46,000 are Europeans{% endraw %}
{% raw %}(Franks), chiefly Greeks and Italians, but including also some{% endraw %}
{% raw %}French and Austriaus, and a few British, Germans, etc. The Mo¬{% endraw %}
{% raw %}hammedans live almost exclusively in the N. and W. quarters of the{% endraw %}
{% raw %}city, the Europeans in the E. quarter, and at Ramleh (p. 19).{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Alexandria has two Harbours. The Fort Est, or E. harbour,{% endraw %}
{% raw %}known in antiquity as the 'Great Harbour' and then sheltered by a{% endraw %}
{% raw %}massive mole, is now accessible only for fishing-boats. The Port{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Ouest, or W. harbour (originally named Eunostos after the son-in-{% endraw %}
{% raw %}law of Ptolemy I.), was not freely used until the time of the later{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Roman emperors. Under the Arabs it was the chief haven, and after¬{% endraw %}
{% raw %}wards came to be called (erroneously) the 'Old Harbour'. Since{% endraw %}
{% raw %}1871 it has been enlarged by the addition of an Outer Harbour,{% endraw %}
{% raw %}about 1800 acres in area. This is protected by a breakwater nearly{% endraw %}
{% raw %}2 M. in length, constructed of solid masses of masonry. The broad{% endraw %}
{% raw %}horizontal surface is 10 ft. above the level of the water at low tide.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}A second pier, or Molo, nearly 1000 yds. in length, protects the{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Inner Harbour, which is about 475 acres in area and on an average{% endraw %}
{% raw %}27 ft. deep. From the beginning of this pier a series of new quays{% endraw %}
{% raw %}extends along the whole E. side of the harbour to the Arsenal (p. 14).{% endraw %}
{% raw %}The port is entered and cleared annually by upwards of 2000{% endraw %}
{% raw %}steamers, more than half of which are under the British flag. The{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Mahmudlyeh Canal (p. 11), which connects Alexandria with the Nile,{% endraw %}
{% raw %}enters the inner harbour by several locks (PI. C, 6). The chief{% endraw %}
{% raw %}exports are cotton, grain, cotton-seed, beans, rice, sugar, onions, etc.{% endraw %}
