{% raw %}Among the Principal Quarters of the ancient city Strabo partic¬{% endraw %}
{% raw %}ularly mentions the Necropolis or city of the dead, at the extreme W.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}end, 'where there are many gardens, tombs, and establishments for em¬{% endraw %}
{% raw %}balming bodies'; Rhakotis, 'the quarter of Alexandria situated above the{% endraw %}
{% raw %}ships' magazines', chiefly inhabited by Egyptians (comp. p. 8); the Royal{% endraw %}
{% raw %}City (Regia; afterwards called Bruchium), which was subsequently walled{% endraw %}
{% raw %}in, and contained the palaces and public buildings, on the mainland{% endraw %}
{% raw %}between the Lochias and the Heptastadium; the Jews' Quarter, situated{% endraw %}
{% raw %}to the E. of the Lochias. Outside the Canopic gate, on the E., lay the{% endraw %}
{% raw %}hippodrome, and farther to the E. was the suburb of Nicopolis (p. 18),{% endraw %}
{% raw %}30 stadia from Alexandria, which possessed an amphitheatre and a race¬{% endraw %}
{% raw %}course.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}The town was regularly built, with streets intersecting each other{% endraw %}
{% raw %}at right angles. The main artery of traffic seems to have been the long{% endraw %}
{% raw %}street beginning at the Canopic gate (comp. p. 15).{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Of the Principal Buildings of ancient Alexandria the scanty relics{% endraw %}
{% raw %}of only a few can be identified (p. 12). The Paneum is doubtless identical{% endraw %}
{% raw %}with the modern Kom ed-Dik (p. 15). The Gymnasium probably lay to{% endraw %}
{% raw %}the W. of this point.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}The theatre, the Sema, and the Museum were situated in the 'Royal{% endraw %}
{% raw %}City' (see above), which originally occupied a fifth and afterwards a fourth{% endraw %}

{% raw %}Theatre lay opposite the island of Antirrhodui, so that the spectators{% endraw %}
{% raw %}had a view of the sea in the background. The Sema was an enclosed{% endraw %}
{% raw %}space, within which were the tombs of Alexander the Great and of the{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Ptolemies. Adjoining the tomb of Queen Cleopatra stood a temple of Lis,{% endraw %}
{% raw %}remains of which have been discovered at the intersection of the Rue{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Nabi Daniel and the Rue de l'Hopital Grec. This discovery should go far{% endraw %}
{% raw %}to settle the site of the eagerly-sought tomb of Alexander.{% endraw %}
