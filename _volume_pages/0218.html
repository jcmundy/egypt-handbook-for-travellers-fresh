{% raw %}Christianity early found its way to Alexandria. According to{% endraw %}
{% raw %}tradition, the Gospel was first preached to the Alexandrians by St.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Mark (whose bones were removed to Venice in 828). The first{% endraw %}
{% raw %}great persecution of the Christians, which took place in the reign{% endraw %}
{% raw %}of Decius (250), was a terrible blow to the Alexandrians. The city{% endraw %}
{% raw %}had for a considerable time been the seat of a bishop, and had{% endraw %}
{% raw %}since 190 possessed a theological school, presided over by Pantaenus{% endraw %}
{% raw %}and Clement of Alexandria, who endeavoured to combine Christ¬{% endraw %}
{% raw %}ianity with the Neo-Platonism which sprang up about this period{% endraw %}
{% raw %}at Alexandria and was taught hy Ammonius Saccas, Herennius,{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Plotinus, and others. A second persecution took place in 257,{% endraw %}
{% raw %}during the reign of Valerian; and shortly afterwards, in the{% endraw %}
{% raw %}reign of Gallienus, the plague carried off a large portion of the{% endraw %}
{% raw %}population.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Christianity, however, still continued to gain ground, and Al¬{% endraw %}
{% raw %}exandria was even regarded as the chief seat of Christian erudition{% endraw %}
{% raw %}and of the orthodox faith under Athanasius. Alexandria was mean¬{% endraw %}
{% raw %}while soon obliged to yield to Constantinople its proud position as{% endraw %}
{% raw %}the centre of Greek thought and science. The sanguinary quarrels{% endraw %}
{% raw %}between the Athanasian party and the Arians under their unworthy{% endraw %}
{% raw %}bishop Georgius further contributed to the rapid decline of the city.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}On the accession of Julian the Apostate (361-363) the pagans of{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Alexandria again instituted a persecution of the Christians. In the{% endraw %}
{% raw %}reign of Theodosius (379-395), however, paganism received its{% endraw %}
{% raw %}death-blow, and Theophilus, the patriarch of Alexandria, displayed{% endraw %}
{% raw %}the utmost zeal in destroying the heathen temples and monuments.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}The famous statue of Serapis was burned and most of the temples{% endraw %}
{% raw %}were converted into churches. The material prosperity of the city{% endraw %}
{% raw %}also fell off so greatly that the municipality was no longer able to{% endraw %}
{% raw %}defray the cost of cleansing the Nile and keeping the canals open.{% endraw %}
