{% raw %}Catacombs. ALEXANDRIA. 2. Route. 13{% endraw %}
{% raw %}(p. 15). The old entrance has been restored, and the exploration of{% endraw %}
{% raw %}the interior is facilitated by wooden bridges and electric light. Ad¬{% endraw %}
{% raw %}mission 5 pias. (tickets obtainable also at the museum).{% endraw %}
{% raw %}A Winding Staircase (PI. A), with a large circular'light-shaft,{% endraw %}
{% raw %}descends into the catacomb; adjacent is a Sarcophagus Chamber{% endraw %}
{% raw %}(PI. R) of later construction. From the entrance to the upper floor{% endraw %}
{% raw %}(B), on each side of which is a semicircular recess, we enter a large{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Rotunda (C). In the middle of this, covered by a kind of cupola,{% endraw %}
{% raw %}is a shaft leading to'the lower stories. To the right lie two Smaller{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Rooms (D, E), with niches and sarcophagi. Above the latter are{% endraw %}
{% raw %}loculi or niche-tombs. To the left is the Triclinium I"unebre{% endraw %}
{% raw %}(PI. F), a large room with a ceiling borne by four pillars. Three{% endraw %}
{% raw %}wide platforms or divans have been hewn out of the rock for the{% endraw %}
{% raw %}banquets held in honour of the deceased. — The Staircase (G),{% endraw %}
{% raw %}which commands a good view of the chief sepulchral chambers,{% endraw %}
{% raw %}divides farther down into two flights, flanking the entrance to the{% endraw %}
{% raw %}lower story (H) and leading to the Vestibule (J) of the grave-{% endraw %}
{% raw %}chapel proper.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}The facade of the vestibule is articulated by two Egyptian col¬{% endraw %}
{% raw %}umns, with elaborate flower-capitals, which bear a frieze adorned{% endraw %}
{% raw %}with the winged solar disk and with sparrow-hawks; above this is{% endraw %}
{% raw %}the flat arch of the pediment. Inside , in deep niches to the right{% endraw %}
{% raw %}and left, are the Statues of the deceased and his wife, carved in{% endraw %}
{% raw %}white limestone in the Egyptian style. The door in the rear-wall of{% endraw %}
{% raw %}the vestibule is surmounted by the winged sun's disk and a Uraeus{% endraw %}
{% raw %}frieze. To the right and left, on pedestals, are two large serpents{% endraw %}
{% raw %}with the Egyptian double crown, the caduceus of Hermes, and the{% endraw %}
{% raw %}thyrsus of Dionysus. Above are shields with heads of Medusa.{% endraw %}
