{% raw %}11 Route 2. ALEXANDRIA. Palace Rds et-Tin.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}We now enter the Sepulchral Chapel (PL K). The sarcophagi{% endraw %}
{% raw %}containing the remains stand in niches (PL a-c) and are hewn, like{% endraw %}
{% raw %}their lids, out of the solid rock. The fronts are adorned, after the{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Greek fashion, with festoons, masks, heads of Medusa, bucrania,{% endraw %}
{% raw %}and bunches of grapes. On the middle one is a reclining figure of{% endraw %}
{% raw %}the deceased. The walls of the niches are decorated with Egyptian{% endraw %}
{% raw %}representations of religious import.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Central Niche (a). Rear Wall: On a bier supported by lions' feet{% endraw %}
{% raw %}rests the mummy, surrounded by Horus, Thoth, and Anubis, the three{% endraw %}
{% raw %}gods of the lower world; below the bier are three canopic vases. Left{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Wall: A priest of the dead, wearing a panther-skin, reads from a roll;{% endraw %}
{% raw %}goddesses sacrifice in front of an altar. Right Wall: similar scene. — Right{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Niche (b). Rear Wall: King sacrificing an Apis bull, with a winged god¬{% endraw %}
{% raw %}dess, with a feather, in the background. Left Wall: Royal sacrifice to{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Osiris. Right Wall: Two gods with long staves. — The representations in{% endraw %}
{% raw %}the Left Niche (c) are similar. — To the right and left of the door are{% endraw %}
{% raw %}representations of the jackal-headed Anubis and the croct dile-headed Sobk,{% endraw %}
{% raw %}each with the armour, shield, and lance of a Roman soldier.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Round the sepulchral chapel runs a Gallery (L), entered from{% endraw %}
{% raw %}the vestibule, with two rows of shelf-tombs (91 in all). Each of{% endraw %}
{% raw %}these contained at least three mummies. The names and ages of the{% endraw %}
{% raw %}deceased, in red paint, are still visible on some of the slabs. — Ad¬{% endraw %}
{% raw %}joining the corridor are four Later Rooms (N-Q), with shelf-tombs{% endraw %}
{% raw %}and sarcophagus-niches. At the hack of the corridor is a Sarco¬{% endraw %}
{% raw %}phagus Chamber (Pi. M), with three tomb-niches and plain pillars.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}We leave the burial-place here by a breach in the wall.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Farther to the W. lie some other previously known tombs, of less{% endraw %}
{% raw %}interest.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}The main portion of the Arab quarter lies on the ancient Hepta¬{% endraw %}
{% raw %}stadium (p. 9), between the E. and the W. harbours. Its chief{% endraw %}
{% raw %}thoroughfare is the Rue de France, with its prolongations the Rue{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Masguid Terbana and Rue Ras et-Tin (PL E, 3), which begins at{% endraw %}
{% raw %}the N.W. corner of the Place Mehemet Ali (p. 11). The Arab quarter{% endraw %}
{% raw %}is adjoined by the Turkish quarter (PL D, E, 2), on what was{% endraw %}
{% raw %}formerly the island of Pharos (p. 8), with less narrow streets and{% endraw %}
{% raw %}handsome houses and gardens. Both these quarters present interest¬{% endraw %}
{% raw %}ing scenes of Oriental life. The Rue Ras et-Tin ends at the vice¬{% endraw %}
{% raw %}regal —{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Palace of Ras et-Tin (PL A, B, 3), a name signifying 'pro¬{% endraw %}
{% raw %}montory of figs'. The palace, which was built by Mohammed 'Ali{% endraw %}
{% raw %}and restored hy Isma'il, is uninteresting. The balcony, however,{% endraw %}
{% raw %}commands a fine view of the extensive harbour. (Admission by{% endraw %}
{% raw %}ticket procured at the consulate.) The Harem, a separate building,{% endraw %}
{% raw %}facing the sea, is built on the model of the seraglio at Constantin¬{% endraw %}
{% raw %}ople. — A visit to the neighbouring Lighthouse (PL A, 4) is inter¬{% endraw %}
{% raw %}esting , especially in the early morning, but admission is granted{% endraw %}
{% raw %}only to those provided with an order to be obtained through the{% endraw %}
{% raw %}consulate. — The Naval Arsenal (PL C, D, 3) is not worth visiting.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}The Rue de la Porte de Rosette (PI. F-I, 4), leading to the{% endraw %}
{% raw %}E. from the centre of the city, is another important thoroughfare.{% endraw %}
