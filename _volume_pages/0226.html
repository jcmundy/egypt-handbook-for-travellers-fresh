{% raw %}of them have been destroyed by the inroads of the sea, and are now{% endraw %}
{% raw %}covered up. Farther on, to the right, is the large Slaughter House,{% endraw %}
{% raw %}built in 1898. On the beach of Meks are a casino, sea-baths, and{% endraw %}
{% raw %}several restaurants and cafes. To the S.W., close to the sea, is the{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Bab el-Arab ('Beduin Gate'), the extremity of a line of fortifications{% endraw %}
{% raw %}extending between the sea and Lake Mareotis (p. 21). The quarries{% endraw %}
{% raw %}of Meks (p. lii) supplied the material for the new harbour-works.{% endraw %}
