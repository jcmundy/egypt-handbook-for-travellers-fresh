{% raw %}441/? M. Rosetta (Hotel du Nil, kept hy Christo), Arabic Reshid{% endraw %}
{% raw %}(a Coptic name), with 14,300 inhab., almost exclusively natives,{% endraw %}
{% raw %}lies at the mouth of the Rosetta arm of the Nile (far1 el-gharbi), the{% endraw %}
{% raw %}ancient Bolbitinic arm (p. lii). During the middle ages and in{% endraw %}
{% raw %}more recent times its commercial prosperity was considerable, until{% endraw %}
{% raw %}the construction of the Mahmudiyeh Canal (pp. 11, 18) diverted its{% endraw %}
{% raw %}trade to Alexandria. The streets contain many small but substantial{% endraw %}
{% raw %}houses in a peculiar, half-European style, with projecting stories{% endraw %}
{% raw %}and windows towards the outside. The many gardens yield ex¬{% endraw %}
{% raw %}cellent fruit. Numerous columns from edifices of the heathen{% endraw %}
{% raw %}and Christian periods, some of them of granite and some of marble,{% endraw %}
{% raw %}are seen lying in various open spaces, particularly in one of con¬{% endraw %}
{% raw %}siderable size near the river, and a number of others are built into{% endraw %}
{% raw %}the houses. The spacious Mosque of Sakhlun is also embellished{% endraw %}
{% raw %}with many ancient columns. The hill of Abu Mandur, to the S. of{% endraw %}
{% raw %}the town, which commands a fine view, is supposed by some topo¬{% endraw %}
{% raw %}graphers to have been the site of the ancient Bolbitine, but it is{% endraw %}
{% raw %}more likely that this lay to the N. of Rosetta. — The fortifications{% endraw %}
{% raw %}to the N. of the town are not shown except by permission of the{% endraw %}
{% raw %}commandant.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}In 1799 M. Bouchard, a French captain of engineers, discovered in Fort{% endraw %}
{% raw %}St. Julien the celebrated Rosetta Stone (p. civ), which afforded European{% endraw %}
{% raw %}scholars a key to the language and writing ofthe ancient Egyptians, which{% endraw %}
{% raw %}had heen lost for nearly 14 centuries.{% endraw %}
