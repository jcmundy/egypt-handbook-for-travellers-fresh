{% raw %}DAMANHUR. 3. Route. 21{% endraw %}
{% raw %}The Lake Mareotis, or Mareia, as it was also called in ancient times,{% endraw %}
{% raw %}bounds Alexandria on the S. side. In Strabo's time it was filled from the{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Nile by means of numerous canals, both from the S. and E., which{% endraw %}
{% raw %}brought great traffic to this inland harbour, while the sea-harbour was{% endraw %}
{% raw %}more important for the export trade. The lake, which lies 8 ft. below{% endraw %}
{% raw %}the sea-level, was once surrounded by a luxuriantly fertile tract of{% endraw %}
{% raw %}country, yielding excellent white wine, which has been extolled by{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Horace and Virgil. During the Arabian and Turkish regime the waters{% endraw %}
{% raw %}of the lake gradually subsided, but in 1801, during the siege of Alexan¬{% endraw %}
{% raw %}dria, the English cut through the neck of land between the lake and the{% endraw %}
{% raw %}sea, a little to the W. of Abukir, thus laying an extensive and fertile{% endraw %}
{% raw %}region under water and destroying about 150 villages. Mohammed rAli{% endraw %}
{% raw %}did all in his power to repair the damage and to improve the environs of{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Alexandria, but 100,000 acres of cultivable land are said still to be covered{% endraw %}
{% raw %}by the sea. The water is now evaporated for the sake of its salt.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}At (17 M.) Kafr ed-Dawdr the first cotton-fields appear (on the{% endraw %}
{% raw %}right).{% endraw %}
{% raw %}A narrow-gauge loop-line diverging here (three trains daily) serves{% endraw %}
{% raw %}the villages of Sidi Ghazi, KOm el-Hanash, Abu el-Matdmir, Hdsh Issa (2200{% endraw %}
{% raw %}inhab.), and Nediba, and rejoins the main line at Damanhur (see below).{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Several Arab villages are seen from thelofty railway-embankment.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}38'/2 M. Damanhur (Railway Hotel), the second station at which{% endraw %}
{% raw %}the express stops (reached in 1 hr.), and the capital of the pro¬{% endraw %}
{% raw %}vince of Behireh, with 22,100 inhab., was the ancient Egyptian{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Time-en-Hor (city of Horus), and the Roman Hermopolis Parva.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}The town lies on an eminence and contains some tolerably sub¬{% endraw %}
{% raw %}stantial buildings. Among them are several manufactories for the{% endraw %}
{% raw %}separation of the cotton from the seeds, and above them tower{% endraw %}
{% raw %}several minarets. The Arabian cemetery lies close to the railway.{% endraw %}
