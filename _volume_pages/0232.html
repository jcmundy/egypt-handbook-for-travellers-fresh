{% raw %}Tanfa, the thriving capital of the province of Gharblyeh,{% endraw %}
{% raw %}which lies between the Rosetta and Damietta arms of the Nile,{% endraw %}
{% raw %}with a population estimated at 57,000, possesses large public build¬{% endraw %}
{% raw %}ings, bazaars, and an extensive palace of the Khedive.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}The Mosque of the Seiyid el-Bedawi is a modern building of no{% endraw %}
{% raw %}special interest. The court contains the basin for ablutions.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Seiyid Ahmed el-Bedawi, probably the most popular saint in Egypt,{% endraw %}
{% raw %}was born in the 12th cent, at Fez, and settled at Tanta after a pilgrimage{% endraw %}
{% raw %}to Mecca.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Travellers may generally visit the mosque without an attendant, but{% endraw %}
{% raw %}must not omit to deposit their shoes at the door. During the fair, however,{% endraw %}
{% raw %}which attracts among other visitors a number of fanatical Mohammedans{% endraw %}
{% raw %}from countries rarely visited by Europeans, it is advisable to procure the{% endraw %}
{% raw %}escort of the shekh of the mosque, to whom an introduction may he{% endraw %}
{% raw %}obtained through the consular agent (fee 1-2 fr.).{% endraw %}
{% raw %}The catafalque of the saint is covered with red velvet adorned{% endraw %}
{% raw %}with embroidery, and is enclosed by a handsome bronze railing. The{% endraw %}
{% raw %}dome is still unfinished. One large and two small schools are con¬{% endraw %}
{% raw %}nected with the mosque. The sebil, or tank, with the small medreseh{% endraw %}
{% raw %}(school) above it, in the space adjoining the mosque, is older.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}The most important of the three annual Fairs of Tanta is that{% endraw %}
{% raw %}of the 'molid' (nativity) of the saint in August, when upwards of{% endraw %}
{% raw %}half-a-million persons congregate here from all the Eastern countries{% endraw %}
{% raw %}bordering on the Mediterranean, and from the Mohammedan part of{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Africa. The streets are lined with booths and stalls, and singers.{% endraw %}
