{% raw %}4. Cairo.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Plan of Cairo. The European names of the streets used formerly{% endraw %}
{% raw %}to be written up in French only, so far as they were written up at all;{% endraw %}
{% raw %}but since the British occupation the Arabic names have been used,{% endraw %}
{% raw %}transliterated on a careless and inexact system (p. clxix). Our plan follows{% endraw %}
{% raw %}this transliteration in general, correcting, however, the more obvious{% endraw %}
{% raw %}defects (e.g. the English 'e£ is represented, as in the text, by '«'). Some{% endraw %}
{% raw %}of the French names have been retained.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}a. Railway Stations. Hotels and Pensions. Restaurants and Cafes.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Railway Stations. 1. Principal Station (PI. B, 1), to the N.W. of{% endraw %}
{% raw %}the town, beyond the Isma'iliyeh Canal, here crossed by the Kantaret{% endraw %}
{% raw %}el-Lemun, for Alexandria, Isma'iliya, Suez, the whole of the Delta, and{% endraw %}
{% raw %}for Upper Egypt. — 2. Matariyeh Station (PI. B, 1), on the E. side of the{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Isma'iliyeh Canal, for 'Abbdsiyeh, Kubbeh, ez-Zeilun, Matariyeh (Heliopolis),{% endraw %}
{% raw %}and el-Merg. — 3. Helwan Station (PI. B, 5), in the S.W. part ofthe town.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}The hotel-commissionnaires, with their omnibuses, and representa¬{% endraw %}
{% raw %}tives of the tourist-agents await the arrival of the fast trains and take charge{% endraw %}
{% raw %}of luggage. Small baggage may also be entrusted to the Arab porters,{% endraw %}
{% raw %}with numbered metal tickets on their arms, who will conduct the traveller{% endraw %}
{% raw %}to the hotel-omnibus or procure a cab for him (tariff, see p. 27). Heavy lug¬{% endraw %}
{% raw %}gage is sent on to the hotel in special vehicles.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Hotels. The leading hotels at Cairo are excellent, and even those of{% endraw %}
{% raw %}the second class are well fitted up, nearly all having electric light, baths,{% endraw %}
{% raw %}etc.; children and servants pay half-price; comp. p. xviii. As all the hotels{% endraw %}
{% raw %}frequently are full, especially in Jan., Feb., and March, it is a wise pre¬{% endraw %}
{% raw %}caution to telegraph for rooms from Alexandria or Port Sa'id.{% endraw %}
