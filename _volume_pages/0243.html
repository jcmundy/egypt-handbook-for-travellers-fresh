{% raw %}Cairo, el-Kdhira, or Masr el-Kdhira, or simply Masr or Misr, is{% endraw %}
{% raw %}situated in 30° 6' N. latitude, and 31° 26'E. longitude, on the{% endraw %}
{% raw %}right bank of the Nile, about 12 M. to the S. of the so-called 'cow's{% endraw %}
{% raw %}belly', the point where the stream divides into the Rosetta and Da¬{% endraw %}
{% raw %}mietta arms. It has not inaptly been styled 'the diamond stud on{% endraw %}
{% raw %}the handle of the fan of the Delta'. On the E. side of the city, which{% endraw %}
{% raw %}covers an area of about 11 square miles, rise the barren, reddish{% endraw %}
{% raw %}cliffs of the Mokattam Hills (p. 108), about 650 ft. in height, which{% endraw %}

{% raw %}the bank of the river and has entirely absorbed Bulak (p. 75), which{% endraw %}
{% raw %}was formerly its harbour.{% endraw %}
