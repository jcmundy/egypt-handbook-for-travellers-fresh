{% raw %}History.{% endraw %}

{% raw %}population consists of Egypto-Arabian townspeople (p. xliv), Fellah{% endraw %}

{% raw %}History of Cairo. At a very remote period a city lay on the{% endraw %}
{% raw %}E. bank of the Nile, opposite the great pyramids, and was called by{% endraw %}
{% raw %}the Egyptians Khere-ohe, or 'place of combat', because Hoius and Set{% endraw %}
{% raw %}were said to have contended here (p. cxx). The Greeks named it Bab¬{% endraw %}
{% raw %}ylon, probably in imitation of some Egyptian name of similar sound.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}The citadel of this town (p. 71) was fortified by the Romans, and{% endraw %}
{% raw %}under Augustus became the headquarters of one of the three legions{% endraw %}
{% raw %}stationed in Egypt. In A.D. 640 Babylon was captured by 'Amr ibn{% endraw %}
{% raw %}el-As, the general of Khalif 'Omar, who subsequently established{% endraw %}
{% raw %}the new capital of the country here, in opposition to Alexandria{% endraw %}
{% raw %}(p. 11), which was not so free from the disturbing Christian element.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}A mosque was built on the site of the conqueror's tent, and the{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Arabic word for tent (Fosfdf) became the name of the new city. The{% endraw %}
{% raw %}latter gradually expanded towards the N., and was extended to the{% endraw %}
{% raw %}N.E. as far as the base of the citadel by Ahmed ibn Tuliin, who{% endraw %}
{% raw %}erected the new quarter of el-Kafd'i'. Ahmed's splendour-loving{% endraw %}
{% raw %}son Khumdruyeh embellished the town with lavish magnificence.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}The modern city of Cairo was founded by Gohar, the general of the{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Fatimite Khalif Mui'zz, after the conquest of Egypt in 969 A.D.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}He erected a residence for the Khalif and barracks for the soldiers{% endraw %}
{% raw %}commanded by him to the N. of el-Kata'i'. At the hour when the{% endraw %}
{% raw %}foundation of the walls was laid, the planet Mars, which the Arabs{% endraw %}
{% raw %}call Kahir, or 'the victorious', crossed the meridian of the new eity;{% endraw %}
{% raw %}and Mui'zz accordingly named the place Masr el-Kdhira, or Kdhira.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Masr, the ancient Semitic name of Egypt, was also applied to Fostat,{% endraw %}
{% raw %}the form Masr el-'Atika (Old Cairo) being only introduced at a later{% endraw %}
{% raw %}date for the sake of clearness. The new town extended rapidly.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Bricks were easily made ofthe Nile mud, the Mokattam hills afforded{% endraw %}
{% raw %}excellent stone, while the gigantic ruins of the ancient Memphis{% endraw %}
{% raw %}on the opposite bank of the river were also used as a quarry. In{% endraw %}
{% raw %}973 Mui'zz took up his permanent residence in the new city of{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Cairo. In 1166 the citadel which still commands the city was erected{% endraw %}
{% raw %}by Salaheddin Yusuf ibn EyyiXb (Saladin) on the slope of the Mo¬{% endraw %}
{% raw %}kattam hills; and the same sultan caused the whole town, together{% endraw %}
{% raw %}with the citadel itself, to be enclosed by a wall, 29,000 ells in{% endraw %}

{% raw %}greatly extended and magnificently embellished. At that period,{% endraw %}
