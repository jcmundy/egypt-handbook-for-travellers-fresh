{% raw %}The Women of the poorer and rustic classes wear nothing but{% endraw %}
{% raw %}a blue gown and a veil. Their ornaments consist of silver, copper,{% endraw %}
{% raw %}or bead bracelets, earrings, and ankle-rings,{% endraw %}
{% raw %}while their chins, arms, and chests are often{% endraw %}
{% raw %}tatooed with blue marks. Similar tatooing{% endraw %}
{% raw %}is also common among the men. In Upper{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Egypt nose-rings are also frequently seen.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}The women of the upper classes are never{% endraw %}
{% raw %}so handsomely dressed in the streets as at{% endraw %}
{% raw %}home. When equipped for riding or walk¬{% endraw %}
{% raw %}ing, they wear a silk cloak, with very wide{% endraw %}
{% raw %}sleeves (tob or sableh), over their home attire.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}They also don the burko', or veil, which con¬{% endraw %}
{% raw %}sists of a long strip of muslin, covering the{% endraw %}
{% raw %}whole of the face except the eyes, and reach¬{% endraw %}
{% raw %}ing nearly to the feet. Lastly they put on{% endraw %}
{% raw %}the habara, a kind of mantle, which in{% endraw %}
{% raw %}the case of married women consists of two{% endraw %}
{% raw %}breadths of glossy black silk. Thus disguised,{% endraw %}
{% raw %}they look unnaturally broad and unwieldy,{% endraw %}
{% raw %}and not unlike bats. Young girls usually wear a white mantle.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}The wealthier ladies, who drive in their carriages attended by eu¬{% endraw %}
{% raw %}nuchs, usually veil their faces up to their eyes with thin white{% endraw %}
{% raw %}gauze in accordance with the fashion of Constantinople. The figures{% endraw %}
{% raw %}of Egyptian women, in early life, are generally upright and grace¬{% endraw %}
{% raw %}ful. They colour their eyelashes and eyelids{% endraw %}
{% raw %}dark, and their finger and toe-nails with{% endraw %}
{% raw %}henna, which gives them a brownish-yellow{% endraw %}
{% raw %}tint. Among other customs we may also{% endraw %}
{% raw %}mention the peculiar mode in which a wo¬{% endraw %}
{% raw %}man carries her child, either astride her{% endraw %}
{% raw %}shoulder, or resting on her hip. With regard{% endraw %}
{% raw %}to circumcision, weddings, and funerals, see{% endraw %}
{% raw %}pp. lxix, etc.{% endraw %}
