{% raw %}CAIRO.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}4. Route, ol{% endraw %}
{% raw %}The Hemali, who belong{% endraw %}
{% raw %}to one of the orders of der¬{% endraw %}
{% raw %}vishes (p. lxvii), are also{% endraw %}
{% raw %}engaged in selling water,{% endraw %}
{% raw %}which they flavour with{% endraw %}
{% raw %}orange-blossom (zakr), while{% endraw %}
{% raw %}others use liquorice ('erk-{% endraw %}
{% raw %}sus) or grape-juice (zeblb).{% endraw %}
{% raw %}There are also numerous{% endraw %}
{% raw %}itinerant vendors of different{% endraw %}
{% raw %}kinds*of fruit, vegetables,{% endraw %}
{% raw %}and sweetmeats, which to{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Europeans usually look very{% endraw %}
{% raw %}uninviting. Lastly, there are{% endraw %}
{% raw %}itinerant cooks, with port¬{% endraw %}
{% raw %}able kitchens, who sell small{% endraw %}
{% raw %}meat-puddings, fish, and{% endraw %}
{% raw %}other comestibles, and{% endraw %}
{% raw %}whose customers eat their{% endraw %}
{% raw %}dinners sitting cross-legged{% endraw %}
{% raw %}by the side of the street.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Most of theArabian Bar¬{% endraw %}
{% raw %}bers have their shops open{% endraw %}
{% raw %}to the street. Their principal occupation consists in shaving the{% endraw %}
{% raw %}heads of their customers in Oriental fashion, an art in which they{% endraw %}
{% raw %}are very expert.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Several times during the day and also at night the solemn and{% endraw %}
{% raw %}sonorous cry of the mueddin, summoning the faithful to prayer (see{% endraw %}
{% raw %}p. lxiii), reverberates from the tops of the minarets. When the{% endraw %}
{% raw %}shops are shut the watchmen (bawwdb) place their beds (serlr) of{% endraw %}
{% raw %}palm-twigs in the streets outside the entrances, and prepare to spend{% endraw %}
{% raw %}the night there ; sometimes they have only mats or rugs to sleep on.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}The street-traffic ceases in the Arab quarters comparatively early,{% endraw %}
{% raw %}while in the European districts it goes on till nearly midnight. But{% endraw %}
{% raw %}during the month of Ramadan it continues throughout the whole{% endraw %}
{% raw %}night even in the Arab quarters.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}The traveller will frequently have occasion to observe the Schools{% endraw %}
{% raw %}(kuttdb),oi which there are about 300 in Cairo, with 8-9000 scholars,{% endraw %}
{% raw %}and one of which is attached to almost every public fountain. He{% endraw %}
{% raw %}will find it very amusing to watch the efforts of the pklh, or school¬{% endraw %}
{% raw %}master , in teaching his pupils with the aid of admonitions and{% endraw %}
{% raw %}blows, while the boys themselves recite verses of the Koran with{% endraw %}
{% raw %}a swaying motion of their bodies, bending over their metal writing{% endraw %}
{% raw %}tablets, and yet finding time for the same tricks as European school¬{% endraw %}
{% raw %}boys. It is not advisable to watch the fikih too closely, as he is{% endraw %}
{% raw %}easily disconcerted and is then apt to be uncivil.{% endraw %}
