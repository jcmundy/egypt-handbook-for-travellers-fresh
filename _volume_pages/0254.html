{% raw %}el-Khordagiyeh, and at the first cross-street on the right we enter{% endraw %}

{% raw %}The Khan el-Khalil (PI. E, 3), which was once the centre of{% endraw %}
{% raw %}the commercial traffic of Cairo, is said to have been founded as{% endraw %}
{% raw %}early as the end of the 13th cent, on the site of ruined tombs of{% endraw %}
{% raw %}the Khalifs by the Mameluke Sultan El-Ashraf Khalil. It forms a{% endraw %}
{% raw %}distinct quarter of the city, and is intersected by a main street{% endraw %}
{% raw %}and numerous cross-lanes, formed by long rows of stalls of tradesmen{% endraw %}
{% raw %}and artizans, all covered over. Here are the headquarters of the{% endraw %}
{% raw %}silk and carpet merchants and the vendors of trinkets. We follow the{% endraw %}
{% raw %}main avenue, the Sikkktel-Badistan, which contains two graceful{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Arab gateways. In the first lane on the left is the Bazaar of the{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Shoemakers, in which the pretty red shoes of the Arabs may be{% endraw %}
{% raw %}purchased. Farther on, to the right of the main street, are some{% endraw %}
{% raw %}large Carpet Bazaars (Asadolla Irani, Hagg Ali Khabestari).{% endraw %}
{% raw %}The prices of Carpets, like those of other Oriental goods, are liable{% endraw %}
{% raw %}to great fluctuation. Those of Baghdad and Brussa (in Asia Minor) are{% endraw %}
{% raw %}the most sought after. As soon as a purchaser appears the dealers spread{% endraw %}
{% raw %}their wares over the whole court for his inspection. If the traveller is{% endraw %}
{% raw %}pressed for time he had better not attempt to make a purchase, as several{% endraw %}
{% raw %}hours must not unfrequently be spent in negotiation before a satisfactory{% endraw %}
{% raw %}bargain is concluded. The usual price of a light kuffiyeh (shawl for the{% endraw %}
{% raw %}head) is about 50 pias., and of one of heavier quality, with red and yellow{% endraw %}
{% raw %}stripes and interwoven with gold thread, 80-100 pias. The fringes are{% endraw %}
{% raw %}generally loosened and adjusted after the completion of the purchase.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Many of the so-called Damascene silks, and particularly the lighter{% endraw %}
{% raw %}knffiyehs in pleasing colours, are manufactured at Lyons and Crefeld.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}The table-covers of red, blue, or black cloth, embroidered with coloured{% endraw %}
{% raw %}silk (£E 1'/j-4), are well worthy of notice. The letters with which they{% endraw %}
{% raw %}are adorned rarely have any meaning.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Taking the second cross-lane on the right and passing through{% endraw %}
{% raw %}an interesting Arab Gateway, with stalactite vaulting, inscriptions,{% endraw %}
{% raw %}serpentine ornamentation, and a few mosaics, we enter the Brass{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Bazaar, in which many travellers are tempted to purchase. Oppo¬{% endraw %}
{% raw %}site the end of the Sikket el-Badistan is the entrance of the mosque{% endraw %}
{% raw %}of Seyidna Hosen.{% endraw %}
