{% raw %}3. The South-Eastern Quarters.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}The route described in this section leads via the Boulevard Mehemet-{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Ali to the Citadel, and thence by a wide curve to the S. back to the{% endraw %}
{% raw %}boulevard. Electric Tramway to the Place Rumeleh, see p. 25.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Starting from the Place 'Atabet el-Khadra (see p. 40), the{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Shari'a Mohammed 'Ali or Boulevard Mehemet-Ali (PL C-E, 3-6),{% endraw %}
{% raw %}1860 yds. in length, leads to the S.E. straight to the foot of the{% endraw %}
{% raw %}citadel. A little more than one-third of the way down the boulevard{% endraw %}
{% raw %}we cross the filled-in bed of the El-Khnllg canal; to the left lies{% endraw %}
{% raw %}the Place Bdb el-Khalk, with the Administration Building (Gouvem-{% endraw %}
{% raw %}orat; PL D, 4), containing the Police Headquarters (p. 26), and the new{% endraw %}
{% raw %}building ofthe Viceregal Library (p. 56). The Shari'a Bab el-Khalk{% endraw %}
{% raw %}(called farther on Habbaniyeh, p. 58) leads to the right to the{% endraw %}
{% raw %}present library (p. 56). About 1/i M. farther on, a side-street leads{% endraw %}
{% raw %}to the Gami'a el-Melekeh Soflya (PL D, 5; entrance by the S. portal),{% endraw %}
{% raw %}a Turkish - Arabian mosque of 1611, with a dome supported by{% endraw %}
{% raw %}antique columns, and ornamented in the Byzantine-Arabian style.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}The pulpit is of marble. A short visit may also be paid to the small{% endraw %}
{% raw %}mosque of El-Burdini (PL D, 5), built in 1630 and restored in 1885,{% endraw %}
{% raw %}lavishly adorned with mosaics, and adjoined by a large and elegant{% endraw %}
{% raw %}minaret. — Farther on the boulevard passes the uninteresting and{% endraw %}
{% raw %}much altered mosque of El-Kisun ('Asun'; PL D, 5), and ends{% endraw %}
{% raw %}near two large mosques. That on the left is the unfinished Gami'a{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Bifa'iyeh, named after an order of dervishes (p. lxvi), and con¬{% endraw %}
{% raw %}taining the family burial-vault of the Khedive Isma'il. — On the{% endraw %}
{% raw %}right rises the —{% endraw %}
{% raw %}**Gami'a Sultan Hasan (PL E, 6), the 'superb mosque', and the{% endraw %}
{% raw %}finest existing monument of Byzantine-Arabian architecture. It was{% endraw %}
{% raw %}built in 1356-59 by Sultan Hasan (p. xcvi; tickets of admission{% endraw %}
{% raw %}necessary, see p. 30; 1 pias. for the use of shoes). The exterior of this{% endraw %}
{% raw %}huge building, which occupies a bush-grown rock below the citadel,{% endraw %}
{% raw %}recalls the broad surfaces of the early-Egyptian temples. The massive{% endraw %}
{% raw %}**Gateway (PL I), 85 ft. high, though its scheme of ornamentation{% endraw %}
{% raw %}was never fully carried out, has been more or less closely imitated{% endraw %}
{% raw %}in the entrances to many other Egyptian mosques. —All the facades{% endraw %}
{% raw %}ofthe mosque are crowned by a unique and boldly projecting cornice,{% endraw %}
{% raw %}of 'stalactite' formation and formerly furnished with pinnacles. The{% endraw %}
{% raw %}shallow central recess of the N.W. facade displays one large window{% endraw %}
{% raw %}and a little ornamentation, while the other recesses are pierced by{% endraw %}
{% raw %}windows of unequal size. The outside ofthe mausoleum is decorated{% endraw %}
{% raw %}in the Byzantine-Seljuk style. — The S. Minaret (PL 1 la) is the high¬{% endraw %}
{% raw %}est minaret in Cairo, measuring 270 ft. from the pavement (that of{% endraw %}
{% raw %}El-Ghurl 213 ft., Kalaun 193 ft., Muaiyad 167 ft., El-Azhar 167 ft.,{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Kdit Bey and Barkuk 164 ft., Tulun 132 ft., 'Amr 105 ft.). The{% endraw %}
{% raw %}corresponding minaret (PL lib) on the N. facade was overthrown{% endraw %}
{% raw %}by an earthquake, but was afterwards rebuilt on a smaller scale.{% endraw %}
