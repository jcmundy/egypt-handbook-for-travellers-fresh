{% raw %}54 Route 4. CAIRO. 3. South-Eastern{% endraw %}
{% raw %}It lies in the quarter Kal'at el-Kebsh, on a hill named Gebel Yeshkur.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}The edifice is said to have been designed by a Christian prisoner{% endraw %}
{% raw %}in imitation of the Ka'ba at Mecca, but without columns, and the{% endraw %}
{% raw %}whole of the building was constructed of entirely new materials.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}The walls consist of brick, coated with stucco. The older part of{% endraw %}
{% raw %}the ornamentation, which is in carved stucco (not moulded) and wood,{% endraw %}
{% raw %}exhibits none of the intricate forms of the Byzantine-Arabian style,{% endraw %}
{% raw %}which appear in the later restorations.{% endraw %}
