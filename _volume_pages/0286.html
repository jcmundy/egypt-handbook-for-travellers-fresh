{% raw %}The Tombs of the Mamelukes, to the S. of the Citadel, in¬{% endraw %}
{% raw %}cluding monuments of various periods, are most conveniently visited{% endraw %}
{% raw %}via the Bdb el-Kardfeh (PL E, 7), whence a good carriage-road leads{% endraw %}
{% raw %}through the midst of the tombs to the Hosh el-Basha (see below).{% endraw %}
{% raw %}The tombs approach close to the city and extend as far as to the{% endraw %}
{% raw %}slopes of the Mokattam. The older tombs are in much poorer{% endraw %}
{% raw %}preservation than the Tombs of the Khalifs, owing to their con¬{% endraw %}
{% raw %}version into modern burial-places. Some are now represented only{% endraw %}
{% raw %}by their minarets. A few are of architectural and artistic interest;{% endraw %}
{% raw %}but historical data as to the names of their builders are entirely{% endraw %}
{% raw %}wanting, for the extant inscriptions upon them are almost exclu¬{% endraw %}
{% raw %}sively verses of the Keran.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}To the S. the imposing dome of the tomb of Imam Shafe'i{% endraw %}
{% raw %}( ca. 1240), of a bluish-grey colour, is conspicuous. Beside it is an{% endraw %}
{% raw %}extensive modern necropolis, chiefly of family-tombs. The dome{% endraw %}
{% raw %}itself and the Byzantine carvings and panelling on the lower part{% endraw %}
{% raw %}of the interior walls are interesting.{% endraw %}
