{% raw %}the upper series, supporting the gallery, consists of alternate groups{% endraw %}

{% raw %}gregation first pay their homage to a number of pictures of saints hanging{% endraw %}
{% raw %}on the walls (the veneration of saints and ofthe Virgin being a prominent{% endraw %}
{% raw %}feature of the Coptic system), and then kneel before the altar and kiss{% endraw %}
{% raw %}the hand of the priest. They then take their stand (for there are no seats){% endraw %}
{% raw %}in the part of the church allotted to them, leaning on crutches which{% endraw %}
{% raw %}they bring for the purpose, as the service often lasts for three hours.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}The service begins with the reading or chanting of prayers and passages{% endraw %}
{% raw %}from the Gospels, partly in the Coptic language, and partly in Arabic,{% endraw %}
{% raw %}in which the priest is assisted by a schoolmaster and a choir of boys.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}During this performance the worshippers, with very few exceptions,{% endraw %}
{% raw %}engage freely in conversation. After a time the burning of incense begins.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}The priest, swinging his censer, leaves the hekal and joins the con¬{% endraw %}
{% raw %}gregation, each member of which he blesses, placing his hand on their{% endraw %}
{% raw %}heads. — The Celebration of the Eucharist is very frequent in the Coptic{% endraw %}
{% raw %}churches, immediately following the ordinary service. — On Palm Sunday{% endraw %}
{% raw %}wreaths of palm are blessed by the priest, which are then worn by the{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Copts under their tarbushes during the whole of the following year as{% endraw %}
{% raw %}amulets against every misfortune that can befall body or soul. — On{% endraw %}
{% raw %}18th January, the anniversary of the Baptism of Christ ('id el-ghitds),{% endraw %}
{% raw %}men and boys plunge into the large font or bath which is to be found{% endraw %}
{% raw %}in most Coptic churches, the water having been first blessed by the priest.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Or they perform the same ceremony in the Nile, into which they first{% endraw %}
{% raw %}pour some consecrated water. On the eve of this festival, as well as on{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Holy Thursday and on the festival of the Apostles, the priest washes the{% endraw %}
{% raw %}feet of the whole of his congregation. — An external form to which the{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Copts attach great weight is the observance of fasts, and a Copt who is{% endraw %}
{% raw %}negligent in this respect will rarely be met with. On these occasions all{% endraw %}
{% raw %}kinds of animal food, not excepting fat, eggs, butter, and cheese, are{% endraw %}
{% raw %}prohibited. — Comp. Butler's Coptic Churches of Egypt' (1884).{% endraw %}
