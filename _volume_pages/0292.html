{% raw %}the Tomb of Shekh 'Abdallah, son of 'Amr. In the W. colonnade,{% endraw %}
{% raw %}which consisted of a single row of columns only, a Pair of Columns{% endraw %}
{% raw %}alone remains. They are placed very close together, and it is said{% endraw %}
{% raw %}that none but honest men could squeeze themselves between them.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}This mosque is almost disused. On the last Friday in the month of{% endraw %}
{% raw %}fasting, however, a solemn service is annually held here, in which the{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Khedive and his grandees take part. On the remaining Fridays throughout{% endraw %}
{% raw %}f he year a handful of poor Muslims, mostly of the working classes, assemble{% endraw %}
{% raw %}tor worship in the venerable but poorly preserved sanctuary. — In 1808{% endraw %}
{% raw %}this mosque witnessed a very remarkable scene. The whole of the Mo¬{% endraw %}
{% raw %}hammedan priesthood, the Christian clergy of every sect, and the Jewish{% endraw %}
{% raw %}rabbis, with one accord, assembled in the mosque of'Amr to pray for the{% endraw %}
{% raw %}rise of the Nile, which had delayed beyond the usual period.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}The traveller will find it not uninteresting to visit one of the{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Kulleh Manufactories on the W. and S. sides of the mosque (bakshish,{% endraw %}
{% raw %}a few copper coins). The porous water-jars (Arabic Kulleh) used{% endraw %}
{% raw %}throughout the whole of Egypt are chiefly manufactured at Keneh{% endraw %}
{% raw %}in Upper Egypt of light-grey clay of very equal consistency. The{% endraw %}
{% raw %}remarkably uniform and delicate porosity of the vessels is produced{% endraw %}
{% raw %}by mixing the clay with ashes. The rapid evaporation caused by{% endraw %}
{% raw %}the porosity of the kulleh cools the liquid within to a temperature{% endraw %}
{% raw %}of 12-14° lower than that of the surrounding air.{% endraw %}
