{% raw %}3. Bulak and the Island of Gezireh.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Bulak (or Boulaq), the river-harbour of Cairo, situated beyond{% endraw %}
{% raw %}the Isma'iliyeh Canal, is reached from the Ezbekiyeh via, the Shdri'a{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Bulak and the bridge of Abu Leleh (PL A, B, 3; electric tramway,{% endraw %}
{% raw %}p. 27). Its narrow streets present a very busy scene, affording a{% endraw %}
{% raw %}more characteristic picture of Oriental life than the capital. Goods{% endraw %}
{% raw %}are conveyed hither from Upper Egypt, from Nubia, and from the{% endraw %}
{% raw %}fertile Delta. The principal quay, nearly opposite the Ghezireh{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Palace Hotel (p. 76), is most frequented between October and De¬{% endraw %}
{% raw %}cember, when the rapids of the river are most easily navigated.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}At the N. end of the town is situated the Arsenal, founded in{% endraw %}
{% raw %}1835, with a manufactory of weapons attached to it. Machinery for{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Egyptian manufactories arriving from Europe is put together at{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Bulak before being sent to its final destination, and all repairs of{% endraw %}
{% raw %}machinery are executed here. Bulak also boasts of a large Iron{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Foundry, a Khedivial Technical School, a Paper Manufactory, a{% endraw %}
{% raw %}House of Correction for Women, a, Lunatic Asylum, and the Govern¬{% endraw %}
{% raw %}ment Printing Ofpce, none of which establishments will interest{% endraw %}
{% raw %}ordinary travellers.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}The island of Gezireh (Geziret Bulak) lies in the Nile opposite{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Bulak. Travellers cross in a few minutes by motor-launch (in{% endraw %}
{% raw %}winter only) or by rowing-boat. The road thither crosses the hand¬{% endraw %}
{% raw %}some Great Nile Bridge adjoining the Kasr en-Nil (PL A, 5; p. 40),{% endraw %}
{% raw %}the extensive barracks of Cairo. The bridge, about 420 yds. in{% endraw %}
{% raw %}length, was built by a French iron company. The buttresses, which{% endraw %}
{% raw %}were constructed with the aid of air-tight 'caissons', are of solid{% endraw %}
{% raw %}stone, and are 55 yds. apart. The bottom of the foundations is{% endraw %}
{% raw %}about 45 ft. below the level of the river when at its lowest. At a{% endraw %}
{% raw %}very early hour in the morning an interesting and picturesque crowd{% endraw %}
{% raw %}of peasantry may be seen congregated here for the purpose of paying{% endraw %}
{% raw %}duty on the wares they are bringing to market. The Nile bridge{% endraw %}
{% raw %}is opened for the passage of vessels through it for an hour in the{% endraw %}
{% raw %}morning and another in the afternoon (announced on the notice-{% endraw %}
{% raw %}boards), and is then impassable for foot-passengers and carriages.{% endraw %}
