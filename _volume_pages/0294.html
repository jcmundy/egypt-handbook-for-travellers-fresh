{% raw %}Below Bulak and the island the Nile is spanned by the Railway{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Bridge mentioned at pp. 23, 131, which can also be used by foot-{% endraw %}
{% raw %}passengers and carriages. The station on the left bank is known{% endraw %}
{% raw %}as Embdbeh. This was the scene of the 'Battle of the Pyramids',{% endraw %}
{% raw %}in which the French under Bonaparte defeated the Mamelukes (July{% endraw %}

{% raw %}4. Shubra and the 'Abbasiyeh.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}The inspection of the viceregal gardens at Shubra requires a Permit,{% endraw %}
{% raw %}to be obtained at the 'dai'ra' or office, of Prince Husen Pasha, at the Kasr{% endraw %}
{% raw %}'Ali (p. 41). — Electric tramway to the 'Abbasiyeh, see p. 27.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}The Shdri'a Shubra and the broad Shubra Avenue, shaded by{% endraw %}
{% raw %}beautiful sycamore and lebbek trees, beyond the Lhnun Bridge and{% endraw %}
{% raw %}the Railway Stations (PL B, A, 1; p. 24), lead straight N. to the{% endraw %}
{% raw %}(272 M.) village and palace of Shubra, on the Nile. To the right, a{% endraw %}
{% raw %}little back from the road, is the beautiful Villa Ciccolani. On the{% endraw %}
{% raw %}left is the former viceregal palace Kasr en-Nuzha, now occupied by{% endraw %}
{% raw %}the Ecole Tewfik. Farther to the right is the Pensionnat du Bon{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Pasteur (p. 30).{% endraw %}

{% raw %}we cross a canal by a drawbridge, turn to the left towards the{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Nile, and follow the embankment to an archway. Passing through{% endraw %}
