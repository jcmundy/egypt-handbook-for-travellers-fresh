{% raw %}The Gebel el-Ahmar, or Red Mountain, rises to the E. of'Abbasiyeh.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}At its base, reached by a carriage-road diverging to the right a short{% endraw %}
{% raw %}distance outside the Bdb Husiniyeh (p. 64), is an old Viceregal Chateau.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}The mountain consists of'a very hard mioeene conglomerate of sand,{% endraw %}
{% raw %}pebbles, and fragments of fossil wood, cemented together by means of{% endraw %}
{% raw %}silicic acid, and coloured red or yellowish brown with oxide of iron.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}For many centuries the quarries here have yielded excellent and durable{% endraw %}
{% raw %}mill-stones, and material for the construction of the roads of Cairo and{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Alexandria.{% endraw %}
