{% raw %}The Palace of Gizeh, a huge rambling edifice, built in a light{% endraw %}
{% raw %}and unsubstantial Oriental style, with about 500 saloons and apart¬{% endraw %}
{% raw %}ments, was erected by the Khedive Isma'il for his harem at a cost{% endraw %}
{% raw %}stated at 120 million francs. In 1889 the Museum of Egyptian{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Antiquities, formerly at Bulak, was installed here, but the process{% endraw %}
{% raw %}of transferring this valuable collection to a more suitable and safer{% endraw %}
{% raw %}edifice, recently completed, has begun (see p. 40).{% endraw %}
{% raw %}The **Museum of Egyptian Antiquities is the most valuable{% endraw %}
{% raw %}collection ofthe kind. It was founded by the eminent French Egypt¬{% endraw %}
{% raw %}ologist Aug. Mariette (d. 1881). The present director is the no less{% endraw %}
{% raw %}eminent G. Maspero; the conservator is Emil Brugsch-Bey, assisted{% endraw %}
{% raw %}by Ahmed Kamal Bey and G. Daressy. — The earliest description of{% endraw %}
{% raw %}the Museum is Mariette's 'Les Principaux Monuments du Musee de{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Boulacq' (18d>i:) ; Maspero also published a' Guide duVisiteur auMusee{% endraw %}
{% raw %}de Boulacq' (1884). The present detailed Catalogue (1894), which{% endraw %}
{% raw %}is sold at the entrance, though not perfect, is quite indispensable{% endraw %}
{% raw %}for students. The more important and the more recently acquired{% endraw %}
{% raw %}objects are provided with full descriptive labels in French. Owing{% endraw %}
{% raw %}to the alterations consequent upon the removal to the new building,{% endraw %}
{% raw %}the following description does not pretend to accuracy on all points.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}The museum is open in winter daily, except Mon. and Frid., from{% endraw %}
{% raw %}9.30 till 4.30, in summer (16th April to 15th Oct.) from 8.30 to{% endraw %}
{% raw %}12; admission 5 piastres, free in summer. Sticks and umbrellas are{% endraw %}
{% raw %}given up at the entrance and returned at the exit (no fee).{% endraw %}
{% raw %}A special room is reserved for Students; apply to the director or to{% endraw %}
{% raw %}one of the conservators. — In the Sale Room (R. lxij duplicates, etc., of the{% endraw %}
{% raw %}antiquities may be purchased, at prices fixed by the Museum authorities{% endraw %}
{% raw %}and plainly marked on the objects offered. Purchases here made have, of{% endraw %}
{% raw %}course, a guarantee of their genuineness (comp. also p. xv). A permit{% endraw %}
{% raw %}to export is given with each purchase. — Travellers going on to Upper{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Egypt should provide themselves here with a General Admission Ticket{% endraw %}
{% raw %}■ if the Service des Antiquites de l'Egypte (£ 1 E.; comp. p. 184), admitting{% endraw %}
{% raw %}them to all monuments.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}In the Garden near the entrance is a tall pedestal supporting{% endraw %}
{% raw %}a Sphinx in red granite, with the names of the Pharaoh Ramses II.,{% endraw %}
{% raw %}added later. On the right of the road to the palace is the top of an{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Obelisk, dating from the reign of Queen Makere, whose figure was{% endraw %}
{% raw %}replaced by sacrificial tables under Thutmosis III. On a platform{% endraw %}
{% raw %}of masonry in front of the main entrance rests the marble sarcophagus{% endraw %}
{% raw %}of Aug. Mariette (see above).{% endraw %}
