{% raw %}N. side: Table Case containing **Golden Ornaments, found in{% endraw %}
{% raw %}1894 by J. de Morgan near the N. and S. brick pyramid sat Dahshur{% endraw %}
{% raw %}(p. 153). The principal pieces, illustrating the high pitch of skill{% endraw %}
{% raw %}attained by Egyptian goldsmiths about 2000 B.C., are the following.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}— 1348. Belonging to Princess Sit-Hathor (12th Dyn.): a. Gold{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Breast Ornament in the shape of a temple, inlaid in a mosaic style{% endraw %}
{% raw %}with cornelian, lapis lazuli, and turquoises; in the middle is the{% endraw %}
{% raw %}name of Isertesen (Sesostcis) II., supported on either side by ahawk{% endraw %}
{% raw %}perched upon the hieroglyphic symbol for 'gold', and wearing the{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Egyptian double crown. 6. Six gold shells, from a necklace, d. Two{% endraw %}
{% raw %}lotus flowers tied together. — 1349. Belonging to the Princess{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Merit: b. Gold Breast Ornament inlaid with gems; at the top is a{% endraw %}
{% raw %}vulture with outspread wings, representing Nekhbet, the patron-{% endraw %}
{% raw %}goddess of the king; below is the name of Usertesen III., to the right{% endraw %}
{% raw %}and left of which are two hawk-headed sphinxes, as symbols of the{% endraw %}
{% raw %}king, each trampling upon two foes. a. Breast Ornament ofthe same{% endraw %}
{% raw %}kind; at the top is the vulture, below is the name of Amenemhet III.,{% endraw %}
{% raw %}on either side of which the king appears, grasping a kneeling Asiatic{% endraw %}
{% raw %}by the hair and smiting him with a club. c. Golden Shell, orna¬{% endraw %}
{% raw %}mented with a water-lily (so-called Nymphaea) inlaid in coloured{% endraw %}
{% raw %}gems, g, h. Two small golden cosmetic boxes, one with zigzag{% endraw %}
{% raw %}ornaments, the other with inlaid gems. i. Two Bracelet Ornaments{% endraw %}
{% raw %}in gold and coloured gems, showing the name of Amenemhet III.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}q, r. Cosmetic boxes in cornelian, lapis lazuli, golden shells, lions'{% endraw %}
{% raw %}heads (from chains), etc. — 1350. Found in the coffin of the Prin¬{% endraw %}
{% raw %}cess Neb-hetepti-khrot: a. Silver diadem inlaid with gems, with the{% endraw %}
{% raw %}urteus-serpent in front; b. Golden vulture's head; c. Two ends of a{% endraw %}
{% raw %}chain in the form of golden hawks' heads ; d. Dagger-blade in gold;{% endraw %}
{% raw %}f. Portion of a scourge. Also bracelets, necklaces, golden pins, etc.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}1361-1363. Table Case: **1361. Ornaments found in the tomb{% endraw %}
{% raw %}of Princess Ita, to the W. of the Pyramid of Amenemhet II. at Dah¬{% endraw %}
{% raw %}shur: a. Dagger with inlaid hilt; b. Bracelet of massive gold,{% endraw %}
{% raw %}c. Hawk in cornelian. Also chains: network of cornelian and porce¬{% endraw %}
{% raw %}lain beads.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}**1362. Ornaments from the tomb of Princess Khnumet: a. Pair{% endraw %}
{% raw %}of golden hawks' heads, from the end of a breast-ornament; b. Gold{% endraw %}
{% raw %}chains: chain with various appendages; e. Clasps of bracelets. Chains{% endraw %}
{% raw %}and appendages of the finest workmanship.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}**1363. Ornaments from the tomb (serdab) of Khnumet:{% endraw %}
{% raw %}a. Golden wreath, with inlaid stones; b. Similar wreath ; c. Golden{% endraw %}
