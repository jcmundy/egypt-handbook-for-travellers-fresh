{% raw %}Room XXV (Ethiopian Room) contains memorial stones of{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Ethiopian kings, found at Napata. 160. Stele of Piankhi (p. lxxxiv),{% endraw %}
{% raw %}celebrating his victories over Egyptian princes; 161. Har-si-yotf,{% endraw %}
{% raw %}describing his victorious campaign in Nubia; 162. Tanut-amon re¬{% endraw %}
{% raw %}counts his campaign against the Assyrians in Memphis. — E. side :{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Esperute records his accession (ca. 650 B.C.). — *164. Portrait-{% endraw %}
{% raw %}head in dark granite of Taharka (the Tirhakah of the Bible), the{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Ethiopian conqueror (25th Dyn.); negro cast of features, nose{% endraw %}
{% raw %}mutilated. The same negro cast is clearly seen in the relief of this{% endraw %}
{% raw %}king on the under-mentioned Esarhaddon Stele.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}W. side: 165. Tablet excommunicating two families, who had{% endraw %}
{% raw %}committed a crime in a temple, from Napata. 166. Fine head,{% endraw %}
{% raw %}from a colossal statue of Ramses II. (Luxor).{% endraw %}
{% raw %}In the middle ofthe room: 167. King Merenptah, holding a{% endraw %}
{% raw %}figure of Osiris (19th Dyn.). — *Stele with inscriptions of Amen¬{% endraw %}
{% raw %}ophis III., referring to his buildings, and of Merenptah, referring{% endraw %}
{% raw %}to his victories over the Libyans ('Israel Stele').{% endraw %}
{% raw %}This stood originally in a temple of Amenophis III. at Thebes and was{% endraw %}
{% raw %}afterwards used by King Merenptah, who inscribed upon the back a hymn,{% endraw %}
{% raw %}concluding with the words: 'Israel is wasted and his seed is brought to{% endraw %}
{% raw %}nothing'. This is the earliest mention of Israel in any Egyptian inscription.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}The stele was discovered by Flinders Petrie in a temple at Thebes (p. 287).{% endraw %}
{% raw %}By the window: *1377. Stele of Amenophis III., referring to his{% endraw %}
{% raw %}victories, with fine reliefs.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}S. side of the corridor leading to R. XXVI: Coloured cast of the{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Esarhaddon Stele, found at Senjirli, and now in the Berlin Museum.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}On the front of the stele appears the Assyrian King Esarhaddon (681-{% endraw %}
{% raw %}668 B.C.), in royal garb and wearing the Assyrian crown, holding a mace{% endraw %}
{% raw %}and a fan(?). Before him stand the defeated Pharaoh, Taharka (of a negro{% endraw %}
{% raw %}type, with the royal serpent on his brow), and a Syrian prince (with{% endraw %}
{% raw %}beard, crown, and long robe), with cords fastened to their lips and held{% endraw %}
{% raw %}by the victorious Esarhaddon in his left hand. The inscription gives a{% endraw %}
{% raw %}minute account of the conquest of Egypt and Ethiopia after the defeat of{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Taharka at Memphis.{% endraw %}
