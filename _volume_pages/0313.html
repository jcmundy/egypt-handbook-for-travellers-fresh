{% raw %}Case B: Vases and portions of statuettes. 381. Tasteful small{% endraw %}
{% raw %}stele of limestone, in the shape of a naos, a votive gift from an{% endraw %}
{% raw %}interpreter of dreams. Various utensils and figures in blue enamel¬{% endraw %}
{% raw %}led earthenware. 383. Small alabaster figure of Venus untying her{% endraw %}
{% raw %}sandal.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Between Cases B and C: 384. Serpent from the temple of{% endraw %}
{% raw %}iEsculapius at Ptolemais (head modern). — Case C: Greek vases,{% endraw %}
{% raw %}chiefly of the 6-4th cent. B.C., some manufactured in the pot¬{% endraw %}
{% raw %}teries of Naucratis, and some imported from the Greek motherland.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}The vases with red figures are the latest, those with brownish-red{% endraw %}
{% raw %}figures on a greyish-yellow ground are the earliest. — Case D:{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Terracottas. On the two top shelves are specimens of the favourite{% endraw %}
{% raw %}grotesque caricature figures; in the two beneath, figures of Venus.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}— Case E: Works in ivory, chiefly fragments of caskets, on some{% endraw %}
{% raw %}of which traces of painting may be seen.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Between Cases E and F:. Cut gems of the Greek period, some{% endraw %}
{% raw %}showing Gnostic inscriptions and designs.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Case F: Terracotta statuettes, resembling the Tanagra figures,{% endraw %}
{% raw %}and obviously in some cases modelled with Tanagra forms. No. 385,{% endraw %}
{% raw %}one of the best, represents a faun lying on a wineskin on the ground,{% endraw %}
{% raw %}from the mouth of which he permits a stream of wine to escape.{% endraw %}
