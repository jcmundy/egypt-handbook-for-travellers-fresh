{% raw %}2. The Mokattam Hills.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}An excursion to the Mokattam Hills is best made on Donkey-back (p. 27).{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Including the return, it takes about 3 hrs., but it may be combined with{% endraw %}
{% raw %}the visit to the smaller Petrified Forest in the manner indicated at p. 110.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}The View is best at sunset, or in the morning between 8 and 9 o'clock.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}The best route to the Mokattam starts from the Citadel, whence{% endraw %}
{% raw %}it ascends almost straight, passing through the Bdb el-Gebel (PL F, 6){% endraw %}
{% raw %}and over the railway-bridge. — The route starting from the Tombs{% endraw %}
{% raw %}of the Khalifs should be avoided on account of the intolerable dust.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}About 3/4 hr. brings us to the top.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}The '"Mokattam Hills (or Gebel Giyushi, as the range of hills to{% endraw %}
{% raw %}the E. of Cairo is sometimes called after the conspicuous old mosque{% endraw %}
{% raw %}situated on the summit) belong to the great range of nummulite{% endraw %}
{% raw %}mountains which extend from N.W. Africa, across Egypt and India,{% endraw %}
{% raw %}to China. This nummulite formation is one of the Eocene, or oldest{% endraw %}
{% raw %}deposits of the tertiary period, and immediately follows the chalk.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}It affords a favourite building-stone, and there are numerous quar¬{% endraw %}
{% raw %}ries on the slopes of the hills.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}The nummulite is remarkably rich in fossils, the chief mass of which{% endraw %}
{% raw %}consists of millions of nummulites (a kind of snail-shell), or large rhizo-{% endraw %}
{% raw %}pods of the polythalamia group. The larger kinds are about one inch{% endraw %}
{% raw %}in diameter, and the smaller about '/s inch. They are also frequently{% endraw %}
{% raw %}seen in the stones of the Pyramids, which are to a great extent con¬{% endraw %}
{% raw %}structed of nummulite limestone. The Greeks also noticed these curious{% endraw %}
{% raw %}fossils, and Herodotus mentions the smallest kinds as being petrified{% endraw %}
{% raw %}lentils, of the sort eaten by the ancient Egyptians.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}The quarries in the slopes of the Mokattam and the higher side-valleys{% endraw %}
{% raw %}of the range also yield a profusion of sea-urchins (clypeaster, cidaris,{% endraw %}
{% raw %}echinolampas, etc.), various kinds of oysters, cerithium, ovula, strombus,{% endraw %}
{% raw %}nerina, furritella, nautilus, bivalves, sharks' teeth, and bones of the{% endraw %}
{% raw %}halicore. Beautiful crystals of isinglass-stone and of strontian also occur.{% endraw %}
