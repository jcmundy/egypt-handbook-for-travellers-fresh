{% raw %}3. Spring of Moses and the Petrified Forest.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}To the natives the Petrified Forest is known as the 'Great' and the{% endraw %}
{% raw %}'Little' Gebel el-Khashab. The scientific traveller will find a visit to the{% endraw %}
{% raw %}former extremely interesting, but most travellers will be satisfied with an{% endraw %}
{% raw %}excursion to the latter, the outskirts of which may be reached in l'/2-2 hrs.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}The expedition may be made in half-a-day on donkey-back. Carriages{% endraw %}
{% raw %}require extra horses, and even then sometimes stick in the sand. — The{% endraw %}
{% raw %}deviation to the so-called Spring of Moses adds rather less than an hour to{% endraw %}
{% raw %}the expedition. — The donkey-boys are usually familiar with the route{% endraw %}
{% raw %}to the Little Petrified Forest, so that a guide may be dispensed with;{% endraw %}
{% raw %}but a visit to the 'Great', near the Bir el-Fahmeh, can hardly be accom¬{% endraw %}
{% raw %}plished without the aid of a well-informed dragoman.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Leaving the Bab en-Nasr (p. 62), we turn to the right to the{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Tombs ofthe Khalifs, pass between the Mokattam (p. 108) and the{% endraw %}
{% raw %}'Red Mountain' (p. 77), and ascend to a desert valley, into which{% endraw %}
{% raw %}the E. spurs of the Mokattam descend. After a ride of V4nr-i dur¬{% endraw %}

{% raw %}the 'Red Mountain' is visible in the desert on our left, we cross a{% endraw %}
