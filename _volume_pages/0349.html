{% raw %}Construction of the Great Pyramid. According to Borchardt's theory this{% endraw %}
{% raw %}pyramid was not built on a single homogeneous plan (p. 115). It was{% endraw %}
{% raw %}originally designed to contain only one sloping corridor hewn in the rocky{% endraw %}
{% raw %}ground and leading through an antechamber (s) to the tomb-chamber (t).{% endraw %}
{% raw %}But before this design was completely carried out, it was exchanged for{% endraw %}
{% raw %}a more comprehensive plan, involving the construction of another chamber,{% endraw %}
{% raw %}now called the Queen's Chamber (g), reached by the corridor marked c, e, f.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}But even this was not final, for Kheops undertook another and greater{% endraw %}
{% raw %}extension, resulting in the construction of the Great Hall (h) and the King's{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Chamber (k).{% endraw %}
{% raw %}The Ascent of the Pyramid, though fatiguing, is perfectly safe.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}The traveller selects two ofthe importunate Beduins (p. 113) and{% endraw %}
{% raw %}proceeds to the N.E. corner of the pyramid where the ascent usually{% endraw %}
{% raw %}begins. Assisted by the two Beduins, one holding each hand, and,{% endraw %}
{% raw %}if desired, by a third (no extra payment) who pushes behind, the{% endraw %}
{% raw %}traveller begins the ascent of the steps, which are each about 3 ft.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}high. The strong and active attendants assist the traveller to mount{% endraw %}
{% raw %}by pushing, pulling , and supporting hioi, and will scarcely allow{% endraw %}
{% raw %}him a moment's rest until the top is reached. As, however, the un¬{% endraw %}
{% raw %}wonted exertion is fatiguing, the traveller should insist on resting{% endraw %}
{% raw %}as often as he feels inclined. 'Iskut walla mdflsh bakshish' (be quiet,{% endraw %}
{% raw %}or you shall have no fee) is a sentence which may often be em¬{% endraw %}
