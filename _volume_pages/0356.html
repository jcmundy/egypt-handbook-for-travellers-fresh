{% raw %}The Excavation of the Sphinx, so far as is now known, was first under¬{% endraw %}
{% raw %}taken by Thutmosis IV. (see below). During the Ptolemaic and Roman{% endraw %}
{% raw %}periods the colossus was several times restored, and was highly admired{% endraw %}
{% raw %}and revered, as numerous inscriptions upon it testify. It is all the more{% endraw %}
{% raw %}curious, therefore, that the Sphinx is mentioned neither by Herodotus nor{% endraw %}
{% raw %}by any later Greek traveller. The mutilations which now disfigure it date{% endraw %}
{% raw %}from the Arab domination. In 1380 it fell a victim to the iconoclastic zeal{% endraw %}
{% raw %}of a fanatic shekh, and it was afterwards used as a target by the barbarous{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Mamelukes. — In the present century, the Sphinx was first completely{% endraw %}
{% raw %}excavated by Caviglia (p. 116), at the cost (4507.) of an English society.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}He discovered the flight of steps which ascended to the stupendous mon¬{% endraw %}
{% raw %}ument, and also found between the paws of the lion a carefully laid{% endraw %}
{% raw %}pavement, at the end of which next to the breast of the Sphinx rose a{% endraw %}
{% raw %}kind of open temple. The latter was enclosed by two partitions, through{% endraw %}
{% raw %}which ran a passage, in the middle of which was a small figure of a re¬{% endraw %}
{% raw %}cumbent lion, facing the Sphinx. In the background and at each side were{% endraw %}
{% raw %}memorial stones erected by Thutmosis IV. and Ramses II. (see below). The{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Sphinx has also been excavated by Mariette, and in 1883 by Maspero.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Between the paws ofthe Sphinx, which are concealed by blocks{% endraw %}
{% raw %}of limestone, stands an Altar, and in front of the breast of the{% endraw %}
{% raw %}colossus is the Memorial Stone of Thutmosis IV., both of red granite.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}On the upper part of the latter, to the right and left, appears Thut¬{% endraw %}
{% raw %}mosis IV., sacrificing to the sun-god Harmachis, who is represented as a{% endraw %}
{% raw %}sphinx. The inscription below relates that the Pharaoh while a prince{% endraw %}
{% raw %}hunting lions fell asleep one day in the shadow of the Sphinx, and that{% endraw %}
{% raw %}the god appeared and commanded the prince to 'free him from the desert{% endraw %}
{% raw %}sand that encumbered him'. As soon as Thutmosis ascended the throne,{% endraw %}
{% raw %}he remembered his dream and caused the Sphinx to he excavated.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}About 50 yds. to the S.E. of the Sphinx is situated the *Granite{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Temple, erroneously called Temple of the Sphinx, but in reality the{% endraw %}
{% raw %}sanctuary of the god Sokaris- Osiris. This large building constructed{% endraw %}
{% raw %}of granite and alabaster, discovered by Mariette in 1853, is a fine{% endraw %}
{% raw %}example of the simple and majestic architecture of a period when{% endraw %}
{% raw %}the art of working the hardest kinds of stone had already attained{% endraw %}
{% raw %}perfection. The exterior of the temple is still concealed by rubbish,{% endraw %}
{% raw %}but the interior has been partly excavated.{% endraw %}
