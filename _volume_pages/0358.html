{% raw %}Between the Granite Temple and the Sphinx excavations have{% endraw %}

{% raw %}from the Ptolemaic or the Roman period, and were built around the{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Sphinx in order to protect it from the shifting sand.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}The tombs surrounding the different pyramids, where the rela¬{% endraw %}
{% raw %}tives and state-officials of the kings were interred, are far inferior{% endraw %}
{% raw %}in interest to the tombs that have been excavated and rendered{% endraw %}
{% raw %}accessible at Sakkara (p. 137) and the graves in Upper Egypt. The{% endraw %}
{% raw %}exterior of the mastabas (p. 137), however, may be studied here{% endraw %}
{% raw %}(especially in the tombs to the W. and S.E. of the Great Pyramid){% endraw %}
{% raw %}to better advantage than at Sakkara. Travellers who propose to visit{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Sakkara may content themselves with an inspection ofthe exterior{% endraw %}
{% raw %}of these graves; otherwise the so-called Tomb of Numbers (p. 128){% endraw %}
{% raw %}and Campbell's Tomb (p. 127) are usually visited.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Travellers who are not pressed for time and who desire to obtain{% endraw %}
{% raw %}a closer view of the second and third Pyramids, are recommended to{% endraw %}
{% raw %}make the following *Circuit of the Pyramid Plateau.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}After having inspected the Great Pyramid (p. 116), we turn{% endraw %}
{% raw %}(following the dotted line on the Map, p. 116) to the left (W.) of{% endraw %}
{% raw %}the entrance, descend the mound of de'bris, and proceed to the N.W.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}angle of the Pyramid, where the levelled space on the ground{% endraw %}
{% raw %}(PL b), intended for the reception of the corner-stone, has been{% endraw %}
{% raw %}exposed to view. Towards the W. and S.W. lie numerous tombs{% endraw %}
{% raw %}presenting an impressive appearance. One of these may be ascended{% endraw %}
{% raw %}in order to inspect the shafts (p. oxliv) leading into the depths.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Those, however, who are not deterred by difficulty will find the tomb{% endraw %}
{% raw %}(5th Dyn.) of Shepses-kef-onkh and his son Imeri (PL c), almost due N.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}of the Second Pyramid, the least inconvenient. This tomb is half-buried{% endraw %}
{% raw %}in sand, and visitors have to crawl through the low entrance in order{% endraw %}
{% raw %}to reach the long vaulted corridor, which is covered with reliefs and{% endraw %}
{% raw %}inscriptions. — The beautiful tomb of Nefer-bew-Ptah, a grandson of Shep¬{% endraw %}
{% raw %}ses-kef-onkh, lies to the left (S ), but is unfortunately quite buried.{% endraw %}

{% raw %}a level surface for this pyramid (p. 121). On the N. side of the{% endraw %}
