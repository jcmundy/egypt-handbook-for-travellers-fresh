{% raw %}A visit to Memphis and Sakkara may easily be accomplished in one{% endraw %}
{% raw %}day. A calm and windless day should be selected (p. 112). Provisions{% endraw %}
{% raw %}should not be forgotten; an acetylene lamp is also desirable; candles may{% endraw %}
{% raw %}be procured at Bedrashen in the Greek 'hakkal', or shop, mentioned at{% endraw %}
{% raw %}p. 131. — Tickets admitting to the monuments of Sakkara may be obtained{% endraw %}
{% raw %}for 5 pias. each at the railway-station of Bedrashen. Travellers, however,{% endraw %}
{% raw %}who possess a general Admission Ticket from the Service des Antiquitis de{% endraw %}
{% raw %}l'Egypte (p. 78) do not require these special tickets. Excursions to Sakkara{% endraw %}
{% raw %}and Memphis are organized by Messrs. Cook, Messrs. Gaze and other tourist{% endraw %}
{% raw %}agents. But the haste with which these excursions are conducted and{% endraw %}
{% raw %}the crowd of tourists taking part in them are out of keeping with the{% endraw %}
{% raw %}dignified and solemn associations of the spot.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}The following arrangement of the journey will be found convenient.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Take the train at 8 a.m. to (3/4-l hr.) Bedrashen, where donkeys and{% endraw %}
{% raw %}drivers (10 pias. there and back) are in waiting. Ride via the site of{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Memphis, where the "Colossi of Ramses (p. 132) are inspected, to the Necro¬{% endraw %}
{% raw %}polis of Sakkara, and thence, passing the *Step Pyramid (p. 134), to (2 hrs.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}in all) Mariette's House (p. 135). For luncheon and a visit to the "Apis{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Tombs (p. 135) and the ""Tombs of Ptahhotep and Ti (pp. 137, 139) 4 hrs. should{% endraw %}
{% raw %}be allowed; and possibly time may be found for the inspection of the{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Onnos Pyramid (p. 134) or the 'Tomb of Mereruka (p. 148). For returning{% endraw %}
{% raw %}to the station of Bedrashen l>/2 hr. more should be reckoned. The train{% endraw %}
{% raw %}from Upper Egypt generally reaches Bedrashen about 5.30 p.m., but the{% endraw %}
{% raw %}railway time-table should be consulted. — Those who are not too fatigued{% endraw %}
{% raw %}may return by the route described above via Abusir to the Mena House{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Hotel (2'/2 hrs.; bargain beforehand with the donkey-driver), and thence{% endraw %}
{% raw %}take the electric tramway to Cairo.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Travellers who wish to devote i'/2-2 days to Sakkara may pass the{% endraw %}
{% raw %}night at Mariette's House (p. 135), for which the previous permission of{% endraw %}
{% raw %}the museum authorities at Gizeh should be obtained. A blanket is a{% endraw %}
{% raw %}sufficient covering in spring.{% endraw %}

{% raw %}left, beyond the Nile, we see Old Cairo, above which rises the long{% endraw %}
