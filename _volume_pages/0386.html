{% raw %}(beginning at the top): Fishing and bird-snaring (two rows);{% endraw %}
{% raw %}fisherman shaking fish from a kind of wicker bow-net into a basket;{% endraw %}
{% raw %}two men seated at a small table cutting up fish; cattle pasturing;{% endraw %}
{% raw %}a cow is represented calving, another is being milked, while an{% endraw %}
{% raw %}overseer leans on his staff close by and a herdsman grasping a calf{% endraw %}
{% raw %}by the legs prevents it running to its mother; to the left, calves{% endraw %}
{% raw %}tethered to pegs in the ground and browsing or frisking; to the{% endraw %}
{% raw %}right, herdsmen in small papyrus boats drive a herd of cattle across{% endraw %}
{% raw %}a river in which lurk two crocodiles; to the left are two dwarfs with{% endraw %}
{% raw %}their master's pet ape and a leash of greyhounds.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Dwarfs with ape and dogs. Captured fish.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}The narrow strip running along the entire N. wall at the bot¬{% endraw %}
{% raw %}tom consists of a procession of 36 female peasants bearing sacri¬{% endraw %}
{% raw %}ficial offerings of meat, poultry, vegetables, fruits, and drink. The{% endraw %}
{% raw %}different figures represent the various estates of the deceased Ti,{% endraw %}
{% raw %}the name of each being inscribed beside its representative.{% endraw %}

{% raw %}to the E. of Mariette's house, beyond a ruined stone pyramid. The{% endraw %}
