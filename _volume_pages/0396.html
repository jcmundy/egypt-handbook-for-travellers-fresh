{% raw %}History. Mansura (i.e. 'the victorious') was founded by Sultan Mo¬{% endraw %}
{% raw %}hammed Kamil Ibn 'Adil in 1222, as an advantageous substitute for Da¬{% endraw %}
{% raw %}mietta (comp. p. 160). The first serious attack made on Mansura was by{% endraw %}
{% raw %}the Crusaders under Louis IX, of France in 1249. After encountering great{% endraw %}
{% raw %}difficulties they succeeded in crossing the Ashmun Canal, but in the neigh¬{% endraw %}
{% raw %}bourhood of Mansura they were repeatedly defeated by the young Sultan{% endraw %}
{% raw %}el-Mo'azzam Turanshah. Their fleet was destroyed, and 'famine-fever'{% endraw %}
{% raw %}broke out. When the ill-fated Crusaders attempted to escape they were{% endraw %}
{% raw %}intercepted by the vigilant Turks, who thinned their ranks terribly and{% endraw %}
{% raw %}captured the king with his brother Charles of Anjou and a number of{% endraw %}
{% raw %}knights. On 6th May, 1250, Louis and his barons were released on pay¬{% endraw %}
{% raw %}ment of a heavy ransom, and on surrendering the town of Damietta.{% endraw %}

{% raw %}The road to the ruins passes through a luxuriant growth of trees{% endraw %}
{% raw %}(lime-trees, silver poplars, and willows, besides the sunt-tree, the{% endraw %}
{% raw %}lebbek, the tamarisk, and the bernuf shrub). A walk of 40 min.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}brings us to the ruins of Behbit el-Hager, the ancient —{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Iseum or Isidis Oppidum of the Romans. The ancient name of{% endraw %}
{% raw %}the place was Hebet or Per-ehbet, or 'House of the god of Hebet'{% endraw %}
{% raw %}(i.e. Horus), of which the modern name is a corruption. Isis also{% endraw %}
{% raw %}was worhipped here. Within a still distincly traceable girdle-wall{% endraw %}
{% raw %}rises a heap of ruins, known as Hager el-Gdmus (buffalo stone),{% endraw %}
{% raw %}which form the remains of the once magnificent Isis Temple of{% endraw %}
{% raw %}BehbIt, built by Nekht-Har-ehbet (30th Dyn.) and Ptolemy II.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Philadelphus. The ruins of the venerable sanctuary form an im¬{% endraw %}
{% raw %}posing and most picturesque mass of blocks, fragments of columns{% endraw %}
{% raw %}and architraves, ceiling slabs, and other remains, altogether about{% endraw %}
{% raw %}400 paces in circumference. The structure consisted entirely of{% endraw %}
{% raw %}beautiful granite, chiefly grey, but partly red in colour.{% endraw %}
