{% raw %}The sculptures (hautreliefs and reliefs en creux) are most elaborately{% endraw %}
{% raw %}executed. Several of the female heads and busts, and some of the cows'{% endraw %}
{% raw %}heads also, are remarkably fine. The inscriptions consist of the usual{% endraw %}
{% raw %}formulae; none of them are historical. In some the hieroglyphics are{% endraw %}
{% raw %}unusually large, in others they are of small and elegant form, and in all{% endraw %}
{% raw %}they are executed in the somewhat florid style peculiar to the age of the{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Ptolemies. On the W. side of the ruins is an interesting large slab of{% endraw %}
{% raw %}grey granite, veined with red, on which is represented the king offering{% endraw %}
{% raw %}a gift of land to Osiris and Isis, 'the great divine mistress'. Higher up{% endraw %}
{% raw %}there is another block of grey granite, with a representation of Isis en¬{% endraw %}
{% raw %}throned, and of the king offering to 'his mother' two small bags of mal¬{% endraw %}
{% raw %}achite and eye-paint. To the E. is another block of grey granite, with{% endraw %}
{% raw %}reliefs. On a grey block of granite, lying in an oblique position, is re¬{% endraw %}
{% raw %}presented the sacred bark of Isis, resembling those seen elsewhere in{% endraw %}
{% raw %}bronze only. The cabin is like a house of two stories, in the upper of{% endraw %}
{% raw %}which sits the goddess, with cow's horns and a disk, on a lotus-tlower,{% endraw %}
{% raw %}and attended on her right and left by winged goddesses. On the N. side{% endraw %}
{% raw %}lies an unusually large Hathor capital, in granite. Numerous remains of{% endraw %}
{% raw %}pillars and architraves also still exist.{% endraw %}
