{% raw %}In 1836 Ferdinand de Lesseps (1805-94) came to Cairo as a{% endraw %}
{% raw %}young consular Sieve, and there had his attention called to Lepere's{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Me'moire regarding the scheme of connecting the two seas, which{% endraw %}
{% raw %}led him to consider its great importance, although Lepere himself{% endraw %}
{% raw %}doubted its feasibility. In 1838 he made the acquaintance of Lieut.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Waghorn, an Englishman (p. 173), whose zealous advocacy of the{% endraw %}
{% raw %}construction of a route between Europe and India via Egypt stim¬{% endraw %}
{% raw %}ulated his zeal for a similar project. In 1841 and 1847 Linant{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Bey, the viceroy's engineer of water-works, and Messrs. Stephenson,{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Negrelli, and Bourdaloue, demonstrated the inaccuracy of Lepere's{% endraw %}
{% raw %}calculations, and proved that the level of the two seas was nearly{% endraw %}
{% raw %}the same, so that the construction of a canal between them was{% endraw %}
{% raw %}possible. In 1854 M. de Lesseps, having matured his plan, laid it{% endraw %}
{% raw %}before Sa'id Pasha, who was then viceroy, and who determined to{% endraw %}
{% raw %}carry it out. Difficulties were thrown in the way of the enterprise{% endraw %}
{% raw %}by the English government during Lord Palmerston's ministry,{% endraw %}
{% raw %}but on 5th Jan., 1856, permission to begin the work was formally{% endraw %}
{% raw %}granted by the viceroy. A considerable time, however, elapsed be¬{% endraw %}
{% raw %}fore the necessary capital was raised, and it was not till 22nd April,{% endraw %}
{% raw %}1859, that the work was actually begun. The viceroy undertook to{% endraw %}
{% raw %}pay many of the current expenses, and provided 25,000 workmen,{% endraw %}
{% raw %}who were to be paid and fed by the company at an inexpensive rate,{% endraw %}
{% raw %}and were to be relieved every three months. In order to provide{% endraw %}
{% raw %}these workmen with water, 4000 water-casks suitable for being{% endraw %}
{% raw %}carried by camels had to be constructed, and 1600 of these animals{% endraw %}
{% raw %}were daily employed in bringing them supplies, at a cost of 8000 fr.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}per day. On 29th Dec, 1863, the fresh-water canal (p. 162) was{% endraw %}
{% raw %}completed, so that the company was thenceforth relieved of the{% endraw %}
{% raw %}enormous expense of supplying the workpeople with water. The{% endraw %}
{% raw %}hands now employed, among whom were a number of Europeans,{% endraw %}
{% raw %}were less numerous, and much of the work was done by machinery,{% endraw %}
{% raw %}of 22,000 horse-power in all.{% endraw %}

{% raw %}level of the Mediterranean, while the S. parts required extensive{% endraw %}
{% raw %}dredging operations. The opening of the Suez Canal was inaugurated{% endraw %}
{% raw %}on 16th Nov., 1869, and the magnificent festivities which took place{% endraw %}
{% raw %}on the occasion are said to have cost the Khedive no less than{% endraw %}
{% raw %}4,200,000*.{% endraw %}
