{% raw %}The Canal is 160 kilometres (100 M.) in length, and the E.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}bank is furnished with posts at intervals of 5 kilometres. Near the{% endraw %}
{% raw %}stations are passing-places for the large steamers, named 'Gare du{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Nord' and 'Gare du Sud' respectively. The Canal is now 28 ft. in{% endraw %}
{% raw %}depth, thus admitting vessels drawing 26^4 ft. of water. The sur¬{% endraw %}
{% raw %}face varies in breadth from 65 to 110 yds., while the width of the{% endraw %}
{% raw %}bottom is 24 yds. only.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}The great mercantile importance of the Canal is apparent from the{% endraw %}
{% raw %}following data. The distance from London to Bombay via the Cape of{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Good Hope is 12,548 English miles, and via, the Suez Canal 7028 31. only.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}The saving thus effected is 44 per cent of the distance. From Hamburg to{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Bombay by the Cape 12,903 51., by the Canal 7383 M.; saving 43 per cent.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}From Trieste to Bombay by the Cape 13,229 M., by the Canal 4816 ML; saving{% endraw %}
{% raw %}63 per cent. From London to Hongkong by the Cape 15,229 31., by the Canal{% endraw %}
{% raw %}11,112 M.; saving 28 per cent. From Odessa to Hongkong by the Cape{% endraw %}
{% raw %}16,629 31., by the Canal 8735 31.; saving 47 per cent. From Marseilles to{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Bombay by the Cape 12,144 31., by the Canal 5022 31.; saving 59 per cent.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}From Constantinople to Zanzibar by the Cape 10,271 31., by the Canal{% endraw %}
{% raw %}4365 31.; saving 57 per cent. From Rotterdam to the Sunda Strait by the{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Cape 13,252 31., by the Canal 9779 31.; saving 26 per cent.{% endraw %}

{% raw %}The dues amount to 9 fr. per ton for laden passenger or cargo{% endraw %}
{% raw %}steamers and war-ships; 6 fr. 50 c. per ton for ships in ballast without{% endraw %}
{% raw %}passengers; 10 fr. for each passenger (children half-price). Ships of war{% endraw %}
{% raw %}and the regular mail-steamers obtain a reduction. The income of the{% endraw %}
{% raw %}company in 1899 was 94,317,505 fr., the expenditure 24,863,166 fr.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Steamers are not allowed to steam through the Canal (except in the{% endraw %}
{% raw %}large Bitter Lakes) above a speed of 6 M. per hr., as their wash wonld{% endraw %}
