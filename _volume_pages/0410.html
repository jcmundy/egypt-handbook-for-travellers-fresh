{% raw %}Port Sa'id, the chief town of the Egyptian province of the{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Isthmus and Suez Canal, lies at the E. extremity of an island which{% endraw %}
{% raw %}belongs to the narrow strip of land separating Lake Menzaleh from{% endraw %}
{% raw %}the Mediterranean. It owes its origin to the Suez Canal, and its{% endraw %}
{% raw %}prosperity during the last ten years has been so marked that it has{% endraw %}
{% raw %}become a serious rival to Alexandria. The population, which in 1883{% endraw %}
{% raw %}was only 17,000, is now about 42,000, including 11,300 Europeans.{% endraw %}
