{% raw %}The numerous masts in the harbour of Port Sa'id remain in sight{% endraw %}
{% raw %}long after we quit that town. The Canal, on the W. bank of which{% endraw %}
{% raw %}runs the steam-tramway to Isma'iliya (p. 164), is constructed in a{% endraw %}
{% raw %}perfectly straight line through Lake Menzaleh. The brackish waters{% endraw %}
{% raw %}of this lake extend over an area of about 1000 sq. M., covering what{% endraw %}
{% raw %}was once one of the most fertile districts in Egypt, and was inter¬{% endraw %}
{% raw %}sected by the three most important arms of the Nile (p. li) in an¬{% endraw %}
{% raw %}cient times, the Pelusiac, the Tanitic, and the Mendesian. Among{% endraw %}
{% raw %}the numerous towns and villages situated here were the important{% endraw %}
{% raw %}cities of Tanis and Tennis. Immense flocks of pelicans and silver{% endraw %}
{% raw %}herons, some flamingoes, and a few herds of buffaloes are to be met{% endraw %}
{% raw %}with here. The operation of draining the lake has been begun, the{% endraw %}
{% raw %}part adjoining the Canal on the E. is already dry. — Rds el-'Esh{% endraw %}
