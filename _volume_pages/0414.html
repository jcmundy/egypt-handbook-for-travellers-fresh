{% raw %}The Canal traverses Lake Balah, from which it is separated by{% endraw %}
{% raw %}a low embankment. At El-Ferdan (p. 164), at the S. end of the{% endraw %}
{% raw %}lake, the Canal passes through the first cutting. At the next passing{% endraw %}
{% raw %}place we obtain a glimpse of the desert.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}The hills of El-Gisr ('the embankment'), which cross the course{% endraw %}
{% raw %}of the Canal at an average height of 52 ft. above the sea-level,{% endraw %}
{% raw %}presented the most serious obstacle to its construction. In order to{% endraw %}
{% raw %}form a cutting through it, no less than 18,767,000 cubic yds. of{% endraw %}
{% raw %}earth had to be removed, and 20,000 fellahin were employed in the{% endraw %}
{% raw %}work before machinery could be-brought into operation. At the top{% endraw %}
{% raw %}of the hill is the deserted village of El-Gisr, with a chapel to the{% endraw %}
{% raw %}"Virgin of the Desert, and a ruined mosque. A flight of steps as¬{% endraw %}
{% raw %}cends to this point from the Canal. The view hence embraces a{% endraw %}
{% raw %}great part of the Isthmus, the frowning 'Ataka Mts. above Suez,{% endraw %}
{% raw %}the mountains of the peninsula of Sinai, the course of the Canal,{% endraw %}
{% raw %}and the green expanse of the Bitter Lakes.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}At the end of the cutting the Canal enters Lake Timsah, or the{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Crocodile Lake, the dredged channel through which is indicated by{% endraw %}
{% raw %}stakes. As we enter the lake we see the Khedivial Chateau (see{% endraw %}
{% raw %}below) above us to the right, and in the S. the mountains of Gebel{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Abu Balah. The lake, which is now about 6 sq. M. in area, and of{% endraw %}
{% raw %}a beautiful pale-blue colour, was, before the construction ofthe{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Canal, a mere pond of brackish water, and full of reeds. On its N.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}bank lies the town of —{% endraw %}

{% raw %}Isma'iliya was the main centre of operations during the con¬{% endraw %}
{% raw %}struction of the Suez Canal, but it afterwards lost its importance.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}A new period of prosperity, however, has begun since the great{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Asiatic and Australian mail-steamers have ceased to call at Alexan¬{% endraw %}
{% raw %}dria, so that the traffic between them and Cairo is carried on via{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Port Sa'id and the Canal. The pretty gardens and plantations and{% endraw %}
{% raw %}the view of the blue lake lend the town the appearance of an oasis,{% endraw %}
{% raw %}with both European and Arabian cultivation. The air is healthy and{% endraw %}
{% raw %}not moist, in spite of the proximity of the water.{% endraw %}
