{% raw %}BITTER LAKES. 14. Route. 171{% endraw %}
{% raw %}A pleasant walk may be taken along the lebbek-avenue, on the{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Quai Me"hemet-Ali, as far as the chateau of the Khedive and the{% endraw %}
{% raw %}water-works. — In a public park in the Avenue Victoria (opposite{% endraw %}
{% raw %}the former Villa Lesseps) several monuments found at Pithom{% endraw %}
{% raw %}(p. 163) are preserved.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Group of Ramses II. seated between the gods Re and Atum: memorial{% endraw %}
{% raw %}stone of Ramses II., with sculptures and inscriptions; recumbent lion with{% endraw %}
{% raw %}human head, dedicated by Ramses II. to Atum; naos with the figure of a{% endraw %}
{% raw %}similar lion (sphinx), dedicated hy Ramses II. in the temple of Pithom.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}The best way of spending a few leisure hours here is to visit the{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Khedivial Chalet, at the influx of the canal into Lake Timsah (near{% endraw %}
{% raw %}the Canal Co.'s Hospital) and the hill of El-Gisr (p. 170; 1-2 hrs.;{% endraw %}
{% raw %}donkey 1 fr.).{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Canal Journey to Suez. After quitting Lake Timsah we pass{% endraw %}
{% raw %}the foot of the Gebel Maryam, which an Arabian legend points out{% endraw %}
{% raw %}as the place where Miriam, when smitten with leprosy for her dis¬{% endraw %}
{% raw %}approval of the marriage of Moses with an Ethiopian woman, spent{% endraw %}
{% raw %}seven days, beyond the precincts of the camp of the Israelites{% endraw %}
{% raw %}(Numbers, xii.). — At the 85th kilometre is situated Tusun, which{% endraw %}
{% raw %}is easily recognised by the whitewashed dome of the tomb of a shekh.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Excavations near Tusun have led to the discovery of many interest¬{% endraw %}
{% raw %}ing fossil'remains of large animals belonging to the meiocene ter¬{% endraw %}
{% raw %}tiary formation, and pieces of fossil wood have also been found here{% endraw %}
{% raw %}(comp. p. 110). — A little farther on (near the 90th kilometre) is{% endraw %}
{% raw %}the cutting which conducts the Canal through the rocky barrier of{% endraw %}
{% raw %}the Serapeum (comp. p. 165).{% endraw %}
{% raw %}The Canal now enters the Large Basin of the Bitter Lakes.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Brugsch identifies the Bitter Lakes with the Marah of the Bible{% endraw %}
{% raw %}(Exod. xv. 23). At each end of the large basin rises an iron light¬{% endraw %}
{% raw %}house, 65 ft. in height. The water is of a bluish-green colour. The{% endraw %}
{% raw %}banks are flat and sandy, but a little to the left rises the not un-{% endraw %}
{% raw %}picturesque range of the Gebel Geneffeh (p. 164). The bed of the{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Little Bitter Lake, which we next traverse, consists entirely of shell-{% endraw %}
{% raw %}formations.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Near Shaluf et-Terdbeh (a station on the right, near the 139th{% endraw %}
{% raw %}kilometre; see p. 164) no less than 40,000 cubic yds. of limestone,{% endraw %}
{% raw %}coloured red and brown with iron, had to be removed in the course{% endraw %}
{% raw %}of the excavation of the Canal. This stone contained teeth and{% endraw %}
{% raw %}vertebrae of sharks, bivalve shells, and remains of Bryozoa. In the{% endraw %}
{% raw %}layer of sand above the limestone were found crocodiles' teeth and{% endraw %}
{% raw %}the remains of hippopotami and other large quadrupeds. The{% endraw %}
{% raw %}monument of Darius near Shaluf is mentioned at p. 165.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}We finally reach the Gulf of Suez, which is here so shallow that,{% endraw %}
{% raw %}but for the Canal, it might be crossed on foot at low tide. It con¬{% endraw %}
{% raw %}tains several islands. On the "W. bank rise the workshop and{% endraw %}
{% raw %}magazines of the Canal Company. Passengers are landed in steam-{% endraw %}
{% raw %}launches.{% endraw %}
