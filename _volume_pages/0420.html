{% raw %}The Springs of Moses, Arabic 'Ain (plural 'Ayun) Musa, form{% endraw %}
{% raw %}an oasis about five furlongs in circumference, the property of{% endraw %}
{% raw %}M. Costa (p. 172) and several Greek residents in Suez. The vege¬{% endraw %}
{% raw %}tation here is very luxuriant. Date-palms and vegetables are cul¬{% endraw %}
{% raw %}tivated by the Arabs, who receive one-half of the yield of dates as{% endraw %}
{% raw %}their reward. Their gardens are enclosed by mud-walls and pal¬{% endraw %}
{% raw %}ings. The springs vary in temperature from 70° to 82° Fahr.; some{% endraw %}
{% raw %}are only slightly brackish, while others are exceedingly salt. The{% endraw %}
{% raw %}largest of them, in the garden farthest to the S., is said to have{% endraw %}
{% raw %}been the bitter spring which Moses sweetened by casting a certain{% endraw %}
{% raw %}tree into it. Beside it is an open colonnade in which the traveller{% endraw %}
{% raw %}may rest and partake of coffee offered by the Arabs.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}On a mound marked by a solitary palm, about 10 min. to the{% endraw %}
{% raw %}S.E. of the gardens (view), is another trickling spring. — Concho-{% endraw %}
{% raw %}logists (p. 172) will find a number of interesting shells on the{% endraw %}
{% raw %}beach at low tide, but the best places are farther to the S."{% endraw %}
{% raw %}An interesting excursion may be made in good weather to the Sub¬{% endraw %}
{% raw %}marine Coral Gardens. We skirt the slope of the coral rock 'Sha'ab', which{% endraw %}
{% raw %}stretches along the coast, becoming better developed the farther S. we go.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Those who are interested in marine biology should land on the rock, which{% endraw %}

{% raw %}16. The Fayum.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}A short Visit to the Fayum, a fertile and attractive district with{% endraw %}
{% raw %}many historical associations, may be accomplished in 3 days. Travellers{% endraw %}
{% raw %}with a slight knowledge of the language and the customs may dispense{% endraw %}
{% raw %}with a dragoman. The nights are spent at Medineh (p. 176), but the tra¬{% endraw %}
{% raw %}veller should be provided with a moderate supply of food. An introduction{% endraw %}
{% raw %}to the Mudir will be of great service (p. 180). 1st Day. Railway from{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Cairo to Medinet el-Faydm; inspect that town and its environs. 2nd Day.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Excursion to the Birket Kartin and Demeh. 3rd Day. Excursion to Hawdra{% endraw %}
{% raw %}(Labyrinth) or Begig; railway back to Cairo. — A complete Tour through{% endraw %}
{% raw %}the Fayum takes about 6 days, and requires a dragoman, who-charges{% endraw %}
{% raw %}30-40 fr. a day for each person, according to the requirements of his{% endraw %}
{% raw %}employers, and for that sum is bound to provide them with a tent, pro¬{% endraw %}
{% raw %}visions (wine excepted), and donkeys, or other means of conveyance, and{% endraw %}
{% raw %}to pay railway-fares and all other expenses (comp. p. xxii).{% endraw %}
