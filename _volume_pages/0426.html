{% raw %}Medinet el-Fayum, usually known as El-Med'neh, is the chief{% endraw %}
{% raw %}town of the province of Fayum and contains about 31,000 inhab.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}(including many Greeks). The Bahr Yusuf (-p. 175) flows through{% endraw %}
{% raw %}the town, its running water forming a feature quite unusual in{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Egyptian towns. The very long covered bazaar contains nothing of{% endraw %}
{% raw %}special interest. The dilapidated mosque of Kait Bey, built on a{% endraw %}
{% raw %}bridge over the Bahr Yusuf, contains numerous antique columns,{% endraw %}
{% raw %}brought from the ancient Arsinoe, some of which have shafts of{% endraw %}
{% raw %}polished marble with Arabic inscriptions, and Corinthian and other{% endraw %}
{% raw %}capitals. Below the mosque, on the bank of the Bahr Yusuf, are{% endraw %}
{% raw %}some remains of ancient masonry. At the "W. end of the town the{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Bahr Yusuf radiates into numerous branches. The dilapidated{% endraw %}
{% raw %}mosque of Soft situated here forms a picturesque foreground.{% endraw %}
