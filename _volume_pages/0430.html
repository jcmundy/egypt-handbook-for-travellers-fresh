{% raw %}A visit to the Pyramid of El-Lahun or Illahun (Egypt. Le-hone,{% endraw %}
{% raw %}i.e. 'mouth of the canal', see p. 175) may be combined with the{% endraw %}
{% raw %}above expedition. The pyramid may be reached from Hawaret el-{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Maktar in 1, or from the Labyrinth in 11/2 hr.; otherwise we proceed{% endraw %}
{% raw %}via the station of El-Lahiin on the light railway (p. 178). The{% endraw %}
{% raw %}pyramid is constructed of Nile bricks, piled up upon a rocky nucleus{% endraw %}
{% raw %}bearing a star-shaped framework of low walls built of massive lime¬{% endraw %}
{% raw %}stone blocks. It has recently been opened by Fraser, and identified{% endraw %}
{% raw %}as the tomb of TJsertesen II. The remains of the ancient embank¬{% endraw %}
{% raw %}ments, once supposed to belong to Lake Mceris, date from the time{% endraw %}
{% raw %}of the Khalifs. Those who are interested in hydraulic engineering{% endraw %}
{% raw %}should inspect the entrance of the Bahr Yusuf into the Fayum.{% endraw %}

{% raw %}The Birket Karun and its Environs.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Travellers who restrict themselves to the Birket Karun and Demeh may{% endraw %}
{% raw %}accomplish their excursion by railway in one day if the trains suit, by{% endraw %}
{% raw %}starting early and returning late. Otherwise a tent must be taken for{% endraw %}
{% raw %}spending the night. Donkeys should be taken by train or sent on early{% endraw %}
{% raw %}in the morning to Abuksa. Arrangements for crossing the lake by boat{% endraw %}
{% raw %}must be made with the shekh of the fishermen; the usual charge is 30 fr.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}per day, with a bakshish for the rowers, of whom 6-8 should he stipulated{% endraw %}
{% raw %}for to avoid delay. The assistance of the Mudir of Medineh (comp. p. 1741{% endraw %}
