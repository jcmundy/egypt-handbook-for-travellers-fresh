{% raw %}UPPER EGYPT.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Mayflower, and Victoria of this line make similar three-weeks{% endraw %}
{% raw %}voyages between Cairo and Assuan from December to May. The{% endraw %}
{% raw %}general arrangements and conditions resemble those of Messrs.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Cook's steamers. The return fare is 35i. and upwards, according to{% endraw %}
{% raw %}the cabin.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Voyages on the Nile are also arranged hy the Thewfikieh Nile{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Navigation Co., programmes of which may be obtained at the of¬{% endraw %}
{% raw %}fices mentioned on p. 26.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Passengers by steamer should beware of the risk of catching{% endraw %}
{% raw %}cold by leaving the windows of their cabin op:n. They should also{% endraw %}
{% raw %}avoid placing themselves too near the edge of the deck; and it is{% endraw %}
{% raw %}well to re-member (e.g. when shaving) that the steamers sometimes{% endraw %}
{% raw %}run aground, especially above Luxor. Liability to delay through{% endraw %}
{% raw %}this last fact, makes it impossible to be sure of reaching Cairo in{% endraw %}
{% raw %}time to make connection with the ocean-steamers.{% endraw %}
