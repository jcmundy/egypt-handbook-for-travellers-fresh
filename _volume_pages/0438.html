{% raw %}Experienced travellers will find a very much cheaper mode of{% endraw %}
{% raw %}conveyance than the dahabiyeh in one of the native Sailing Boats or{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Feluka, which are used for the transport of sugar-cane, cotton, etc.,{% endraw %}
{% raw %}and are to be found in all the larger towns, such as Cairo, Minyeh,{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Assiut, Girgeh, Assuan, etc. No luxury, of course, must be looked{% endraw %}
{% raw %}for, but its absence is compensated by the close relations with the{% endraw %}
{% raw %}land and people into which the traveller is brought. Woollen cover¬{% endraw %}
{% raw %}lets, a mattress, towels, an oil cooking-stove (obtainable in Cairo),{% endraw %}
{% raw %}tinned meats, a filter (sir), and insect-powder must not be forgotten.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}A young attendant with some knowledge of cooking may be obtained{% endraw %}
{% raw %}for 2 or 3 fr. per day. A written contract must be carefully drawn{% endraw %}
{% raw %}up to include all details.{% endraw %}
