{% raw %}418 M. Kailwat in 14-15 hrs. (£ 2 E. 60, 11 E. 30, 41i/2 pias.); two{% endraw %}
{% raw %}through-trains daily, with restaurant-cars by day and sleeping-cars by{% endraw %}
{% raw %}night. So-called 'trains de luxe' also run in the season. Payment at the{% endraw %}
{% raw %}station may be avoiued by previously buying vouchers at the agencies of{% endraw %}
{% raw %}either Cook or Gaze, and exchanging them at the railway-station through{% endraw %}
{% raw %}the dragoman of the agency — Passengers should be at the railway-station{% endraw %}
{% raw %}early, as tlie processes of ticket-taking and luggage-weighing are by no{% endraw %}
{% raw %}means expeditious. A time-table (Indicateur des Chemins de Fer) showing{% endraw %}
{% raw %}the names of stations in French and giving distances in kilometres may{% endraw %}

{% raw %}stations, travellers are recommended to provide themselves with a supply{% endraw %}

{% raw %}sugar-cane, eggs, bread, and water (better avoided) are offered for sale at{% endraw %}
