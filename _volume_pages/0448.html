{% raw %}On the right bank of the Bahr Yusuf, 10 M. to the W. of Benisuef,{% endraw %}
{% raw %}lies the village of Henassiyeh el-Medineh (vulgo, Henassiyeh Omm el-Kimdn,{% endraw %}
{% raw %}'rubbish-heaps of Henassiyeh'), beside the mounds of debris, covering{% endraw %}
{% raw %}an area of ',2 sq. M., which mark the site of the ancient Heracleopolis.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}The Egyptian name of the town was Henen-selen, from which are derived{% endraw %}
{% raw %}the Coptic Hnes, and the Arabic Ahnds and Henassiyeh. It was the capital{% endraw %}
{% raw %}of a nome (the Graeco-Roman Heracleopolites) and the chief seat of the{% endraw %}
{% raw %}worship of the ram-headed god Hershef, identified by the Greeks with{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Heracles (whence the name of the town). The ichneumon also was re¬{% endraw %}
{% raw %}vered here. Among its chief shrines was a temple erected under the Middle{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Empire and a new building by Ramses II., but all have practically van¬{% endraw %}
{% raw %}ished ; and four columns of a late period, probably dating from a Byzantine{% endraw %}
{% raw %}church, are all that projects above the rubbish-heaps. The ancient necro¬{% endraw %}
{% raw %}polis lies on the W. side of the Bahr Yusuf.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Another road, traversing the Wddi Baydd, which opens near the vil¬{% endraw %}
{% raw %}lage of Baydd, on the E. hank of the Nile, opposite Benisuef, leads through{% endraw %}
{% raw %}the desert to the Convents of SS. Anthony and Paul (p. xxkix), a few leagues{% endraw %}
{% raw %}from the Red Sea.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}As far as Minyeh the space between the E. bank and the hills{% endraw %}
{% raw %}remains narrow, the limestone rocks frequently abutting on the river{% endraw %}
{% raw %}in unbroken walls or rounded bluffs. Few villages are seen on this{% endraw %}
{% raw %}bank, but the fertile alluvial tract on the W. side, 10-12M. in width,{% endraw %}
{% raw %}is thickly populated and carefully cultivated, exhibiting in profusion{% endraw %}
{% raw %}all the cereals that grow on the Nile, date-palms, and sugar-cane.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}The sugar-factories, which are a monopoly of the Khedive, follow{% endraw %}
{% raw %}each other in rapid succession. They are connected by the railway,{% endraw %}
{% raw %}and short branch-lines, used in harvest-time only, run from them{% endraw %}
{% raw %}to the plantations lying farther to the W. Their lofty brick and iron{% endraw %}
{% raw %}chimneys impart a very modern-industrial air to the ancient land{% endraw %}
{% raw %}of the Pharaohs. Large barges with sugar-canes or with fellahin{% endraw %}
{% raw %}'factory-hands' are met on the river. The juice is expressed from{% endraw %}
{% raw %}the cane and then refined by being boiled twice in closed vessels.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}The boat passes several large islands. On the W. bank lie Ba-{% endraw %}
{% raw %}ranka and Bibeh (Beba el-Kobra, p. 189), with large sugar-factories.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}The channel now contracts, and numerous islets are passed. 95 M.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Feshn(Fachn; rail, stat, p. 189), on the W.bank, is l'/o M. from the{% endraw %}
{% raw %}river. Near the village of El-Hibeh (El-Hebah), on the E. bank,{% endraw %}
{% raw %}about 2'/2 M. farther up, are the ruins of an ancient town, con¬{% endraw %}
{% raw %}jectured to be Het-benu ('phoenix-castle'), the capital of a province.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}The Town Walls, which are in good preservation, were restored under{% endraw %}
{% raw %}the 21st Dyn., as is proved by numerous bricks stamped with the names{% endraw %}
{% raw %}of Princess Est-em-kheb and her husband Men-kheper-re, or of their son{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Pinotem (II.), high-priest of Ammon. — Ruins of a temple, built by{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Sheshonk I. and Osorkon I. (22nd Dyn.), have also been discovered here.{% endraw %}
