{% raw %}to Assiut. GEBEL ET-TER. 18. Route. 195{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Thrice a month a camel-post leaves Maghagha for (3-4 days) the 'Small{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Oasis' of Bahriyeh, also called Oasis of Behnesa, because it is connected{% endraw %}
{% raw %}with Behnesa by another desert-route (comp. p. 190).{% endraw %}
{% raw %}The Nile-channel is very wide here (several islands); farther on{% endraw %}
{% raw %}both banks are flat. — At (113 M.) Sharona (Charounah; E. bank){% endraw %}
{% raw %}are tombs of the end of the 6th Dynasty. Near Kom el-Ahmar, to{% endraw %}
{% raw %}the S., are the ruins of a large temple built by Ptolemy I., and{% endraw %}
{% raw %}tombs of a late period, belonging to the town of Het-seteni, in the{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Cynopolitan nome. — 118 M. Beni Mazar (W. bank) is a railway-{% endraw %}
{% raw %}station (p. 189). — About 3 M. farther up, close to the E. bank, are{% endraw %}
{% raw %}the insignificant remains of Shekh. el-Fadhl, near which is Ha-{% endraw %}
{% raw %}matha. The discovery of a large number of dog-mummies here{% endraw %}
{% raw %}proves that we are standing on the site of the necropolis of Cyno-{% endraw %}
{% raw %}polis, the 'city ofthe dogs'. Several trough-like hollows and clefts{% endraw %}
{% raw %}have been found here, some of which, in the rocks, are of con¬{% endraw %}
{% raw %}siderable size ; but no inscriptions have been discovered. Cynopolis{% endraw %}
{% raw %}itself, in which Anubis was worshipped and dogs were held sacred,{% endraw %}
{% raw %}lay, according to Ptolemy, on an island in the Nile.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Opposite, I1/4 M. from the "W. bank, lies El-Keis, the Egyptian{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Ka'is, which superseded Cynopolis. Farther to the W., on the verge{% endraw %}
{% raw %}of the Libyan desert, lies Behnesa (p. 189).{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Near (13372 M.) Kolosaneh (Kolosna; rail, stat., p. 190), on the{% endraw %}
{% raw %}"W. bank, the Nile divides into three arms, forming two considerable{% endraw %}
{% raw %}islands. Opposite (E. bank) lies Surarlyeh (el-Seririeh). To the N.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}and S. quarries are worked in the limestone rock. Among the{% endraw %}
{% raw %}quarries is a small Rock Chapel, built under Merenptah and dedi¬{% endraw %}
{% raw %}cated to Hathor. On the external wall is Ramses III. between Hathor{% endraw %}
{% raw %}and another deity.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}On the W. bank lies the railway-station of Samallut (p. 190). A{% endraw %}
{% raw %}little farther to the S., at the mouth of a side-valley on the E.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}bank, rise the steep rocky sides of the Gebel et-Ter ('bird-moun¬{% endraw %}
{% raw %}tain'), with an extensive flat top bearing the Coptic convent Der{% endraw %}
{% raw %}el-Bukra, also known as Der el-'Adra (convent of the Virgin).{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Visitors are drawn up a vertical cleft in the rock by means of a{% endraw %}
{% raw %}windlass (bukra). The convent, which consists of a group of miser¬{% endraw %}
{% raw %}able huts, occupied not only by the monks but by laymen with{% endraw %}
{% raw %}their wives and children, is surrounded by a wall of hewn stone,{% endraw %}
{% raw %}erected in the Roman period. The foundation of the church is{% endraw %}
{% raw %}ascribed to the Empress Helen; the sanctuary is hewn in the solid{% endraw %}
{% raw %}rock and possesses a gate, now half-buried, adorned with Byzan¬{% endraw %}
{% raw %}tine ornamentation.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}A legend, recorded hy Makrizi, relates that on the saint's day of the{% endraw %}
{% raw %}convent all the bukir birds in the neighbourhood assembled here and thrust{% endraw %}
{% raw %}their heads, one after the other, into the cleft of the rock until one died.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}These birds are described as being black and white, with a black neck{% endraw %}
{% raw %}ringed near the head. The convent is named also Der el-Bvkir after them.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}On the E. bank, about l/% hr. farther on and 1 M. from the river,{% endraw %}
{% raw %}lies the village of Tehneh et-Tahtina ("Tehneh of the mill'), with{% endraw %}
{% raw %}two groups of ancient tombs.{% endraw %}
