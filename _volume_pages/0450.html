{% raw %}196 Route 18. MINYEH. From Cairo{% endraw %}
{% raw %}The group to the N. belongs to the Grseco-Roman period, that to the{% endraw %}
{% raw %}S. to the ancient kingdom. Near the tombs of the time of the Ptolemies{% endraw %}
{% raw %}are several Greek inscriptions. One sepulchral chapel, containing some{% endraw %}
{% raw %}representations of a late date, is interesting. A Greek or Roman grandee{% endraw %}
{% raw %}is here shown sacrificing to a number of Egyptian deities. The only in¬{% endraw %}
{% raw %}scriptions extant are on the inner side of the door. Higher up on the{% endraw %}
{% raw %}rock-walls are two horses in the Roman style, held hy men. Farther to{% endraw %}
{% raw %}the S. is a colossal image, carved out ofthe rock, of Ramses III. sacrificing{% endraw %}
{% raw %}to the god Sobk. — The very ancient group of tombs to the S., the in¬{% endraw %}
{% raw %}scriptions on which are in very bad preservation, are believed to belong{% endraw %}
{% raw %}to the necropolis of the town of Akoris, mentioned by Ptolemy alone{% endraw %}
{% raw %}and belonging to the nome of Cynopolis. Mounds mark the site of the{% endraw %}
{% raw %}ancient town.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}152 M. Minyeh (Minia) lies on the W. bank of the Nile, which{% endraw %}
{% raw %}is here over l/2 M. broad. At the railway-station (p. 190) is a buffet{% endraw %}
{% raw %}with good bedrooms (2 fr.) and in the town are three hotels kept{% endraw %}
{% raw %}by Greeks. The well-built and handsome town, with 20,400 in¬{% endraw %}
{% raw %}hab., is the seat of the Mudir of a district containing 267 towns{% endraw %}
{% raw %}and villages and 548,600 inhabitants. There is a telegraph-office{% endraw %}
{% raw %}at the railway-station, and adjacent is the post-office. The town{% endraw %}
{% raw %}possesses a hospital and several mosques. Parts of the street running{% endraw %}
{% raw %}along the river are planted with trees. A handsome bridge, with{% endraw %}
{% raw %}locks, spans the canal. To the N. of the town is a lightly-built{% endraw %}
{% raw %}chateau, with a large fruit-garden (many apple-trees). The large{% endraw %}
{% raw %}sugar-factory is the oldest in Egypt, and a visit to it during the{% endraw %}
{% raw %}sugar-harvest is of great interest; most of the officials are French{% endraw %}
{% raw %}and very obliging. Market-day in Minyeh presents a very gay and{% endraw %}
{% raw %}characteristic picture of Oriental life.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Opposite Minyeh, on the E. bank, lies Kom el-Kefara, with{% endraw %}
{% raw %}tombs of the Middle Empire.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Zdwiyet el-Metln (Zdwiyet el-Amwdt) is situated on the E.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}bank, 4^2 M. above Minyeh. — To the S. of the village lies the{% endraw %}
{% raw %}fine cemetery of the citizens of Minyeh, with its numerous domed{% endraw %}
{% raw %}tombs and chapels. Faithful to the custom of their ancestors under{% endraw %}
{% raw %}the Pharaohs, the inhabitants still ferry their dead across the river{% endraw %}
{% raw %}and bury them near the ancient necropolis.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}A few minutes' walk towards the S. brings us to the large{% endraw %}
{% raw %}mound of rubbish known as Kom el-Ahmar ('the red mound'),{% endraw %}
{% raw %}which runs parallel with the Nile. Climbing over this, we reach{% endraw %}
{% raw %}the ancient Rock Tombs, which are situated in a row among the{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Arabian hills, with their gates towards the river.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}The tombs, nineteen in all, are those of princes and grandees of{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Hebenu, and nearly all date from the close of the Early Empire. They are{% endraw %}
{% raw %}unfortunately in bad preservation, and some of them have been destroyed{% endraw %}
{% raw %}by violence, the stones being removed for use in building. The most in¬{% endraw %}
{% raw %}teresting are those of Khunes and of Nefer-sekheru, superintendent of the{% endraw %}
{% raw %}storehouses of Upper and Lower Egypt, under the New Empire.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}167 M. Benihasan, on the E. bank.{% endraw %}
