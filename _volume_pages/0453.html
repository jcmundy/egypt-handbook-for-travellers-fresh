{% raw %}The Vestibule which formerly stood behind an open court, is{% endraw %}
{% raw %}borne by two columns with sixteen edges and tapering towards{% endraw %}
{% raw %}the top. The cornice projects considerably above the architrave{% endraw %}
{% raw %}and is ostensibly supported by fine laths hewn, like all the rest of{% endraw %}
{% raw %}the structure, out of the living rock. The resemblance of these laths{% endraw %}
{% raw %}to the mutules of the Doric order is worthy of mention.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}The Tomb Chamber was divided by two pairs of columns into{% endraw %}
{% raw %}three slightly vaulted sections. Most of the scenes were painted on{% endraw %}
{% raw %}a thin layer of stucco, with which the limestone walls were coated.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}At the foot is a long inscription cut in the rock, in lines of a greenish{% endraw %}
{% raw %}colour, 2'/2 ft- high, containing the above interesting excerpt from{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Egyptian provincial history. In 1890 the royal names were cut out{% endraw %}
{% raw %}of the rock by some vandal hand and the paintings have also un¬{% endraw %}
{% raw %}fortunately suffered so much in the last 30 or 40 years, that the{% endraw %}
{% raw %}subjects of some of them are now almost indistinguishable.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Entrance Wall (W.). Over the door we see the statue ofthe deceased{% endraw %}
{% raw %}being transported to the temple, preceded hy dancers in curious attitudes ;{% endraw %}
{% raw %}below are carpenters. To the left (N.) of the door is the est.ite-office of{% endraw %}
{% raw %}the deceased, with servants weighing silver, measuring grain, and bringing{% endraw %}
{% raw %}corn into the barns, while scribes seated in a colonnaded hall register{% endraw %}
{% raw %}the amounts. The next two rows show the operations of ploughing, har¬{% endraw %}
{% raw %}vesting, and threshing. Still lower down is a Nile-boat, bearing the mummy{% endraw %}
{% raw %}of the deceased, as the inscription informs us, to Abydos (the grave of Osiris).{% endraw %}
{% raw %}In the fifth row is a representation of the vintage and of the gathering of{% endraw %}
{% raw %}figs and vegetables. The cattle in the water and the fishing scene (at the{% endraw %}
{% raw %}foot) depict life by the river. — N. Wall (to the left on entering). At the{% endraw %}
{% raw %}top is the deceased hunting in the desert. Below, to the right, he is re¬{% endraw %}
{% raw %}presented on a large scale inspecting various proceedings in his province.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}In the third row from the top two of his officials introduce to him a Caravan{% endraw %}
{% raw %}of Asiatics, including men, women, and children, clad in foreign gaily-coloured{% endraw %}
{% raw %}garments and accompanied by their goats and asses. The sharply cut features,{% endraw %}
{% raw %}hooked noses, and pointed beards of these strangers unmistakeably proclaim{% endraw %}
{% raw %}their Semitic nationality. The inscription describes them as 37 Amus (i.e.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Semitic Beduins) bringing eye-salve to the governor of the province. Khnem-{% endraw %}
{% raw %}hotep's secretary hands him a list of the visitors. The lowest rows depict{% endraw %}
{% raw %}the cattle and poultry of the deceased. — Rear Wall (E.). To the left the{% endraw %}
{% raw %}deceased appears with his wife in a papyrus-boat, hunting waterfowl with{% endraw %}
{% raw %}darts. All manner of birds fly about and nest in the thicket of reeds; in{% endraw %}
{% raw %}the river are fish, a crocodile, and a hippopotamus; below is a fishing{% endraw %}
{% raw %}scene. To the right is a companion picture, showing the deceased with{% endraw %}
{% raw %}two fish transfixed by his lance. In the centre of the wall is the door of{% endraw %}
{% raw %}a recess, containing a seated figure of the deceased. Above this door is{% endraw %}
{% raw %}the deceased catching birds with a net. — S. Wall (to the right). To the{% endraw %}
{% raw %}left the deceased is seated at table, with all kinds of sacrificial gifts heaped{% endraw %}
{% raw %}before him. To the right are processions of servants and priests bringing{% endraw %}
{% raw %}gifts for the dead. In the lowest rows are cattle, gazelles, antelopes, and{% endraw %}
{% raw %}poultry, brought to he sacrificed, and the slaughtering and cutting up of{% endraw %}
{% raw %}the sacrificial animals. — Entrance Wall. In the top row to the right (S.){% endraw %}
{% raw %}of the door are men washing; below, potters, men felling a palm, the{% endraw %}
{% raw %}deceased in a litter inspecting his ship-carpenters. In the third row are{% endraw %}
{% raw %}two ships carrying the children, harem, and dependents of the deceased{% endraw %}
{% raw %}to the funeral festival at Abydos. In the fourth row are women engaged{% endraw %}
{% raw %}in spinning and weaving and bakers. The lowest row contains men con¬{% endraw %}
{% raw %}structing a shrine, a sculptor polishing a statue, etc.{% endraw %}
