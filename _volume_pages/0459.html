{% raw %}About 4 M. inland (W.) from Roda, between the Bahr Yusuf and the{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Nile, near the village of Ashmunen, lie the ruins of the once famous{% endraw %}
{% raw %}city of KJimvnu (Coptic Shrnun), the Hermopolis of the Greeks, which{% endraw %}
{% raw %}from a very remote period was the chief seat of the worship of Thout,{% endraw %}
{% raw %}the god of writing and science. This town was also the capital of the{% endraw %}
{% raw %}nome of hares, in Upper Egypt, whose princes under the Middle Empire{% endraw %}
{% raw %}were buried on the E. bank of the Nile at el-Bersheh (see below). Nothing{% endraw %}
{% raw %}now remains of the extensive temples of the ancient city. The French{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Expedition found a fine Portico of the Ptolemaic period, with two rows{% endraw %}
{% raw %}of six columns each. — At Tuna el-Gebel, near Ashmunen, are the extensive{% endraw %}
{% raw %}necropolis of the ancient city and a rock-inscription (much dilapidate!) of{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Amenophis IV. (p. 203).{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Nearly opposite Roda, on the E. bank, amid palms of unusual{% endraw %}
{% raw %}size and beauty, lies the village of Shekh 'Abddeh, to the E. of which{% endraw %}
{% raw %}are the ruins of Antinoupolis, the town erected by Hadrian in{% endraw %}
{% raw %}honour of his favourite Antinous. The handsome youth is said to{% endraw %}
{% raw %}have drowned himself here, to fulfil the oracle which predicted a{% endraw %}
{% raw %}heavy loss to the emperor and so to prevent a more serious disaster.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}The remains of a temple of Ramses II., the relic of an earlier{% endraw %}
{% raw %}foundation on this site, may be traced. The vestiges of public{% endraw %}
{% raw %}buildings are now exceedingly scanty, though the French Expe¬{% endraw %}
{% raw %}dition saw a triumphal arch, a theatre, and streets flanked with{% endraw %}
{% raw %}columns. The streets and ground-plans of the houses, however,{% endraw %}
{% raw %}are still recognisable. The rooms were small and the walls were{% endraw %}
{% raw %}made mainly of Nile bricks. There are some underground apart¬{% endraw %}
{% raw %}ments of flat Roman bricks, reached by stone staircases. Near the{% endraw %}
{% raw %}ruins of one of the largest buildings lies a marble basin, which{% endraw %}
{% raw %}must have had a circumference of at least 23 ft. Among the palms{% endraw %}
{% raw %}in the village lies a fine Corinthian capital.{% endraw %}
