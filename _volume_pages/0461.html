{% raw %}When Amenophis IV. (p. lxxxii) became converted to the ex¬{% endraw %}
{% raw %}clusive worship ofthe sun and abjured the ancient gods, he quitted{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Thebes, the capital until that time, and withdrew with his court to{% endraw %}
{% raw %}a new sacred spot. This was situated in the Hermopolitan nome in{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Central Egypt, on both banks ofthe Nile, and its boundaries may{% endraw %}
{% raw %}be traced to this day by inscriptions chiselled on the rocks near{% endraw %}
{% raw %}el-Hawdla (p. 210) and et-Tell (E. bank) and near Tuneh and Gildeh{% endraw %}
{% raw %}(W. bank). The new royal residence-town was founded on the E.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}bank and speedily prospered. Temples and palaces sprang up, beside{% endraw %}
{% raw %}the imposing royal abode arose the dwellings ofthe nobles, and lordly{% endraw %}
{% raw %}tombs were prepared for the king and his favourites in the hills to{% endraw %}
{% raw %}the east. But after the death of Amenophis the ancient religion{% endraw %}
