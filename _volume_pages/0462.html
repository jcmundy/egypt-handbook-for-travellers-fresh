{% raw %}About li/2 M. to the N. of the landing-place at Hagg-Kandil{% endraw %}
{% raw %}are the ruins of the city. The position of the Palace op Ambn-{% endraw %}
{% raw %}orHis IV. is indicated by a house (known to the Arabs as el-Kenlseh){% endraw %}
{% raw %}erected by Prof. Flinders Petrie to protect the sumptuously painted{% endraw %}
{% raw %}*Stucco Pavements, which he discovered in the course of his ex¬{% endraw %}
{% raw %}cavations in 1891-92. There are four pavements, two in each room{% endraw %}
{% raw %}of the protective house. The 1st Pavement (in the first room),{% endraw %}
{% raw %}which originally decorated a hall of the harem borne by twelve{% endraw %}
{% raw %}columns, is divided into two sections by a-1- shaped band of cap¬{% endraw %}
{% raw %}tive Asiatics and negroes and bows (emblematic of the so-called{% endraw %}
{% raw %}'nine nations of archers', the hereditary foes of Egypt), which the{% endraw %}
{% raw %}king thus trod under foot as he passed through the hall. In the{% endraw %}
{% raw %}centre of each section is a rectangular pond with water-plants and{% endraw %}
{% raw %}fishes, surrounded by a thicket of marsh-plants, representing the{% endraw %}
{% raw %}banks of the pond, enlivened with birds and animals. This thicket{% endraw %}
{% raw %}was interrupted by the columns, the traces of which are quite{% endraw %}
{% raw %}distinct. The entire pavement was surrounded with a border of{% endraw %}
{% raw %}painted vases and bouquets of flowers. — The 2nd Pavement, in{% endraw %}
{% raw %}the same room, is in poor preservation, though ornamental vases{% endraw %}
{% raw %}and bouquets may be distinguished. — The *3rd Pavement (in the{% endraw %}
{% raw %}second room) is the finest. It also is divided into two symmetrical{% endraw %}
{% raw %}halves by a central band of bows and captives. On each side, in{% endraw %}
{% raw %}the centre, is a pond surrounded by a border of marsh-plants and{% endraw %}
{% raw %}animals (interrupted by the columns), while at the sides (beyond{% endraw %}
{% raw %}the columns) is a second similar border. The whole pavement,{% endraw %}
{% raw %}like No. 1, is surrounded with a border of vases and nosegays. The{% endraw %}
{% raw %}calves frisking in the marshy thicket are admirably drawn and{% endraw %}
{% raw %}highly lifelike in their movements. The hall to which this pave¬{% endraw %}
{% raw %}ment belonged had sixteen columns, the bases of three of which{% endraw %}
{% raw %}are still in position. — The 4th Pavement resembles the others in{% endraw %}
{% raw %}arrangement but is in inferior preservation.{% endraw %}
