{% raw %}to Assiut. TELL EL-'AMARNA. 18. Route. 205{% endraw %}
{% raw %}The Tombs of Tell el-'Amarna are essentially the same in point of{% endraw %}
{% raw %}structure as the tombs of the 18th Dyn. at Thebes (p. 287). Each is im¬{% endraw %}
{% raw %}mediately preceded by a Fore Court, which was originally surrounded by a{% endraw %}
{% raw %}brick wall. Thence a wide door admits to the Main Chamber, hewn in the{% endraw %}
{% raw %}rock, and in many cases with columns supponing the roof. The Chamber{% endraw %}
{% raw %}containing the statue of the deceased is next reached, either by another{% endraw %}
{% raw %}door, or by a corridor, passing between two other apartments. Many of{% endraw %}
{% raw %}the tombs are unfinished, in consequence of the early death of Ameno¬{% endraw %}
{% raw %}phis IV. and the subsenuent return of the court, to Thebes. — The tombs{% endraw %}
{% raw %}(in both groups) are marked with black numbers (1-25), running from N. to S.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Tomb 1 belonged to Huye, superintendent of the royal harem{% endraw %}
{% raw %}and steward to the queen-mother. In the entrance is the deceased{% endraw %}
{% raw %}praying. The Main Chamber has two clustered columns, of which,{% endraw %}
{% raw %}however, only that on the left side is standing. Entrance Wall. To{% endraw %}
{% raw %}the right are the king and queen seated at table, below the queen{% endraw %}
{% raw %}are two princesses facing each other, to the right are guards, etc.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Here, as in all similar representations in the tombs at Tell el-{% endraw %}
{% raw %}'Amarna, the sun appears above the royal couple, with rays ending{% endraw %}
{% raw %}in hands. Below are musicians, a banquet-table, bowing servants,{% endraw %}
{% raw %}soldiers, etc. Right End Wall. The temple of the sun is represented{% endraw %}
{% raw %}with the chief altar in the colonnaded court and statues ; to the right{% endraw %}
{% raw %}the king leads his mother to her palace, escorted by guards (behind{% endraw %}
{% raw %}and below); above is the sun with the rays ending in hands (see{% endraw %}
{% raw %}above). Rear Wall. This is occupied, on each side of the door, by{% endraw %}
{% raw %}two companion scenes (much injured), showing the king on the{% endraw %}
{% raw %}balcony of the palace. Left End Wall. The king is being carried in{% endraw %}
{% raw %}a litter to the temple of the sun (right), accompanied by a large{% endraw %}
{% raw %}retinue. Entrance Wall. To the left, a scene resembling that to the{% endraw %}
{% raw %}right (see above). — On both walls of the door to the next room the{% endraw %}
{% raw %}deceased appears in prayer. The following Chamber (unfinished){% endraw %}
{% raw %}contains the mummy-shaft, surrounded by a parapet hewn in the{% endraw %}
{% raw %}solid rock. — In the Last Chamber (serdab ; p. cxliv) is a recess{% endraw %}
{% raw %}with the colossal seated figure of the deceased, the features of which{% endraw %}
{% raw %}have been defaced. On the walls are mourners,-men with sacrificial{% endraw %}
{% raw %}gifts, a carriage, chairs, etc.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Tomb 2, whose owner is unknown, deserves special attention{% endraw %}
{% raw %}because its construction was still going on under Sakere, the son-{% endraw %}
{% raw %}in-law and successor of Amenophis IV. The Main Chamber has two{% endraw %}
{% raw %}columns; the rear-chambers are unfinished. Among the representa¬{% endraw %}
{% raw %}tions in the former the following may be mentioned. Entrance Wall.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}To the left is the king seated under a canopy holding the stem of a{% endraw %}
{% raw %}goblet, which the queen is filling with water; adjacent stand three{% endraw %}
{% raw %}princesses. To the right, the king, in a balcony of the palace, is{% endraw %}
{% raw %}handing down golden ornaments to a woman and a man; behind{% endraw %}
{% raw %}the balcony (to the right) are the royal chariot and fan-bearers;{% endraw %}
{% raw %}below, servants with food and drink. Right Wall. The king seated{% endraw %}
{% raw %}upon a chair in his palace; interesting representations of wrestlers;{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Libyans aud Syrians bringing tribute.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}The other tombs lie 3/4 M. to the S.E., in another hill-slope.{% endraw %}
