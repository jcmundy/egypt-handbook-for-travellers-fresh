{% raw %}Travellers'with abundance of time may visit also Tomb 3, of Ahmose,{% endraw %}
{% raw %}'fan-bearer on the ri^bt hand of the king'. The tomb is preceded by a{% endraw %}
{% raw %}spacious court. Over the entrance-door is the deceased worshipping the{% endraw %}
{% raw %}name of the sun; to the right and left of the door he appears praying in{% endraw %}
{% raw %}his official costume (with fan and axe). On the Right Wall ot the Main{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Chamber the king is represented, enthroned in his palace, handing a vase{% endraw %}
{% raw %}to a courtier; above are four rows of soldiers, armed with shields and{% endraw %}
{% raw %}spears, etc., marching to the temple, followed by the royal chariot (merely{% endraw %}
{% raw %}sketched in red pigment). The Side Chambers, on each side of the cor¬{% endraw %}
{% raw %}ridor, contain mummy-shafts, and door-shaped steles on the rear-walls.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Tomb 4, one of the largest and most interesting, belonged to{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Meri-Re, high-priest of the sun. The reliefs are now rather dark{% endraw %}
{% raw %}and require to be well lighted. — This tomb, like the others, is{% endraw %}
{% raw %}preceded by a spacious court. The entrance-door is embellished{% endraw %}
{% raw %}with a concave cornice, and on its inner side with a representation{% endraw %}
{% raw %}of the deceased in prayer. It admits us to a Vestibule, on the right{% endraw %}
{% raw %}and left walls of which are door-shaped steles, behind which rise{% endraw %}
{% raw %}large nosegays of flowers like columns. The other walls are covered{% endraw %}
{% raw %}with inscriptions. — The Main Chamber beyond was originally borne{% endraw %}
{% raw %}by four columns, of which two remain. In the doorway, to the right,{% endraw %}
{% raw %}is the deceased, to the left his wife, praying. Entrance Wall. To{% endraw %}
{% raw %}the left is the king at a palace-window throwing down golden orna¬{% endraw %}
{% raw %}ments. Left Wall. The scenes here represent the king driving in{% endraw %}
{% raw %}his chariot from the palace (on the left, above) to the temple of{% endraw %}
{% raw %}the sun (see below), preceded by his guards and followed by the{% endraw %}
{% raw %}queen, princesses, and retinue in chariots and on foot. Rear Wall.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}To the left is the temple of the sun, at the entrance of which priests{% endraw %}
{% raw %}in humble attitude await the king. To the right are the barns and{% endraw %}
{% raw %}other accessories of the temple, enclosed in a garden. Right Wall.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Above, the king and royal family, emerging from the palace (right{% endraw %}
{% raw %}top corner), are greeted by the priests in front of the temple. Below,{% endraw %}
{% raw %}the king and queen inspect the barns and stables of the temple.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Entrance Wall. To the right are the king and queen, accompanied{% endraw %}
{% raw %}by their daughters, worshipping the sun; Meri-Re and another priest{% endraw %}
{% raw %}stand beside the altars. Below are the royal retinue and priests;{% endraw %}
{% raw %}at the bottom, two carriages and blind singers. — The two following{% endraw %}
{% raw %}rooms are unfinished.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Tomb 5, of Pentu, a physician, is much damaged. In the entrance is{% endraw %}
{% raw %}the deceased praying, with an inscription in front of him containing a hymn{% endraw %}
{% raw %}to the sun. On the Left Wall of the first chamber are the king and queen{% endraw %}
{% raw %}praying to the sun, which rises over the pylon of the temple. The statue{% endraw %}
{% raw %}of Pentu, which stood in the last room, has been chiselled away. —{% endraw %}
