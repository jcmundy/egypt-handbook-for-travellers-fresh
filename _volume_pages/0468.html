{% raw %}In the hills enclosing the plain of Tell el-'Amarna there are numerous{% endraw %}

{% raw %}the Early Empire and at the beginning of the Middle Empire.{% endraw %}

{% raw %}The Arabian Mts., rising in precipitous rocky walls, approach{% endraw %}
{% raw %}the river. Swallows, ducks, and other birds inhabit the caves in{% endraw %}
{% raw %}the porous rock on the banks, and fly in and out in screaming{% endraw %}
{% raw %}crowds. The cliffs on the right bank of this part of the Nile are{% endraw %}
{% raw %}known as Gebel Abu Feda. The stream below them is considered{% endraw %}
{% raw %}the most dangerous part of the channel between Cairo and Assuan.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Violent winds blow round the crags, and numerous sand-banks{% endraw %}
{% raw %}impede navigation.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Near ed-Der and el-Kuser (on the E. bank) are several rock-{% endraw %}
{% raw %}tombs, without inscriptions, dating chiefly from the Early Empire,{% endraw %}
{% raw %}and also some ancient quarries.{% endraw %}

{% raw %}tress of heaven) were worshipped It was known to the ancient{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Egyptians as Gbsu and was capital of the Lower Sycamore Nome.{% endraw %}

{% raw %}numerous graves of the Middle Empire have recently been discovered,{% endraw %}
{% raw %}the interesting contents of which are now in the museum at Gizeh. Grseco-{% endraw %}
