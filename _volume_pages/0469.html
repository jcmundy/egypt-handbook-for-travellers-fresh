{% raw %}Ma'abdeh, a small village on the E. bank, lies on a narrow{% endraw %}
{% raw %}strip of fertile land between the Nile and the S. end of the rocky{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Gebel Abu Feda. The hills to the N.E. of Ma'abdeh contain tombs{% endraw %}
{% raw %}of the Early Empire. To the S. is the Gebel Kurneh, with a quarry{% endraw %}
{% raw %}exhausted in the reign of Sethos II. (inscription).{% endraw %}
{% raw %}About 3V2 M. to the N.E., on the plateau of the Arabian hills, is{% endraw %}
{% raw %}the so-called Crocodile Grotto, in which a large number of mummies of{% endraw %}
{% raw %}crocodiles were found, besides human mummies and a celebrated papyrus{% endraw %}
{% raw %}MS. containing fragments of Homer's Iliad. The expedition is not worth{% endraw %}
{% raw %}the trouble, as practically nothing is to be seen except the charred remains{% endraw %}
{% raw %}of the mummies of crocodiles. — To the S.E. of Ma'abdeh lies the village{% endraw %}
{% raw %}of 'Arab el-'Alyat, with tombs and quarries.{% endraw %}
