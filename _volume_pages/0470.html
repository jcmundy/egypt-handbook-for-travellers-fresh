{% raw %}Assiut.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}The tourist-steamers spend V2 day here. — Railway Station, see p. 191.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Hotel. New Hotel, near the station, well spoken of.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Post & Telegraph Office. — Steamboat Agencies. — Hospital, with physician{% endraw %}
{% raw %}educated in Europe. — Public Baths, well fitted up.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Consular Agents. The American consular agent is Bestadrot el-Khaiydt{% endraw %}
{% raw %}(also German consul), one of the leading inhabitants ofthe town, residing{% endraw %}
{% raw %}on the left side of the road from the harbour to the station. There are{% endraw %}
{% raw %}also French and Austrian consular representatives.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Pottery and other Oriental Goods may be obtained in the Bazaar at lower{% endraw %}
{% raw %}prices than at Cairo. European goods are dearer.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Assiut, or Siut (the name of which still preserves the ancient{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Egyptian Syowt), enjoyed considerable importance, even in anti¬{% endraw %}
{% raw %}quity, chiefly owing to its favourable situation in the midst of an{% endraw %}
{% raw %}extensive and fertile plain and at the beginning of a great caravan{% endraw %}
{% raw %}route leading to the oases in the Libyan desert and thenee to the{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Sudan. The town was the capital of the Upper Sycamore Nome and{% endraw %}
{% raw %}the chief seat of the worship of the god Wep-wat, who was repre¬{% endraw %}
{% raw %}sented as a jackal or wolf of the desert. This latter circumstance{% endraw %}
{% raw %}gave rise to the Greek name Lycopolis, or 'wolf town'. The modern{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Assiut, which extends for about a mile from E. to W., is the largest{% endraw %}
{% raw %}town (over 42,000 inhab.) in Upper Egypt, the capital of a pro¬{% endraw %}
{% raw %}vince, and the residence of the Mudir. It has, however, lost part of{% endraw %}
{% raw %}its commercial importance since the great caravans from W. Africa{% endraw %}
{% raw %}have frequented other routes and places. The fine pottery of Assiut,{% endraw %}
{% raw %}especially its bottles and pipe-bowls, is justly celebrated and forms{% endraw %}
{% raw %}an important article in its export trade, which also deals in linen,{% endraw %}
{% raw %}embroidered leather goods, carved ivory, natron, soda, and corn.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Near the harbour are several large palm-gardens, in which also grow{% endraw %}
{% raw %}pomegranate, fig, and other fruit trees. These gardens are let at{% endraw %}
{% raw %}high prices and produce rich harvests of fruit. The streets and{% endraw %}
{% raw %}bazaars are full of busy life, especially on Saturdays, when the{% endraw %}
{% raw %}people of the neighbourhood flock into the market.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Plotinus, the greatest of the Neo-Platonic philosophers (205-270 A. D.),{% endraw %}
{% raw %}was born here in the beginning of the 3rd cent., and his system was not{% endraw %}
{% raw %}uninfluenced by the priestly doctrines of his native town. From the be¬{% endraw %}
{% raw %}ginning of the 4th cent, onwards Christianity was dominant in the town{% endraw %}
{% raw %}and neighbourhood. Pious believers took refuge in the caves of the{% endraw %}
{% raw %}necropolis to live a life of penitence apart from the world. One of these,{% endraw %}
{% raw %}John of Lycopolis, at the end of the 4th cent., bore the reputation of a{% endraw %}
{% raw %}saint and even of a prophet. Theodosius sent an embassy to him to en¬{% endraw %}
{% raw %}quire the outcome of the civil war. The anchorite foretold a complete{% endraw %}
{% raw %}but bloody victory, and this prophecy was fullilled in the victory of{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Theodosius over Eugenius at Aquileia in 394 A.D. — The Egyptian Mission{% endraw %}
{% raw %}of the American Presbyterians (100 stations, 26 churches, 97 schools) has{% endraw %}
{% raw %}one of its chief stations at Assiut (director, Rev. Mr. Alexander).{% endraw %}
