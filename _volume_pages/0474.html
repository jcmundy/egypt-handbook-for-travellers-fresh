{% raw %}57*/2 M. Akhmim, a steamboat and mail station on the E.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}bank, also reached from Sohag by a shorter land-route, is a thriving{% endraw %}
{% raw %}little town with 28,000 inhab., including 8000 Copts. The weekly{% endraw %}
{% raw %}market on Wed. is much frequented, and the bazaar is well-stocked.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}The numerous cotton mills produce the cloth for the blue shirts of{% endraw %}
{% raw %}the fellahin and for the long shdla (pi. shdldt), or shawls with fringes,{% endraw %}
{% raw %}which the poorer classes wear on state occasions and for protection{% endraw %}
{% raw %}against cold. Akhmim stands on the site of Khemmis or Panopolis,{% endraw %}
{% raw %}which was the capital of a separate nome. The Egyptians named it{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Epu and also Khente-Min, after its god, the ithyphallic Min (p. cxxvi),{% endraw %}
{% raw %}whence proceed the Coptic Shmin and the Arabic Akhmim.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Herodotus (11,91) distinguishes the citiiens of Khemmis as the only{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Egyptians who favoured Greek customs and relates that they erected a{% endraw %}
{% raw %}temple to Perseus, worshipped him with Hellenic rites, and held games{% endraw %}
{% raw %}in his honour. The citizens claimed Perseus as a native of their town{% endraw %}
{% raw %}and told the garrulous Halicarnassian that he had visited Khemmis, when{% endraw %}
{% raw %}on his way to Libya in pursuit of the Gorgon's head, and had recognised{% endraw %}
{% raw %}them as his kinsmen. A statue of him stood in the temple. From time{% endraw %}
{% raw %}to time the hero revisited Khemmis, leaving, as a sign of his presence,{% endraw %}
{% raw %}a sandal, two ells long; the finding of this was considered a portent of{% endraw %}
{% raw %}good fortune. — Strabo mentions the weavers and stone-cutters of Panopolis.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}— Khemmis still flourished in the Roman period, and its ancient and{% endraw %}
{% raw %}famous temple was finally completed in the 12th year of Trajan. After{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Christianity established itself here the vicinity of Panopolis became crowded{% endraw %}
{% raw %}with convents. Nestorius, Bishop of Constantinople, who had been banished{% endraw %}
{% raw %}to the oasis of Hibeh (Khargeh) on account of his disbelief in the divine{% endraw %}
{% raw %}motherhood of the Virgin Mary, was attacked there by the plundering{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Blemmyes, and carried captive into the Thebai'd, where he surrendered{% endraw %}
{% raw %}himself ta the prefect of Panopolis, to avoid a charge of wilful flight. He{% endraw %}
{% raw %}died in Panopolis-Akhmim. Even after the conquest of Egypt by Islam{% endraw %}
{% raw %}the temple of the 'great town1 of Akhmim was, as Abulfeda and other{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Arabs relate, among the most important remains of the days of the Pharaohs.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}The temple ruins now lie outside the town, to the N. The temple was{% endraw %}
{% raw %}dedicated to Min (Pan), the town-god Of one of these temples the only{% endraw %}
{% raw %}remains are a few stones of the 18th Dynasty and some scanty fragments{% endraw %}
{% raw %}of a building of the Ptolemaic and Roman period. These are reached by{% endraw %}
{% raw %}the water when the Nile overflows its banks and are gradually being{% endraw %}
{% raw %}swept away. The second temple, farther to the N.W., built, according to{% endraw %}
{% raw %}the above-mentioned inscription, under Trajan, is represented by a few{% endraw %}
{% raw %}blocks only.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}In 1884 an extensive Necropolis was discovered among the low hills{% endraw %}
{% raw %}about 3 M. to the N.E. of Akhmim. The route thither leads via (2!/4 M.){% endraw %}
{% raw %}el-Hawdwish, in a hill beyond which are numerous tombs of the New Empire{% endraw %}
{% raw %}mingled with some of the Ancient Empire. To the N. and W. is a Christian{% endraw %}
{% raw %}cemetery, of the 5-15th cent., and in the vicinity is a Coptic convent. The{% endraw %}
{% raw %}tombs to the N., which are the oldest, date from the Roman, Ptolemaic, and{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Egyptian periods. Farther up the mountain are tombs of the 6th Dynasty.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}— To the S. of Akhmim is a rock-chapel constructed under King Eye{% endraw %}
{% raw %}(18th Dyn.).{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Continuing our journey up the Nile, we soon see, close to the{% endraw %}
{% raw %}E. bank, the conspicuous convent-village of Der el-Hadid, resembl¬{% endraw %}
{% raw %}ing a fortress. About 100 men, women, and children occupy the{% endraw %}
{% raw %}convent. The church is lighted by cupolas.{% endraw %}
