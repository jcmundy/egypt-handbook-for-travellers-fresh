{% raw %}About 71/2 M. to the W. of Menshiyeh, beside the village of{% endraw %}

{% raw %}Beside the village of El-Ahaiwa, on the E. bank, are other burial-{% endraw %}

{% raw %}shekh's tomb, are the ruins of an Egyptian brick fortress. — On{% endraw %}
{% raw %}the W. bank is the village of El-Ahaiwa el-Gharblyeh.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}The Gebel Tukh, on the Arabian bank, approaches close to the{% endraw %}
{% raw %}stream, about 3 M. below Menshiyeh. Extensive quarries (with{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Greek, Latin, and Demotic inscriptions) exist here, especially{% endraw %}
{% raw %}near Shikh Musa; these yielded building material for Ptolema'is.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}88 M. Girgeh.(Guerga), on the W. bank, is a railway-station (p.191){% endraw %}
{% raw %}and has post and telegraph offices. It was formerly the capital of{% endraw %}
{% raw %}the province of Girgeh (comp. p. 215), and contains over 17,000{% endraw %}
{% raw %}inhab., of whom 4800 are Copts. Many of the houses in the town{% endraw %}
{% raw %}are built of burnt brick and decorated with glazed tiles. The bazaar{% endraw %}
{% raw %}resembles those of other Nile towns. Outside the town lies a Roman{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Catholic convent, which is probably the oldest but one in Egypt;{% endraw %}
{% raw %}the abbot is a member of the Fraternity of the Holy Sepulchre.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}The town looks very picturesque as seen from the river. The Nile{% endraw %}
{% raw %}makes a sharp bend here, and the effect is as if the W. bank, on{% endraw %}
{% raw %}which the town stands, was at right angles to the E. bank. The{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Arabian mountains rise like walls, and the four tall minarets ofthe{% endraw %}
{% raw %}town, on the opposite bank of the Nile, seem to vie with them in{% endraw %}
{% raw %}height. A picturesque group on the river-brink is formed by an old{% endraw %}
{% raw %}and dilapidated mosque and a tall minaret beside it.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}At Meshaikh, on the E. bank, opposite Girgeh, scholars will find in¬{% endraw %}
{% raw %}teresting remains of a temple built by Ramses II. and restored by Merenptah.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Meshaikh is a village of the Aulad Yahya, on the site of the ancient Lepido-{% endraw %}
{% raw %}tonpolis. Above the village are some ancient rock-tombs, the chief of which{% endraw %}
{% raw %}belonged to Enher-mose, a high-priest of This in the reign of Merenptah{% endraw %}
{% raw %}(19th Dyn.). About 3 M. to the N. is the old Coptic convent of Der el-Meldk,{% endraw %}
{% raw %}the large cemetery of which is still used by the Christian inhabitants{% endraw %}
{% raw %}of Girgeh. The Arabian mountains, which approach close to the river{% endraw %}
{% raw %}beyond the village, contain numerous tombs, four of which, at a con¬{% endraw %}
{% raw %}siderable elevation, deserve special attention as being the resting-places{% endraw %}
{% raw %}of grandees of the ancient This (see below). Their inscriptions and re¬{% endraw %}
{% raw %}presentations are now scarcely visible. — The village El-Birbeh, 3'/2 M.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}to the N. of Girgeh, perhaps occupies the site of THs (Egypt. Tine), the{% endraw %}
{% raw %}capital of the most ancient Egyptian kings (see p. Ixxv) and of a nome of{% endraw %}
{% raw %}the same name.{% endraw %}
