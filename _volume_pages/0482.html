{% raw %}South Wing. — This building consists of a series of rooms, all{% endraw %}
{% raw %}more or less ruinous, a slaughter-yard, storerooms, etc. The most{% endraw %}
{% raw %}important, to which a visit should be paid even if all the others be{% endraw %}
{% raw %}omitted, is the long corridor known as the *Gallery of the Kings,{% endraw %}
{% raw %}entered from the left side of the second hypostyle hall, between the{% endraw %}
{% raw %}second and third row of columns. On the right wall is the famous{% endraw %}
{% raw %}*List op Kings. Sethos I. with the censer, and the crown-prince{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Ramses (with the side-lock of youth) reciting hymns from a book,{% endraw %}
{% raw %}are seen revering their royal ancestors, the names of 76 of whom are{% endraw %}
{% raw %}inscribed in the two upper rows. The list begins with Menes, the{% endraw %}
{% raw %}first king of Egypt, and extends down to Sethos, the names of un¬{% endraw %}
{% raw %}important or illegitimate rulers being omitted. Above the list is the{% endraw %}
{% raw %}inscription: 'The performance of the prayer for the dead — May{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Ptah-Sokaris-Osiris, lord of the tomb, who dwells in the temple of{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Sethos, increase the gifts for the kings of Upper and Lower Egypt —{% endraw %}
{% raw %}by King Sethos; 1000 loaves of bread, 1000 barrels of beer, 1000{% endraw %}
{% raw %}cattle, 1000 geese, 1000 incense-offerings, etc., by King Sethos for{% endraw %}
{% raw %}King Menes' etc. (here follows the list). In the lowest row the{% endraw %}
{% raw %}phrases 'by King Men-mat-re', 'by the son of Re Sethos' are repeated{% endraw %}
{% raw %}over and over again.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}This list of kings is of great historical importance, as partly by its{% endraw %}
{% raw %}aid it has been possible to fix the order of succession of the Egyptian{% endraw %}
{% raw %}kings. Another similar list is mentioned at p. 255.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}On the left wall of the corridor we again meet Sethos and the{% endraw %}
{% raw %}youthful Ramses. The father holds a censer in his left hand, while{% endraw %}
{% raw %}the son, adorned with the priestly panther-skin, pours a libation on{% endraw %}
{% raw %}the altar in front of him. The inscription contains in systematic{% endraw %}
{% raw %}order the names of these objects of worship, with their homes, whom{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Sethos has honoured with sacrificial gifts. —In the centre of the right{% endraw %}
{% raw %}wall a door leads into a narrow Passage, beyond which is a vaulted{% endraw %}
{% raw %}stone staircase which probably led to the roof of the temple.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}The Reliefs in the passage date from Ramses II. On the right wall{% endraw %}
{% raw %}the king and a prince appear lassoing a bull in presence of the jackal-{% endraw %}
{% raw %}headed good Wep-wat. On the left wall Ramses and a prince offer geese{% endraw %}
{% raw %}to Ammon and Mut; farther to the left five gods are netting birds; Ramses{% endraw %}
{% raw %}paces out the precincts of the temple; Ramses conducts four sacred cows{% endraw %}
{% raw %}to Khons and King Sethos.{% endraw %}
