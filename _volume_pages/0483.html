{% raw %}A few minutes to the N. of the Temple of Sethos I. lies the{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Temple of Eamses II., which also was dedicated to Osiris. It is in{% endraw %}
{% raw %}a very ruinous state, but still presents many features of interest.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}The scanty traces of a spacious court may be made out in front of{% endraw %}
{% raw %}the present entrance. Within, the ground-plan of a peristyle court{% endraw %}
{% raw %}(decorated with Osiris-columns like the Ramesseum, p. 284), two{% endraw %}
{% raw %}halls, the sanctuaries beyond them, and various other rooms can still{% endraw %}
{% raw %}be traced; but the average height of the remaining walls is only{% endraw %}
{% raw %}5-6 ft. To judge by the extant remains, this temple was a much{% endraw %}
{% raw %}more sumptuous and more carefully built structure than any of the{% endraw %}
{% raw %}other buildings of Ramses II. known to us. Not only fine-grained{% endraw %}
{% raw %}limestone, but also red and black granite (for the doors), sandstone{% endraw %}
{% raw %}(for the columns), and alabaster (for the innermost shrine) were used{% endraw %}
{% raw %}in its construction. The still brilliantly coloured mural decorations{% endraw %}
{% raw %}in the rear-rooms are executed in delicate low relief recalling the{% endraw %}
{% raw %}admirable sculptures under Sethos I.; the ruder reliefs 'en creux'{% endraw %}
{% raw %}make their appearance only in the court and the first hall, with the{% endraw %}
{% raw %}adjoining rooms. The reliefs in the first court represent a grand{% endraw %}
{% raw %}procession.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}On the right (N.) wall peasants are shown bringing oxen, antelopes,{% endraw %}
{% raw %}geese, and other animals to four priests, of whom the first records the{% endraw %}
{% raw %}gifts, while the second offers incense; farther to the right, the animals are{% endraw %}
{% raw %}being slaughtered. On the left (E. <fc S.) walls are similar scenes. To the{% endraw %}
{% raw %}left as we enter are persons with sacrificial gifts, who are met by a pro¬{% endraw %}
{% raw %}cession of priests, soldiers, the royal war-chariot, captive negroes and{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Asiatics, etc. The colouring of the figures is surprisingly well preserved.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}In the rooms behind are much damaged religious representations.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}On the outside of the temple, N. side, is an inscription relating to{% endraw %}
