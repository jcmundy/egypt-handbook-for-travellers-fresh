{% raw %}If we compare the temple of Dendera with a similar structure{% endraw %}
{% raw %}of the earlier period, such as the temple of Abydos or the great{% endraw %}
{% raw %}national sanctuary of Karnak, we find it not less beautiful in its{% endraw %}
{% raw %}own way, though of course far from competing with these gigantic{% endraw %}
{% raw %}structures in magnificence or extent. Its chief characteristics are a{% endraw %}
{% raw %}fine symmetry of proportions and dignified adaptation to its pur¬{% endraw %}
{% raw %}poses. Neither the figures nor the inscriptions sculptured on the{% endraw %}
{% raw %}walls compare in masterly execution with those in the tombs of{% endraw %}
{% raw %}the ancient kingdom or with those in temples dating from the{% endraw %}
{% raw %}reigns of Thutmosis III. and Sethos I. ; but we cannot refuse our{% endraw %}
{% raw %}admiration even to these products of later Egyptian art.{% endraw %}

{% raw %}tween the columns in front and nearly to the roof on the E. side;{% endraw %}
{% raw %}hence the floor of the temple appears sunken and is reached by a{% endraw %}
{% raw %}flight of steps.{% endraw %}

{% raw %}The exterior front of the hall is enclosed by six balustrades be¬{% endraw %}
{% raw %}tween the columns in the first row. Between the central pair of{% endraw %}
{% raw %}columns is a door, half the height of the columns. The interior{% endraw %}
{% raw %}walls of the pronaos are decorated with four rows of representations.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}These depict the ruler (in succession the Roman emperors Augus¬{% endraw %}
{% raw %}tus, Tiberius, Caligula, Claudius, and Nero) advancing with votive{% endraw %}
{% raw %}offerings for Hathor and the other gods worshipped in the temple. The{% endraw %}
{% raw %}reliefs (chiselled out) on the balustrades between the columns (PI. a-f){% endraw %}
{% raw %}refer to the ceremonial entrance of the ruler into the sanctuary.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Reliefs on the Balustrades. To the right of the Entrance (PI. a,{% endraw %}
{% raw %}b, c). In awe see the king, wearing the crown of Lower Egypt, quitting{% endraw %}
{% raw %}the palace, followed by his guardian-spirit and preceded by a priest offer¬{% endraw %}
{% raw %}ing incense. In 6 the hawk-headed Horus and the ibis-headed Thout{% endraw %}
{% raw %}sprinkle the king with the symbols of life; in c the goddesses of the south{% endraw %}
{% raw %}and of the north bestow blessings upon the king. To the left (on the W.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}wall) is a relief: the king is conducted before Hathor by the gods Mont{% endraw %}
{% raw %}of Thebes and Atum of Heliopolis. — The representations on PI. e, d. f,{% endraw %}
{% raw %}to the left of the entrance, are similar, except that in d the king wears{% endraw %}
