{% raw %}The rear-wall of this great vestibule forms the facade of the{% endraw %}
{% raw %}temple proper; it is surmounted by a hollow cornice and a round{% endraw %}
{% raw %}moulding. In the centre is a door leading to the —{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Hypostyle Hall, the roof of which is supported by six columns,{% endraw %}
{% raw %}with elaborate foliage - capitals on which heads of Hathor also{% endraw %}
{% raw %}appear. The base and lowest two drums of each column aTe of{% endraw %}
{% raw %}granite, the remainder of sandstone. Eight square apertures in the{% endraw %}
{% raw %}ceiling admit the light. Four rows of reliefs on the walls exhibit{% endraw %}
{% raw %}the king sacrificing to the gods of Dendera or celebrating sacred{% endraw %}
{% raw %}rites. In this and all the following rooms of the temple, with the{% endraw %}
{% raw %}exception of the second storeroom to the left of this hall and the{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Crypts (p. 229), the cartouches ofthe king are left empty, probably{% endraw %}
{% raw %}because the priests were in doubt as to which ruler should be{% endraw %}
{% raw %}selected for honour in the unsettled times during which the temple{% endraw %}
{% raw %}was built.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Some of the Reliefs in the lower row , representing the ceremonies{% endraw %}
{% raw %}performed by the king at the foundation of a temple, deserve notice. To{% endraw %}
{% raw %}the right of the Entrance (PI. g): the king, wearing the crown of Lower{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Egypt, quits his palace, preceded by a priest offering incense; to the left{% endraw %}
{% raw %}the king cleaves the earth with a hoe on the site of the temple — turns{% endraw %}
{% raw %}the first sod, as we should express it —; in front of him is the goddess{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Hathor. — To the left of the Entrance (PI. ft): the king, with the crown{% endraw %}
{% raw %}of Upper Egypt, quits his palace (as above); to the right he presents{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Hathor with bricks, representing the building-material for the new temple.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}On each side of this hall are three Chambers, used as laboratory,{% endraw %}
{% raw %}treasury, and storerooms for the sacrificial incense, etc. The in¬{% endraw %}
{% raw %}scriptions and representations show the Pharaoh in presence of{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Hathor, the lion-headed Horus of Dendera, and other gods.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}We next enter the First Antechamber, which is lighted by{% endraw %}
{% raw %}apertures in the roof and walls, and is decorated with four rows of{% endraw %}
{% raw %}mural reliefs. The latter represent the king worshipping, etc.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}in presence of Hathor and the other deities of Dendera. To the{% endraw %}
{% raw %}right and left are passages, leading to the staircases which ascend{% endraw %}
{% raw %}to the roof of the temple (p. 230). On the left also is a small{% endraw %}
{% raw %}chamber probably used for some religious rite.{% endraw %}
