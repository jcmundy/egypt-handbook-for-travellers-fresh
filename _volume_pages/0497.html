{% raw %}The central door leads to the profoundly dark Sanctuary, in{% endraw %}
{% raw %}which the sacred boats with the images of the gods formerly stood.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}The king alone, or his sacerdotal representative, might enter this{% endraw %}
{% raw %}sacred precinct and in solitude commune with the deity. Only{% endraw %}
{% raw %}once a year was this permitted even to him, at the great festival of{% endraw %}
{% raw %}the New Year. The reliefs on the walls depict the rites which the{% endraw %}
{% raw %}king had to perform on entering the sanctuary, and the sacrifices{% endraw %}
{% raw %}which he had to offer.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}There are three rows of Reliefs, but only the lowest can be distinctly{% endraw %}
{% raw %}seen even with the aid of a candle or a magnesium lamp. The reliefs{% endraw %}
{% raw %}are so arranged that each scene on the Left Wall (PI. k), beginning at{% endraw %}
{% raw %}the entrance, is followed in historical sequence by the corresponding scene{% endraw %}
{% raw %}on the Right Wall (PI. 1). 1 (left) The king ascends the steps to the shrine{% endraw %}
{% raw %}of the gods; 2 (right) removes the band fastening the door; 3 (1.) breaks{% endraw %}
{% raw %}the seals on the door; 4 (r.) opens the door; 5 (1.) gazes upon the god¬{% endraw %}
{% raw %}dess; 6 (r.) prays to her with his arms hanging down; 7 (1.) offers incense{% endraw %}
{% raw %}before the sacred boats of Hathor and Horus of Edfu; and (8; r.) before{% endraw %}
