{% raw %}We now return to the first antechamber, and ascend one of{% endraw %}
{% raw %}the Staircases (p. 228), which lead hence to the roof ofthe temple.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}The East Staircase, which ascends straight to the roof with easy{% endraw %}
{% raw %}steps, is dark, being lighted only by a single opening in the wall. The{% endraw %}
{% raw %}West Staircase is a kind of spiral staircase, with ten rectangular bends{% endraw %}
{% raw %}to the right, lighted by several windows, with tasteful symbolical repre¬{% endraw %}
{% raw %}sentations of the sun shining through them. The walls of both stair¬{% endraw %}
{% raw %}cases are embellished with reliefs of the ceremonial procession of the{% endraw %}
{% raw %}priests with the images of Hathor and her fellow-gods at the great New{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Year's Festival. The left wall presents us with a view of the procession{% endraw %}
{% raw %}ascending from the lower rooms of the temple, the right wall shows it{% endraw %}
{% raw %}descending. The priests are headed by the king; some of them wear{% endraw %}
{% raw %}masks representing the lesser deities. After completing the circuit of the{% endraw %}
{% raw %}lower rooms, the procession ascended to the roof of the temple, in order{% endraw %}
{% raw %}that 'the goddess Hathor might be united with the beams of her father{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Re' (the sun-god). — The W. staircase passes a small room (situated above{% endraw %}
{% raw %}the storeroom adjoining the second antechamber), with three windows{% endraw %}
{% raw %}looking into the court. Higher up is a small court with two rooms opening{% endraw %}
{% raw %}off it, corresponding to the chambers for the cult of Osiris on the E. side{% endraw %}
{% raw %}of the terrace (see below).{% endraw %}
{% raw %}We now reach the Temple Roof, which has various levels, the{% endraw %}
{% raw %}highest being above the great vestibule or pronaos (p. 229). At{% endraw %}
{% raw %}the S.W. angle of the lower terrace, which we reach first, stands a{% endraw %}
{% raw %}small open Pavilion, supported by 12 Hathor-columns. Adjoining{% endraw %}
{% raw %}the terrace on the N. are three rooms, situated above the chambers{% endraw %}
{% raw %}to the left of the smaller hypostyle hall. These were used in the{% endraw %}
{% raw %}worship of the slain and risen Osiris, as curious representations{% endraw %}
{% raw %}and numerous inscriptions indicate. The second room, separated{% endraw %}
{% raw %}from the first by pillars, formerly contained the famous Zodiac of{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Dendera (now in the Bibliotheque Nationale at Paris), the only{% endraw %}
{% raw %}circular representation of the heavens found in Egypt. The re¬{% endraw %}
