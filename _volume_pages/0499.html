{% raw %}Finally a walk round the outside of the temple will be found{% endraw %}
{% raw %}interesting. The exterior walls, which are covered with inscrip¬{% endraw %}

{% raw %}The reliefs on the E. and W. walls date from the reign of Nero and{% endraw %}
{% raw %}other Roman emperors. The large scenes on the S. rear-wall show{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Ptolemy XVI. Caesar, son of Julius Caesar, and his mother, Cleo¬{% endraw %}
{% raw %}patra, in presence of the gods of Dendera. The faces are purely{% endraw %}
{% raw %}conventional, and in no sense portraits. The projecting lions' heads{% endraw %}
{% raw %}on the sides of the building were probably intended to carry off the{% endraw %}
{% raw %}rain-water.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}To the right (N.E.) ofthe entrance to the temple of Hathor lies{% endraw %}
{% raw %}the so-called Birth House, a small temple half buried in debris.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Similar 'Birth Houses' were erected beside all large temples of the{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Ptolemaic period. They were dedicated to the worship of the sons{% endraw %}
{% raw %}of the two deities revered in the main temple, in the present{% endraw %}
{% raw %}case to Har-sem-tewe (p. 226). This 'Birth House' was built by{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Augustus and some of its reliefs were added by Trajan and Hadrian.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}On the N. and S. sides is a colonnade with lotus-columns, the{% endraw %}
{% raw %}abaci of which are adorned with figures of Bes (p. cxxvi).{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Interior. We first enter a vestibule, from which open, on the right{% endraw %}
{% raw %}a chamber with a flight of steps and a door to the colonnade, and on the{% endraw %}
{% raw %}left two other apartments. A door in the middle admits to a wide space, out{% endraw %}
{% raw %}of which open three other doors. Those at the sides lead to corridors,{% endraw %}
{% raw %}while that in the centre opens into the birth-chamber proper, the mural{% endraw %}
{% raw %}reliefs in which represent the birth and nursing of the divine infant.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Buried in rubbish immediately to the S. of the Birth House is{% endraw %}
{% raw %}a large Coptic Church.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Behind the temple of Hathor (to the S.W.) is a Temple oe{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Isis, consisting of a vestibule and three chambers. The unattract¬{% endraw %}
{% raw %}ive and uninteresting building, which is partly covered with rub¬{% endraw %}
{% raw %}bish, owes its origin to the Emperor Augustus.{% endraw %}
