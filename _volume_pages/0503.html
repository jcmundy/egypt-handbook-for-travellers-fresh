{% raw %}(W. bank). — Khizdm (E. bank; rail, station, p. 192) has a necro¬{% endraw %}

{% raw %}On the left bank, as we draw near Thebes, rise high limestone{% endraw %}
{% raw %}hills, presenting precipitous sides to the river, from which, however,{% endraw %}
{% raw %}they are separated by a strip of fertile land. The right bank is flatter,{% endraw %}
{% raw %}and the Arabian hills retreat farther into the distance. Before{% endraw %}
{% raw %}reaching the point where the W. chain projects a long curved mass{% endraw %}
{% raw %}of rock towards the river, we see to the left first the great obelisk,{% endraw %}
{% raw %}then the pylons of the temple of Karnak, half-concealed by palm-{% endraw %}
{% raw %}trees. When we clear the abrupt profile of the W. cliffs and new{% endraw %}
{% raw %}formations are visible at its foot, we may catch a distant view of{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Luxor towards the S.E. None of the buildings on the W. bank are{% endraw %}
{% raw %}visible until the steamer has ascended as high as Karnak; then first the{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Colossi of Memnon and afterwards the Ramesseum and. the Temple{% endraw %}
{% raw %}of Der el-bahri come into view. As we gradually approach Luxor,{% endraw %}
{% raw %}we distinguish the flags flying above the consular dwellings. The{% endraw %}
{% raw %}castellated villa of a Dutch resident is conspicuous in the back¬{% endraw %}
{% raw %}ground. In a few minutes more the steamer halts, close to the{% endraw %}
{% raw %}colonnades of the temple. — 39 M. Luxor (see below).{% endraw %}
