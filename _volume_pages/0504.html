{% raw %}The Donkeys on the E. side of Thebes are good and have good saddles.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}To Karnak 1 fr. or Is., and as much more when the traveller is called for or{% endraw %}
{% raw %}keeps the ass for the day. On the W. side the donkeys, which are much{% endraw %}
{% raw %}more heavily worked, are not so good, but they are fairly well saddled.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Charge 2 fr. per day. The hotels on the E. bank provide donkeys; on{% endraw %}
{% raw %}the W. bank they must be ordered beforehand.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Antiquities. The traveller in Thebes is frequently tempted to purchase{% endraw %}
{% raw %}antiquities. Half the population of Luxor is engaged in traffic with an¬{% endraw %}
{% raw %}tiquities, and the practice of fabricating scarabsei and other articles{% endraw %}
{% raw %}frequently found in tombs is by no means unknown to the other half.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Many of the articles offered for sale are so skilfully imitated that even{% endraw %}
{% raw %}experts are sometimes in doubt as to their genuineness; the ordinary{% endraw %}
{% raw %}traveller seldom or never secures an authentic specimen. Travellers are{% endraw %}
{% raw %}specially warned against purchasing papyri without expert advice. Purchases{% endraw %}
{% raw %}should not be made in presence of the dragomans, as these individuals{% endraw %}
{% raw %}by tacit agreement, receive a percentage from the dealers, which is, of{% endraw %}
{% raw %}course, added to the price. Only as many piastres as they ask shillings{% endraw %}
{% raw %}should ever be offered to the importunate hawkers of antiquities at the{% endraw %}
{% raw %}temples and tombs. Those who desire a genuine memorial of antiquity{% endraw %}
{% raw %}should apply to the director of the hotel or to the British or German{% endraw %}
{% raw %}consular agents (the latter of whom has a collection of antiquities well{% endraw %}
{% raw %}worth seeing). Prices vary greatly; 10-20s., or even more, must be paid{% endraw %}
{% raw %}for a good scarabseus with fine polish. Good and reliable specimens may{% endraw %}
{% raw %}be obtained from Mohammed Muhasseb and 'Abd el-Megid.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Photographs. Good photographs are produced hy A. Bealo in Luxor;{% endraw %}
{% raw %}but even in Shepheard's and other hotels in Cairo, excellent photographs{% endraw %}
{% raw %}of Egyptian temples are sold at moderate prices. Those by Sibah are{% endraw %}
{% raw %}excellent.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Distribution of Time. Travellers by the tourist-steamers are more or{% endraw %}
{% raw %}less committed to the prescribed programme (comp. p. 186). Those who{% endraw %}
{% raw %}are at liberty to arrange their time for themselves will find the following{% endraw %}
{% raw %}programmes convenient.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Three Days' Visit. 1st day. Luxor and Karnak (E. hank). Though{% endraw %}
{% raw %}visitors are sometimes advised to reserve this, the most gigantic of the{% endraw %}
{% raw %}monuments, to the last, it is really desirable to visit Karnak first of all,{% endraw %}
{% raw %}before fatigue has begun. The traveller who visits Karnak on the first{% endraw %}
{% raw %}day proceeds then to view the other lions, with the satisfactory feeling{% endraw %}
{% raw %}that Thebes has fulfilled his highest expectations; and he will not fail to{% endraw %}
{% raw %}take a later opportunity to return to refresh and confirm his first im¬{% endraw %}
{% raw %}pression. A visit by moonlight is exceedingly attractive, but travellers{% endraw %}
{% raw %}are advised not to make it alone, even although there is nothing to fear{% endraw %}
{% raw %}from robbers. — Visitors should ride early to Karnak, while the temple{% endraw %}
{% raw %}of Luxor, easily reached in a few minutes from the steamer or hotel,{% endraw %}
{% raw %}may be reserved for an afternoon-visit.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}2nd day. Cross the river early, visit the Colossi of Memnon, Medinet{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Habu, Der el-Medineh, the Ramesseum, and finally some of the Tombs{% endraw %}
{% raw %}of Shekh rAbd el-Kurna. The view at sunset from this point is of in¬{% endraw %}
{% raw %}comparable beauty and interest.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}3rd day. Cross the river early, visit the temple of Sethos I. at Kurna,{% endraw %}
{% raw %}ride to Biban el-Muluk with the famous Tombs of the Kings, then cross{% endraw %}
{% raw %}the ridge to visit the terrace temple of Der el-bahri.{% endraw %}
