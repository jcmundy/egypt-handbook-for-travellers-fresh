{% raw %}and they finally succeeded in usurping the throne and in uniting{% endraw %}
{% raw %}for a time the royal title with the priestly office. But the sun of{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Thebes began to set when the royal residence was transferred to{% endraw %}
{% raw %}the Delta under the princes of the 21st Dynasty. In the 7th cent.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}B.C. the armies ofthe Assyrians penetrated as far as Thebes and{% endraw %}
{% raw %}plundered it; the Ethiopians planted their rule here and honoured{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Ammon with buildings and inscriptions; the princes of the 26th{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Dyn. did for Sai's what the princes of the 18th and 19th Dyn. had{% endraw %}
{% raw %}done for the city of Ammon, but they also paid their homage to the{% endraw %}
{% raw %}great god of Thebes by erecting smaller buildings there. The in¬{% endraw %}
{% raw %}vading army of Cambyses ascended as far as Upper Egypt, but seems{% endraw %}
{% raw %}to have done little or no damage at Thebes. Nektanebes, one of{% endraw %}
{% raw %}the native Egyptian princes who maintained themselves against the{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Persians, found time and means to add a handsome pylon to the{% endraw %}
{% raw %}temple of Ammon. Alexander the Great and the princes of the{% endraw %}
{% raw %}house of the Lagidae probably found Thebes still a -great though{% endraw %}
{% raw %}decadent city, and they assisted to embellish it, as many buildings{% endraw %}
{% raw %}dating from the period of the Ptolemies still attest. The town of{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Ptolema'is (p. 217), founded and endowed with many privileges by{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Ptolemy L, soon became the capital of Upper Egypt, and rapidly{% endraw %}
{% raw %}proved a dangerous rival to the ancient metropolis. For a brief{% endraw %}
{% raw %}interval Thebes, though politically and economically weakened, re¬{% endraw %}
{% raw %}covered its independence under native princes, by putting itself{% endraw %}
{% raw %}at the head of the revolt in Upper Egypt against the Macedonian{% endraw %}
{% raw %}domination under Epiphanes. But the rebellion was speedily{% endraw %}
{% raw %}crushed and Thebes once more reduced to the rank of a provincial{% endraw %}
{% raw %}town. Its decline steadily continued and the great city gradually{% endraw %}
{% raw %}became a mere congeries of villages. Under Ptolemy X. Soter II.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}it again rebelled, but after a siege of three years was captured and{% endraw %}
{% raw %}destroyed. In spite of its evil fate it once more revolted, taking{% endraw %}
{% raw %}part in the Upper Egyptian insurrection in 30-29 B.C. against the{% endraw %}
{% raw %}oppressive taxation of the Romans. Cornelius Gallus, the prefect{% endraw %}
{% raw %}(p. xc), overthrew the rebels and utterly destroyed the ancient town.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Thenceforward Thebes is only mentioned as a goal of inquisitive{% endraw %}
{% raw %}travellers, who under the Roman emperors were attracted to the Nile{% endraw %}
{% raw %}by two monuments in particular — the pyramids and the musical{% endraw %}
{% raw %}colossus of Memnon on the W. bank at Thebes.{% endraw %}
