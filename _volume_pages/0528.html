{% raw %}The breadth (inside measurement) of this great hall is 338 ft.,{% endraw %}

{% raw %}enough to accommodate the entire church of Notre Dame at Paris.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}The roof is supported by 134 columns arranged in 16 rows, of{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Hypostyle Hall of Karnak. (From Maspero's Archeologie e'gyptienne.){% endraw %}
{% raw %}which the two central rows are higher than the others and consist{% endraw %}
{% raw %}of clustered papyrus-columns with calyx-capitals, while the other{% endraw %}
{% raw %}columns have bud-capitals.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}The hall is divided into three aisles. The roof of the lofty cen¬{% endraw %}
{% raw %}tral aisle or nave is supported by the two central rows of columns{% endraw %}

{% raw %}of the latter being met by placing square pillars above them. The{% endraw %}
{% raw %}spaces between these pillars were occupied by windows (one on{% endraw %}
