{% raw %}The columns are not monolithic but are built up out of semi-drums.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}The material is a reddish sandstone. Each of the twelve columns in the{% endraw %}
{% raw %}two central rows is ll3/s ft. in diameter, and upwards of 33 ft. in circum¬{% endraw %}
{% raw %}ference, i.e. as large rs Trajan's Column in Rome or the Vendome Column{% endraw %}
{% raw %}in Paris. It requires six men with outstretched arms to span one of{% endraw %}
{% raw %}these huge columns. Their height is 69 ft., that of the capitals 11 ft.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}The remaining 122 columns are each 42>/2 ft. in height and 6V2 ft. in{% endraw %}
{% raw %}diameter.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}The walls of the hall, the shafts of the columns, the abaci, and{% endraw %}
{% raw %}the architrave are covered with inscriptions and reliefs, many of{% endraw %}
{% raw %}which still retain their ancient colouring. These date from the{% endraw %}
{% raw %}reigns of Sethos I. and Ramses II., the former of whom embellished{% endraw %}
{% raw %}the N. half of the hall (as far as the 10th row of columns), the{% endraw %}
{% raw %}latter the S. half. Only a single column (the first in the 6th row){% endraw %}
{% raw %}bears the name of Ramses I. Ramses III., Ramses IV., Ramses VI.,{% endraw %}
{% raw %}and Ramses XII. have also recorded their names. The scenes on{% endraw %}
{% raw %}the columns show the king adoring the gods of Thebes. Those by{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Sethos I. are in delicate flat relief, those by Ramses II. in ruder{% endraw %}
{% raw %}sunk relief.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Among the beautiful Reliefs of Sethos I. (Walls a, b, c) the finest is that{% endraw %}
{% raw %}on the E. half of the N. wall (PI. c). We here see the king kneeling{% endraw %}
{% raw %}before the god Harmachis, who is seated beneath a canopy, while behind{% endraw %}
{% raw %}the king stands the lion-headed goddess Wert-hekaw holding a palm{% endraw %}
{% raw %}branch, from which hang various symbols. To the left the king kneels{% endraw %}
{% raw %}beneath the sacred tree of Heliopolis, on whose leaves the god Thout is{% endraw %}
{% raw %}inscribing his majesty's name.{% endraw %}
