{% raw %}On passing through the third pylon into the Central Court,{% endraw %}
{% raw %}we come first upon an Obelisk, the last survivor of four, erected in{% endraw %}
{% raw %}pairs by Thutmosis I. It is 76 ft. high and stands upon a base 6 ft.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}square. On each face ofthe obelisk are three vertical inscriptions,{% endraw %}
{% raw %}the central one being the dedicatory inscription of Thutmosis I.,{% endraw %}
{% raw %}and the other two additions by Ramses IV. and Ramses VI. The{% endraw %}
{% raw %}bases of the other obelisks are adjacent. The obelisks marked the{% endraw %}
{% raw %}entrance to the temple in the reign of Thutmosis I.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}A deviation may here be made to inspect the Historical Reliefs on the{% endraw %}
{% raw %}exterior walls of the great hypostyle hall (comp. p. 251).{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Next follows Pylon IV, in a most ruinous condition, beyond{% endraw %}
{% raw %}which are the scanty remains of the First Colonnade, originally{% endraw %}
{% raw %}embellished with columns and colossal statues of Osiris. Within{% endraw %}
{% raw %}this colonnade rose the two great Obelisks of Queen Makere, made{% endraw %}
{% raw %}of fine pink granite from Assuan, and covered at the apex with{% endraw %}
{% raw %}electrum (a mixture of gold and silver). The Right Obelisk has been{% endraw %}
{% raw %}overthrown, and the top has fallen some distance to the N. The{% endraw %}
{% raw %}*Left Obelisk, still standing, is 97'/2 ft- high and its diameter at{% endraw %}
{% raw %}the base is 8^2 ft.; it is the tallest obelisk known, with the excep¬{% endraw %}
{% raw %}tion of that in the piazza in front of the Lateran at Rome, which is{% endraw %}
{% raw %}105 ft. high. The inscriptions record that the obelisks were made{% endraw %}
{% raw %}in 7 months. Originally there was only the customary single ver¬{% endraw %}
{% raw %}tical inscription on each face; but afterwards, on the upper parts{% endraw %}
{% raw %}reliefs were added, showing Makere, Thutmosis I., and Thutmosis II.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}sacrificing to Ammon. The names and figures of Ammon were ob¬{% endraw %}
{% raw %}literated by Amenophis IV., but restored by Sethos I.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}This Colonnade underwent various transformations under the 18th Dy¬{% endraw %}
{% raw %}nasty. When Thutmosis I. built it originally it had two rows with five{% endraw %}
{% raw %}columns in each to the left, and two with six columns in each to the{% endraw %}
{% raw %}right, supporting roofs. When Makere erected the obelisks in the 16th{% endraw %}
{% raw %}year of her reign, the columns to the right and two pairs of those to the{% endraw %}
{% raw %}left were removed. Thutmosis III. surrounded the obelisks with a sand¬{% endraw %}
{% raw %}stone structure which concealed more than half of them, and portions of{% endraw %}
{% raw %}which still remain. Finally under Amenophis II. the right half of the colon¬{% endraw %}
{% raw %}nade was restored and six pairs of papyrus-columns were erected, of{% endraw %}
{% raw %}which, however, only the stumps are now left.{% endraw %}
