{% raw %}representations in the usual style of the cities and tribes subdued{% endraw %}

{% raw %}S., to the left 'the tribes of the UppeT Retenu (i.e. Syria), which{% endraw %}

{% raw %}Beyond the sixth pylon we enter the Fore-Court of the Sanc¬{% endraw %}
{% raw %}tuary, in which are two large Granite Pillars erected by Thut¬{% endraw %}
{% raw %}mosis III: The S. (right) pillar bears the lily of Upper Egypt, the{% endraw %}
{% raw %}N. pillar the papyrus of Lower Egypt. Here also are a *Bust of a{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Colossal Statue of Ammon and a headless Statue of the Goddess{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Amonet, in reddish sandstone, both dedicated by King Tut-enkh-{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Amon, whose name was later replaced by that of Haremheb, his{% endraw %}
{% raw %}successor.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}To the right of the fore-court is a colonnade with papyrus-columns,{% endraw %}
{% raw %}built by Thutmosis III., whence a fine view of the ruins is obtained.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Behind the entrance are reliefs of the time of Sethos II.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}The Sanctuary, built by Philip Arrhidaeus on the site of an{% endraw %}
{% raw %}earlier chamber, is not yet completely exhumed. It is built entirely{% endraw %}
{% raw %}of pink granite, and is divided into two chambers. The front{% endraw %}
{% raw %}chamber, opening to the W., is 19 ft. long, the hinder one, open¬{% endraw %}
{% raw %}ing to the E., over 25 ft. The roof, which was adorned with{% endraw %}
{% raw %}yellow stars upon a blue ground, has been completely destroyed.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}The walls both outside and inside were covered with reliefs, the{% endraw %}
{% raw %}colouring of which is still in good preservation at some points.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Interior Walls. In the first chamber Philip appears offering sacrifices{% endraw %}
{% raw %}to various forms of Ammon. The figures and inscriptions are picked out{% endraw %}
{% raw %}with bluish-green pigment. The reliefs in the second chamber are larger{% endraw %}
{% raw %}and better executed, but in poorer preservation. Here, to the left, Philip{% endraw %}
{% raw %}is seated at a banquet.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Round the outside of the Sanctuary runs a raised Corkidoe. On the{% endraw %}
{% raw %}S. side of the first chamber are 4 rows of reliefs, the lowest of which is{% endraw %}
{% raw %}destroyed: 1. Rites attending the entrance of the Sanctuary by the king;{% endraw %}
{% raw %}the goddess Amonet gives milk to the king. 2, 3. The sacred boat of{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Ammon on different festal occasions. On the S. wall of the second{% endraw %}
{% raw %}chamber are four reliefs, representing the king sacrificing to various forms{% endraw %}
{% raw %}of Ammon, etc. On the N. wall of the first chamber the king offers two{% endraw %}
{% raw %}small trees to Ammon-Kametf. On the N. wall of the second chamber{% endraw %}
{% raw %}are reliefs similar to those on the S. wall. The opposite will, as well as{% endraw %}
{% raw %}the back of the Sixth Pylon in the fore-court, is occupied-by inscriptions,{% endraw %}
{% raw %}known as the Annals of Thutmosis III., detailing the warlike acquisitions{% endraw %}
{% raw %}of that monarch. The relief above the inscription to the right of the{% endraw %}
{% raw %}black granite door depicts Thutmosis III. conferring gifts on the temple{% endraw %}
{% raw %}(two obelisks, vases, necklaces, chests).{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Opening off this corridor are a number of rooms, all more or less in{% endraw %}
{% raw %}ruins, which were built and embellished with reliefs by Queen Makere.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}As in all other places, the names and figures of the queen have been{% endraw %}
{% raw %}scratched out and replaced by those of Thutmosis II. or Thutmosis III.{% endraw %}
