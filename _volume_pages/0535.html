{% raw %}N. and S. Buildings. THEBES (E. BANK). 24. Route. 257{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Between this temple of Ptah and the great hypostyle hall of the{% endraw %}
{% raw %}temple of Ammon are the remains of three small Chapels of the Late{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Period, all entered from the E.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}The southernmost and largest was built towards the close of the{% endraw %}
{% raw %}26th Dyn. by the majordomo Peteneit. In the doorway we see Psamme¬{% endraw %}
{% raw %}tikh III. and the Queen Enkh-nes-nefer-eb-re sacrificing to Ammon and{% endraw %}
{% raw %}other gods. Beyond is a hall built of brick, with four stone columns. To{% endraw %}
{% raw %}the right of the entrance to the Sanctuary is Nitocris, wife of Psamme¬{% endraw %}
{% raw %}tikh II., to the left is Amasis. — The central chapel was erected by an{% endraw %}
{% raw %}otherwise unknown Sheshonk in the reign of Amasis, who is represented{% endraw %}
{% raw %}on the left jamb of the entrance-door. On the right jamb is Queen Enkh-{% endraw %}
{% raw %}nes-nefer-eb-re, to whose household Sheshonk belonged. The walls of this{% endraw %}
{% raw %}temple are of brick, with the exception of the sanctuary, which is of stone.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}— The ~H. chapel, the oldest, was built in the reign of Taharka (25th Dyn.),{% endraw %}
{% raw %}who, along with the princess Shepenwepet, appears on the walls.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}d. The Southern Buildings.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Situated to the S. of Ramses IT.'s girdle-wall (p. 255) is the{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Sacred Lake, named by the Arabs Birket el-Mallaha or Lake of the{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Salt Pit, as the water has become saline and undrinkable through{% endraw %}
{% raw %}infiltration. The banks were anciently faced with hewn stones, and{% endraw %}
{% raw %}traces of these are still to be seen on the W., S., and especially on{% endraw %}
{% raw %}the N. side.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Between this lake and the girdle-wall are a few half-ruined chambers,{% endraw %}
{% raw %}partly constructed of alabaster. The ruins nearest the lake date from the{% endraw %}
{% raw %}reign of Thutmosis III., those immediately adjoining the temple-wall from{% endraw %}
{% raw %}the reign of the Ethiopian Taharka.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}We now proceed to visit the Connecting Buildings between the{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Temple of Ammon and the Temple of Mut, which were begun by{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Queen Makere but were probably never completed. Quitting the Cen¬{% endraw %}
{% raw %}tral Court of the Temple of Ammon, we enter a court enclosed on{% endraw %}
{% raw %}two sides by walls and on the rear by Pylon VII., all of which are{% endraw %}
{% raw %}in ruins. On the exterior of the W- wall was inscribed the famous{% endraw %}
{% raw %}treaty made by Ramses II. with the Hittites (p. 252). On the E. wall{% endraw %}
{% raw %}is a long inscription (PL a), describing the contests of King Meren¬{% endraw %}
{% raw %}ptah with the Libyans and the peoples of the Mediterranean (Etrus¬{% endraw %}
{% raw %}cans, Achsans, etc.); and farther on is a triumphal relief (PI. 6) of{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Merenptah in the presence of Ammon grasping a group of enemies{% endraw %}
{% raw %}by the hair and smiting them with his club.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Pylon VII was built by Thutmosis III., whose victories are cel¬{% endraw %}
{% raw %}ebrated on both sides of it. In front of the S. side are the remains{% endraw %}
{% raw %}of two colossal statues of Thutmosis III., in red granite.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Beside the easternmost of the two walls which unite Pylon VII{% endraw %}
{% raw %}with Pylon VIII lies (to the left) a small Chapel, dating from the{% endraw %}
{% raw %}reign of Thutmosis III. Round the Sanctuary runs a gallery supported{% endraw %}
{% raw %}by pillars connected with each other by balustrades. Farther on, on{% endraw %}
{% raw %}the same wall, is a representation (PI. c) of Ramses II. sacrificing.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Pylon VIII is in comparatively good preservation, although it{% endraw %}
{% raw %}was built by Queen Makere, and is thus the most ancient part of the{% endraw %}
{% raw %}entire building. Makere's names were removed from the reliefs by{% endraw %}
