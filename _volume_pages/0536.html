{% raw %}258 Route 24. THEBES (E. BANK). 2. Karnak:{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Thutmosis II. Sethos I. restored the reliefs from which Ameno¬{% endraw %}
{% raw %}phis IV. (p. 203) had removed all allusions to Ammon; but he{% endraw %}
{% raw %}frequently inserted his own name instead of replacing those of the{% endraw %}
{% raw %}ancient kings.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}N. Side. Left Tower (E.). 1. Sethos I. sacrificing to various gods;{% endraw %}
{% raw %}farther to the right, 2. Thutmosis II. (originally Makere) before the lion-{% endraw %}
{% raw %}headed goddess Wert-hekaw and Hathor; behind the king are priests{% endraw %}
{% raw %}carrying the sacred boat of Ammon; beneath appears Thutmosis I. before{% endraw %}
{% raw %}the Theban triad. The inscription in front of this king refers to the{% endraw %}
{% raw %}accession of Makere. — Right Tower (W.), from left to right: 1. Sethos I.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}(originally Makere) before the hawk-headed Mont, who holds before him{% endraw %}
{% raw %}the symbol for 'life'; behind are priests carrying the boat of Ammon;{% endraw %}
{% raw %}beneath are two rows representing Ramses III. in presence of various{% endraw %}
{% raw %}gods. 2. Thutmosis II. (originally Makere) before Ammon and Khons;{% endraw %}
{% raw %}behind the king are the goddess Wert-hekaw and Thout, the latter writ¬{% endraw %}
{% raw %}ing upon a palm-leaf; beneath, Ramses III. before the gods. — On the Jambs{% endraw %}
{% raw %}ofthe Central Doorway are inscriptions of Thutmosis III. and Thutmosis II.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}On each side in the Gateway is Ramses II. before various deities.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}S.Side. Left Tower(W.). Amenophis II. seizing captured enemies by the{% endraw %}
{% raw %}hair and smiting them with his club; before him is Ammon (inserted by{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Sethos I.). Right Tower (E.), a similar scene. — On the Door Jambs are{% endraw %}
{% raw %}inscriptions of Thutmosis II. (left) and Thutmosis III. (right). — Leaning{% endraw %}
{% raw %}against the right door-post is an inscription on red granite, recording the{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Asiatic campaigns of Amenophis II. The lower part is broken off. — On{% endraw %}
{% raw %}the E. Side of the right (E.) tower are reliefs and inscriptions of high-{% endraw %}
{% raw %}priests of Ammon in the reign of Sethos II.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Six colossal seated figures of kings were originally placed before{% endraw %}
{% raw %}the S. side of this pylon. The best-preserved of these are: to the W.,{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Amenophis I. (limestone) and Thutmosis II. (red breccia); to the{% endraw %}
{% raw %}E., Thutmosis II. Both the figures of Thutmosis retain their lower{% endraw %}
{% raw %}part only; an inscription on the back of that to the W. records{% endraw %}
{% raw %}that Thutmosis III. restored it in the 42nd year of his reign.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}The following Pylon IX, built by King Haremheb, has collapsed.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}The reliefs and inscriptions (of Haremheb, Ramses II., Ramses IV.,{% endraw %}
{% raw %}and Ramses VI.) are of little interest. On the S. side are the remains{% endraw %}
{% raw %}of two colossal statues, which Ramses II. 'usurped'.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Between Pylons IX and X lies a square court, surrounded by a{% endraw %}
{% raw %}wall, which is interrupted on the left (E.) side by the ruins of a{% endraw %}
{% raw %}small Temple of Amenophis II,{% endraw %}
{% raw %}In front of the temple is a Gallery, borne by 14 square pillars em¬{% endraw %}
{% raw %}bellished with reliefs. Thence a granite portal admits us to a large Hall,{% endraw %}
{% raw %}the roof of which rested on 16 square pillars with concave cornices at{% endraw %}
{% raw %}the top. On the four sides of each pillar appears Amenophis II. before{% endraw %}
{% raw %}some deity. This hall is flanked by smaller chambers, two of which{% endraw %}
{% raw %}have pillars like those of the main hall. Most of the sculptures on the{% endraw %}
{% raw %}walls and pillars are executed in fine low relief, only a few being in{% endraw %}
{% raw %}sunk relief. Much of the colouring is in good preservation.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}On the E. Wall of the court, to the right (S.) of the temple, are{% endraw %}
{% raw %}several important reliefs of Haremheb. At PI. d we see the king{% endraw %}
{% raw %}conducting to the Theban triad a number of captives from the in¬{% endraw %}
{% raw %}cense-yielding land of Punt, who bear sacks of gold and gems and{% endraw %}
{% raw %}other gifts. At PI. e, to the right of the door, the king appears{% endraw %}
{% raw %}leading fettered Syrian captives before the Theban triad.{% endraw %}
