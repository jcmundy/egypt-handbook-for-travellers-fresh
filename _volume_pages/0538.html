{% raw %}The Temple of Medamut, dedicated to the war-god Mont, was situated{% endraw %}
{% raw %}in the N. suburb of Thebes, called Melu. The erection of this temple dates{% endraw %}
{% raw %}from the time of Amenophis II., of the 18th Dynasty. His name occurs{% endraw %}
{% raw %}on the large granite pillars which are still standing and which probably{% endraw %}
{% raw %}formed part of the Sanctuary. The latter was adjoined by a large edifice,{% endraw %}
{% raw %}lying approximately from E. to W. Much farther to the W. was a Pylon,{% endraw %}
{% raw %}facing the river, but now destroyed; it was built hy Sethos I. and Ramses II.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Under the Ptolemies an addition was made to the earlier temple, including{% endraw %}
{% raw %}the five columns, which are now the most conspicuous and most interest¬{% endraw %}
{% raw %}ing feature of the ruins. These belonged to a covered colonnade. Between{% endraw %}
{% raw %}the two clustered columns with calyx-capitals is a door, with inscriptions{% endraw %}
{% raw %}of Euergetes II. and Neos Dionysos. The two clustered columns with bud-{% endraw %}
{% raw %}capitals and the fifth isolated column probably belonged to the original{% endraw %}
{% raw %}temple of the Middle Empire and were afterwards incorporated in the{% endraw %}
{% raw %}rebuilding during the Ptolemaic period.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}B. THE WEST BANK AT THEBES.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}An early start should be made. Guides, Donkeys, etc., see p. 233. Lun¬{% endraw %}
{% raw %}cheon should be taken. A ferry-boat plies from the two chief hotels at{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Luxor to the W. bank (there and back Is.). Donkeys are usually found{% endraw %}
{% raw %}on the W. hank, but a large party is recommended to order them the{% endraw %}
{% raw %}night before.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}On the West Bank lay the Necropolis, or City of the Dead, and{% endraw %}
{% raw %}also a large number of temples. These latter, dating mostly from{% endraw %}
{% raw %}the New Empire, were dedicated to Ammon, the principal deity of{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Thebes, and were also used in the worship of deceased kings. They{% endraw %}
{% raw %}were adjoined by dwellings for the priests, libraries, and sometimes{% endraw %}
{% raw %}sohools. In the vicinity were groves and lakes, besides granaries,{% endraw %}
{% raw %}stables for the sacrificial animals, barracks for the guards, prisons,{% endraw %}
{% raw %}etc. Close by lay villages inhabited by the numerous workmen{% endraw %}
{% raw %}who found employment in connection with the cemeteries: masons,{% endraw %}
{% raw %}painters, builders, and above all embalmers, to whose care the bodies{% endraw %}
{% raw %}were committed. Gradually a whole city arose here, like the quarters{% endraw %}
{% raw %}beside the tombs of the Mamelukes at Cairo. Under the New Em¬{% endraw %}
{% raw %}pire its management was placed in the hands of a special official,{% endraw %}
{% raw %}known as 'prince of the West and director of the workers at the{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Necropolis'.{% endraw %}
