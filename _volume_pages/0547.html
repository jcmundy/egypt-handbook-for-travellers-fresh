{% raw %}The Walls of the tombs, from the entrance to the final chamber,{% endraw %}
{% raw %}were covered with sacred pictures and texts, ^a knowledge of which was{% endraw %}
{% raw %}essential for the deceased in the future life. The prevailing conception{% endraw %}
{% raw %}at Biban el-Muluk was that the deceased king, accompanied by the sun-{% endraw %}
{% raw %}god (or rather absorbed in the sun-god), sailed through the underworld{% endraw %}
{% raw %}at night in a boat; thus those scenes and texts were preferred which{% endraw %}
{% raw %}described this voyage and instructed the deceased as to the exact route.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}These texts were taken from two books, closely related to each other.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}One was called 'The Book of that which is in the Underworld'. According{% endraw %}
{% raw %}to this, the underworld (Twat; p. cxxiv) is divided into 12 regions, cor¬{% endraw %}
{% raw %}responding to the 12 hours of night; and the descriptions in the book{% endraw %}
{% raw %}were therefore likewise in 12 chapters. In each of these the river with{% endraw %}
{% raw %}the boat of the sun floating on it is represented in the middle; in the{% endraw %}
{% raw %}boat stands the ram-headed sun-god, surrounded by his retinue, and{% endraw %}
{% raw %}bringing for a short time light and life to the regions he traverses. Above{% endraw %}
{% raw %}and below are shown the two banks of the river, thronged hy all manner{% endraw %}
{% raw %}of spirits, daemons, and monsters, which greet the sun and ward off his{% endraw %}
{% raw %}enemies.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}The second book, known as the 'Book of the Portals', reproduces the{% endraw %}
{% raw %}same conceptions. The nocturnal journey of the sun through the 12 re¬{% endraw %}
{% raw %}gions of the underworld is again represented, and, as in the first hook,{% endraw %}
{% raw %}these regions are conceived of as provinces or nomes. Massive gates,{% endraw %}
{% raw %}guarded by gigantic serpents, separate one region from another; each gate{% endraw %}
{% raw %}bears a name known to the sun-god, and the deceased must know it also.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Two gods and two fire-spitting snakes guard the approach and greet the{% endraw %}
{% raw %}sun-god.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}A third work, which may be called 'The Sun's Journey in the Under¬{% endraw %}
{% raw %}world", contains still more gloomy and unattractive representations. The{% endraw %}
{% raw %}sun-god has arrived in the underworld and addresses a speech to the{% endraw %}
{% raw %}spirits and monsters, which are carefully depicted in long rows.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Recourse was had to other works also for the decoration of the king's{% endraw %}
{% raw %}tombs. The chief of these were the 'Praising of Ri\ and 'The Book of{% endraw %}
{% raw %}the Opening of the Mouth'. The former, which was used in the first cor¬{% endraw %}
{% raw %}ridors, contains a long-winded hymn to the sun-god, to he recited in the{% endraw %}
{% raw %}evening as the sun entered the underworld. In the course of the hymn{% endraw %}
{% raw %}the god is invoked under 75 different names and is depicted in as many{% endraw %}
{% raw %}forms. — The text and illustrations in the second of these works teach{% endraw %}
{% raw %}the multifarious ceremonies which had to be performed before the statue{% endraw %}
{% raw %}of the deceased king in order to indue it with life and ensure it the use{% endraw %}
{% raw %}of its organs, so as to enable it to eat and drink in the tomb.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Strabo tells of 40 tombs'worthy of a visit', the scholars of the French{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Expedition mention 11, while at present 41 are accessible, on the entrances{% endraw %}
{% raw %}of which their numbers are inscribed. Pausanias, A?lian, Heliodorus,{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Ammianus Marcellinus, and other ancient authors refer to them as the{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Syringes (aupirfs?) of Thebes, which name also cccurs in the Greek in¬{% endraw %}
{% raw %}scriptions within the tombs. The word 'Syrinx' meant first a shepherd's{% endraw %}
{% raw %}pipe formed of longish reeds, then it came to mean a hollow passage,{% endraw %}
{% raw %}and thus was applied to the long rock-hewn passages of Biban el-Muluk.{% endraw %}
