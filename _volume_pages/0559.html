{% raw %}Room XIII. One of the two pillars in this room has fallen. On the{% endraw %}
{% raw %}other appear the king, Ptah, and Osiris. Round the three main walls{% endraw %}
{% raw %}runs a bench, decorated with a hollow cornice; the small pillars{% endraw %}
{% raw %}which originally supported it have been destroyed. The representa¬{% endraw %}
{% raw %}tions on the back-wall are almost entirely obliterated. On the Left{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Entrance Wall and the Left Wall appears the sun's journey during{% endraw %}
{% raw %}the 7th hour of night (7th chapter of the 'Book of that which is in{% endraw %}
{% raw %}the Underworld').{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Middle Row. The sun-god once more is shown in his boat, on the{% endraw %}
{% raw %}prow of which stands Isis, to drive away evil spirits with her spells. In{% endraw %}
{% raw %}front of the boat a large serpent, which had threatened the sun-god, has{% endraw %}
{% raw %}been overcome by the goddess Selket and a god. Farther on are four god¬{% endraw %}
{% raw %}desses with swords, and four small mounds of sand, representing the{% endraw %}
{% raw %}graves of the gods Atum, Khepere, Re, and Osiris. Above these is a{% endraw %}
{% raw %}square enclosure upon which are the heads of enemies or slaughtered{% endraw %}
{% raw %}slaves, as charms to ward off evil spirits. Top Row. Spirits and daemons ;{% endraw %}
{% raw %}human-headed serpent; a god ('Flesh of Osiris') seated upon a throne{% endraw %}
{% raw %}beneath a serpent; three foes of Osiris, beheaded by a'lion-headed god;{% endraw %}
{% raw %}the god Yenku, holding a cord binding three foes lying on the ground ;{% endraw %}
{% raw %}three human-headed birds wearing crowns, etc. Bottom Row. Horus, before{% endraw %}
{% raw %}whom are the twelve star-gods who conduct the sun at night; twelve{% endraw %}
{% raw %}star-goddesses approaching the grave of Osiris, upon which a crocodile{% endraw %}
{% raw %}rests. The god's head projects from the grave-mound.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}On the Rear Wall is the sun's journey during the 8th hour of{% endraw %}
{% raw %}night (8th chapter of the 'Book of that which is in the Under¬{% endraw %}
{% raw %}world').{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Middle Row. The boat of the sun towed by eight men, preceded by{% endraw %}
{% raw %}nine followers of Re, who are represented by the hieroglyph for 'follow',{% endraw %}
{% raw %}with a head attached to it in front. Four rams (forms of the god Tatenen){% endraw %}
{% raw %}head the procession. Top and Bottom Rows. Dwellings of deceased gods{% endraw %}
{% raw %}and spirits, the doors of which open as the sun-god approaches, showing{% endraw %}
{% raw %}the occupants restored to life. In each house in the top row are three gods{% endraw %}
{% raw %}(first the nine gods of Heliopolis), with serpents and spirits below them.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}On the Right Entrance Wall and Right Wall is the journey during{% endraw %}
{% raw %}the 6th hour of night (6th chapter of the above-mentioned book).{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Middle Row. The boat of the sun is here preceded by Thout, with{% endraw %}
{% raw %}the head of an ape (his sacred animal), holding in his hand an ibis (also{% endraw %}
{% raw %}sacred to him), and by a goddess carrying the pupils of the eyes of Horus.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}The remainder of the row is taken up by a house, in whichstand twelve{% endraw %}
{% raw %}spirits together with the recumbent figure of the god Khepre, surrounded{% endraw %}
{% raw %}by a serpent with five heads. Four of the twelve spirits represent the kings{% endraw %}
{% raw %}of Upper Egypt, four the kings of Lower Egypt, and four other beings. In{% endraw %}
{% raw %}the Top and Bottom Rows are other spirits. In the latter are serpents, with{% endraw %}
{% raw %}the heads of the four genii of the dead upon their backs, also nine fiery{% endraw %}
{% raw %}serpents with swords, all intended to annihilate the foes of the sun-god.{% endraw %}
