{% raw %}*No. 34. The Tomb of Thutmosis III., discovered by Loret in{% endraw %}
{% raw %}1899, lies in an abrupt and narrow rocky ravine, about 275 yds. to{% endraw %}
{% raw %}the S. of the Tomb of Ramses III. The entrance, reached by a steep{% endraw %}
{% raw %}flight of steps, is closed by an iron door. A sloping corridor descends{% endraw %}
{% raw %}hence to a staircase, with broad niches to the right and left, beyond{% endraw %}
{% raw %}which another corridor leads to a rectangular shaft, about 20 ft. deep,{% endraw %}
{% raw %}probably intended as a protection against grave-robbers but now{% endraw %}
{% raw %}crossed by a hand-bridge. The ceiling is adorned with white stars{% endraw %}
{% raw %}on a blue ground.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Farther on we enter a room borne by two unadorned pillars. The{% endraw %}
{% raw %}ceiling is decorated with stars, and the walls bear the names of 741{% endraw %}
{% raw %}different gods and daemons. — In the left corner of the rear-wall is{% endraw %}
{% raw %}a staircase leading to the Tomb Chamber, with has the form of an{% endraw %}
{% raw %}oval king's ring. Two square pillars bear the ceiling, with its yellow{% endraw %}
{% raw %}stars on a blue background. The walls are covered with excellently{% endraw %}
{% raw %}preserved scenes and citations from the 'Book of the Underworld'.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}The representations on the pillars are of special interest. On one{% endraw %}
{% raw %}face of the first pillar stands a long religious inscription; on the{% endraw %}
{% raw %}second face are Thutmosis III. and the Queen-Mother Isis in a{% endraw %}
{% raw %}boat (at the top), the king suckled by Isis in the form of a tree{% endraw %}
{% raw %}(below), and the king followed by his wives Meryt-Re, Ah-sat,{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Nebtkhrow, and the Princess Nofret - ere; on the third face are{% endraw %}
{% raw %}daemons. The second pillar has daemons and another long inscription.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}The Sarcophagus is of red sandstone, and its scenes and inscrip¬{% endraw %}
{% raw %}tions are in red paint; it was empty when the tomb was opened,{% endraw %}
{% raw %}and the mummy of the king was found at Der el-bahri (p. 283).{% endraw %}
{% raw %}The objects found in the four small adjoining rooms are now in{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Gizeh Museum (p. 103).{% endraw %}
