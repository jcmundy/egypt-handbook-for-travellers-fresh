{% raw %}It should be noted that Makere in her capacity as ruler of Egypt is{% endraw %}
{% raw %}uniformly represented with the traditional attributes of kingship, viz. the{% endraw %}
{% raw %}short apron and elaborate beard, though these, of course, are properly{% endraw %}
{% raw %}appropriate to men only.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}The temple was dedicated to Ammon; but the goddess Hathor{% endraw %}
{% raw %}and Anubis, god of the dead, also had chapels here, and several{% endraw %}
{% raw %}chambers were devoted to the worship of the queen (who was prob¬{% endraw %}
{% raw %}ably buried in close proximity) and of her parents.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}The plan of this temple is remarkable, and is quite different{% endraw %}
{% raw %}from all others in Egypt. It occupies three courts or terraces,{% endraw %}
{% raw %}rising one above the other from the level ground; these are con¬{% endraw %}
{% raw %}nected with each other by inclined planes, which divide the whole{% endraw %}
{% raw %}into a N. half, to the right, and a S. half, to the left. At the W. side{% endraw %}
{% raw %}of each court is a raised platform, supporting a covered colonnade.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}The stages were cut out of the E. slope of the mountain, and sup¬{% endraw %}
{% raw %}port was given to the outer and inner walls by means of blocks of{% endraw %}
{% raw %}the finest sandstone.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}An Avenue of Sphinxes led from the plain to the temple, end¬{% endraw %}
{% raw %}ing at the gateway forming the entrance to the temple-precincts.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}In front of the gate, in square enclosures of masonry, stood two{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Persea trees (Mimusops Schimperi), the stumps of which are still{% endraw %}
{% raw %}extant.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}We first enter the Lower Court. This is in a very dilapidated{% endraw %}
{% raw %}condition, but its two Colonnades have lately been restored. Each{% endraw %}
{% raw %}colonnade consisted of 22 columns arranged in a double row. The{% endraw %}
{% raw %}columns in the back row were sixteen-sided, while the others{% endraw %}
{% raw %}were square in front and seven-sided behind. Little now remains{% endraw %}
{% raw %}of the reliefs and inscriptions that once adorned the walls.{% endraw %}
