{% raw %}Within the S. colonnade, at intervals of about 10 ft., are holes hewn{% endraw %}
{% raw %}in the rock, which were formerly filled with Nile mud and used for plants.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Remains of palms, vines, and the like are still extant.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}We now ascend the approach to the Central Court, on the S.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}side of which we can best observe the caTeful workmanship of the{% endraw %}
{% raw %}retaining walls, intended to obviate any movement ofthe soil. They{% endraw %}
{% raw %}consist of finely polished blocks of limestone, with simple but{% endraw %}
{% raw %}effective ornamentation. Broad pilasters, only 3 inches in depth,{% endraw %}
{% raw %}and placed at wide intervals, project from the wall. Above each{% endraw %}
{% raw %}are a gigantic hawk and a serpent. The W. side of the court is{% endraw %}
{% raw %}bounded by a terrace bearing two colonnades, that to the right{% endraw %}
{% raw %}named the Birth Colonnade, that to the left the Punt Colonnade.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}The Birth Colonnade contains 11 pairs of square pillars sup¬{% endraw %}
{% raw %}porting the roof. On all four sides of the pillars is the same scene:{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Ammon (a later addition) laying his hand in blessing upon the{% endraw %}
{% raw %}shoulder of Makere (figure defaced throughout) or Thutmosis III.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}The inscriptions and representations on the walls of the colonnade{% endraw %}
{% raw %}refer to the procreation and birth of the queen (PI. e). Among{% endraw %}
{% raw %}these are two fine figures of the queen-mother Ahmes, once in{% endraw %}
{% raw %}presence of the ram-headed Khnum and the frog-headed Heket,{% endraw %}
{% raw %}and once in presence of the ibis-headed Thout. — Two steps at the{% endraw %}
{% raw %}N. end of this colonnade descend to a Vestibule with 12 sixteen-{% endraw %}
{% raw %}sided columns in three rows. On the walls are fine reliefs.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}In the S. Wall is a small Recess with representations of the queen (ef¬{% endraw %}
{% raw %}faced) before various deities; above appears the queen (again scratched{% endraw %}
{% raw %}out) before Osiris. To the left of the recess is Anubis with the queen{% endraw %}
{% raw %}standing behind him; to the right are Nekhbet and Harmachis, between{% endraw %}
{% raw %}whom were the names of the queen. — In the N. Wall is a similar Recess.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Above it is Thutmosis III. making a wine-offering to the hawk-headed{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Sokaris, god of the dead; to the right, Anubis and the queen (sciatched{% endraw %}
{% raw %}out); to the left, the queen (scratched out) standing in a chapel before the{% endraw %}
{% raw %}fetish of Eme-wet. — On the W. (rear) Wall, the queen (scratched out) sacri¬{% endraw %}
{% raw %}ficing to Ammon (to the left) and to Anubis (to the right), with the sacri¬{% endraw %}
{% raw %}ficial gifts heaped up before each god.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Three steps at the back of the antechamber lead to a Chapel of Anubis,{% endraw %}
{% raw %}which has three chambers with pointed vaulting. The colouring of the{% endraw %}
{% raw %}mural decorations is admirably preserved, though the figure of the queen{% endraw %}
{% raw %}is invariably scratched out. They represent Makere (Thutmosis occurs{% endraw %}
{% raw %}once on the E. wall of the second chamber) before various deities, espe¬{% endraw %}
{% raw %}cially Anubis.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}On the N. side of this court is another Colonnade, the roof of{% endraw %}
{% raw %}which is supported by 15 sixteen-sided columns. It is unfinished{% endraw %}
{% raw %}and the walls were never decorated. Behind it are four chambers{% endraw %}
{% raw %}(now walled up), the walls of which were also left undecorated.{% endraw %}
