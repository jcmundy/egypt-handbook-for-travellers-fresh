{% raw %}(on a smaller scale). Rear Wall: Makere between Hathor and Ammon,{% endraw %}
{% raw %}who holds the hieroglyph for 'life' before her face. Above the{% endraw %}
{% raw %}entrances to the recesses (PL t, u), Makere and Thutmosis offer{% endraw %}
{% raw %}milk and wine to Hathor.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}We return hence to the Central Court and ascend the inclined{% endraw %}
{% raw %}approach which brings us first to a much ruined Terrace, the roof{% endraw %}
{% raw %}of which was borne by a double row of pillars and sixteen-sided{% endraw %}
{% raw %}columns. A granite doorway here gives access to the —{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Upper Court. The reliefs of a festive procession which adorned{% endraw %}
{% raw %}this hall have suffered severely at the hands of the Coptic monks.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Turning sharp to the right (N.), we reach a door (PL x) admitting{% endraw %}
{% raw %}to a Vestibule, which had three sixteen-sided columns. Opposite{% endraw %}
{% raw %}the door is a small Recess (PL y), with representations of Queen{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Makere in good preservation.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Rear Wall of the recess: Makere (erased) before Ammon. Side Walls :{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Makere seated at table, with the priest En-metf in front of her. The figure{% endraw %}
{% raw %}of the queen is uninjured, contrary to the usual practice ; but the priest's{% endraw %}
{% raw %}figure was defaced by Amenophis IV.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}To the left of the vestibule we enter an open Court, in which is{% endraw %}
{% raw %}an *Altar, approached by ten steps, and dedicated by Makere to{% endraw %}
{% raw %}the sun-god Re-Harmachis. It is one ofthe very few altars that have{% endraw %}
{% raw %}come down to us from Egyptian antiquity on their original sites.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}In the W. wall of this court is a small recess, 5-6 ft. in height, with{% endraw %}
{% raw %}sacrificial scenes on the walls. The figure of Makere receiving the{% endraw %}
{% raw %}offerings has been scratched out.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}A door in the N. wall of this court admits to a Chapel, compris¬{% endraw %}
{% raw %}ing two chambers, the ceilings of which, decorated with yellow{% endraw %}
{% raw %}stars on a blue ground, are vaulted in the pointed style. With a{% endraw %}
{% raw %}few unimportant exceptions the reliefs on the walls have been care¬{% endraw %}
{% raw %}fully chiselled away by Thutmosis III. and Amenophis IV. On the{% endraw %}
{% raw %}side-walls of the ist Chamber (PI. D) Makere offers sacrifices to var¬{% endraw %}
{% raw %}ious deities, mainly deities of the dead, such as Anubis, Sokaris,{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Osiris, Eme-wet, and also to Ammon. Above a bench against the{% endraw %}
{% raw %}end-wall is a representation of Makere and Thutmosis I. before the{% endraw %}
{% raw %}fetish of Eme-wet. 2nd Chamber. On the right wall: Thutmosis I.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}(originally Makere) and his mother Senyseneb (with figures unin¬{% endraw %}
{% raw %}jured), sacrificing to Amnion. On the left wall: Makere and her{% endraw %}
{% raw %}mother Ahmes sacrificing to Ammon. On the end-wall: Makere and{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Anubis.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}On the S. side of the upper court are several chambers now in{% endraw %}
{% raw %}ruins, and a well-preserved Sacrificial Hall, with a vaulted roof,{% endraw %}
{% raw %}which was used for the cult of the manes of Makere and is adorned{% endraw %}
{% raw %}with reliefs.{% endraw %}

{% raw %}officials bringing sacrificial gifts to Makere, and above are three rows of{% endraw %}
{% raw %}sacrificial objects of various kinds. Makere herself (effaced) is seated to{% endraw %}
