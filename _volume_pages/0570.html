{% raw %}6, The Ramesseum.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}This temple may be reached from the landing-place on the W. bank{% endraw %}
{% raw %}in about V2 hr.; from the Colossi of Memnon in '/• br.; and from Medinet{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Habu or Der el-bahri in about 20 minutes.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}The **Ramesseum, the large temple built by Ramses II. on the{% endraw %}
{% raw %}W. bank and dedicated to Ammon, is unfortunately only half{% endraw %}
{% raw %}preserved. We may in all probability identify it with the 'Tomb of{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Osymandyas' described by the Augustan historian Diodorus, although{% endraw %}
{% raw %}his description does not tally in all points with the extant remains.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Strabo seems to have referred to it briefly as the Memnonium, or{% endraw %}
{% raw %}building of Memnon.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Osymandyas is a corrupt form of User-ma-re, the praenomen of Ramses II.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}We begin our inspection at the great Pylon, which formed the{% endraw %}
{% raw %}E. entrance to the temple. This was originally 220 ft. broad, but{% endraw %}
{% raw %}its ruined exterior is now more like a quarry than a building. Many{% endraw %}
{% raw %}representations on the broad surface of its W. Side, next the first{% endraw %}
{% raw %}court, are in fair preservation and easily recognizable with an opera-{% endraw %}
{% raw %}glass (especially by afternoon-light). They refer to the Syrian{% endraw %}
{% raw %}campaigns of Ramses II., especially to the war with the Hittites in{% endraw %}
{% raw %}the 5th year of his reign, which is also commemorated on the pylon{% endraw %}
{% raw %}at Luxor (p. 240).{% endraw %}
{% raw %}On the N. Wing, to the extreme left, we observe the Asiatic fortresses,{% endraw %}
{% raw %}taken by Ramses in the 8th year of his reign. Thirteen of the original{% endraw %}
{% raw %}eighteen are still recognizable, each with an inscription containing its{% endraw %}
{% raw %}name. The captives are led by Egyptian princes. In the Middle, below,{% endraw %}
{% raw %}is the Egyptian army on the march, the infantry and charioteers in two{% endraw %}
{% raw %}rows, with traces of an inscription at the foot; above appears the Egyptian{% endraw %}
{% raw %}camp, within a rampart of shields, presenting an animated scene. The{% endraw %}
{% raw %}chariots are drawn up in long lines, while the unharnessed horses are{% endraw %}
{% raw %}being foddered; close by are the heavy baggage-waggons with their teams,{% endraw %}
{% raw %}unperturbed by the great lion of the king, which reclines before him. The{% endraw %}
