{% raw %}Nakht, both situated on the hillside.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}No. 118 is the Tomb of Ramose, a vizier, who flourished in{% endraw %}
{% raw %}the reign of the heretical king, Amenophis IV. (p. lxxxii). When{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Amenophis transferred his residence from Thebes to Tell el-'Amarna{% endraw %}
{% raw %}(p. 203), he was followed by Eamose, who left his Theban tomb{% endraw %}
{% raw %}unfinished. As one of the few monuments dating from the begin¬{% endraw %}
{% raw %}ning of the reign of Amenophis IV., the period at which the transi¬{% endraw %}
{% raw %}tion from the ancient religion to pure sun-worship was accomplished,{% endraw %}
{% raw %}this tomb is of great historical importance and a visit to it is espe¬{% endraw %}
{% raw %}cially recommended to travellers who have not seen Tell el-'Amarna.{% endraw %}
