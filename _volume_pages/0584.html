{% raw %}294 Route 24. THEBES (W. BANK). 8. Der el-Medineh.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}the mutilation of many of the inscriptions and also its present name{% endraw %}
{% raw %}(Der = monastery).{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Adjoining the temple on the left is an archway of bricks.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}We first enter a large Vestibule (PI. A), the roof of which (now{% endraw %}
{% raw %}mostly fallen in) was supported by two palm-columns. Light was{% endraw %}
{% raw %}admitted through openings in the end-walls. Separated from it by{% endraw %}
{% raw %}two columns with rich floral capitals and two pillars adorned with{% endraw %}
{% raw %}heads of Hathor is the Pronaos (PI. B). Screen-walls rose between{% endraw %}
{% raw %}the pillars and columns, and between the columns, which bear figures{% endraw %}
{% raw %}of the deified sage Amenhotep and the god Imhotep, is a doorway,{% endraw %}
{% raw %}open at the top. Only the left screen is now left. The walls of this{% endraw %}
{% raw %}hall are embellished with incised reliefs, representing the king sacri¬{% endraw %}
{% raw %}ficing to various deities. Towards the top of the left wall is a taste¬{% endraw %}
{% raw %}ful window, which originally lighted a staircase. From the pronaos{% endraw %}
{% raw %}three doors open into as many Chapels. Above the hollow cornice over{% endraw %}
{% raw %}the doorway to the Central Chapel (PI. C) are seven heads of Hathor.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}On the interior walls appears Philopator, sometimes accompanied by{% endraw %}
{% raw %}his sister Arsinoe; and on the jambs of the entrance-door are four{% endraw %}
{% raw %}gods with bulls' heads. On the left wall of the Left Chapel (PL D){% endraw %}
{% raw %}is a remarkable representation of the Judgment of the Dead (PL a).{% endraw %}
{% raw %}To the right is enthroned Osiris, god of the underworld, and in front{% endraw %}
{% raw %}of him are the 'fetish' of Eme-wet (p. exxvi), the four genii of the{% endraw %}
{% raw %}dead upon a lotus-flower, the 'Devourer of the Underworld' in the{% endraw %}
{% raw %}form of a hippopotamus, and Harpocrates, resting upon a crooked{% endraw %}
{% raw %}staff. The ibis-headed Thout inscribes the verdict. To the left Anubis{% endraw %}
{% raw %}and Horus weigh the heart of the deceased; two goddesses of truth,{% endraw %}
{% raw %}with feathers on their head, conduct the deceased into the judgment{% endraw %}
{% raw %}hall. Above is the deceased praying to the 42 judges of the dead.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}On the rear-wall (PL b) of this chapel Philopator offers incense{% endraw %}
{% raw %}before Osiris and Isis. On the right wall (PI. c) appears, to the left,{% endraw %}
{% raw %}the sacred boat of Osiris-Sokaris, with standards, etc., beside it.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}To the right the king offers incense to Anubis, who holds a disk,{% endraw %}
{% raw %}and to the ithyphallic Min. On the lintel of the door is a four-{% endraw %}
{% raw %}headed ram (the god of the four winds), above which is a flying{% endraw %}
{% raw %}vulture, worshipped by four goddesses. — On the door-jambs, the{% endraw %}
{% raw %}king with three hawk-headed and three jackal-headed genii. — The{% endraw %}
{% raw %}reliefs in the Right (S.) Chapel (PL E) are of little interest.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}The valley of Der el-Medineh is rich in Tombs of various periods,{% endraw %}
{% raw %}particularly of the 20th Dyn., with the colours of the paintings{% endraw %}
{% raw %}marvellously preserved. They are not shown without the permission{% endraw %}
{% raw %}ofthe Inspector General (p. 297).{% endraw %}
{% raw %}The following are interesting: No. 20. Tomb of Sennutem, an official{% endraw %}
{% raw %}of the Necropolis (p. 260), with a vaulted chamber and well-preserved{% endraw %}
{% raw %}mural scenes of religious import. — No. 12. Tomb ofPeskhetu, another{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Necropolis official. The vaulted chamber contained a sarcophagus made{% endraw %}
{% raw %}of blocks of limestone. The walls are covered with religious texts and{% endraw %}
{% raw %}scenes. To the right of the door is the deceased, lying on the ground{% endraw %}
{% raw %}below a palm-tree and praying; to the left, his father (with white hair){% endraw %}
{% raw %}and other relatives.{% endraw %}
