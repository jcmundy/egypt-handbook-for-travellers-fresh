{% raw %}8. Dfr el-Medineh. THEBES (W. BANK). 24. Route. 295{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Proceeding farther into the valley, we reach a tomb with a wide{% endraw %}
{% raw %}entrance from which there is a fine view of Der el-Medineh and of{% endraw %}
{% raw %}the fertile plains to the E., traversed by the Nile and bounded by{% endraw %}
{% raw %}the distant Arabian mountains. In the foreground are seen Medinet{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Habu, the Colossi of Memnon, and the Eamesseum, and on the{% endraw %}
{% raw %}other side of the river the gigantic ruins of Karnak.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}The hill of Kurnet Murrai, 1/2 M. to the S.E. of Der el-Medineh,{% endraw %}
{% raw %}is occupied by a number of fellah huts. Scattered among these are{% endraw %}
{% raw %}several tombs of the 18th Dyn., most of which are of little interest.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}The only one that need be mentioned is the —{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Tomb of Huye, who was governor of Ethiopia under Tut-enkh-{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Amon. The key is kept by the Inspector General at Medinet Habu{% endraw %}
{% raw %}(to the S.E.).{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Entrance Wall. To the right (PI. a) appears the ceremonial in¬{% endraw %}
{% raw %}vestiture of Huye as governor of the Sudan, in presence of the king; his{% endraw %}
{% raw %}friends congratulate Huye. To the left (PI. 6) stands the deceased, with{% endraw %}
{% raw %}his male and female relatives behind him, and two Nile boats before{% endraw %}
{% raw %}him; below is another row of people; farther{% endraw %}
{% raw %}to the right is the governor of Ethiopia, with _______{% endraw %}
{% raw %}rows of people bringing tribute. — On the left I{% endraw %}
{% raw %}end-wall (PI. c) appears the deceased, sacrificing{% endraw %}
{% raw %}to the jackal-headed Anubis on his left and to{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Osiris on his right. — On the rear-wall to the{% endraw %}
{% raw %}left (PI. d) is Huye, bearing the fan and crooked{% endraw %}
{% raw %}staff, the symbols of his dignity, and presenting{% endraw %}
{% raw %}to the king the tribute from Nubia, brought by{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Nubian chiefs. Behind him are large specimens,{% endraw %}
{% raw %}including a Nubian landscape standing upon a{% endraw %}
{% raw %}table covered with panther-skins and cloths: in{% endraw %}
{% raw %}the centre is a conical hut, with dum-palms, giraffes, and negroes at the sides.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Higher up are red and blue gems in cups, rings of gold, sacks of gold-dust,{% endraw %}
{% raw %}shields covered with golden plates and gay skins, footstools, chairs, benches,{% endraw %}
{% raw %}and head-rests of ebony, a chariot, etc. Three rows of Nubian chiefs, dressed{% endraw %}
{% raw %}(with a few exceptions) in the Egyptian style, are received by Huye and{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Amenhotep in the king's name. In the top row, behind the chiefs, their{% endraw %}
{% raw %}princess, shaded by an umbrella, approaches in a chariot drawn by oxen,{% endraw %}
{% raw %}and is followed by chiefs wearing ostrich-feathers in their hair, which is{% endraw %}
{% raw %}plaited into a kind of hood (as is the custom to this day among these{% endraw %}
{% raw %}tribes). The procession is closed by a brown and a black Ethiopian{% endraw %}
{% raw %}woman, with pendent breasts. The former carries a child in a basket on{% endraw %}
{% raw %}her back, and each woman leads a nude boy behind her. In the second{% endraw %}
{% raw %}and third rows are Nubians bringing the produce of their country, in¬{% endraw %}
{% raw %}cluding gold, panther-skins, a giraffe, and- oxen. Each ox has a brown{% endraw %}
{% raw %}and a black human hand most singularly fixed on its horns. More to the{% endraw %}
{% raw %}left are five rows of ships (the lower rows much damaged). Five Ethiopian{% endraw %}
{% raw %}princes kneel upon the deck of the second boat. Cattle and other goods{% endraw %}
{% raw %}are being brought to Egypt in the smaller vessels below. — On the rear-{% endraw %}
{% raw %}wall to the right (e) appears the king, with Huye before him presenting{% endraw %}
{% raw %}the Syrian tribute. Amenhotep, brother of Huye, is bringing pieces of{% endraw %}
{% raw %}lapislazuli on a dish. By his right hand hangs a breastplate, set with{% endraw %}
{% raw %}precious stones. The representations of the Syrian grandees approaching{% endraw %}
{% raw %}the king, with their gifts, on the right, are unfortunately obliterated. —{% endraw %}
{% raw %}The sepulchral inscription, which should have occupied the right end-{% endraw %}
{% raw %}wall (PI. /), was never executed. On each side of the vacant space are{% endraw %}
{% raw %}offerings to Huye. The rest is destroyed.{% endraw %}
