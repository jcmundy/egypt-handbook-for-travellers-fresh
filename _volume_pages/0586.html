{% raw %}The Tombs of the Queens, called by the Arabs Biban el-Harim{% endraw %}
{% raw %}or Blbdn es-Sultdndt, and sometimes Biban el-Haggi Hammed, do{% endraw %}
{% raw %}not demand a visit except from travellers who have abundant{% endraw %}
{% raw %}leisure.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}On the way from Der el-Medineh to the (L/4 hr.) Tombs of the{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Queens we pass a number of historically important steles of the{% endraw %}
{% raw %}18th, 19th, and 20th Dyn., placed against the ridge. One of these{% endraw %}
{% raw %}shows Eamses III. before Ammon; Mer-segret, goddess of the West,{% endraw %}
{% raw %}offers him her breast; behind is Harmachis. The inscription refers{% endraw %}
{% raw %}to the campaigns of the king. On another Eamses III. appears before{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Horus, and his father Set-nakht before Ammon. — The road from{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Medinet Habu (p. 297) to the (V2 l>r-) Tombs of the Queens crosses{% endraw %}
{% raw %}the desert to the W., and passes through a mountain-valley with{% endraw %}
{% raw %}bare and lofty sides of limestone, picturesquely formed and carved{% endraw %}
{% raw %}with inscriptions to the gods of the regions of the dead.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}The tombs belong mainly to the 19th and 20th Dynasties, but{% endraw %}
{% raw %}a few are of the 18th Dynasty. Altogether upwards of 20 have{% endraw %}
{% raw %}been discovered, many unfinished{% endraw %}
{% raw %}and entirely without decoration,{% endraw %}
{% raw %}and in their rough and blackened{% endraw %}
{% raw %}condition, resembling mere caves in{% endraw %}
{% raw %}the rocks. It is rare to find either in¬{% endraw %}
{% raw %}scriptions or representations carved{% endraw %}
{% raw %}in the stone ; even in the finest tombs{% endraw %}
{% raw %}the limestone walls were more often{% endraw %}
{% raw %}covered with plaster which could be{% endraw %}
{% raw %}adorned with paintings without much{% endraw %}
{% raw %}difficulty.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}The most interesting tomb lies{% endraw %}
{% raw %}on the S. side of the valley. This is{% endraw %}
{% raw %}the Tomb of Queen Titi. It consists{% endraw %}
{% raw %}ofthe usual antechamber (PL 1) open{% endraw %}
{% raw %}to the N., a long passage (PL 2), and{% endraw %}
{% raw %}a large chapel (PL 3) with a small{% endraw %}
{% raw %}chamber on each of its three sides.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}In this as in most ofthe better pre¬{% endraw %}
{% raw %}served tombs ofthe Queens the fresh¬{% endraw %}
{% raw %}ness of the colour is extraordinary.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}On each side of the entrance to the passage kneels Maat, the goddess{% endraw %}
{% raw %}of truth, protecting those who enter wilh her wings. On the left wall{% endraw %}
