{% raw %}The Facade of the Right Wing (PL 6) shows the king smiting his{% endraw %}

{% raw %}presenting the peoples overcome by Eamses III. (Kheta, Emor,{% endraw %}

{% raw %}Facade of the Left Wing (PL c) is a corresponding picture of Eamses{% endraw %}
{% raw %}smiting his foes before Ammon-Ee; below are Nubians (negroes){% endraw %}
{% raw %}and Libyans.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}In the Court between the towers are seated figures of the lion-{% endraw %}
{% raw %}headed goddess Sekhmet, in black granite. On the walls, between{% endraw %}
{% raw %}the first and second stories, are a number of curious consoles or{% endraw %}
{% raw %}brackets, which are adorned with the busts of four captive enemies,{% endraw %}
{% raw %}and formerly bore statues of the king. The reliefs on the walls are{% endraw %}
{% raw %}mostly sacrificial in character.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Right (N.) Wall of the Court. The representations from right to left{% endraw %}
{% raw %}are: 1. Ramses sacrificing to Set (defaced) and to Newt; below is the king{% endraw %}
{% raw %}presenting two rows of captives to Ammon. 2. The king offering wine to{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Atum and a goddess; below, the same offering flowers to Enhor and to a{% endraw %}
{% raw %}goddess. 3. Mont (defaced) and Atum present the king to Ammon. —{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Left (S.) Wall of the Court, 4. The king presents an image of Ammon{% endraw %}
{% raw %}to Harmachis and Maat; below, he leads two rows of captives before Am¬{% endraw %}
{% raw %}mon (the Libyans in the lower row, drawn full-face, should be noticed).{% endraw %}
{% raw %}5. The king offers incense to the Moon-god and to Sefkhet-ehui; below,{% endraw %}
{% raw %}he presents an image of Maat to Ptah and Sefkhet. In the second story is{% endraw %}
{% raw %}a window with attractive ceiling-decorations. 6. The king before Ammon,{% endraw %}
{% raw %}with Mut and Thout behind him.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}In the Gateway (PL a) leading to the fore-court the king appears{% endraw %}
{% raw %}leading two rows of fettered captives before Ammon (on the left),{% endraw %}
{% raw %}and smiting a band of enemies (on the right).{% endraw %}
{% raw %}A staircase in the S. tower ascends to the two Apartments in{% endraw %}
{% raw %}the upper story of the middle structure, the floor separating which{% endraw %}
{% raw %}has disappeared. The wall-reliefs of the lower room have vanished,{% endraw %}
{% raw %}but those of the upper room (harem scenes) are still quite distinct.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}The window commands a fine view of the temples and ruined village{% endraw %}
{% raw %}on the W. and of the plain to the E. Similar reliefs adorn the walls{% endraw %}
{% raw %}of the other (inaccessible) rooms.{% endraw %}
