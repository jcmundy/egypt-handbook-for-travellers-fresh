{% raw %}The inner side of the central edifice, through which we pass{% endraw %}
{% raw %}by the Gateway a, bears reliefs showing the king in various posi¬{% endraw %}
{% raw %}tions as the conqueror of his enemies.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}We now enter the Outer Court, 85 yds. long, between the pavilion{% endraw %}
{% raw %}and the main temple. In the middle stands a small Gateway (PL B),{% endraw %}
{% raw %}erected by Nektanebos. To the right lies the Temple of the 18th{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Dyn. (see p. 303). To the left is a small Temple op Amenertai's,{% endraw %}
{% raw %}the mother-in-law of Psammetikh I. (comp. p. lxxxv).{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Through a large Portal we enter a Fore - Court and thence pass to{% endraw %}
{% raw %}the vaulted Sanctuary, which was completely surrounded by a corridor.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}On the Left Wing of the portal we see Amenertai's sacrificing to Ammon{% endraw %}
{% raw %}(above), and standing holding two sistra before Ammon and Mut (below).{% endraw %}
{% raw %}On the Right Wing Amenertai's sacrifices to Ammon (above), and to Am¬{% endraw %}
{% raw %}mon and a goddess (below). The temple is adjoined on the right, by three{% endraw %}
{% raw %}chapels, dedicated to Shepenwepet, Nitocris (daughter and granddaughter{% endraw %}
{% raw %}of Amenertai's), and Meht-wesekhet (wife of Psammetikh I.).{% endraw %}
{% raw %}The *Main Temple of Ramses III. was built on exactly the{% endraw %}
{% raw %}same plan as the Eamesseum and was dedicated like it to Ammon.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}The large First Pylon is covered with representations and in¬{% endraw %}
{% raw %}scriptions. On the Right Tower (PL C), to the right, the king is{% endraw %}
{% raw %}shown before Ammon-Be-Harmachis, grasping a band of enemies{% endraw %}
{% raw %}by the hair and smiting them with his club. The hawk-headed god{% endraw %}
{% raw %}hands him the curved sword and leads to him by a cord the captured{% endraw %}
{% raw %}lands, which are represented in the accustomed manner (p. 251){% endraw %}
{% raw %}by circular walls enclosing their names and surmounted by bound{% endraw %}
{% raw %}enemies. Beneath are two other rows of representations of con¬{% endraw %}
{% raw %}quered lands. Farther to the left, between the grooves for the flag-{% endraw %}
{% raw %}staffs, is a similar but much smaller scene, representing the king{% endraw %}
{% raw %}smiting two foes in presence of Ammon ; and beneath is a long in¬{% endraw %}
{% raw %}scription, describing in poetic but exceedingly exaggerated language{% endraw %}
{% raw %}the victory won by Eamses III. over the Libyans in the 11th year{% endraw %}
{% raw %}of his reign. At the foot Ammon is seated to the left, with Ptah{% endraw %}
{% raw %}standing behind, inscribing the king's name on a palm-leaf. The{% endraw %}
{% raw %}king kneels before Ammon, under the sacred tree, and -receives{% endraw %}
{% raw %}from the god the hieroglyphs for 'jubilee ofthe reign', suspended{% endraw %}
{% raw %}on a palm-branch, as a symbol of long life. Thout writes the king's{% endraw %}
{% raw %}name on the leaves of the tree, and beside him stands the goddess{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Sefkhet-ebui. To the right of the portal, below, is a stele of the{% endraw %}
{% raw %}12th year of the king (imitated from a stele of the 35th year of{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Eamses II. at Abu-Simbel, p. 372), containing a dialogue between{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Ptah and the king. The Left Tower (PL D) repeats these scenes{% endraw %}
{% raw %}and inscriptions.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Through the Central Portal (PL d), embellished within and{% endraw %}
{% raw %}without by representations of the king worshipping the gods, we{% endraw %}
{% raw %}enter the First Court, forming an approximate square of 115 ft.{% endraw %}
