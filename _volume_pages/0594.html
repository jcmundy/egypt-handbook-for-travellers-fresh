{% raw %}The following chambers are all very dilapidated, and only the{% endraw %}
{% raw %}lower parts of the walls and columns remain.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}We first enter the Great Hypostyle Hall. The roof was formerly{% endraw %}
{% raw %}supported by 24 columns in 4 rows of six, of which the central row{% endraw %}
{% raw %}was considerably thicker than the others. On the walls are represen¬{% endraw %}
{% raw %}tations of the king in presence of various deities. An interesting{% endraw %}
{% raw %}relief on the S. wall shows the magnificent gold vases presented by{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Eamses III. to Ammon, Mut, and Khons.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Three Smaller Chambers (PL G,H,J) follow, two with four couples{% endraw %}
{% raw %}of columns, and one with four pillars. In Eoom H are two groups{% endraw %}
{% raw %}of red granite, one (left) representing Ammon and Maat, the other{% endraw %}
{% raw %}(right) the king and the ibis-headed Thout. — The other rooms at{% endraw %}
{% raw %}the back were dedicated to different deities. From Eoom G a stair¬{% endraw %}
{% raw %}case ascended to those on the upper floor.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}The great hypostyle hall is adjoined also at the sides (N. and S.){% endraw %}
{% raw %}by two series of rooms (PL 1-11), of which those to the left formed{% endraw %}
{% raw %}the Treasury of the temple.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}The Representations on the walls of these treasure-chambers refer to{% endraw %}
{% raw %}the costly objects stored within them. Room 1 (which was also access¬{% endraw %}
{% raw %}ible from the W. terrace of the second court): The king presents Ammon{% endraw %}
{% raw %}with papyrus-holders in the form of lions with the king's head or kneeling{% endraw %}
{% raw %}figures of the king. Room 2.- The king presents to Ammon costly vessels,{% endraw %}
{% raw %}with lids in the shape of the heads of rams, hawks, or kings, and table-{% endraw %}
{% raw %}equipages shaped like recumbent rams or sphinxes. Room 3: The king{% endraw %}
{% raw %}presents Ammon with sacks of precious stones. Room 4: The king offers{% endraw %}
{% raw %}costly table-services, ornaments, golden harps, silver, lead, etc. Room 5:{% endraw %}
