{% raw %}In the N.E. angle of the great girdle-wall, near the small temple, lies{% endraw %}
{% raw %}the Sacred Lake, a basin about 60 ft. square, with two flights of steps. —{% endraw %}
{% raw %}About 45 yds. to the N.W. of it is a Nilometer. A doorway, bearing the{% endraw %}
{% raw %}name of Nektanebos, admits to a chamber beyond which is a corridor,{% endraw %}
{% raw %}whence the staircase of the Nilometer descends to a depth of 65 ft.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}About 200 paces to the S. of the pavilion of Ramses III. (p. 297) is{% endraw %}
{% raw %}a small unfinished Ptolemaic Temple, now known as Kasr el-'AgHz, erected{% endraw %}
{% raw %}by Euergetes II. to the god Thout. It consists of a wide vestibule and three{% endraw %}
{% raw %}rooms, one behind another. On the left entrance-wall of the second room{% endraw %}
{% raw %}we see the king sacrificing to Thout, Imhotep, and the deified sage Amen¬{% endraw %}
{% raw %}hotep ; the lower row of reliefs alone is finished, the others are merely{% endraw %}
{% raw %}sketched out. — Still farther to the S. stood a small Temple ofthe Roman{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Period, dedicated to Isis. It was erected by Hadrian and Antoninus Pius,{% endraw %}
{% raw %}while the ruined pylon bears the names of Vespasian, Domitian, and Otho,{% endraw %}
{% raw %}the last of exceedingly rare occurrence owing to the emperor's short reign{% endraw %}
{% raw %}(69 A.D.). The temple consists of a cella surrounded by apartments. A{% endraw %}
{% raw %}staircase leads to the roof from a room to the extreme left of the entrance.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Between these two small temples traces of a town may be made out.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}These include remains of a Palace of Amenophis HI., which in structure{% endraw %}
{% raw %}and embellishments resembled that of Amenophis IV. at Tell el-'Amarna{% endraw %}
{% raw %}(p. 204). The site, completely ruined and plundered, is known as Malkata.{% endraw %}
