{% raw %}306 Route 24. THEBES (W. BANK). 10. Colossi{% endraw %}
{% raw %}The Northern Colossus is the famous vocal statue of Memnon.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}To the left of the king stands his mother Metemwa, to the right his{% endraw %}
{% raw %}wife Teye; a third figure, between the legs, is destroyed. On each{% endraw %}
{% raw %}side of the seat two Nile-gods were represented in sunk relief,{% endraw %}
{% raw %}winding the representative plants of Egypt (papyrus and lily) round{% endraw %}
{% raw %}the hieroglyph for 'to unite', a symbol of the union of Upper and{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Lower Egypt.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Attention began to be directed to the Musical Phenomenon con¬{% endraw %}
{% raw %}nected with the colossus, about the beginning of the Eoman em¬{% endraw %}
{% raw %}pire, after it had been broken. When it became known that the N.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}colossus emitted a musical note at sunrise, a new myth was in¬{% endraw %}
{% raw %}vented to explain the fact. Memnon who had fallen at Troy ap¬{% endraw %}
{% raw %}peared as a stone image at Thebes and greeted his mother Eos{% endraw %}
{% raw %}with a sweet and plaintive note when she appeared at dawn. The{% endraw %}
{% raw %}goddess heard the sound and the morning-dews are the tears she{% endraw %}
{% raw %}shed upon her beloved child. Strabo, who is the first author to{% endraw %}
{% raw %}mention the phenomenon, expresses doubt as to its genuineness;{% endraw %}
{% raw %}but Pausanias and Juvenal (p. 328) accept it as a fact. By later{% endraw %}
{% raw %}observers the sound is compared to that of a stroke upon metal, or{% endraw %}
{% raw %}even of a trumpet-blast and of human voices singing. The sound{% endraw %}
{% raw %}was heard only at or soon after sunrise, though by no means in¬{% endraw %}
{% raw %}variably then, in which case the god was supposed to be angry.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}The phenomenon ceased altogether after the time of Septimius{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Severus, who caused the restoration of the upper portions, perhaps{% endraw %}
{% raw %}with a view to propitiate the angry god. The restoration was not{% endraw %}
{% raw %}very skilfully managed, with five courses of sandstone blocks.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Letronne has proved that the resonance of the stone is on no account{% endraw %}
{% raw %}to be explained as a mere priestly trick, and in the opinion of{% endraw %}
{% raw %}eminent physicists it is perfectly possible that a hard resonant{% endraw %}
{% raw %}stone, heated by the warm sunlight suddenly following upon the{% endraw %}
{% raw %}cold nights in Egypt, might emit a sound, caused by the splitting{% endraw %}
{% raw %}off of minute particles from the surface. A similar phenomenon{% endraw %}
{% raw %}has been observed elsewhere.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}The numerous Greek and Latin Inscriptions, in prose and verse, in¬{% endraw %}
{% raw %}scribed upon the legs of the figure by travellers under the Roman empire,{% endraw %}
{% raw %}are peculiarly interesting. These are more numerous on the left than on{% endraw %}
{% raw %}the right leg, and none are beyond the reach of a man standing at the{% endraw %}
{% raw %}foot of the statue. The earliest were carved in the reign of Nero, the{% endraw %}
{% raw %}latest in those of Septimius Severus and Caracalla, and the most numer¬{% endraw %}
{% raw %}ous (27) in that of Hadrian. Only one Egyptian (who is responsible for{% endraw %}
{% raw %}a short demotic inscription) is found among these scribblers. The in¬{% endraw %}
{% raw %}scriptions were for the most part the work of men of some eminence, in¬{% endraw %}
{% raw %}cluding 8 governors of Egypt, 3 epistrateges of the Thebaid, 2 procurators,{% endraw %}
{% raw %}etc. Many, though not all, are dated. Nearly all of them afford proof that{% endraw %}
{% raw %}only the N. colossus emitted the famous sound. The oldest inscription{% endraw %}
{% raw %}dates from the 11th year of Nero's reign. — Many of the great officials who{% endraw %}
{% raw %}visited the marvels of Thebes were accompanied by their wives. The{% endraw %}
{% raw %}colossus was frequently dumb, in which case the visitor usually waited{% endraw %}
{% raw %}until a more favourable occasion. Many were so struck with the pheno¬{% endraw %}
{% raw %}menon that they were not content till they had heard it three or four times.{% endraw %}
