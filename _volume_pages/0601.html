{% raw %}On the W. bank, opposite a large island, rises (I872 M.) a ridge{% endraw %}
{% raw %}with two summits, known as Gebelen, i.e. the 'two mountains', on{% endraw %}
{% raw %}the higher of which is the tomb of a Shekh Musa, beside the ruins{% endraw %}
{% raw %}of a temple of the Middle Empire. At the village of Gebelen, at{% endraw %}
{% raw %}the W. base of the hill, lie the ruins of the ancient Crocodilopolis.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Extensive excavations carried on at this spot by Maspero yielded{% endraw %}
{% raw %}sarcophagi of the Middle Empire and also numerous domestic articles, etc.,{% endraw %}
{% raw %}appropriate to persons of comparative poverty. In the vicinity are graves{% endraw %}
{% raw %}of crocodiles. In the immediate neighbourhood also once stood the ancient{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Aphroditespolis, also called Pathyris (House of Hathor) from the Egyptian{% endraw %}
{% raw %}name of Hathor, the goddess of the town. For some time this town was{% endraw %}
{% raw %}the capital of a separate nome.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Beside the village of Dahabiyeh (Debabieh), on the E. bank, are{% endraw %}
{% raw %}quarries with interesting inscription's.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}20 M. (E. bank) Ma'alla, near which are tombs of the New Em¬{% endraw %}
{% raw %}pire. Farther on we pass Asfun el-Matd'na (W. bank; rail, station),{% endraw %}
{% raw %}with large sugar-factories, the ancient Asphynis (Egypt. Hesfen).{% endraw %}
{% raw %}The river-banks are picturesquely clad with extensive groves of{% endraw %}
{% raw %}palms, but the smoking chimneys of the sugar-factories interfere{% endraw %}
{% raw %}with the beauty of the scenery.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}36 M. Esneh (W. bank; rail, station, p. 307), with the adjoining{% endraw %}
{% raw %}town of Enyt, was even in antiquity one of the most important places{% endraw %}
{% raw %}in Upper Egypt. Its Egyptian name was Te-snet, whence came{% endraw %}
{% raw %}the Coptic Sne and the Arabic Esneh. The Greeks called it Lato-{% endraw %}
{% raw %}polis, after the latos, a kind of fish venerated here. Esneh is now a{% endraw %}
{% raw %}district capital with 15,000 inhab. (mostly Copts), and has post and{% endraw %}
{% raw %}telegraph offices, a druggist's shop, and numerous coffee-houses.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}The small Coptic Church is of no special interest. The old quay{% endraw %}
{% raw %}on the bank of the Nile, near the Mudlrlyeh, also contains some{% endraw %}
{% raw %}fragmentary inscriptions of the Eoman imperial epoch. To the N.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}of the town lies a small Palace of the Khedive (Kasr Efendlna), now{% endraw %}
{% raw %}used as a government-school. In the vicinity is a government-{% endraw %}
{% raw %}hospital. — About 3/4 M. from the steamer-quay, and still to a large{% endraw %}
{% raw %}extent buried in debris, is the chief object of interest, viz. the —{% endraw %}
{% raw %}*Tempib op Khnum, the ram-headed local deity, associated with{% endraw %}
{% raw %}whom were Neith, identified by the Greeks with Athena, and Satet.{% endraw %}
