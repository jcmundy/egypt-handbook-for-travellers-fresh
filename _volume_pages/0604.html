{% raw %}At this little temple we turn, direct our steps towards the Nile,{% endraw %}
{% raw %}and in Y4 hr. reach the Bock Temple, on the right side of the val¬{% endraw %}
{% raw %}ley, recognizable from a distance by the Stair leading up to it. The{% endraw %}
{% raw %}latter consists of 41 steps hewn in the rock, with a massive bal¬{% endraw %}
{% raw %}ustrade on each side. The temple was constructed under Ptolemy IX.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Euergetes II. (Physkon), and provided with reliefs and inscriptions{% endraw %}
{% raw %}by him and by Ptolemy X. Soter II.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}On reaching the platform at the top of the stair, we pass through a{% endraw %}
{% raw %}doorway to a Vestibule, not quite 33 ft. wide, which was supported by{% endraw %}
{% raw %}columns with elaborate floral capitals, while the exterior sides were{% endraw %}
{% raw %}bounded by low walls built between columns. Thence another door, the{% endraw %}
{% raw %}right half of which is still standing, leads into a smaller Hall (only 19 ft.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}wide), which also was bounded by low walls between columns. The floor{% endraw %}
{% raw %}is covered with ruins. The door leading to the Rock Chamber opened at{% endraw %}
{% raw %}the back of this hall. This chamber, which was originally a tomb{% endraw %}
{% raw %}chamber dating from the New Empire, has a vaulted ceiling, with vultures{% endraw %}
{% raw %}hovering in the centre. At the top of the walls is a frieze made of the{% endraw %}
{% raw %}name of Ptolemy X. between heads of Hathor. Below are inscriptions{% endraw %}
{% raw %}and much injured representations of the king and queen before various{% endraw %}
{% raw %}deities. This sanctuary also was especially dedicated to Nekhbet.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}On an isolated hill, about 1M. from the steamboat landing-place,{% endraw %}
{% raw %}are a number of *Eock Tombs, placed side by side on the S. slope.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Most of these date from the end of the Middle Empire or from the{% endraw %}
{% raw %}beginning of the New Empire. There are 31 tombs in all, but only{% endraw %}
{% raw %}6 repay a visit.{% endraw %}
