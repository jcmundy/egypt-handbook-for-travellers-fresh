{% raw %}316 Route 27. EDFU. Temple{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Horus, before Hathor and Horus, 'Uniter of the two lands', and be¬{% endraw %}
{% raw %}fore other gods of Edfu. On the front of the pylon-towers are four{% endraw %}
{% raw %}wide incisions, two on each side of the central portal. These were{% endraw %}
{% raw %}intended to support large flag-staffs, which were farther secured{% endraw %}
{% raw %}by means of clamps fastened in the holes still to be seen in the{% endraw %}
{% raw %}masonry directly above. The small rectangular apertures in the{% endraw %}
{% raw %}towers served to admit light and air to the chambers and staircases{% endraw %}
{% raw %}within (see below).{% endraw %}
{% raw %}A passage, 5-6 ft. in height, leads through each pylon-tower to the{% endraw %}
{% raw %}outside of the stone girdle-wall, which is embellished with colossal reliefs{% endraw %}
{% raw %}(Ptolemy XI. before the gods of Edfu) and bears the inscriptions mentioned{% endraw %}
{% raw %}at p. 315. In front of the W. tower lie two colossal hawks in dark{% endraw %}
{% raw %}granite. One is quite shattered; the other has the figure of a priest in{% endraw %}
{% raw %}the costume of the 1st cent. B.C. before it, and bore a crown fastened in{% endraw %}
{% raw %}the square hole on the head.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}The Court, between the pylon and the great hypostyle hall, is{% endraw %}
{% raw %}a spacious enclosure, paved with broad flags, and surrounded on{% endraw %}
{% raw %}three sides by a covered colonnade of 32 columns. In this court rose{% endraw %}
{% raw %}the great altar upon which offerings were made to the gods of Edfu{% endraw %}
{% raw %}in presence of the assembled people. The columns supporting the{% endraw %}
{% raw %}roof of the colonnade are embellished with rich floral and palm{% endraw %}
{% raw %}capitals. The incised reliefs on the shafts show the king before{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Horus and the other gods of Edfu; the name of the king has not{% endraw %}
{% raw %}been inserted in the inscriptions. On the rear-walls of the colon¬{% endraw %}
{% raw %}nades are three rows of large reliefs, showing the Pharaoh (Ptolemy{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Soter II. or Ptolemy Alexander) in communion with the gods or{% endraw %}
{% raw %}triumphing over his foes in the guise of the god Horus. The same{% endraw %}
{% raw %}representations occur over and over again in wearisome repetition{% endraw %}
{% raw %}throughout the temple.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}On the S. wall (PI. a), to the left of the entrance: 1. The king, wear¬{% endraw %}
{% raw %}ing the crown of Lower Egypt, quits his palace in order to visit the{% endraw %}
{% raw %}temple; a priest offers incense in front of him. 2 Horus and Thout, at{% endraw %}
{% raw %}the entrance to the temple, pour the water of consecration upon the king.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}— These scenes are repeated to the right of the entrance (PI. b), where,{% endraw %}
{% raw %}however, the king wears the crown of Upper Egypt.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}The doors to the right and left of a and 6 lead to the staircases{% endraw %}
{% raw %}in the interior of the pylon (ascent, see p. 318). The E. and W.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}exits (PL c) are built up.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}The Back of this court is formed by the front of the great hypo¬{% endraw %}
{% raw %}style hall, which is crowned by a concave cornice. On each side of{% endraw %}
{% raw %}the large portal are three balustrades between the columns. Upon{% endraw %}
{% raw %}these, facing the court, are reliefs representing EueTgetes II. with{% endraw %}
{% raw %}pendent arms or offering sacrifice to Hathor of Dendera (on the two{% endraw %}
{% raw %}central balustrades) or to the hawk-headed Horus of Edfu (on the{% endraw %}
{% raw %}others).{% endraw %}
