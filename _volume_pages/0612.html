{% raw %}pre-Ptolemaic temple (p. 315). The most interesting Reliefs are those{% endraw %}
{% raw %}in the lower row on the right (E.) wall. 1. The king (Philopator){% endraw %}
{% raw %}removes the lock from the shrine of Horus. 2. He opens the door of{% endraw %}
{% raw %}the shrine. 3. He stands in a reverential attitude, with pendent{% endraw %}
{% raw %}arms, before the god. 4. He offers incense to his deified parents,{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Euergetes I. and Berenice. 5. He offers incense before the sacred{% endraw %}
{% raw %}boat of Hathor.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}The Sanctuary is surrounded by a Coueidoe from which ten small{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Side Chambers (PI. i-x) open. These, used for various religious rites and{% endraw %}
{% raw %}for storerooms, are adorned with reliefs and are faintly lighted by holes{% endraw %}
{% raw %}in the roof. In each of the corner-rooms (PI. in and vui) is the entrance{% endraw %}
{% raw %}to a crypt, closed by a stone slab.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}We now return to the small hypostyle hall and pass through{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Eoom xix to the Inner Passage between the exterior of the temple{% endraw %}
{% raw %}proper and the interior of the girdle-wall. On the outside of the{% endraw %}
{% raw %}temple walls are lions' heads as water-spouts and four rows of reliefs{% endraw %}
{% raw %}and inscriptions. At the foot of the wall appear the king, the queen,{% endraw %}
{% raw %}and a procession of local deities approaching the three chief gods of{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Edfu. On the inner side of the E. girdle-wall (PI. hh) the king ap¬{% endraw %}
{% raw %}pears before the various gods of Edfu ; on the N. wall (PL gg) are{% endraw %}
{% raw %}similar scenes and long hymns to the gods of Edfu. On the W.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}wall (PL ff) are more important reliefs and inscriptions represent¬{% endraw %}
{% raw %}ing the contests of the god Horus with his enemies who are de¬{% endraw %}
{% raw %}picted as crocodiles and hippopotami.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}The chief are the following: 1st Scene (below, to the right). The king,{% endraw %}
{% raw %}standing on shore, attempts to transfix a hippopotamus, which bends its{% endraw %}
{% raw %}head aside. Horus, who is accompanied by his mother Isis, does the same;{% endraw %}
{% raw %}in his left hand he holds a chain, and in his right a javelin; beside him{% endraw %}
{% raw %}is his mother Isis, and behind, at the helm, is a small Horus. — 2nd Scene.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}The king appears on land, before two ships, in each of which are a Horus{% endraw %}
{% raw %}and an assistant with a boar's head. Horus holds the hippopotamus with{% endraw %}
{% raw %}a chain and pierces its head with a javelin. — 5th Scene. The hippopo¬{% endraw %}
{% raw %}tamus lies on its back, with a chain fastened to i's hind-feet. — 7th Scene{% endraw %}
{% raw %}(the finest of all). Horus, in a ship with expanded sail, aims a blow with{% endraw %}
{% raw %}his right hand at the head of a hippopotamus, the hind-foot of which is{% endraw %}
{% raw %}caught in a line held in the god's left hand. Isis kneeling in the bow of the{% endraw %}
{% raw %}boat holds the head of the animal by a cord. The king, standing on the{% endraw %}
{% raw %}bank with two attendants armed with javelin and knife, seeks to pierce{% endraw %}
{% raw %}the skull of the hippopotamus. — Farther to the left (opposite the pylon):{% endraw %}
{% raw %}The king, the ram-headed Khnum, the hawk-headed Horus, and the ibis-{% endraw %}
{% raw %}headed Thout are dragging a net, in which are not only birds, fishes, and{% endraw %}
{% raw %}a stag, etc., but also two Asiatics and negroes, the ancestral foes of Egypt.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}A subterranean staircase, curving to the E., leads from the E.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}part of the passage round the temple to an ancient Nilometer, out¬{% endraw %}
{% raw %}side the temple, also reached from without by a spiral staircase. On{% endraw %}
{% raw %}the walls of the latter is the scale, with demotic numbers. The sub¬{% endraw %}
{% raw %}terranean communication with the river has been interrupted.{% endraw %}
