{% raw %}Eoman emperors, more especially under Tiberius. The Ptolemaic{% endraw %}
{% raw %}reliefs are easily distinguished from those of the emperors, as they{% endraw %}

{% raw %}arrangements the temple of Ombos resembles other temples of the{% endraw %}
{% raw %}same period (at Dendera, Edfu, and Philai), but it differs from{% endraw %}
{% raw %}them in being dedicated to two deities instead of to one only. Each{% endraw %}
{% raw %}of these two deities had his own special worship and festivals, so{% endraw %}
{% raw %}the entire building is bisected longitudinally by an imaginary line,{% endraw %}
{% raw %}each half having its own gateways, doorways, and chapels. The S.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}or right half was dedicated to Sobk, the N. or left half to Haroeris.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}The Pylon, at the entrance to the temple-court, had two doors.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}The left half has completely vanished; while only the lower part of{% endraw %}
{% raw %}the central pillar (between the doors) and ofthe right wing remain.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}On the outer side (facing the river) of the right wing are the follow¬{% endraw %}
{% raw %}ing representations, from left to right: PL a. Sobk, Hathor, and Khons-{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Hor, the gods worshipped in the right half of the temple. PL b. Hiero¬{% endraw %}
{% raw %}glyphic text of 52 lines. PL c. Emp. Domitian, with the crown of Upper{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Egypt, accompanied by 14 gods and goddesses, offering gifts to the above{% endraw %}
{% raw %}mentioned deities.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}At the corner to the right (above PL c) the upper row of representations{% endraw %}
{% raw %}also has been preserved: six spirits (the last three with jackals' heads){% endraw %}
{% raw %}carrying a (defaced) shrine, followed by images of the gods bearing the{% endraw %}
{% raw %}symbols "¥" ('life') and ('happiness') on long poles; finally a large{% endraw %}
{% raw %}figure of the king, preceded by a priest offering incense and followed by{% endraw %}
{% raw %}his guardian spirit (with a king's head upon a sceptre).{% endraw %}
{% raw %}The Court, as at Edfu, was surrounded on three sides by colon¬{% endraw %}
{% raw %}nades. Only the lower portions of the 16 columns are now left.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}The reliefs on these, the colouring of which is in places admirably{% endraw %}
{% raw %}preserved, represent Tiberius sacrificing to the gods. On the inner{% endraw %}
{% raw %}side of the right wing of the pylon are two doors, one of which{% endraw %}
{% raw %}(PL d) admits to a small apartment, the other (PL e) leads to a{% endraw %}
{% raw %}staircase by which the roof of the pylon was gained. The square{% endraw %}
{% raw %}basement in the centre of the court was probably an altar; let into{% endraw %}
{% raw %}the ground beside it are two small granite troughs, into which{% endraw %}
{% raw %}drink-offerings may have been poured. In this, as in all the other{% endraw %}
{% raw %}rooms of the temple, the ancient pavement is in excellent preserva¬{% endraw %}
{% raw %}tion. The rear of the court is separated from the adjoining great{% endraw %}
{% raw %}hypostyle hall by screen-walls, in which are two large portals and{% endraw %}
{% raw %}two smaller doorways. On the screen-wall to the right (PL f) is a{% endraw %}
{% raw %}relief of the hawk-headed Horus and the ibis-headed Thout pouring{% endraw %}
{% raw %}the water of consecration upon King Neos Dionysos, to the left of{% endraw %}
{% raw %}whom is the crocodile-headed Sobk, the lord of the right half of the{% endraw %}
{% raw %}temple. On the left wall (PI. g) the same scene takes place before{% endraw %}
{% raw %}the hawk-headed Haroeris, to whom the left half of the temple is{% endraw %}
{% raw %}dedicated. The screen-walls are crowned with serpents, with sun{% endraw %}
{% raw %}disks on their heads.{% endraw %}
