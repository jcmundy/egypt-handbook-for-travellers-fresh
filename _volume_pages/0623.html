{% raw %}ASSUAN. 5,9. Route. 327{% endraw %}
{% raw %}As we approach Assuan (E. bank), the scene presented to us is{% endraw %}
{% raw %}one of great and peculiar beauty. On the hill to the right is the{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Kubbet el-hawa (p. 334) ; straight in front of us lies the N. extrem¬{% endraw %}
{% raw %}ity of the island of Elephantine (p. 330), with the Savoy Hotel.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Sandstone now gives place to masses of granite on the banks and{% endraw %}
{% raw %}in the channel of the stream.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}68 M. Assuan.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}29. Assuan and its Environs.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}The Tourist Steamers usually remain here 2 days. — Railway, see{% endraw %}
{% raw %}p. 30s; the station lies to the S. of the town.{% endraw %}

{% raw %}by special boat; ''Catakact Hotel, belonging to Messrs. Cook (manager,{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Herr Steiger; English housekeeper), situated opposite Elephantine, near{% endraw %}
{% raw %}the lowest cataract, with lawn-tennis courts; 'Assuan Hotel (belonging{% endraw %}

{% raw %}quay (with unpretending bedrooms).{% endraw %}

{% raw %}quay. — Cook's Office in the vicinity.{% endraw %}

{% raw %}Catholic Church, to the N. of the town.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}British Vice-Consul, Major W. H. Hunter. — Physician. Dr. Leigh Canney.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Nubian Articles are everywhere offered for sale: ostrich feathers and{% endraw %}
{% raw %}fans, silver rings and armlets, ivory hoops, weapons of the dervishes{% endraw %}
{% raw %}(mostly, however, manufactured, in Assuan), amulets, horns, basket-work,{% endraw %}
{% raw %}and aprons of leather fringe (the costume of the women of the Sudan,{% endraw %}
{% raw %}which they oddly call 'Madama Nubia'). Grey and black ostrich feathers{% endraw %}
{% raw %}are comparatively cheap (8 piastres = 2 fr. each), larger and perfect white{% endraw %}
{% raw %}feathers cost 10-20 fr. apiece and upward. Travellers, however, will find{% endraw %}
{% raw %}it more convenient to buy these in Cairo. — The Bazaar is distinguished{% endraw %}
{% raw %}for its excellent local pottery of great beauty of form.{% endraw %}

{% raw %}Assu&n, Greek Syene, Coptic Suan, with about 13,000 inhab.,{% endraw %}
{% raw %}lies on the E. bank, partly on the plain and partly on a hill, in N.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}lat. 24° 5' 30". The fertile strip here is narrow, but supports{% endraw %}
{% raw %}numerous date-palms, the fruit of which enjoys a high reputation.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Assuan is the capital of El-Hedud, the southernmost province of{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Upper Egypt, embracing the old province of Esneh and the district{% endraw %}
{% raw %}between the first two cataracts. It is the seat of the provincial au¬{% endraw %}
{% raw %}thorities and of a garrison. The once considerable trade in the pro¬{% endraw %}
{% raw %}ducts of the Sudan and Abyssinia has greatly fallen off since the{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Mahdist revolt. The Nile here divides into several arms, separated{% endraw %}
{% raw %}by granite rocks and islands, the largest of which is Elephantine{% endraw %}
