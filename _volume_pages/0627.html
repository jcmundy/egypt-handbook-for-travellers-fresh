{% raw %}Among the ruins of the ancient city to the E. of the railway-{% endraw %}
{% raw %}station lies a small and not very interesting Ptolemaic Temple,{% endraw %}
{% raw %}now buried in rubbish with the exception of the facade. The key{% endraw %}
{% raw %}is kept by an official of the Administration des Antiquite's. Built{% endraw %}
{% raw %}by Euergetes I. and Philopator, but never quite completed, the{% endraw %}
{% raw %}temple was dedicated to Isis of Syene.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}In the centre of the facade is the Mais Pobtal, crowned with a con¬{% endraw %}
{% raw %}cave cornice; it is now closed, the present entrance being by a gap in the{% endraw %}
{% raw %}wall to the left. Left Jamb: above, Euergetes presenting an image of Maat{% endraw %}
{% raw %}to Ammon; below, Euergetes offering milk to a goddess. Right Jamb:{% endraw %}
{% raw %}above, Euergetes before Min-Ammon; below, Euergetes sacrificing to{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Hathor. Lintel: to the right, Euergetes sacrificing to Osiris, Isis, and{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Harpocrates, and Euergetes before the crocodile-headed Sobk of Syene and{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Hathor; to the left, Euergetes presenting an image of Maat to the cataract-{% endraw %}
{% raw %}gods Khnum, .Satis, and Anukis, and Euergetes and his wife Berenice{% endraw %}
{% raw %}before Isis. — The Intekiok consists of a hall with two pillars and three{% endraw %}
{% raw %}chapels. The two side-chapels are filled up; the reliefs in the central{% endraw %}
{% raw %}chapel show Euergetes (once accompanied by Berenice) before the various{% endraw %}
{% raw %}deities of Syene.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Near the temple is a rock-inscription of the reign of Amenophis IV.:{% endraw %}

{% raw %}A ride (donkey there and back 5 pias.) may be taken to the Camp{% endraw %}
{% raw %}of the Bisharin, situated within an ancient Arab cemetery, 3/4 M.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}to the E. of the town. These Arabs with their families live in{% endraw %}
{% raw %}wretched tents covered with mats. Beduins of the 'Ababdeh tribe,{% endraw %}
{% raw %}differing both in type and dress from the Bisharin, also have tents-{% endraw %}
{% raw %}here. Both tribes speak a curious language, allied to Egyptian and{% endraw %}
{% raw %}other E. African dialects. They support themselves by cattle-rearing{% endraw %}
{% raw %}and by trading, especially with senna-leaves, which they collect in{% endraw %}
{% raw %}the desert and sell at good prices. The exhibitions and dances ('fan¬{% endraw %}
{% raw %}tasia') which they perform are simply invented to entertain the{% endraw %}
{% raw %}tourist and have nothing national or characteristic about them.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}The handsome Bisharin children, with thick curly hair, hawk chains{% endraw %}
{% raw %}and other small articles about the town. — A fine *View is com¬{% endraw %}
{% raw %}manded by the Tomb of Shekh Harun, above the camp, to the right.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}We may return via the ancient Arab Cemeteries, which are{% endraw %}
{% raw %}situated in the desert to the S. of the town. Each grave is marked{% endraw %}
{% raw %}by a rectangle of unhewn stones and a slab bearing an inscription.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}The tombs of the richer dead are small domed erections.{% endraw %}
