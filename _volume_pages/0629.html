{% raw %}Elephantine. ASSUAN. 29. Route. 331{% endraw %}
{% raw %}By far the most interesting object on the island is the *Nilo-{% endraw %}
{% raw %}meter, beside a sakiyeh on the E. side facing Assuan, known to{% endraw %}
{% raw %}the Arabs as Mikyds. After more than a thousand years of neglect{% endraw %}
{% raw %}it was repaired and restored to use in 1870 in the reign of the{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Khedive Isma'il, as is recorded by French and Arabic inscriptions.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}At the top of the staircase is an inscribed block of granite. On the{% endraw %}
{% raw %}walls of the staircase are damaged Greek inscriptions of the im¬{% endraw %}
{% raw %}perial epoch, giving the water-levels. The scales date from the{% endraw %}
{% raw %}later imperial epoch, and the ells are marked in Greek (and also in{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Demotic) characters. The new scale is inscribed on marble tablets.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}— Strabo's accurate description of this nilometer is not yet anti¬{% endraw %}
{% raw %}quated: —{% endraw %}
{% raw %}'The Nilometer is a well built of regular hewn stones, on the bank{% endraw %}
{% raw %}of the Nile, in which is recorded the rise of the stream, not only the{% endraw %}
{% raw %}maximum but also the minimum and average rise, for the water in the{% endraw %}
{% raw %}well rises and falls with the stream. On the side of the well are marks,{% endraw %}
{% raw %}measuring the height sufficient for the irrigation and the other water-{% endraw %}
{% raw %}levels. These are observed and published for general information. . . .{% endraw %}
{% raw %}This is of importance to the peasants for the management of the water,{% endraw %}
{% raw %}the embankments, the canals, etc., and to the officials on account of the{% endraw %}
{% raw %}taxes. For the higher the rise of water, the higher are the taxes'.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Farther to the S., opposite the Cataract Hotel, lies a massive{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Ancient Structure. Many of the blocks used were taken from earlier{% endraw %}
{% raw %}edifices and are covered with inscriptions of different dates. The{% endraw %}
{% raw %}rock-inscriptions close to the stream should also be noticed.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}The entire S. part of the island is covered with great mounds{% endraw %}
{% raw %}of rubbish formed by the ruins of the Ancient Town of Elephantine.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Among some brick walls a few paces to the W. of the quay we may{% endraw %}
{% raw %}distinguish the foundations of a small temple, constructed with{% endraw %}
{% raw %}stones brought from earlier edifices, including the drums of columns{% endraw %}
{% raw %}and blocks with the cartouches of Thutmosis III., Eamses III., and{% endraw %}
{% raw %}other monarchs. From the solitary stump of a column now stand¬{% endraw %}
{% raw %}ing we learn that Trajan was the builder. Close by is the top of a{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Greek altar in red granite, with the inscription, 'To the great god{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Amnion'. About 50 paces to the E. a granite portal, once the entrance{% endraw %}
{% raw %}to some large temple, dominates all this part of the island. The{% endraw %}
{% raw %}reliefs upon it show Alexander, the son of Alexander the Great{% endraw %}
{% raw %}(comp. p. lxxxvii), sacrificing to the ram-headed Khnum and other{% endraw %}
{% raw %}deities of Elephantine. Inscribed blocks and sculptured fragments{% endraw %}
{% raw %}lie around in all directions. Various other buildings, seen and de¬{% endraw %}
{% raw %}scribed by the savants of the French Expedition, were pulled down{% endraw %}
{% raw %}about the beginning ofthe 19th century. Among these were temples{% endraw %}
{% raw %}built by Amenophis III. and Thutmosis III.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}The higher parts of the island, especially a granite promontory{% endraw %}
{% raw %}on the W. bank, command a fine *View of the black and brown,{% endraw %}
{% raw %}rough and smooth rocks ofthe cataract, among which the Nile flows.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}A row round the island is recommended (i/a hr.). — The islet of{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Gadinarti or Sirdar's Island, between Elephantine and the W. bank of{% endraw %}
{% raw %}the Nile, belongs to Lord Kitchener.{% endraw %}
