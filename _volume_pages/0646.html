{% raw %}The rocky island of Bigeh (Egypt. Senmei), the goddess of which{% endraw %}
{% raw %}was Hathor, is the most interesting of the Cataract Islands near{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Philse. It is easily reached by small boat in a few minutes from{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Philse, of which it commands a picturesque view. We land at{% endraw %}
{% raw %}the ancient quay and ascend by an ancient staircase. In front of us{% endraw %}
{% raw %}lie the remains of a Ptolemaic temple (Neos Dionysos), of which{% endraw %}
{% raw %}part of the hypostyle hall is still standing, with its plant-columns{% endraw %}
{% raw %}united by screen-walls. On the E. side is a door, into which an apse{% endraw %}
{% raw %}has been built. The ruins are now occupied by a Nubian hamlet.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Behind the temple (to the W.) is a headless seated statue of Am¬{% endraw %}
{% raw %}enophis II., and beside the temple is the fragment of a seated figure{% endraw %}
{% raw %}of Thutmosis HI. There are numerous inscriptions on the island.{% endraw %}

{% raw %}rock-inscriptions, some dating as far back as the Middle Empire.{% endraw %}
