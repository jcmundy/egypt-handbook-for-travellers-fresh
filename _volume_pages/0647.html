{% raw %}On the second day we advance steadily, ascending almost imper¬{% endraw %}
{% raw %}ceptibly, through a monotonous plain, intersected in all directions{% endraw %}
{% raw %}by small undulating heights. All around us extends the inter¬{% endraw %}
{% raw %}minable yellowish-gray, sun-bleached rocks of the desert; not a{% endraw %}
{% raw %}trace of organic life is visible, not a single green tree or shrub. At{% endraw %}
{% raw %}the hill of el-Karn ('the horn'), which rises to the left of the cara¬{% endraw %}
{% raw %}van-route, about midway between Bir 'Ambar and Laketa, the road{% endraw %}
{% raw %}from Keneh is joined by those from Kuft and Luxor. The only{% endraw %}
{% raw %}variety is afforded by an occasional Mabwala or Mahatta. The Mab-{% endraw %}
{% raw %}walas are simply spaces covered with camel's dung. They occur on{% endraw %}
{% raw %}every great caravan route at regular intervals and are of the utmost{% endraw %}
{% raw %}importance as sign-posts showing the road. The Mahattas or halt¬{% endraw %}
{% raw %}ing-places are 7*/2-9 M. apart and serve also as measures of distance.{% endraw %}
