{% raw %}LOWEE NUBIA.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Ethiopian Pharaohs were forced to give way before the Assyrians,{% endraw %}
{% raw %}and their kingdom was restricted to Nubia, of which the N. border{% endraw %}
{% raw %}then lay near Philae (Konosso, p. 344).{% endraw %}
{% raw %}The Egyptian civilization gradually declined in Nubia. The{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Egyptian hieroglyphic writing became corrupted and a native Ethio¬{% endraw %}
{% raw %}pian hieroglyphic and cursive character was developed (p. cix),{% endraw %}
{% raw %}which, about the beginning of the Christian era, began to be used{% endraw %}
{% raw %}for writing the native Nubian language even in official documents.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}About 600 B.C. the royal residence was transferred from Napata to{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Mero'i (p. 384), which lay farther to the S. Comparatively full{% endraw %}
{% raw %}information has come down to us concerning the earlier kings who{% endraw %}
{% raw %}dwelt at Meroe. We know also of the unsuccessful campaign ofthe{% endraw %}
{% raw %}army of Psammetikh I. against Lower Nubia (about 590 B.C.) and{% endraw %}
{% raw %}of the attempts of Cambyses (525 B.C.) to conquer Ethiopia, which{% endraw %}
{% raw %}were foiled mainly by the energetic Ethiopian King Nastesen.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}After that an almost impenetrable veil falls over the history of the{% endraw %}
{% raw %}country.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}During the Ptolemaic and Eoman Impebial Epochs the S.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}boundary of Egypt was near Hierasykaminos (p. 363), and it was{% endraw %}
{% raw %}occasionally pushed farther S. as far as Primls (p. 368), On the{% endraw %}
{% raw %}other hand the Nubians sometimes succeeded in extending their{% endraw %}
{% raw %}power as far N. as Philae, and perhaps even occupied part of Upper{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Egypt. When the Eomans first came to Egypt Ethiopia was gov¬{% endraw %}
{% raw %}erned by a succession of queens named Candace. One of these{% endraw %}
{% raw %}attacked the Eoman province, but was repulsed by the Eoman gov¬{% endraw %}
{% raw %}ernor Petronius in 23 B.C. The Ethiopian kingdom seems soon{% endraw %}
{% raw %}afterwards to have fallen into disorder and to have split up into a{% endraw %}
{% raw %}number of small principalities. About this time also, the Blem¬{% endraw %}
{% raw %}myes, a nomadic race of the E. desert, who had previously acknow¬{% endraw %}
{% raw %}ledged the suzerainty of Ethiopia, assumed an aggressive attitude.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Not content with harassing the N. parts of Upper Nubia, they also{% endraw %}
{% raw %}carried their depredations into the Eoman territory in S. Egypt,{% endraw %}
{% raw %}until finally the Eomans gave way before them. Diocletian (about{% endraw %}
{% raw %}300 A.D.) withdrew from Nubia altogether, retiring within the{% endraw %}
{% raw %}bounds of Egypt proper, to the N. of Philae. The Blemmyes, how¬{% endraw %}
{% raw %}ever, in alliance with the Nubians, continued their attacks on Upper{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Egypt; but in 451 A.D. they were defeated by Marcianus, who con¬{% endraw %}
{% raw %}cluded a peace with them (p. 337).{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Christianity established itself at Philae in the 4th cent., and{% endraw %}
{% raw %}thence extended throughout Nubia, where the temples were con¬{% endraw %}
{% raw %}verted into churches. In 640 A.D. Egypt and the Upper Nile Valley{% endraw %}
{% raw %}fell into the hands ofthe Mohammedans. 'Amr (p. xciii) penetrated{% endraw %}
{% raw %}as far as Dongola and imposed tribute upon Nubia, but no enduring{% endraw %}
{% raw %}subjugation of the country was effected. Shams ed-D61a, brother{% endraw %}
{% raw %}of Saladin, took possession of the fortress of Ibrim (p. 368) in{% endraw %}
{% raw %}1173 and plundered the church-treasury; but Christianity yielded{% endraw %}
{% raw %}to Islam very gradually, and a Christian kingdom lingered at Soba{% endraw %}
