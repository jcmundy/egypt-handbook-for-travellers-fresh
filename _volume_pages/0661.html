{% raw %}The following Three Booms (PL D, E, F) have well-preserved{% endraw %}
{% raw %}reliefs, with vivid colouring, depicting the emperor in presence of{% endraw %}
{% raw %}the gods of Talmis and other deities. Stumps of the two columns{% endraw %}
{% raw %}that bore the roof of Eoom E still remain. Small chambers have{% endraw %}
{% raw %}been constructed in the S. walls of Eooms D and E. From Eoom D{% endraw %}
{% raw %}we reach the Staircase, which ascends in the thickness of the wall{% endraw %}
{% raw %}to the roof of Eoom F. Thence the higher roofs of the front portions{% endraw %}
{% raw %}of the temple are easily reached by steps. Another staircase leads{% endraw %}
{% raw %}from the roof of Eoom E to the top of the external wall. Descending{% endraw %}
{% raw %}a few steps to the left, we reach a Chapel formed in the thickness of{% endraw %}
{% raw %}the wall. This comprizes two rooms (with a crypt in the second){% endraw %}
{% raw %}and was probably intended for the cult of Osiris.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}The Inner Passage (PI. G) round the temple is entered by doors{% endraw %}
{% raw %}in the court and vestibule. The unfinished lions' heads on the ex¬{% endraw %}
{% raw %}terior of the temple proper are water-spouts. On the rear-wall is{% endraw %}
{% raw %}a large relief of the emperor before the gods; below are smaller{% endraw %}
{% raw %}reliefs. The two representations of Mandulis, on the girdle-wall{% endraw %}
{% raw %}opposite these reliefs, were probably originally protected by a small{% endraw %}
{% raw %}wooden chapel. At PL a, on the S. side, is a dilapidated Nilometer.{% endraw %}

{% raw %}an uncovered court enclosed by columns and screen-walls, and a{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Rock Chamber (PL L), of which only the door has reliefs (the{% endraw %}
{% raw %}emperor before the gods). — Another well-preserved Chapel (PL K),{% endraw %}
{% raw %}in the N.E. angle, probably dates from the Ptolemaic temple.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Outside the temple are Gateways and ruins of a massive wall{% endraw %}
{% raw %}of hewn stones. On the W. hill-slope are Quarries, while the{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Remains of the Ancient City, the houses and girdle-wall of which{% endraw %}
{% raw %}are easily traceable, stretch down into the plain. In the latter are{% endraw %}
{% raw %}towers of rough stone.{% endraw %}
