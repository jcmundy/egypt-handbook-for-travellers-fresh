{% raw %}DENDUE. 32. Route. 359{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Abu Tarfa (W. bank). About 1 M. to the S. is a rock-cave with{% endraw %}
{% raw %}the tomb of a shekh. The low rapids here, caused by granite rocks{% endraw %}
{% raw %}in the Nile, are known as the Bab Abu Hor.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}12'/2 M. Murwau (on both banks). At the village of Kuboshdb, on{% endraw %}
{% raw %}the W. bank, are ancient quay-walls with a Nilometer and an un¬{% endraw %}
{% raw %}finished little cave-chapel. A little to the N. is a curious irriga¬{% endraw %}
{% raw %}tion arrangement hewn in the rock.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}On the W. bank, opposite the village of Dendur (E. bank), ap¬{% endraw %}
{% raw %}pears the small —{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Temple of Dendur, built by Augustus, and dedicated to various{% endraw %}
{% raw %}gods, including Pete-ese and Pe~-H6r, the sons of Kuper, two local{% endraw %}
{% raw %}deified heroes. The temple stands upon a platform, immediately at{% endraw %}
{% raw %}the foot of the mountains, and is preceded by a broad terrace. Ofthe{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Pylon, which formed the entrance to the temple, only the stone door¬{% endraw %}
{% raw %}way remains; the towers, probably built of brick, have disappeared.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}At the top of the portal is the winged sun-disk, and within and{% endraw %}
{% raw %}on the front (E.) and back (W.) is the Pharaoh sacrificing to various{% endraw %}
{% raw %}gods. Beyond the portal we enter an open Court, which was orig¬{% endraw %}
{% raw %}inally enclosed by brick walls. The facade of the Temple is adorned{% endraw %}
{% raw %}with two columns with rich floral capitals, connected with the side-{% endraw %}
{% raw %}walls by screen-walls. The temple comprizes a Vestibule and two{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Smaller Chambers. The former is embellished with reliefs of the{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Pharaoh in presence of the gods, which in the Christian period,{% endraw %}
{% raw %}when the hall was used as a church, were covered with Stucco, bear¬{% endraw %}
{% raw %}ing pictures of saints. The smaller rooms have no decoration. In{% endraw %}
{% raw %}the rear-wall of the innermost is a shallow Recess, with representa¬{% endraw %}
{% raw %}tion of the local saints Pete-ese and Pe-H6r praying to Isis and Osiris.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}In the top of this wall also is a deep crypt, which could be closed{% endraw %}
{% raw %}by a large and a small stone. The N. and S. Exterior Walls of the{% endraw %}
{% raw %}temple are adorned with interesting reliefs. On the N. wall, to the{% endraw %}
{% raw %}left of the door from the vestibule, appears the Pharaoh ottering a{% endraw %}
{% raw %}piece of cloth to Pe-H6r, on the S. wall, below, to the left of the{% endraw %}
{% raw %}door, he sacrifices to Pete-ese, who is accompanied by a woman,{% endraw %}
{% raw %}probably his wife.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Among the various Inscriptions placed on the walls by visitors to the{% endraw %}
{% raw %}temple, is one in Coptic, in which a certain Presbyter Abraham records{% endraw %}
{% raw %}that he erected a cross here in the church at the command of the Nubian{% endraw %}
{% raw %}King Eispanome. This appears within the S. side-door of the vestibule.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Above the temple is a small Rock Chapel, with a door showing the{% endraw %}
{% raw %}hollow cornice. In the cliffs to the N. of the temple are some Quarries.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}The stones lying in front of these are, perhaps, remains of ancient houses.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Beyond (18'/2 M.) Merlyeh, a commune on both banks, the banks{% endraw %}
{% raw %}of the Nile become flatter and are partly cultivated.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}23 M. Girsheh (E. bank), situated in a wide belt of cultivated{% endraw %}
{% raw %}land, lies besides the ruins of the ancient Byzantine fortress of{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Sdbagura, which stretches up the hill from the river and is enclosed{% endraw %}
{% raw %}by strong walls of masonry.{% endraw %}
