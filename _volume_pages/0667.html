{% raw %}On the E. bank, nearly opposite Dakkeh, lies the village of{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Kubban, with the ruins of a Fortress of the Middle Empire. This{% endraw %}
{% raw %}was enclosed by a lofty wall of sun-dried bricks, mats, and wooden{% endraw %}
{% raw %}logs, and was defended by a ditch. Within the S.E. angle ofthe{% endraw %}
{% raw %}wall lay a small temple. — Adjacent are two almost completely{% endraw %}
{% raw %}demolished Temples, one to the S., the other to the N. of the village.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Near the former is a Cisimihewn in the rock, to the E. of which are{% endraw %}
{% raw %}several tombs of the Middle Empire, excavated in the ground.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}From Kubban a route led through the extensive Wddi 'Aldki to the{% endraw %}
{% raw %}numerous gold-mines there, which were worked until the middle ages.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Granite mortars and mills and other apparatus used in the search for gold{% endraw %}
{% raw %}are still to be seen, especially in the Wddi Khawanib.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}At (38 M.) Kurteh, on the W. bank, are the ruins of a small{% endraw %}
{% raw %}temple, dedicated to Isis, on the site of an earlier shrine of the{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Middle Empire. — In the river lies the large and well-cultivated{% endraw %}
{% raw %}island of Derdr, known also as Gezlret Kurteh. Opposite the S. end{% endraw %}
{% raw %}of this island, and to the S. of the hamlets of Ofedulneh and Birbeh,{% endraw %}
{% raw %}lies the Temple of Ofedulneh, also called Temple of Maharaka. This{% endraw %}
{% raw %}marks the site of the ancient town of Hierasykaminos, which lay on{% endraw %}
{% raw %}the extreme boundary of the Egyptian kingdom under the Ptolemies{% endraw %}
{% raw %}and the Eomans. The now much ruined temple dates from the{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Eoman period, but it was left unfinished and never fully decorated{% endraw %}
{% raw %}with reliefs ; it was dedicated to Isis and Serapis. The only remains{% endraw %}
{% raw %}now left are those of a rectangular court, which was surrounded{% endraw %}
{% raw %}on three sides by covered colonnades. The main axis of the court{% endraw %}
{% raw %}runs E. and W.; the entrance is on the E. side. No columns are now{% endraw %}
{% raw %}standing except five on the S. side, once connected with each other{% endraw %}
{% raw %}by screen-walls. In the N.E. corner is a remarkable spiral staircase{% endraw %}
{% raw %}of masonry, which led to the roof of the colonnade. — About. 16 ft.{% endraw %}
