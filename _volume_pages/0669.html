{% raw %}87M. Korosko (E. bank), with a post and telegraph office, is{% endraw %}
{% raw %}the chief place in the district of the same name, embracing the{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Nile valley from Shaturmeh to Faras (p. 376). On the bank rises{% endraw %}
{% raw %}a handsome mosque. The village is situated in a plain, bounded{% endraw %}
{% raw %}by a semicircle of mountains, and it is the starting-point ofthe great{% endraw %}
{% raw %}caravan-route to Abu Hammed (p. 382; 8-10 long days' journey).{% endraw %}
{% raw %}This road strikes direct across the desert, the chief resort of the{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Ababdeh and Bisharin Beduins, avoiding the three upper cataracts,{% endraw %}
{% raw %}and cutting off the windings of the Nile. During the Mahdist insur¬{% endraw %}
{% raw %}rection Korosko was of considerable strategical importance and was{% endraw %}
{% raw %}held by a strong garrison; now, however, the forts and barracks are{% endraw %}
{% raw %}deserted.— The traveller may ascend ('/^hr.; steep and stony){% endraw %}
{% raw %}the hill of Awas el-Guardni, close to Korosko. On the summit is{% endraw %}
{% raw %}the tomb of the saint to whom the hill owes its name, a frequented{% endraw %}
{% raw %}pilgrim-resort. The view extends over the Nile valley as far as Derr.{% endraw %}

{% raw %}The E. bank of the Nile between Korosko andErmenneh (p. 369){% endraw %}
{% raw %}is the most fertile portion of Nubia. The belt of cultivable land{% endraw %}
{% raw %}along the river is comparatively broad; and it is irrigated by means{% endraw %}
{% raw %}of water-weels (sakiyeh) of curious construction. — A little beyond{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Korosko the Nile valley trends to the N.W., so that the N. wind{% endraw %}
{% raw %}which prevails in winter frequently retards the sailing-boats. On{% endraw %}
{% raw %}the left bank rises a picturesque chain of hills. On the E. bank{% endraw %}
{% raw %}lies Rika; on the W. bank is (3M.) Abu Handal.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}At a little distance from the W. bank, deeply sunk in the yellow{% endraw %}
{% raw %}desert sand, lies (9!/2 M.) the —{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Temple of 'Amada, which dates from the 18th Dyn. and was de¬{% endraw %}
{% raw %}dicated to Ee-Harmachis. Its erection and decoration were begun{% endraw %}
{% raw %}under Thutmosis III. and Amenophis II., and completed under{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Thutmosis IV. The figures of Ammon, which were defaced by{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Amenophis IV., were restored by Sethos I. In Christian times the{% endraw %}
{% raw %}temple was used as a church and the reliefs were covered with white¬{% endraw %}
{% raw %}wash, which has had the effect of preserving the original colouring,{% endraw %}
{% raw %}as we see now that the whitewash has peeled off.{% endraw %}
