{% raw %}23*/2 M. Kabd, a village on the E. bank, off which lies a large{% endraw %}
{% raw %}island known as Gezlret Ibrlm or Abu Rds. A little inland, behind{% endraw %}
{% raw %}a grove of palms and the huts of Ellesiyeh, are several Rock Grottoes.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}The largest of these dates from the reign of Thutmosis III. We first{% endraw %}
{% raw %}enter a chamber with a pointed roof. The representations on the{% endraw %}
{% raw %}walls (much defiled by bats) show Thutmosis III. in intercourse with{% endraw %}
{% raw %}various deities or sacrificing to them. Among these deities is in¬{% endraw %}
{% raw %}cluded King Usertesen III., who was worshipped in Nubia as a god.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Behind is a vaulted room, with a recess containing three statues.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}The inscriptions on the outside, beside the entrance and on the rocks{% endraw %}
{% raw %}farther on, are more interesting. On each side of the entrance are{% endraw %}
{% raw %}pompous inscriptions of Thutmosis III.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}On the W. bank, about IV2 M. inland from the village of Anibeh,{% endraw %}
{% raw %}lies an interesting rock-tomb ofthe reign of Eamses VI. (20th Dyn.),{% endraw %}
{% raw %}belonging to on official named Pennewt.{% endraw %}

{% raw %}Midway between Pennewt's tomb and the river are the remains{% endraw %}

{% raw %}New Empire. One chamber still retains fragments of the original{% endraw %}
{% raw %}paintings upon stucco that embellished the interiors.{% endraw %}
