{% raw %}As we approach the temple, we first reach a Fore Court (PL A),{% endraw %}
{% raw %}hewn out of the rock, to which a flight of steps ascends from the{% endraw %}
{% raw %}river. At the back of this space rises the imposing facade of the{% endraw %}
{% raw %}temple. The front of this terrace is embellished with rows of captives{% endraw %}
{% raw %}and a hollow cornice, and is bounded by a balustrade, bearing in¬{% endraw %}
{% raw %}scriptions in honour of Ammon and Ee-Harmachis. Behind the{% endraw %}
{% raw %}balustrade originally stood figures of hawks and small statues of{% endraw %}
{% raw %}kings. Here our attention is attracted by the four *Colossiof Ramses II.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}(PL a, b, c, d), hewn out of the cliff against which their backs are{% endraw %}
{% raw %}placed, and arranged in pairs on each side of the entrance to the{% endraw %}
{% raw %}temple. Each of these figures is over 65 ft. in height, i.e. larger than{% endraw %}
{% raw %}the Colossi of Memnon (p. 305); but in spite of the enormous scale the{% endraw %}
{% raw %}workmanship is admirable, and the countenances have a singularly{% endraw %}
{% raw %}pleasant and intelligent expression. They are best viewed from the{% endraw %}
{% raw %}sand-hill to the N. of the temple or from the river. The mild coun¬{% endraw %}
{% raw %}tenance and characteristic nose of Eamses IL are best preserved in{% endraw %}
{% raw %}the first colossus on the S. (PL a). The second colossus has un¬{% endraw %}
{% raw %}fortunately been deprived of its head and shoulders, which now lie{% endraw %}
{% raw %}on the ground before it. The upper part of the third colossus was{% endraw %}
{% raw %}patched up under Sethos I., who added the support under the{% endraw %}
{% raw %}right arm.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Upon his head the king wears the double crown; his hands rest upon{% endraw %}
{% raw %}his knees; and from his neck hangs a ring bearing the name of Ramses II.,{% endraw %}
{% raw %}which is also carved upon the upper arm and between the legs. To the{% endraw %}
{% raw %}right and left of each colossus and between their legs are smaller figures{% endraw %}
{% raw %}of other members of the royal family. To the left of the first colossus{% endraw %}
{% raw %}(PI. a) is Princess Nebt-tewe, to the right, Bent-Anat, between the legs an{% endraw %}
{% raw %}unidentified princess. To the left of the second colossus (PI. 6) is Tu-e,{% endraw %}
{% raw %}the mother of Ramses II., to the right is his wife Nefret-ere, and between{% endraw %}
{% raw %}his legs, Prince Amen-her-khopshef. On each of the thrones of colossi b{% endraw %}
{% raw %}and c, on the sides next the entrance, are two Nile-gods, wreathing the{% endraw %}
{% raw %}floral emblems (papyrus and lily) of Lower and Upper Egypt round the{% endraw %}
{% raw %}hieroglyphic symbol for 'to unite' (comp. p. lxxix), while below is a row{% endraw %}
{% raw %}of fettered prisoners, those on the left being Negroes, those on the right,{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Syrians.{% endraw %}
