{% raw %}We now enter the rock-temple, the interior of which measures{% endraw %}
{% raw %}about 180 ft. from the threshold to the back of the innermost cham¬{% endraw %}
{% raw %}ber. The first chamber, the Great Hypostyle Hall (PL B), corres¬{% endraw %}
{% raw %}ponding to the open court with covered colonnades in temples built{% endraw %}
{% raw %}in the open air, is 54 ft. broad and 58 ft. deep. The ceiling is sup¬{% endraw %}
{% raw %}ported by eight square pillars, against which stand Osiris-figures of{% endraw %}
{% raw %}the king (30 ft. high), holding the scourge and the crook. The figures{% endraw %}
{% raw %}in the N. row wear the double crown, those in the S. row the crown{% endraw %}
{% raw %}of Upper Egypt. The artistic effect of these admirably executed{% endraw %}
{% raw %}statues is very fine; the best is the fourth figure in theN. row, with its{% endraw %}
{% raw %}intelligent expression and well-preserved characteristic nose. The{% endraw %}
{% raw %}ceiling ofthe central aisle is adorned with flying vultures, those of{% endraw %}
{% raw %}the side-aisles with stars and the names of the king. The reliefs on{% endraw %}
{% raw %}the wall, still vividly coloured, are of great historical value. On{% endraw %}
{% raw %}the N. half of the Entrance Wall (PL h) the king is shown grasping{% endraw %}
{% raw %}a band of enemies by the hair and smiting them with his club, in{% endraw %}
