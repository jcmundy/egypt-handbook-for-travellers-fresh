{% raw %}Temple. ABU-SIMBEL. 34. Route. 373{% endraw %}
{% raw %}South Wall (left; PL k). At the top are five reliefs : 1. The{% endraw %}
{% raw %}king before a ram-headed god and a lion-headed goddess; 2. The{% endraw %}
{% raw %}king dedicates four rows of packages; 3. The king offers incense to{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Ptah; 4. The king kneeling under the sacred tree of Heliopolis,{% endraw %}
{% raw %}before Harmachis; Thout and Sefkhet-ebui stand close by; 5. The{% endraw %}
{% raw %}king before Ammon. Beneath are three large warlike scenes. 1. The{% endraw %}
{% raw %}king in his chariot, followed by three princes, at the storm of a{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Syrian fortress. The defenders on the battlements are pierced with{% endraw %}
{% raw %}his arrows and sue for mercy. Beneath, a herdsman flees with his{% endraw %}
{% raw %}herd towards the town. 2. The king on foot, treading upon a pros¬{% endraw %}
{% raw %}trate enemy, and piercing a Libyan with a lance so that his blood{% endraw %}
{% raw %}gushes forth. 3. The triumphal return of the king from battle with{% endraw %}
{% raw %}the captured negroes.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}North Wall (right; PL I). The subject on this wall is the{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Battle of Kadesh, the culminating event in the Hittite war, with{% endraw %}
{% raw %}which we have already become acquainted in the Ramesseum (pp. 284,{% endraw %}
{% raw %}285), and at Luxor, Karnak, and Abydos.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}In the Lower Half of the representation we see first the march{% endraw %}
{% raw %}of the Egyptian army, which consists of infantry and charioteers;{% endraw %}
{% raw %}then (between the doors to Rooms -Fand G), the Egyptian camp, with{% endraw %}
{% raw %}the shields of the soldiers arranged round it in a kind of stockade.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}The bustle of the camp is represented with great vivacity; the un¬{% endraw %}
{% raw %}harnessed horses receiving their fodder, the resting soldiers, the{% endraw %}
{% raw %}camp-followers, etc.; to the right is the royal tent. The third picture{% endraw %}
{% raw %}shows the king on his throne, holding a council of war with his{% endraw %}
{% raw %}officers. Below two spies are being compelled by blows to yield{% endraw %}
{% raw %}their secret. In the last scene (to the right) the chariots of the{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Egyptians and Hittites are already engaged in battle. — The scenes{% endraw %}
{% raw %}in the Upper Half transport us to the midst of the flight. To the left{% endraw %}
{% raw %}the king dashes in his chariot against his enemies, who have sur¬{% endraw %}
{% raw %}rounded him in their chariots; he launches his arrows against them.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}In the centre is the fortress of Kadesh, surrounded by the Orontes.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Its defenders watch the fight from the battlements. To the extreme{% endraw %}
{% raw %}right is the king in his chariot, inspecting his officers, who count{% endraw %}
{% raw %}the severed hands of the enemy and bring fettered prisoners.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Rear (W.) Wall. To the right (PL m) of the central door is{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Ramses II. leading two rows of captured Hittites before Harmachis,{% endraw %}
{% raw %}his own deified figure, and the lion-headed Wert-hekaw; to the left{% endraw %}
{% raw %}(PL n) he leads two rows of negroes before Ammon, the deified{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Ramses, and Mut. — Between the two last (S.) pillars is an in¬{% endraw %}
{% raw %}teresting Stele (PL p) of the 35th year of Ramses II., on which is a{% endraw %}
{% raw %}long inscription recording in florid terms that Ramses erected large{% endraw %}
{% raw %}edifices for Ptah of Memphis and presented rich gifts to him.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Adjoining this large hall are eight Chambers (PL F-N), probably{% endraw %}
{% raw %}used to store the temple utensils and furniture. Round the walls{% endraw %}
{% raw %}of these, which are covered with inferior reliefs, run stone benches.{% endraw %}
