{% raw %}On the W. bank, opposite AVadi Haifa, a little to the N. of a{% endraw %}
{% raw %}large and conspicuous sycamore - tree, lay the ancient town of{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Beheni, the site of which may be reached by boat from Tewfikiyeh{% endraw %}
{% raw %}in 1-2 hrs. (according to the wind). The remains of two temples are{% endraw %}
{% raw %}still extant. The North Temple, dating from the beginning of the{% endraw %}
{% raw %}12th Dyn., was built of brick, with the exception ofthe pillars in{% endraw %}
{% raw %}the first hall and the jambs of the entrance-door. It is much dam¬{% endraw %}
{% raw %}aged and sanded up. The South Temple, about 50 yds. distant, was{% endraw %}
{% raw %}exhumed in 1887 by Col. Smith, but is again largely covered with{% endraw %}
{% raw %}sand. It is in better preservation than the other, its sandstone walls{% endraw %}
{% raw %}and pillars still rising to the height of 5-6 ft. This S. temple was{% endraw %}
{% raw %}built by Thutmosis III. and Hatshepsowet and dedicated to Horus{% endraw %}
{% raw %}of Beheni. The cartouches and figures of Hatshepsowet have been{% endraw %}
{% raw %}systematically defaced or converted into those of Thutmosis II.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}(p. lxxxii). The temple stands from E. to W. Close to the river-{% endraw %}
{% raw %}bank, where traces of a quay may be made out, is a brick Pylon,{% endraw %}
{% raw %}the tower of which rises like a massive pillar. Behind is a large{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Hall, the present form of which is due to a reconstruction, probably{% endraw %}
{% raw %}at the beginning of the 20th Dynasty. The pillars and columns on{% endraw %}
{% raw %}which the roof rested bear not only the original reliefs and inscrip¬{% endraw %}
{% raw %}tions of Thutmosis III. but also numerous hieroglyphic inscriptions{% endraw %}
{% raw %}added by officials of the Middle Empire. There are also Greek,{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Carian, and Ethiopian inscriptions. On one pillar is a long and{% endraw %}
{% raw %}pompous inscription celebrating the deeds of Thutmosis III. Behind{% endraw %}
{% raw %}this hall stood the Temple Proper, the back of which adjoined the cliff,{% endraw %}
{% raw %}while on the other three sides were colonnades with 'proto-Doric'{% endraw %}
{% raw %}columns. Within were a Transverse Hall, communicating with{% endraw %}
{% raw %}the Sanctuary, and several other chambers. The admirable mural{% endraw %}
{% raw %}reliefs, many of which retain their colouring, represent the Pharaoh{% endraw %}
{% raw %}in presence of the gods. — Beside the temples lie the ruins of the{% endraw %}
{% raw %}ancient town, and on a rock to the W. are inscriptions of the Middle{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Empire. In the ground close by are ancient graves.{% endraw %}
