{% raw %}378 Route 35. SECOND CATARACT.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}*Excursion TO THE Second Cataract (5-6 hrs.). — The camels{% endraw %}
{% raw %}or donkeys should be sent in advance to the ruined temples of Beheni,{% endraw %}
{% raw %}whither the traveller proceeds by boat (see p. 377); or the start may be{% endraw %}
{% raw %}made from the W. bank immediately opposite Tewfikiyeh.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}The route at first skirts the stream, then turns inland towards{% endraw %}
{% raw %}the desert, and finally, gradually ascending, returns to the stream{% endraw %}
{% raw %}shortly before the cataract is reached.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}The Second Cataract is little inferior in scenic beauty to the{% endraw %}
{% raw %}First Cataract at Assuan. It lies at the N. end of the gorge of the{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Batn el-Hagar ('belly of stones'), which begins at the island of{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Dal, about 90 M. to the S., and through which the river descends in{% endraw %}
{% raw %}a series of rapids, over rocks of graywacke, greenstone, and granite,{% endraw %}
{% raw %}forming numerous islands in its course. The best point of view is{% endraw %}
{% raw %}the abrupt rocky hill of Abusir, on the W. bank. At the foot of the{% endraw %}
{% raw %}hill, contrasting strongly with the green of the tamarisk-trees, lies{% endraw %}
{% raw %}a chaos of glistening black boulders, through which the river forces{% endraw %}
{% raw %}its way in foaming cataracts, especially fine at the time of the in¬{% endraw %}
{% raw %}undation. In the distance to the N. are the white houses and slen¬{% endraw %}
{% raw %}der minaret of Tewfikiyeh on the E. bank, and the great sycamore{% endraw %}
{% raw %}and the pylon of the S. temple of Beheni (p. 377) on the W. bank;{% endraw %}
{% raw %}to the W., beyond the broad river, rise the mountains ofthe desert;{% endraw %}
{% raw %}to the S. lie the rocky islets among the rapids. Numerous modern{% endraw %}
{% raw %}travellers have left inscriptions on the rocks of Abusir.{% endraw %}
