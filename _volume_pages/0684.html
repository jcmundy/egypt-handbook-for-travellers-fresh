{% raw %}The Anglo-Egyptian Sudan (pp. xxxi, cii), occupying an area{% endraw %}
{% raw %}approximately equal to that of Central Europe, extends from a line{% endraw %}
{% raw %}drawn at Faras (p. 376), above Wadi Haifa, on the N., to beyond{% endraw %}
{% raw %}the tenth parallel of latitude on the S. It includes the provinces of{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Dongola, Berber, Khartum, Kassala, Kordofdn, Fashoda, and Sen¬{% endraw %}
{% raw %}naar, and the smaller districts of Wddi Haifa and Suakin. The{% endraw %}
{% raw %}British and Egyptian flags fly side by side in the Sudan, and the{% endraw %}
{% raw %}rights of the joint possessors are defined by a convention signed on{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Jan. 19th, 1899. The interest on the Sudan war-loan (p. ciii) is{% endraw %}
{% raw %}guaranteed to the British Empire, while Egypt bears the cost of{% endraw %}
{% raw %}the army and is responsible for any deficit (^E 98,000 in 1900).{% endraw %}
{% raw %}The army consists of 16 battalions of Egyptian troops and a few{% endraw %}
{% raw %}companies of British soldiers. The Governor-General and Sirdar of{% endraw %}
{% raw %}the Egyptian Army is a British officer, appointed by the Khedive{% endraw %}
{% raw %}on the recommendation of the British government, without whose{% endraw %}
{% raw %}consent he may not be dismissed. The Mudirs (p. xxxii) of the pro¬{% endraw %}
{% raw %}vinces are British officers also. At present military government and{% endraw %}
{% raw %}martial law prevail throughout the Sudan, though a civil court of two{% endraw %}
{% raw %}instances was established at Khartum in March, 1901 (comp. p. xx).{% endraw %}
{% raw %}An ad valorem tax of 20 per cent is levied upon the export of gum{% endraw %}
{% raw %}(400 tons in 1900), india-rubber, ivory, and ostrich-feathers;{% endraw %}
{% raw %}certain articles of general consumption are taxed 10 per cent of their{% endraw %}
{% raw %}value in the towns; and among the other sources of revenue are taxes{% endraw %}
{% raw %}upon date-palms, irrigation-wheels, and boats, the pedlars' tax,{% endraw %}
{% raw %}prospecting and mining licences, the house tax, and the land sale{% endraw %}
{% raw %}tax. The considerable import trade in cotton goods and articles{% endraw %}
{% raw %}for barter with the natives is in the hands of Greek and Arab{% endraw %}
{% raw %}merchants. Beside the Egyptian coins the Maria Theresa dollar,{% endraw %}
{% raw %}equivalent to about 20 pias., is current.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}The climate resembles that of Upper Egypt (p. lxi), though the{% endraw %}
{% raw %}maximum of temperature is higher and the occasional variations{% endraw %}
{% raw %}have a greater range. At Khartum the maximum heat is reached{% endraw %}
{% raw %}twice a year, in April and September (109° Fahr. on 21st Sept., 1900).{% endraw %}
