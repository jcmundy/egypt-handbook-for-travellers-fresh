{% raw %}THE SUDAN. 36. Route. 381{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Violent sand-storms are frequent from June to August, followed{% endraw %}
{% raw %}by deluges of rain, which are apt to cause fever. — Those who do{% endraw %}
{% raw %}not travel in the tourist-trains and who intend to visit other points{% endraw %}
{% raw %}besides Khartum should provide themselves with camp-beds and{% endraw %}
{% raw %}bedding, mosquito-nets, filters, cooking utensils, provisions, qui¬{% endraw %}
{% raw %}nine, etc. A servant is quite indispensable, and may be obtained{% endraw %}
{% raw %}in Assuan. — Fire-arms may not be carried without a gun-licence{% endraw %}
{% raw %}(gun 50 pias., revolver 25 pias.), to be obtained at the mudiriyeh at{% endraw %}
{% raw %}the camp at Wadi Haifa (p. 377). The possession of military (i.e.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}non-sporting) ammunition requires a special licence.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Means ok Travel. Outside the ordinary tourist-track, the traveller{% endraw %}
{% raw %}is dependent upon Camels and Sailing Boats, for both of which he has to{% endraw %}
{% raw %}provide equipment: for the former a saddle, saddle-bags, and water-skins ;{% endraw %}
{% raw %}tor the latter an awning. No package intended to be carried by a camel{% endraw %}
{% raw %}should weigh more than 250 lbs., and each package should have a pendant{% endraw %}
{% raw %}of the same weight. All arrangements should be made with the aid of{% endraw %}
{% raw %}the Ma'mUr, or head of the sub-district, and the contract should be signed{% endraw %}
{% raw %}in his presence. — It is advisable to pitch the tent for a few days at Wadi{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Haifa, so as to have opportunity of becoming accustomed to tent-life.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Visitors to Khartum usually feel languid and indolent for the first few{% endraw %}
{% raw %}days of their stay, and are apt to suffer from swollen glands.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Spoet. Excellent shooting may he enjoyed in the Sudan, subject to{% endraw %}
{% raw %}the Wild Animals Preservation Ordinance issued in 1900. Details may be{% endraw %}
{% raw %}learned from 'Notes for Travellers and Sportsmen in the Sudan' (1901;{% endraw %}
{% raw %}price 1*. or 5 pias.) and from the official Sudan Gazitle (p. 385), to be ob¬{% endraw %}
{% raw %}tained from Angelo H. Capato, agent at Wadi Haifa. An ordinary Shooting{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Licence costs £E 5 per annum; the right to'shoot buffaloes, elephants,{% endraw %}
{% raw %}hippopotami, and various large antelopes costs £E 25 per annum, besides{% endraw %}
{% raw %}a Special Fee for each animal bagged. Fowling is free. Rhinoceroses,{% endraw %}
{% raw %}giraffes, zebras, wild asses, and elands may not be hunted within the Sudan.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}For Dealings with the Natives a supply of appropriate articles for{% endraw %}
{% raw %}gifts and barter (beads, brass bracelets, etc.) is requisite. A friendly but{% endraw %}
{% raw %}tirm demeanour should be adopted, though care should be taken not to{% endraw %}
{% raw %}arouse their easily excited religious fanaticism. The Nubian tribes are{% endraw %}
{% raw %}inclined to be suspicious and malicious, but they are cowardly and rarely{% endraw %}
{% raw %}venture upon open hostility. The black Nuba and Bantu tribes are childish,{% endraw %}
{% raw %}lazy, and untrustworthy.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Literature. The traveller may farther consult: Count von Gleichen's{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Handbook of the Sudan (2 vols.; London, 1898); R. Statin's Fire and Sword{% endraw %}
{% raw %}in the Sudan (London; 1696); Sleevens's With Kitchener to Khartum (Lon¬{% endraw %}
{% raw %}don; 1898); Wingate's Mahdiism and the Egyptian Sudan (London; 1900);{% endraw %}
{% raw %}and Sir C. Wilson's From Korti to Khartum (London, 1885). All these may{% endraw %}
{% raw %}he obtained in Cairo. The Sudan Almanack (Is. or 5 pias.) will be on sale{% endraw %}
{% raw %}in and after 1902.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}36. From Wadi Haifa to Khartum.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}575 M. Sudan Military Railway. A Train de Lu.ru, with dust-proof{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Sleeping and dining cars, runs twice a week from Wadi Haifa to Khartum{% endraw %}
{% raw %}in 28-35 hrs. (fare £E 10; return-fare £E16; meals 70 pias per day).{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Return-tickets are valid only by the same train, which ieturns from{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Khartum three days after its arrival. — An Ordinary Train performs the{% endraw %}
{% raw %}journey daily in 40-50 hrs. (becoming a Mail Train on Tues. and Frid. on{% endraw %}
{% raw %}the arrival of the steamboats irrm Assuan). The 'first-class' fare is £E 2.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}61 pias., for which the traveller has at his disposal a roomy, eight-wheeled{% endraw %}
{% raw %}baggage-truck, in which he may erect his bed and cooking apparatus.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Provisions should be taken from Wadi Haifa, and also water and water-{% endraw %}
{% raw %}coolers (sir), as the crowd at the watering-stations is usually very great.{% endraw %}
