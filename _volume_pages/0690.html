{% raw %}Khartum (1240 ft.), the capital of the Sudan and the residence{% endraw %}
{% raw %}of the governor-general, is situated in 15° 40' N. lat., on the left{% endraw %}
{% raw %}bank of the Blue Nile, immediately above its confluence with the{% endraw %}
{% raw %}White Nile (comp. p. xlix). The name ('elephant's trunk') refers to{% endraw %}
{% raw %}the shape of the long peninsula that ends on the N.W. in the Ras{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Khartum (p. 388). The town was built in 1823-30 hy Mohammed{% endraw %}
{% raw %}'Ali, and quickly rose to prosperity as the southernmost depot of{% endraw %}
{% raw %}the trade of Egypt, so that it is said to have had 70,000 inhab. in{% endraw %}
{% raw %}1882. During the rebellion of the Mahdi (Mohammed Ahmed){% endraw %}
{% raw %}General Gordon, who was despatched hither by the British gov¬{% endraw %}
{% raw %}ernment to withdraw the garrisons in the Sudan, entered the town in{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Feb., 1884, and defended it until Jan. 26th, 1885. The town was{% endraw %}
{% raw %}reduced to ruins by the Mahdists; but the work of rebuilding it was{% endraw %}
{% raw %}begun in 1898. Most of the houses are of brick, though free use{% endraw %}
{% raw %}is also made of Kerreri sandstone, and are surrounded by fine{% endraw %}
{% raw %}gardens.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Along the bank of the Nile runs the River Avenue, a promenade{% endraw %}
{% raw %}over 2 M. in length, planted with palms and other trees. At its E.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}extremity lies the hamlet of Burri; and as we proceed thence towards{% endraw %}
{% raw %}the W. we pass successively the British Barracks (PL h), a fragment{% endraw %}
{% raw %}of the old town-wall (el-Istaham), the Gordon Memorial College{% endraw %}
{% raw %}(PL g; see below), and the Hospital (PL f). The Gordon Memorial{% endraw %}
{% raw %}College, for which Lord Kitchener obtained the necessary funds by{% endraw %}
{% raw %}public subscription throughout the British Empire, is a large and{% endraw %}
{% raw %}nandsome building in which native youths are trained by English{% endraw %}
{% raw %}and Egyptian teachers for an official eareer. The gardens and villas{% endraw %}
{% raw %}of the British officials add a picturesque feature to the scene, many{% endraw %}
{% raw %}ofthe houses being built in the verandah cottage style. That farthest{% endraw %}
{% raw %}to the E. belongs to Slatin-Pasha. Farther to the W. are military{% endraw %}
{% raw %}stores and the workshops of the Water Transport Department; and{% endraw %}
{% raw %}finally a school of forestry. The Palace of the Sirdar (PL b) is built{% endraw %}
